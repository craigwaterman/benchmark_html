Natural Hair Icon: Taijah Shanee’ Walker

May 1, 2014
2 Flares 2 Flares ×

Tell us about yourself:
Hello there! My full name is Taijah Shanee’ Walker, pronounced Asia with a T and Sha-Nay (like Shanaynay on Martin). I am from Carteret New Jersey and I attended William Paterson University for four years and graduate May 2013 with a bachelors in Marketing.
Life after college consists of me having three jobs, being a Youtube Vlogger (2tadita) and modeling. I also have an independent business called Do it All Diva (doitalldiva.weebly.com) which consists mainly of me styling all types of hair but I also do nail designs and makeup.

How long have you been natural? And why?
I’ve been natural my entire life, because my mother never gave in to my begging of getting a perm. It was not until my junior year of high school that I stopped straightening my hair every two weeks and began to embrace my natural curls.

How would you describe your hair?
My hair is definitely a mixture between coarse kinky and silky curly. To explain further, the curls in the back of my hair and my roots are tighter and do not retain moisture as much as my curls in the front of my head and my ends. My hair is my favorite accessory on my body. I’ve always loved different styles, whether it be with my natural hair or a protective style. My favorite part about having these different hairstyles is that I have a different personality with each of them. When I have blonde hair, I’m sassy! When I’m a red head I’m seductive, and having black hair is very classy and chic.

What is your hair regimen (including fav products)?
My hair regimen is fairly simple. Every Sunday I use Mane n’ Tail’s Deep Moisturizing Conditioner, as soon as I enter the shower I let it work its magic in resorting the moisture in my hair and making my hair manageable again. Literally, all I do is apply the conditioner on my ends up to my scalp, give my scalp a small massage and leave my hair alone as I shower. I rinse out the conditioner before I leave the shower.
Sometimes during the deep conditioning, I use a shampoo (yes while the conditioner is still in my hair). I only apply the shampoo on my scalp and massage my scalp during that time, then rinse immediately after. I have a fear of the shampoo getting on my strands and making my hair dryer than what it is, that’s why I apply it on top of my conditioner. Shea Moisture Raw Shea Butter Moisture Retention Shampoo is really great because it does not dry my hair out. I rarely use shampoo, only after a protective style or if I have product buildup.
After I rinse out the conditioner, I use an old T-shirt to wrap my hair in, so water will not go all over the bathroom. My hair dries so fast! I literally keep the T-shirt on my hair for one minute. If my hair completely dries, I spray it again with rain water (I have a video about rain water benefits on my Youtube Channel), making it a little damp, not too wet.
I then use Cantu Shea Butter Leave in Conditioning Repair Cream, followed by an oil (coconut or extra virgin olive oil usually) then I use Nature’s Blessings Hair Pomade to seal everything. I’ve been using Nature’s blessing since I was a baby and I really feel that it has helped with my hair growth throughout the years. I repeat the same process on Wednesdays, if my hair is really dry.

Have you always liked your natural hair? If not, how did you come to embrace it?
I did not always like my natural hair. I grew up in a town where most of the girls had long, pretty straight hair, so that is what I wanted. I would get tired that it took hours to style my curls and beg my mother for perm, and thank goodness she never gave in! It was not until my junior year of high school that my boyfriend at the time asked me to keep it curly. He was in love with my natural hair, and I was in love with him so I began to wear wash and gos to school everyday, not to mention it was so much easier and faster to do my hair in the morning. The compliments I received were awesome and I realized I never appreciated my natural hair until others pointed out how unique it is.

What are some of your problem areas (if any) that require extra care and attention?
My problem areas are my scalp because it is so dry and since I recently bleached my ends, those are dry as well. I make sure I pay special attention to those areas, applying more products to ensure that I do not get a lot of breakage.

What are your staple hair styles?
My staple hair style is the afro puff! I love to just let my hair BE FREE! It looks the best on day three because it is so BIG and FLUFFY and most of the curls are still in-tact! I also love the poof in a ponytail because I don’t like my hair in my face all the time and it can be a very elegant look.

What have your experiences been as a ‘natural.’ Any memorable reactions from family or others?
My most memorable experience as a natural is that I get asked what my nationality is ALL THE TIME. Its quite funny when people try to guess. I usually get that I can be a West Indian or Dominican mix because of my hair texture. Truthfully, I am unaware of my ethnicity and I do plan to find out exactly what I am.

What is your hair goal?
My hair goal is to reach waist length. I know it is really hard to achieve that length and I will not have the same curl pattern, but I love long hair! I will miss my afro but having waist length hair will all be worth it.

Hair Crush?
My hair crushes are two YouTube Vloggers, Chime Edwards and the late Longhairdontcare2011. Have you seen these ladies’ hair?! BEAUTIFUL, long natural hair that is either waist length or longer. They give me inspiration to keep taking care of my natural hair so that I can achieve that length.

Anything else you would like the readers to know? Inspirational words?
Embracing my natural hair has really allowed me to be more comfortable with myself. Before wearing my hair curly, I was so caught up in trying to keep my hair straight and like everyone else’s that all I was doing was not appreciating the beauty of my curls and damaging them. I believe that coincides with myself. I no longer want to emulate anyone else. I embrace everything that God has blessed me with. Others see that I exude confidence in myself, and that’s what attracts them to me. So, be yourself, love yourself and love your natural!
