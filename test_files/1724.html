    I've always loved making a difference. I would always join volunteer groups that help out thru the community in high school & college.

At that time I wanted to find a way to help my cousin and his family, his son had leukemia for the 2nd time in his young life, 1st time - 1 yrs old, 2nd time - 4 yrs old. I wanted to find a way to help them and I did. :)

    Also, with another company I work for... we get to pick an organization to donate money to each year and because two of my co-workers & myself knows or has a kid that has leukemia or brain cancer... we got our manager to donate it to the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society in our city. Also once a month my family and I make dinner for the families at the local Ronald McDonald House, they were also a big help when my nephew had to get treatment in Arizona.

    Last year I also participated with a organization focused on bringing families together, I helped them plan a weekend event and get volunteers to help with the fair. We had to do everything from organizing & getting vendors, entertainment, advertisement, etc etc etc... It was so much fun, yet so stressful... but I loved doing it either way. I wish I could have learned more about event planning. That's one of the things I want to do in the future.
