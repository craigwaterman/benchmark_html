Life & Times of a Twenty-something Momma

Wednesday, September 24, 2008

Time for a blog...

I love to write... always have, probably always will. I used to write poems, then I wrote stories. Journals were my outlet for the longest time, and if I'm mad, you'll likely get a letter (I can't call them notes because they're MUCH too long for that) instead of my trying to talk to you. I can get my thoughts out much easier via my fingertips on a computer keyboard than I can via my mouth, which tends to run a little faster than my brain can process.

So that's how I got where I am. I have lots to sort through, lots to think about, and even more to babble on & on about.


Who am I?

I'm a mommy... first and foremost, it's my best accomplishment to date. I love that little blonde guy more than anything else in the world, and am so thrilled to be along for the ride that is and will continue to be his life.

I'm a wife... sometimes not the best one, or even a mediocre one, but I am still a wife. I married my husband 3 years ago, and it's been a rollercoaster ever since, but no matter what, I love him & that's what matters deep down.

I'm a daughter... my relationship with my father is improving, but I'm still reminded fairly often about why it needed improving in the first place. My relationship with my mother is made even better by the fact that we're both mothers now. I have a lot to learn about being selfless, and I'm hoping she'll teach me. It's not an easy task, but she's not one to shy away from a challenge.

I'm a sister... my first 'job,' and the one I hold almost as closely to my heart as being a mommy. I'm the big sister to a 19 yr old named Kendra, and a 12 yr old named Braydon. Mess with them, and I'll break your kneecaps mobster style. I'm the protector, a role I often take too seriously, but I'm ok with that & most of the time, they are too.

I'm a granddaughter... to some of the best people in the world. I've lost my paternal grandfather & grandmother in the passed 2 yrs. My Grandma Louise wasn't my 'blood' grandma, but she was the closest thing I had to a grandma on my dad's side, and I'm thankful every day for that. My Grandpa Ken was the most amazing guy... he makes me proud to be his granddaughter. Every day, I miss him more, and wish that I had more time. They came into my life (and back into my dad's after 10+ yrs) when I was 5, and I lost him when I was 25... it just doesn't seem like enough. My mom's parents are the most amazing grandparents I could ask for. My Grandma Helen has attempted to give me everything I've wanted... she's been a friend, a sounding board, and most importantly, a soft place to fall when I just need to 'be' myself and not put up the tough girl exterior. She's currerntly battling Leukemia, and it's been hard on me, because I can't get to see her enough and I feel guilty, but she knows I love her, even if I can't get by there all the time like I used to. My Grandpa Bob is great, always available for a laugh, and there to make you learn 'life's lessons,' but willing to help if needed.

I'm a friend... sometimes not a good one, but I am a friend. I have a couple of great girlfriends who I am so thankful for. If it weren't for them, sometimes I'd go crazy - lol.

That's a little about me, and if you're reading this, I'm glad you've stopped into read... I may be boring at times, but hang out a bit, it gets better. :)
