Guest Post || Finding My Blogging Voice

Hello there, I trust you all had a fabulous New Year and have recovered from any hangovers! Today the beautiful Daphn is guest posting for me. I have been reading her blog ever since she contacted me about doing a guest post and I love it! Today she will be touching on how she found her blogging voice which I think is something all us bloggers can relate to! 

Hi there lovely readers of Sarah’s beautiful blog! I’m Daphne from The Daphne Files, a personal blog that captures my life’s chronicles. I’m so happy with this opportunity that Sarah gives to tell a little about myself and my blog. 

I’m a 40 year old girl from The Netherlands, living together with my husband, my teenage son and daughter and our two feline companions. While I absolutely love living here, I’m a sucker for all things English, especially English detective shows on TV (Midsomer Murders is definitely my favorite ;-). I’m a stay at home mom and a digital designer (blogger templates, clipart, logos etc.) in the hours that my kids are at school.

Some of you might remember that Sarah offered a similar opportunity to guest post while she was away last Spring/Summer. I thought about joining her guest post team back then, but the reason why I didn’t was that my blog didn’t feel like me. Or actually, I didn’t feel like myself back then.
Blogging has really been a journey to find my voice (as it is for most bloggers) and along that journey I didn’t only start to find my voice, but also learned a lot more about myself. One of the things I learned is that I’m a better writer when I feel happy. While a lot of artists make their best art work in times of trouble and turmoil, my writing skills seem to thrive when I’m happy and in a good place. And last Spring, I just wasn’t. And it showed through my writing.

Actually, it showed so much, that when I finally began to see sunshine again by the end of Summer the blog just didn’t feel like me anymore. The writing, the subjects of my blog posts, the design… I looked at it and thought: it needs a makeover, a very big makeover. And so I did. I started from scratch, with a new name, a new URL and a new design. Of course it comes in handy that I’m a designer myself, so the layout of the blog was pretty easy, but then it was time to write the first blog post again on a crisp and clear blog. Slowly but surely I’m finding my way again in blogging and when I look at my new blog I’m happy with what I see and it feels like me. Exactly what blogging should be like.
And this time I started the blog for me, myself and I 😉 Sounds pretty selfish right? But I guess most bloggers start a blog as some kind of a diary, to chronicle their adventures in life. Of course it’s great when other people follow along with these stories and become faithful readers of those chronicles, but a blog often starts out as a creative outlet of the blogger, especially the more personal blogs.

Right now I’m letting the blog develop naturally. I write about my life and the things that keep me busy. I’m not a big adventurer or traveler, but there’s lots of beauty to be found in the region where I live and with two teens in the house it’s never dull. You will probably read about autism on my blog every now and then, because both my kids are affected by it. My daughter definitely in a milder way than my son, who will probably not be able to lead a ‘normal’ life in the future. Although it’s an important fact in my life, I try not to let it control my life 24/7. Life itself and my kids are so much more than autism alone.
So if you want to take a peak at my life’s chronicles, you are more than welcome to visit The Daphne Files . All you have to do is use coupon code SARAH at the check out when you decide to buy a premade Blogger template.
Love, Daphne
