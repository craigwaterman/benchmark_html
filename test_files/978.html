“Thinner”- Horror, Thriller

I took a body fat test and my results say my body fat is at 38 percent; I’ve accepted that I am “skinny fat.” I’m not too sure how this happened…maybe it’s due to my lack of outdoor activities, or maybe it’s the fact that I watch about 5-7 movies a week, or maybe it’s because I have a microwave in my movie room because I just can’t walk ALL the way down stairs to heat up my food while watching a movie, also known as laziness.  I think I’ll blame genetics, big bones and Obama. I am now on a quest to lose 10% body fat.

What better way to motivate me then the movie “Thinner”. Family man Billy is an obese lawyer who just won a big case. He decides to go out and celebrate, after an evening of eating and drinking, his wife helps him celebrate with some driving fellatio. Billy is very distracted and accidentally hits and kills an old gypsy women. Since Billy is buddy-buddy with the judge and police officer in the case, it easily gets thrown out and no one is charged. While exiting the courthouse, a family member of the gypsy rub’s Billy’s cheek and says “thinner”. Soon, Billy is losing weight very fast. He is eating ravenously but just keeps losing weight.  Billy and his family start to worry that it could be a health condition making him lose weight so fast. But he starts to wonder if the gypsies put a curse on him and visits the judge and police officer to make sure they are alright as well. Turns out Billy’s problems aren’t as bad as theirs and now needs to figure out how to stop this curse.

This movie is based off Stephen King’s novel so I had a strong feeling I would like it. It’s a thriller and drama more than a horror movie, nothing scares you but entertains you in a dark way. The plot is very consuming while watching Billy race against time and his weight. I really enjoyed Robert John Burke who played Billy. Not only does Billy’s body transform, but also his personality. His jolly and upbeat nature is now threatening, fearful and desperate. Billy’s body fat makeup is very obvious and fake; it took a long time in film for body fat suits and makeup to actually look real, so try to ignore it.

Watch it.

Barko

Maybe I need to work out like this.
