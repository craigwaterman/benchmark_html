Gastroscopy/biopsies: a pain like no other

Posted on September 13, 2015 by carolle

The day came and I was not looking forward to it. I never ever had this test done to me before and god knows I have to go through many tests with that health of mine. I had opted for a sedation because I know myself and having something down my throat was  scary. The nasoscopy is already not my cup of tea.  But the doctor opted for the throat spray because of my asthma and all the breathing and heart complications. I was not reassured but I hoped for something smooth. I had to wait a little for the results of my INR before the endoscopy. All was ok with the INR so we went ahead.
Well, it was all but smooth. Swallowing a tube , the gag reflex set in and I started to go into a panic for a few minutes. After I settled down the doctor carried on the endoscopy. It is not easy to keep control of your body when you are basically having to breathe through your nose and feel that tube scrapping your oesophagus.
But it was a walk in the park compared to what came next and that was taking many samples for biopsies. A lot of sample were taken and each time I could feel my inside tearing apart. So painful that my eyes started to cry. My breathing ended up all over the place and my lungs were not happy. I am glad the doctor noted I had a difficult time with that procedure. Frankly I hope not to have to go through this again because I will be very stressed.
Everyone was nice at the hospital though, nurses and doctor, from start to end.
I had two tough days because of the pain. The day of the endoscopy I was not able to eat anything too consistent or even drink , it was so painful. I think it will take a bit of time to heal. I eat smooth food and I can drink. The pain is here but bearable.
I just have to wait for the biopsies results and that means stress in my head but I will try to occupy my mind with better thoughts. Meditation may come handy ;)
