My fight with acne...



When people hit puberty some are lucky and only get the odd spot. or you can be like me and get full blown uncontrollable acne. It got worse as I got older and moved down my body.

it started off as a few spots in my hairline, it proceeded to cover my forehead, cheeks, chest and back. a few of the spots were as sore as they looked. 



I know some people get it a lot worse than me but either way it still makes you feel self conscious about your appearance.

often I would hear people say "I would hate to have loads of spots" "i'm glad I only get the odd one"
when I heard them say things about that it always made me feel horrible about myself because I thought they were talking about me. they probably were but hey. 

when the spots first started I went to my doctor and he put me on the pill and gave me a cream to put on the spots. I think it was something like duac cream.
a few months had gone and the spots were still getting worse so my doctor changed the cream to a substance called zineryt. I had to put this on the spots at night time. I fount that this really dried my skin out and made it sore so i went back to my doctor, he put me back on the duac cream and changed my pill to dianette. I was on this for about 3 years and not much had changed, my skin was scared and still covered in spots. the pill had made me put weight on and I wasn't feeling great at all.

to top off my confidence issues when I started my hair and media make up course we had to work on each other which meant taking our makeup off. all of the other girls of course had great skin and their makeup always looked good. when I had my makeup done it looked good but I never had the 'flawless base'.

as well as this we were invited to primary schools to help the year 6 girls with hair and make up tips. we had to tell them about skin care, we would say to them 'always make sure you look after your skin and remove make up correctly otherwise you could end up with very oily and spotty skin' and of course that would be the time all the young girls will look at me with disgust as if I was dirty and they were reluctant for me to do there make-up.

It was about this time my doctor finally referred me to the dermatologist at the hospital and I was put on strong drugs, I was told I would have to come back every four weeks to have my liver checked as the tablets could damage it. Also I had to sign a sort of contract saying I would not get pregnant while on the tablets so every four weeks I had to have a pregnancy test too.

I was on the tablets for four months and my skin has cleared so well.


I have an appointment at the hospital tomorrow to see if I need another few weeks on the tablets or whether or not I can start laser treatment for the scarring.

fingers crossed it's the end :)

all I would say to people is that it does get better eventually. and if you are one of these 'lucky' people who have no spots be mindful of who you mention it to or who's around you when you talk about your flawless skin as it really does get people down.
