Travel broadens the mind

Muzungu

August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

I’m sat here on the edge of my seat, a cool glass of mango juice in one hand and a newspaper in the other.  I’m sat overlooking the quiet tame walkway that is Main Street, Jinja, Uganda.  The typical free spirited Muzungu’s (white travelers) disembarked from their yoga class behind me here at Source Cafe.  The sun glistening from my German stein with a gentle breeze brushing through my braided hair.  Jinja, the source of the Nile! Home to local Ugandans, Ex pats, Kiwi rafters, charity founders and gap year kids.  A place where there is exhilarating highs yet tranquil evenings.  With boda boda’s (motorbikes) weaving in and out of traffic to matatu’s overpacked with people similar to that of a kid squeezing sweets in a tube to avoid extra payment. 

A short boda ride costing just 50 pence along with the interference of Ugandans continuously asking “How are you”?  You arrive at the infamous river Nile, New Zealanders    drug like adrenaline hotspot.  Travelers from around the world disembark to encounter the fearful, whirlwind ride that is rafting down the Nile.  Or to feel the indescribable feeling as you Bungee jump into the legendary river.

The crisp warm air in the evening is delicious especially over a Ugandan beverage in a newly established bar.  ‘The Office‘ a new Ugandan establishment.  Share light competitions with the locals at pool, or ‘white girl‘ dance on stage with famous reggae singers.  The atmosphere is electric, like a scene from a street dance movie.  Strangers crowding round in a circle, taking it in turns to show off their moves in the middle of the group.  I wiped the sweat from my forehead and scoped the room, watching the african beats, the footwork and the indescribable rhythm that Africans are born with.  Singing along to an African interpreted Michael Jackson song, dancing until my feet were aching.  
Outside main street, your a world away from the internet cafes and markets but into a world with dusty roads with no pavements.  This is a world where children play with plastic bottles, adults wear rags, chickens wander fields, meat is surrounded by flies hung all day in the heat and where shoes cease to exist.

Yet the smiles of the children, the happiness of the locals and the care free persona of Muzungus makes this one of the most extraordinary places that I have encountered.  This is a world I would much rather live in, I am a muzungu… “How are you?”

The best travel tattoos

August 24, 2014August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

I love travel and I am all for sharing the love of travel with association with body art.  Although I a not a fan of most tattoos, even though I have a tattoo myself.  I am all for self expression and in particular using tattoos as a way of expressing the love for travel.  

Here are some of my favourite ideas for travel tattoos.

…Of course the first image would be of my own tattoo.  It says ‘Obruni’ which in Ghanian means white person.  I went travelling to Ghana two years ago and met my two best friends there.  We lived and worked in the school and were greeted every morning by the school children shouting ‘Obruni’.  And when we met up in England as both a friendship and memory thing we all got obruni tattooed on us .  It not only represents a friendship, but also the fact that this was the first place I have travelled by myself and hopefully the first of many.  My friends and the country holds a dear place in my heart and that is why I have been tattooed with Obruni.  

I love the word wanderlust, as the word just represents the kind of person I am… I have always wanted to have this tattooed, until a friend told me that everyone has it done and it’s kind of a hippy thing.  I also love compass tattoos, this is why this is one of my favourite tattoo ideas

How better to represent travel then to get a world tattoo, I love these tattoos.  But I don’t think that I would be brave enough to get it.  I have a massive obsession with maps, and this would be perfect for me if I had the balls to do it.  

Aeroplanes- Believe it or not, but I am scared of flying yet I long to travel the world.  Weird I know! But I love the idea of plane tattoos symbolising to travel the world.  I love this particular image because it’s small and cute.  

I love random word tattoos, they are small quirky and just great fun.  Adventure is what everyone longs to do, and I’m sure that this person has seen many an adventure.  The writing and positioning is so random and creative, that I love it.

I was originally tempted to get a hot air balloon tattoo, but the ones I have seen are massive… and I would not like one that big.  For me it would obviously represent travel, but also a memory.  I took a hot air balloon ride over south Africa, and it was in that very moment when I looked down and saw the shanty towns and children in rags running… that there was poverty like this in the world. It was my first experience of poverty.. and from then on I have been a keen advocate in charities across Africa.  Maybe one day, I’ll get this tattoo!

Probably one of the most common travel tattoos is the anchor, but I think it looks amazing and can also represent being grounded and one with the earth.  

I love this tattoo, it reminds me of the quote ‘A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step’.  

I love elephants, and I was tempted to get an elephant tattoo.. I have rode elephants many times, for me it is a big part in the representation of travel.  I particularly love this tattoo, I saw it on an Urban Outfitters model on their website and fell in love with it.  The cartoon is fun and quirky.

And finally… 

The Hamsa hand tattoo… I first came across this symbol of good luck in Morocco and fell in love with the symbol.  It as a tattoo looks amazing, my can be quite common 

I think that when deciding on my travel tattoo, there wasn’t many out there.. so I would love to see or hear about yours… get in touch! 

My top things to see and do in London

August 15, 2014August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

I have just returned from a fantastic couple of days in london.  This is my first time visiting the city, and I am in love with everything about it.  I love the cafes on street corners, the variety of people, the history and art and of course the shops! 

I was very limited to what I could do for the couple of days.. but here are a few things I think you should see in London, some of them I have! Some I haven’t! 

Buckingham Palace

A must see to experience where the Queen lives. Tours are also available, which would be well worth it.  I’ll be sure to go on a tour next time I visit London.  Although there is a huge crowed of people it’s lovely to sit on the statue in front and watch the world go by.

A west end show

There are so many shows available, make sure you go to the right ticket office to get discounted prices and excellent seats.  I went to see a show called ‘Once’ which was fantastic with amazing music.  You can’t come to london without seeing a show! 

The Underground.. This is fantastic! Get yourself an Oyster card, you can get this from the station! It’s easy to use and quick.. although you can end up spending a fortune! 

Harrods- Even if you can’t afford anything in there, you have to have a look and experience the overpriced handbags a must for a sightseeing day in London.

Number 10 Downing street- Although I am yet to visit, I think that this would be something that i want to see when visiting London 

Tower of London is also something I haven’t seen but would love to

Westminster Abby and big ben is also on my top list 

Finally shopping- There are many shopping districts but the charity shops in london are amazing.. you have to experience these!  

I’ll hopefully be revisiting London very soon, and I’ll be sure to fill you in on how fantastic it is 

Top ten things you must experience in… UGANDA

August 5, 2014August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

Uganda is such a beautiful country that is positioned in East Africa.  The country is full of vast greenery, a variety of bird species and a big list of things to do whilst there.  I have just returned from a Ugandan adventure and here are my top things to experience whilst you are out there.

1. Bungee jumping in Jinja.  There are a vast variety of things to do in Uganda, but probably the number one would be to do a bungee jump in the river Nile.  Otherwise known as Nile High Bungee.  The best place to stay when doing this is ‘Adrfit’

2.Safari.. I have done this twice now in Uganda and it’s a great experience.  Go with the travel company Safari Wildz.  They will sort out accommodation and food! it’s a fantastic trip!  

The accomodation on safari varies, I stayed in luxury tents which were very comfortable and sociable with a camp fire and a cook on site. Unwind in the day by the pool or enjoy african dancing and a drink at the bar.  A fantastic experience

3. Sipi Falls, although the journey can be quite long, the views outside your dorm room window are breathtaking, which is well worth the trip.  You can also abseil down the waterfall and hike back up to your accomodation.  There is also opportunities for coffee making and rock climbing… something you have to experience whilst in Uganda.

4. White Water rafting in Jinja. Rafting down the Nile is scary, exhilarating and literally breathtaking.  There are opportunities to kyak down the rapids to.  You can choose from Grade 1-Grade 5.  When doing this stay at Adrfit or Nile River Explorers. 

5. Hairy Lemon island.  Although I have no yet been to this amazing place, I’ve heard wonderful things and heard it is a backpackers dream.. check it out and let me know!

6. Murchison falls.  Visit the waterfalls at Murchison, the sites are breathtaking.

7. Clubbing in Uganda.. check out the nightlife, especially in Jinja.. I recommend ‘The Office’, ‘Casino’ and ‘Queens palace’.

8. Yoga.. pop into Source cafe in Jinja, or NRE and try your hand at Yoga.  Doing Yoga on the grass outside with the beautiful scenery is both relaxing and beautiful.

9. Quad biking- Although I am yet to experience Quad biking in Uganda.. my friends all say that it’s fun and energetic! try your hand at it!

10. Horse riding.  A fun day out horse riding and exploring Uganda and the greenery.  There are also overnight horse riding available, where you sleep under the stars alongside your horse.

All in all Uganda is a fantastic place to visit, I have been twice now and look forward to visiting one of the most relaxed beautiful countries of Africa.  

If anyone has any other suggestions of what to do in Uganda.. please share your ideas! 

My Top ten Travel Movies

July 31, 2014August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

I love to travel and love movies that have travel at the heart of them….

There are not many travel movies around, that inspire you to pack up your stuff and run away.  But here are some of my favourites.

1. Eat, Pray, Love

Probably one of my favourite movies, Elizabeth Gilbert both the author and the character undergo’s a journey of self discovery and on the way she eats, prays and falls in love.  It has all three of my favourite places; Rome (where I have let been, but want to), India (my favourite country in the world, where I have been many times) and Bali (The destination I long to see).  The book is equally as fantastic.  And Elizabeth Gilbert is probably one of the most inspirational people to me.  She also has a follow on book called ‘Commited’ Which I am yet to read.

Set: United states, Rome, India and Bali

2. The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel

A film with an all star staff set in the most beautifully cultural country on earch.. India.  This film makes travel lovers eager to visit for the first time or again to the inspirational country of India. The hotel in the film is actually a hotel in Jaipur.  I was looking myself to go there.. but it’s quite pricey.  One day eh!

Set: India and England

3.  Life of Pi

 

Both the book and film are equally as brilliant, although not much travel! The film at the beginning shows the beauty of India and the French colony interpretation.  The actors and storyline is fantastic.. a film not to be missed.

Set: India  

4. Slumdog Millionaire

A fantastic movie that explores the lives of the orphaned child living in the poverty of India.  And yes, maybe I do have an obsession with India. The story explores the millionaire winner telling the story of how he knows all of the answers.  Amazing to watch especially if you want to know the real India.

Set: India

5. The Bucket List 

A great inspirational film to motivate you to do things you want to do before you die.  Two great actors and an incredible story.

Set: All over the place

6. Vicky, Christina, Barcelona 

 A fantastic story that explores love, art, sex, tourism, Barcelona and passion.  A story with a bit of a twist exploring the relationships between friendships, men and Barcelona.  A fiery travel film.

Set: Barcelona

7. My Life in Ruins

 A feel good girly film, about a tour guide who feels hopeless about life and love.

Set: Greece

8. Mamma Mia

The picturesque views and sights on this little island are just beautiful.  A fantastic feel good film with Mamma Mia classics.

Set: Greek island

9. Under the Tuscan Sun

A fantastic film that gets you in the mood to spontaneously buy a house and do it up and live a life in the sun.

Set: Italy

10. Letters to Juliet

A lovely film set in Italy, where you can see the beauty of the country down the cobbled streets.

Set: Italy  

 

Nile High Bungee

July 28, 2014August 24, 2014 awomanconsumedbywanderlustLeave a comment

I could feel the breeze through my hair, the nerves conjuring up, and the sweat from my forehead beginning to drip down my face. “How did I end up here?”, I continue to repeat the ever fearful words. My mouth almost dry with a mixture of excitement and nerves, the shouting of spectators in the distance and the cords round my ankles, feeling heavier… “How did I end up here”. Looking down from a swinging platform into the murky water of the Nile. The urge for adventure got me here, but the nerves and butterflies in my belly wouldn’t forget to flutter. The local gentleman next to me, shirtless and exposed to the burning sun shouted to me; “Smile for the camera, give the camera a big wave”. I waved half heartedly to the spec like image in the distance, ‘The zoom on his camera must be good’ I remember thinking. I’ve been stood on this platform looking down over the edge for a good ten minutes. I can feel the eyes of the brave lined up behind me. Then the chant I didn’t want to hear… “Three, two, one, …. Bungee”. I stopped in my tracks, and stood still. Frozen from nerves and the fear that I was going to fall possibly to my death. “How did I end up here,” They chanted again, “Three, two, one… Bungee”. I stood still, pretending to admire the view. I contemplated, taking the ropes off my ankles and walking back down the steps. But adrenaline took over and I glanced over the edge of the platform one last time. The recognisable words were chanted again… “Three, two, one, Bungee”….. They pushed me! The feeling was indescribable, falling from a height with the feeling of nothing stopping you. I screamed and shouted until I hit the nile. Under I went in the great pool of water with half my body consumed by the Nile. The elastic bounced, and I was again thrown up in the air.. a somewhat similar experience to flying I would imagine. As they lowered me down, a small boat came to drag me down from the sky. “Grab the oar”… the men shouted to me. As I spun round I attempted to grab the oar, but my dizzy state meant that I was scrambling round trying to regain my focus for several bewildering minutes. After moments of indescribable adventure I took a boda boda (motorbike) back to the school I had been staying at. I arrived outside the classroom, my home for a month. And stared at the mattress on the floor. No electricity and no water. “How did I end up here”? I began to say to myself… Yet, I’ve decided to do it all again next year.
