Food and the Sex of Your Baby

Does eating certain foods mean you are more likely to have a boy, or vice versa? There is a lot of folklore associated with this concept. The science behind it tell us that the sex of the baby is determined at the moment the egg and sperm join during conception, and that nothing during the pregnancy can change that. But after decades of research, there is still some controversy over whether or not external factors can determine the baby’s sex.

In the past, some studies have shown that a diet high in sodium and potassium (generally including a lot of vegetables, bananas, fish and meat) may favor having a boy. A diet high in magnesium and calcium, such as milk, beans, cereals, cheese and nuts, may mean a girl.

Regardless, it is imperative to eat a healthy, balanced diet when you are trying to get pregnant, and throughout pregnancy.

To Swim or not to Swim

If you are considering taking your infant swimming, or enrolling him in swimming classes, there are many angles to consider. The general consensus is that swimming is a healthy, beneficial activity good for an infant’s body and mind – and potentially a great bonding activity between children and parents. It is important to remember, however, that an infant is never “drown proof”. Taking swimming classes does not mean your baby will be immune to drowning. The classes generally certainly teach him or her how to be comfortable and calm in the water.

For more information on infant swimming classes, visit http://www.infantswim.com/.

Baby Shower Games

As your due date approaches, you are probably thinking about having a baby shower. Baby showers are always more fun with games. Here are a few great games you may want to play at yours:

“Baby Picture Game” – Ask for everybody’s baby pictures before the shower. Make a collage with a line under each picture, and have guests guess which picture belongs to which guest.

“Safety Pin Jar” – Pass around a jar full of baby safety pins. The person who guesses the correct number of pins in the jar wins a prize.

“Baby Bingo” – Prior to opening the baby shower gifts, number each gift on the box starting with one. If you know that 15 people will be attending your shower, pre-print bingo cards with numbers 1 through 15 and randomly place them on 15 cards. When the mommy-to-be opens the presents, she grabs the present she wants to open and reads the number out loud. As soon as someone receives bingo, they win a prize.

Baby Sleep Guide

Some babies sleep through the night. The majority, however, do not. This can be a frustration – and a point of exhaustion for many new parents. It is very normal for babies to have erratic sleep cycles. But it is also important to try to set up a sleeping schedule for infants, since sleep is imperative to proper development. During their first year of life, babies need an average of 14 hours of sleep a day.

Parenting.com has a fantastic guide to helping your baby sleep, including a section on safety issues while sleeping.

Stroller Guide

There are many different kinds of strollers on the market. Here are some things to consider before you purchase one.

From birth to six months, your baby does not have much head and neck control. During that time, you will need a stroller onto which you can attach your infant seat, one that has a bassinet, or one rated for newborns. Until your baby is ten months of age, you will also want to be careful about jogging while pushing a stroller, as the baby’s head and neck should still not take that much jarring.

Some major factors to consider in purchasing a stroller include the size of the wheels, the weight of the stroller, the kind of safety harness (a five-point harness is best) and the options such as storage baskets, leg rests, snack trays, etc.
