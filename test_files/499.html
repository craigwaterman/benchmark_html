How to Look and Feel Good…It’s More than Just Hairspray and Lipstick!

*Disclaimer: Get ready to hear the word “damn”, A LOT. Somehow that word really allowed me to get my point across.

* * * * *

Beauty; they say it’s all in the eye of the beholder.  I would agree with that statement.

The beauty industry is a multi billion dollar industry that touts the best moisturizers, smoothest lip glosses and greatest hair gels.  And when it comes to everyones’ beauty “ritual” it’s going to vary from person to person.

For me however, it’s an all encompassing approach and covers two main areas: mind and body.  Yes, just like everyone else, I like to look nice and feel good, but my approach to getting there goes beyond hairspray and lipstick.

There are about 10 main things that make up my “beauty” regimen to make me look good and feel good and some of them just might surprise you.

* * * * *

1) Hydrate: I recently wrote a post on the importance of water and WHY it is so vital for optimal health and you can read all about it here.  But to be honest, the reason I love guzzling down the H2O and staying hydrated is because it makes me feel so damn good!  It leaves my skin glowing, invigorates me when I’m feeling bogged down and sluggish and purifies my body.  If you feel good, you inevitably will look good because that essence and confidence will exude from you.  Who knew a beauty product could do all that, be so easily accessible and be FREE!

2) Moisturize: Every damn day! Probably 8-10 times a day because I wash my hands so damn much! I may or may not have a problem. 

3) Sweat & Exercise: It shouldn’t come as a surprise to you guys by now that I love lifting heavy weights and getting a good sweat sesh on.  And I do those things because I love them, not because someone says I should, or because it’s going to make me look a certain way.  I do them because it makes me feel effing awesome and powerful, the bonus is the aesthetics!  And when you feel awesome, that beauty shines through.  Find some form of movement that makes you feel amazing, and do it, do it a lot.

4) Eat what I want: I eat a clean diet but I always make room for the things that I love, like chocolate for instance. Confession: I eat chocolate almost every single day. I’m not going to sit here and suggest you do the same because I’ve worked damn hard to be able to eat the way I do.  What I will say is that I choose to eat in such a way where I can enjoy everything in moderation because that makes me HAPPY, HAPPY, HAPPY! What works for me; the way that I choose to fuel my body (and indulge) won’t necessarily work for you, but finding a happy balance is key.  Being able to eat a clean and sound diet the majority of the time whilst making room for those little indulgences that you really love does take patience, hard work and practice. To be able to listen to your body and know what works and what doesn’t, doesn’t happen overnight. However, once you are able to do that, you will be able to clear up a lot of mental space.

5) Stay connected with my crew: My family and friends are the most vital things in my life.  Staying connected and always finding time for them is extremely important to me and ultimately my happiness.  No matter how busy my life gets, I always make room to see and connect with them on a regular basis.  Nothing beats making memories, smiling, laughing and enjoying the occasional glass of wine (or two) with my girlfriends.  Time is one thing you can never get back, so I try to make sure that I make the most of it when I’ve got it! 

6) Love thyself: I know it sounds uber cheesy, but being able to fully appreciate and accept WHO YOU ARE might be one of the most underrated pieces of the puzzle; do not mistake it for being full of yourself though.  I am NOT PERFECT by any stretch of the imagination, but, I choose to accept who I am and what I have to offer right now.  Having a positive relationship with yourself first is what is going to make you have a positive relationship with others and in turn, radiate beauty.  This doesn’t mean I don’t strive to do more and challenge myself, or that I don’t have moments where I beat myself up over the things I don’t have or should be doing, I just don’t let myself dwell on it too long; ever learning and growing.

7) Fresh air: I love a good walk. Leisurely and aimless strolls are my jam. My husband doesn’t get it.  There is something very therapeutic about stomping the pavement and breathing in fresh air. It’s not about the exercise, or clocking miles or calories, it’s more the mental reset I’m able to achieve.  Clear my mind of the junk and shut off for a while.

8) Awesome leggings: I‘ve professed my love for funky leggings before and the reason being is that they make me feel so damn great! My second home is the gym so I don’t find myself dressing up very often and by dress up, I mean put on a pair of jeans. Granted, given the opportunity, my outfit of choice will always be leggings and a tee.  If I can find a way to dress them up, I will.  Although there have been occasions where my husband has had to step in and tell me no, just no, that doesn’t work. However, because I live in my leggings, I like to have fun with them. Bright colours, funky prints, a little mesh; like they say, if you look good you feel good right?

9) Greens: Every damn morning! I guzzle down a scoop of Greens+ each and every morning.  Despite the fact that most of my meals consist of protein and veggies, I can’t go wrong with a little extra to pick up any slack. Greens, it does your body good.

10) Be Happy: I’m making this my final point because what it honestly comes down to is finding the things in life that bring me both joy and fulfillment; ultimately the things that make me happy.  So for me, what makes me feel beautiful, what makes me shine (if I may so myself), and what makes me happy is a compilation of the things listed above.

That’s it guys! It might not be your typical “beauty” regimen, but these are the things that make me feel beautiful from the inside out.  What does it for me, won’t necessarily do it for you.

My challenge to you is to write a list of what your “beauty” regimen looks like and share it with me.  I want to know what makes you shine and feel beautiful.
