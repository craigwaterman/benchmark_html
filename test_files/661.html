British Grand Prix Race Report - An Exciting Weekend in Silverstone

10th July 2015

The Inside Track

What a weekend at Silverstone, with what was easily the most exhilarating racing so far this season!

After a beautiful sunny day on Saturday at the Qualifying session, Felipe was sitting in third and Valtteri fourth to secure an all-Williams second row ahead of the main race on Sunday. Hamilton was on pole, with his teammate Rosberg behind in second.

The race on Sunday began with an exhilarating start, as Felipe sprung off the start line and sailed past the surprised Mercedes’ with Valtteri following right behind him. Felipe led comfortably for the first lap while Valtteri was facing intense pressure from Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg who are clearly not used to chasing others! Mercedes even sent their crew out into the pit lane early on to try to fool Williams into making a pit stop, but their ‘dummy stop’ didn’t work and Williams continued to race on.

Felipe and Valtteri held onto the lead until the first round of pit stops which resulted in Lewis Hamilton taking the lead, but Nico was still back in 4th place.

To add to the tension the rain started at one end of the track so all the teams now had to decide when to move to intermediate tyres. Sadly, Nico enjoyed the challenge of a slightly slippery circuit and passed both the Williams’. Lewis and Sebastian Vettel made the right call and changed tyres with just over 10 laps to go. Everyone else immediately followed their lead, but their brave call essentially gave Lewis a comfortable victory and Sebastian vaulted several places into 3rd place behind Nico Rosberg. This was the final order at the chequered flag, with Felipe and Valtteri taking 4th and 5th respectively. It was a good result for WILLIAMS MARTINI RACING but the team were a little deflated because they could have got a great result if the rain had stayed away.

However the pace shown by the Williams drivers puts them in a great position to fight for another podium at the Hungarian Grand Prix in a few weeks time.
