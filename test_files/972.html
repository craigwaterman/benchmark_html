Please share our poster on your Facebook wall. It could save a life or two. The Online PPD Poster



This website is a place for women who are experiencing mood disorders after giving birth or adopting a baby (or have had past experience with these issues), as well as their families and friends and health professionals with an interest in reproductive mental health. Women who had original diagnoses of a postpartum mood disorder but were later diagnosed differently are also welcome.
This is a quick note to request that all active members try to use the forums appropriately by doing the following:

  • Post in the specific forum set aside for your topic. If you are posting about something not related to Postpartum Mood Disorders, for example, please use our forum dedicated to general topics not related to PPMDs.
  • Remember that the group moderators and members are NOT a substitute for medical care. Be sure to talk concerns over with your health care provider.
  • Members are expected to post what is true and correct to their knowledge. Members should, when possible, give references and/or links to the health/medical information they provide, when it is not personal experience.
  • Refrain from using the forums to solicit money or goods from other members. It's also inadvisable to offer money or goods to other members.
  • Express permission must be obtained from the administrator(s) of the forums before soliciting interviews from or requesting participation in any polls, surveys, or studies from any of our members.

    • Tonya Rosenberg - Founder - The Online PPD Support Group

    As moms with Postpartum Mood Disorders, we often face challenging decisions regarding medications and their impact on pregnancy as well as breastfeeding. Often quoted, we need to take care of ourselves first much like when we are on a plane and are told to apply the oxygen mask to ourselves first and loved ones second. These decisions do not come easy and I hope that the links and resources that follow prove to be helpful in your decision making process. Of course your physician should be involved but you also owe it to yourself and your infant to educate yourself regarding the risks of taking medications while nursing and while pregnant.

    Links for your provider to call for consultation:
    www.emorywomensprogram.org/contactus.asp
    www.psych.uic.edu/researc...tation.htm

    Helpful Books:

    Medication & Mothers Milk: A Manual of Lactational Pharmocology
    by Dr. Thomas W. Hale, Ph.D
    THE reference book for any and all medications and their levels in breastmilk. (Runs about $30, local La Leche League Leaders should have a copy of this)

    The Nursing Mothers Herbal
    by Sheila Humphrey
    A great reference for nursing mothers regarding herbal and natural remedies.

    The Ultimate Breastfeeding Book of Answers Revised and Updated: The Most Comprehensive Problem-Solving Guide to Breastfeeding from the Foremost Expert in North America
    By Dr. Jack Newman & Teresa Pitman
    The title says it all!

    When Breastfeeding Is Not an Option: A Reassuring Guide for Loving Parents by Peggy Robin
    Highly recommended read for mothers who find themselves unable to nurse due to medications required for depression.

    Websites:

    www.kellymom.com/ppd/index.html - Terrific resource for the breastfeeding mom with PPD.

    neonatal.ttuhsc.edu/lact/...spage.html - Thomas Hales medication forum. Health Professionals can post questions but anyone can read the information.
