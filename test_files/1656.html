Foods to avoid before bedtime

Posted on August 28, 2013 by arndtrenate

0

Bedtime foods to avoid!
images: pinterest.com

Hi guys,

Are you sometimes struggling to sleep at night?  Well maybe it can be because of the foods you take in before you hit bed?  I thought it would be helpful to in-light you which foods to avoid before going to bed and they include:

Pasta – Pasta is one of the fattest foods you need to avoid before going to bed.  It is a very rich source of carbohydrates that will eventually turn into fat when you hit the bed.

Pizza – Your digestive system would have a hard time processing it during the day: try to imagine how difficult it would be for your digestive organs to do that during the evening, when they should rest.

Candy – You are what you eat, but did you know that you also dream what you eat? Fatty foods and sugary products (especially candy) have an impact on your brain waves and they can trigger nightmares.

Red Meat – Although you should not avoid red meat overall given the fact that this is a good and nutritious source of proteins and iron, a big juicy stake will prevent you from achieving that deep, relaxing and refreshing sleep we all need after a long and stressful day.

Chocolate – Dark chocolate is very good for your brain and for your memory, but at the same time it is bad for your waistline: especially if you plan to eat it at night, when all the calories will be stored as fat given the fact that your body is at rest.

Vegetables – Veggies are delicious, nutrient-filled diet foods, but they are certainly not a great choice if you plan to go to bed soon, and for a very simple reason: vegetables such as onions, broccoli or cabbage contain high amounts of insoluble fiber which will make you feel full for a longer period of time.

Alcohol – Alcohol is definitely a sleep killer, given the fact that it can cause night sweats and cause you to wake up repeatedly during the night.

Snacks And Chips – Processed snack foods should be avoided at all times, not just before you go to bed, given the fact that they contain high amounts of monosodium glutamate that is known to be linked to a variety of sleep disorders.

So let this be a reminder to you the next time you want to grab for one of these foods before going to bed.  And also and important tip to remember is to stop eating 2 to 3 hours before going to bed.

Love,

Great Post Workout Foods To Indulge In

Posted on August 28, 2013 by arndtrenate

2

Delicious post workout foods to enjoy!
images: pinterest.com

Hi guys,

I am always drained of energy after I have worked out, so we need good nutrients to replenish our bodies. By eating the right foods after your workout you will instantly feel better and recover more quickly. There are a lot of different foods to choose from, but some are better than others and I thought of sharing them with you. Yum yum!

Chicken
Chicken is low in calories, it’s easy to cook and it’s low in carbohydrates. In addition to this, chicken is high in protein, which is what your body needs after you burn calories and build muscle.

Tuna
Tuna is another nutritious fish to enjoy after working out because it’s low in fat and high in protein. It’s also got heart healthy omega-3 fatty acids, so you will have stronger muscles without as much soreness.

Eggs
Eggs are so versatile and really do taste great! It’s just important to remember that egg whites are much healthier than whole eggs, as the yolks have a lot of cholesterol and calories in them. Eating more egg whites after you work out will provide your body with the protein that it needs.

Salmon
Salmon is great for your muscles because it’s high in protein as well as omega-3 fatty acids. It will also help decrease muscle inflammation that can occur after doing weight-bearing exercises, which can increase your performance and reduce soreness. There are tons of ways to prepare salmon!

Quinoa
Quinoa is a great replacement for rice because it’s higher in protein and has quite a bit of fiber in it as well. Eating this after working out will result in your body getting vitamins, nutrients, fiber and protein that it really needs to have.

Sweet Potatoes
Sweet potatoes are bursting with essential nutrients, including: vitamin A, vitamin C, vitamin B6, potassium, fiber, vitamin B5 and copper. Eating one of these instead of a regular potato will do your body good, especially after an intense workout session.

Hummus
The chickpeas that hummus is made of have protein in them as well as slow-releasing carbohydrates. This means that you will feel energised after your workout, even if it was difficult and exhausting.

So rather indulge in these foods after every workout! Enjoy!

Renate xx

Dressing for your Hourglass-shaped Body

Posted on August 16, 2013 by arndtrenate

3

The curvy and fabulous Hourglass Figure!
Images: pinterest.com

Hi guys,

Are your shoulders and hips in similar proportion?  Do you have a tiny waist? Well then you have the fabulous hourglass shape that needs to be embraced correctly!  Your best assets are those enviable feminine curves, so show them off and celebrate your curves without going overboard.

Celebrities rocking out this body shape include Salma Hayek, Scarlett Johansson, Beyoncé and the sexy Kim Kardashian.

Here are some tips for your shape:

  • Wear fitted dresses that celebrate your shape.
  • Never hide your curves in baggy, shapeless clothing.
  • Make use of waist belts to enhance your shape.
  • Definitely invest in a good bra that showcases your bust.
  • Always have versatile, flattering wrap dresses in your closet.

Remember to always embrace those fabulous curves!
