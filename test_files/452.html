Friday, August 21, 2015

Life Changes and Travelling Woes

Our move to San Antonio has been pretty... well, crazy.

When Nick drove to his interview, Soren was only a week old, and James was turning 3. But - he had to take the chance. He had to go because the opportunity was so good.

Well, less than a week later it was confirmed, he got the job! We would be relocating to San Antonio.

He started his job in July, and I spent two weeks with a newborn and two toddlers alone. Thankfully, my in laws were such a great support and help during that time. I spent a week with my mom, and the next week packing up our things.

We didn't have a house, or apartment yet but being away from Nick so long was hard. The kids were sad because of them missing daddy, and daddy was sad from missing the kids, especially the tiny one.

After two weeks, we decided, house or no house, our family was better together. So for two weeks we stayed in a hotel. Very interesting staying in a hotel with three kids. 

The first week our car died. I locked us out of the hotel at night, after our car had died. A very frustrating week for us. The second week it got better. Then, we went back to Lake Jackson for the weekend. We are currently waiting for approval for an apartment, which we should get next week. It'll be a huge relief to have our own place again.

We stayed a few months with my in laws as we looked for a house - and now we see why we couldnt find one. We weren't meant to stay there. We were meant to move to this beautiful city.

I am thankful for this opportunity. For Nick to be in a field he loves. I am excited for our future here, even though the start has been rough. It has been a wild ride, and I could not be happier. I look forward to all of our adventures in this city!

Sunday, June 21, 2015

Happy Fathers Day!



Our newest addition has finally made his arrival, and just in time for Fathers Day! 

Soren was born at 9:53am on June 18th, 21 inches long, and 8lbs 14oz.

They start my pitocin at 6:00am, contractions didn't start until around 6:30am. All of 3.5 hours! I will post a birth story later.

Fathers Day is holiday where I don't understand my feelings, and that is okay. I have a lot to celebrate, but many things are missing at the same time.

Firstly, I am thankful for how many men have been in my life, taking on a fatherly role and showing me love. I have had many, many great influences growing up, and even now.

Secondly, I miss my grandpa more than words can describe. My heart aches so badly. I have missed him so much through all these huge milestones in my life. I've missed his voice, his laugh, his encouragement and wisdom. I've missed him scolding me, telling me when I was wrong and always guiding me back onto the right path. I am so beyond lucky that he raised me, and I just wish he could see the outcome of all of his hard work. I know how much he loved Richelle, and how much he would love James and Soren as well. There is not a day that goes by where I don't miss him, but it's simply amplified on holidays such as these.

Lastly, I am so thankful for my husband. Many women will brag about their spouse, and that is fine. I don't want to brag about him, I simply want to tell people about him. For me, his actions are normal. For others, his actions are close to supernatural. Afterall, what kind of man can make a promise at 19 and stand by it without fail? Not many. He is my support, my best friend, and I adore him. We laugh together, we speak freely and we understand each other. I don't worry where his heart lies, because I know it's with his family. I have watched many of my friends struggle with their spouses being unfaithful, absent with their children, and sometimes just disrespectful or abusive. I am thankful that I don't experience that. We don't always see eye to eye, but we do always find a way to agree. But, Nick being a daddy, an amazing daddy, is what I will always love most about him. His children are his world. He doesn't stop because he knows he has to keep doing better for them. He isnt selfish, because he knows they come first. He loves them deeply.

I can not have asked for better men to be in my life than those I have. I hope all the men who are dads had a wonderful day.

Friday, May 15, 2015

The Mothers Love.


I never wanted to be a mom. Ever. I didn't like children, I didn't want children, and I prayed against having children. As strange as it is, I even remember being a child telling my grandparents how I didn't ever want kids.

Why not? The answer is several layers deep. The main reason, I believed I would fall into the same line as the women in my life, and I didn't feel as if that would be good enough for another child to endure.

I was afraid I would not love them, I would leave them behind to continue my own life, that I would give birth to a person and throw them away. I was afraid.


And then, shortly after marriage I ended up pregnant anyway. I cried. A lot. Mostly by myself. My husband was nervous, but he always wanted to be a daddy.

I fought with myself, terrified that I wouldn't be good enough for my child.

Well, one child turned into two, and two is becoming three.

I can honestly say, I am completely happy with the fact I am a mother now. I am not a maternal person, I don't think. Though, my kids now may tell you different.

The love I have for them is overwhelming, it is not possible to accurately describe how I feel for them. Though, I still have fears of not being good enough for them, I am not afraid of them going through what I did as a child.

My children are my everything, the reason why I get up in the morning and stay up late at night. They love me unconditionally. They really do. They think of me often and miss me when I'm not home. These two, soon to be three, tiny people in my home have changed the way I view every aspect of my life.

My oldest turns 5 this year, and the fact that I've been a successful mother for that long as impressed me. They are healthy, smart, normally well mannered, fully potty trained and loving children. They have big imaginations, weird quirks, and silly personalities. There are not enough words to describe the two I have now.

I can't imagine life without them. I can't imagine my husband not being a daddy, as it has shown him in a light I had never knew existed before. My love for him is so much more as a daddy than just as a husband. His patience, kindness and love for them (and me) has blown me away.

This Mothers Day was another one that felt bittersweet, but as the years continue to pass they become more sweet than bitter.

I never wanted to be a mother, yet I was meant to be one. I am still afraid, but I know with the love and support I have, it is not possible for me to fail. In just a few short weeks we complete our family. My love for my husband and the tiny children with me will never shake or falter. They are my everything.

Wednesday, April 1, 2015

The Fools Day.

Technically, I am about 20 minutes late for April fools as I write this out. No good jokes this year, to my dismay. Overall, another uneventful normal day.

But, this day is important, because this marks the month I close a chapter that has been in my life for 5 years. We have finally sold our Condo. Finally! We lived in it for a year and a half, if that. We wanted to rent it out, and it never happened. We wanted to sell sooner, and that never happened. The day before we even put it on the market we got an offer. Perfect. I am so thrilled to be selling it, that it will now be there for someone else. It has many good memories, and some bad memories, too. I am just happy.

It is also the last step in our adventure to get approved for a home loan. Once we close we get to reapply, again, and see what we can afford and what we cant. We keep praying for the perfect house to be available when we start actively looking for a new home. Of course, job market is sky high here, so houses are getting sold faster than they can keep them up. If its on the market, it gets sold within the month. It's been interesting to watch, and it'll be more interesting when we are in the group of people waiting for the perfect house, trying to get it before someone else does.

I just can't wait. Next summer we will have our three children, all being able to run and play in the backyard. I think that is the craziest thing to me, knowing that next July I will have a big kid, and two tots running in a yard. I love it.

This month will hopefully bring great new changes into our lives, and it's also the month of vacation! Nick and I will be taking a weekend to ourselves. This is the first time since our honeymoon we could afford to, and the last chance we have until our tiniest is old enough for me to be okay with leaving him over night. We are going on 6 years of marriage, so I suppose a weekend vacation is needed. I am excited to spend quality time with Nick, even though I am a little nervous about leaving Richelle and James for two nights. It will be worth it, though.

Friday, January 16, 2015

2015.

We are barely two weeks into 2015 and we have already been taken by surprise by what 2015 is offering us.


Christmas was wonderful, all the love we received from family and friends, and the numerous toys my children received (too many. too. many. toys.)

New Years was great, even though we went to bed before midnight, we did a count down with our children and then we all went to bed. They were pretty excited that they said up until the "New Year".

But, so far, 2015 seems to be our year. We are in the process of finding a house now. We have a few in mind that we like, but aren't 100% yet. I have very high hopes to find one quickly.

Our lease is up at the end of Feb, so I am excited and nervous to move from our apartment. We have made it home and have grown and learned so much from our two years here. Many victories and losses have happened. I will forever be thankful for the time we spent here. 

And now, as we start getting closer we get to start planning trips. We will be having many visitors in the next few months, and Nick and I are going to take a weekend trip, without children, in a few months. It's strange, because I have never been away from either of my kids for more than 24 hours. Only a handful of times have they stayed the night with someone else.

I don't worry about them at all, I just miss them so much. Yet, I know it'll be good for us. 

Mom plans on visiting in the summer ( in our new house! whichever one it may be), and a few more family members from both side are planning, but not officially coming just yet. We are so excited for all the family time this year will bring and the new adventures we will go on. To 2015! May this year be ours :)

Monday, December 1, 2014

December already.


December, December. Already December.

Thanksgiving was a lot of fun. We went to his parents for lunch, and got to surprise his parents with a very special gift! Which we later shared with the rest of his family and mine.

My brother in law and his lovely girlfriend stayed with us from Wednesday night until Sunday evening, which was also so much fun. I love having them here, and my kids just adore them.

My kids got to talk to "pops" and "nana" on the phone for a couple minutes. We tried to get them to talk longer, but the phone call was in the middle of building a gingerbread house. So, Richelle announced, "Can't talk now. I'm busy." Thankfully both pops and nana had a good sense of humor about it, especially since they knew the kids were just busy having fun.

Also, a fun game to play for anyone who isn't easily offended.


It gets better when you take a shot every time you win a round. Just saying.


AND THEN.


Saturday night we got to decorate the tree.



The Santas in that picture make me so happy. My grandpa gave them to me when Richelle was still an infant because he wanted them to be taken care of well and knew that I would do that. They represent so much of the holidays to me, being able to grow up with them and then also allowing them to grow up with my children. My grandma, and great granny put a lot of effort into these guys. They were always my favorite decorations and they will always be my favorite.

With my family we have started our own traditions.

Which is a mixture between my family, Nicks, and what we wanted ourselves.

I didn't get the tradition of opening most of our gifts on Christmas Eve, we each only open one, and it will always be PJs to sleep in before Santa comes.

But, this year, I am a little extra excited.

Both of my kids know the value of gifts that are given, they feel the love behind them. They also have a lot of fun picking out gifts for others. Last year Richelle thought very, very carefully before getting Mams and Papa a rubber chicken. She really thought they would love it, so she asked to give it to them.

This year, James stole that rubber chicken and it is now somewhere in his room.




Overall, this is a very exciting time for us.

Thanksgiving has passed, we spent a lot of time with family and friends. We are gearing up for Christmas and even more family time. And after the holiday season comes one of our biggest changes..

Moving from our Apartment and finding a HOUSE. I can not wait to see what is in store for us, I just pray we can find one and won't end up back in an apartment. No matter what, we will be okay. 2015 will be our year.

Monday, November 17, 2014

The Most Wonderful Time of Year!



The most wonderful time of year. Halloween has passed and the turkeys are coming! The Christmas trees are going up, and the weather is getting colder.

If your kids are anything like mine.. well, this is how our mornings have been. Sleeping in, or them trying to sleep in each day. The warmth of the bed just calls to us when the air is chilly.

Yet, my kids also come alive in this weather. Their silliness and giggles seem endless. Sometimes a little too silly yet I almost never seem to mine. After all, Richelle got my quirks so if I complain about them, I am also complaining about my own.

Watching my two babies grow into toddlers, and turning into "big kids" is so scary for me.

I mean, just look.


That isn't a baby. That is barely even a toddler! He looks like a tiny man. Well, I mean.. he is a tiny man, but really! (Side note, look at those gorgeous blue eyes! Still can not get over them and he has had them for 2.5 years).

Then.. there is this.


Excuse the "I just worked a 10 hour shift" face. She is big enough to brush my hair and "braid" it. When did this even happen? She is still impressing the doctors by how incredibly advance she is (Thank you, husband, for providing the genuis genes). I mean, really. This girl.. I can't even being to tell you how smart she is. She is reading, writing, spelling, adding, and subtracting. By the time she goes into kindergarten they won't have anything to teach her. 

Let me clarify, all of the things she is doing, she is still in the process of learning, but she has another full school year before she goes into public school. So, during that time, she will probably get 90% of it down to an art.

Speaking of art, you should see her butterflies that she draws. They're pretty awesome.


But, you know what is best of all this season?

The thankfulness.



For James' class, they asked him what he was most thankful for. His answer, though not surprising was his sister. They have a love beyond any sibling bond I have see. They are best friends, they hold hands when they walk, give kisses goodnight and always 'hang out' together. She reads to him, shows him how to play angry birds, lets him watch his 'thomas train' and shares her heart with him. She is the perfect older sister and he is a great little brother. Not only does she share her heart with him, he always shares his with her. He will get anything she needs without hesitating, he snuggles with her and always runs to her side when she is hurt.

Seriously, these two kids are perfect. I wish everyone could have kids as funny and awesome as mine, but not everyone is as lucky as I am.
