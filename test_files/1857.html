7 Peloton Coaches Share Their Favorite Busy-Night Dinners

August 27, 2015

You can eat healthy, at home, fast! Take it from our coaches and try one of these yummy recipes tonight.

The lazy days of summer are ending, which means it’s time to get back into a dinner routine that is both healthy and easy on your schedule. And who better than our coaches–folks that live on level 11 pretty much every day, and somehow always manage to stay seriously healthy at the same time–to share with you their favorite quick and easy recipes for an ultra-nutrish dinner?

Jessica King: “Match any lean protein with a bowl of greens and complex carbs and you win. On hot, late summer days, I like to substitute ice cream with acai bowls, which are both delicious and nutritious! And avocado toast wins at any time of the day.”

Robin Arzon: “Here’s my mix: Raw kale and watercress as a base. Then a layer of steamed eggplant and broccoli with turmeric. Top with crumbled tempeh. Then go crazy with hot sauce and  pine nuts all over.”

Nicole Meline: “At least once a week, I turn to this yummy recipe. Baked salmon, kale tossed with EVOO, goji berries, and walnuts, and whatever seasonal veg I’m into. Lately it’s been baked eggplant with some pomegranate seeds and Greek yogurt magic.”  

Cody Rigsby: “I’m typically coming home at night from teaching class or the gym, so dinner is my chance to recharge and refuel after an active day. I find that I can never go wrong with sweet potatoes mixed with a dash of cinnamon and coconut oil to add some healthy fat. I always pair this with some baked chicken and steamed seasonal veggies.”

Kim Fischer: “I make my own take on Sweetgreen‘s Harvest Bowl: A kale salad with steamed wild rice, chicken, apples, cashews, cucumber, and balsamic vinaigrette. It’s filling and nutritious!”

Jenn Sherman: “I’ve been obsessed with the organic turkey burgers from Whole Foods. I’ve been throwing them on the grill, then putting them on top of a bed of steamed spinach with some quinoa thrown in for a tasty 5-minute meal.”

Hannah M. Corbin: “I love anything with a quinoa or power green base. Greens, quinoa, and as many veggies as I can find to put on top. Stir in a little olive oil/lemon juice combination and almost always [sugar-free] hot sauce!”

Do you have a favorite, healthy, go-to dinner to round out a busy day? Share in the comments!
