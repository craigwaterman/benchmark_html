Argan Oil


I've been determined to move my skincare and makeup routine to a more natural and healthy selection of products. About 6 or so months ago I replaced my moisturizer with a bottle of 100% argan oil I purchased from a Hautelook sale. Using argan oil as a moisturizer was made "mainstream" when Josie Maran started selling it as a moisturizer for Sephora, but there are many cheaper alternatives out there with the same quality.  I don't think I will ever go back to using chemically created moisturizers.


Some of the benefits of Argan oil:
- Contains vitamin E to help soothe skin and provide anti-aging benefits
- Has many fatty acids for moisture retention.  This includes Omega 6, which helps with reducing acne inflammation
- Light and fast absorbing
- Can use in hair to smooth frizz and mend split ends (Many companies sell sprays for just this purpose  under the name "Moroccan Oil")
- Use as a hand lotion to help with dry cuticles.
- Doesn't clog pores

What I've noticed from my own use:
- My skin feels soft and moisturized but never oily. When I run my hands over my face 5 minutes after application, it doesn't even feel like I used oil. My face actually feels oilier in the morning if I went to sleep without any moisturizer vs after I use argan oil at night.
- Suitable for use in both summer and winter.
- Rumor has it this oil will help with hair growth (eyelashes, eyebrows). I don't want to make any claims about the eyelash part, but I honestly feel like it helped my eyebrows grow. I went a bit overzealous with the tweezers once and the hair wouldn't grow back for quite awhile. However, after I started applying argan oil to my eyebrows I felt like it grew out quicker than normal. In fact, I think I've needed to pluck my eyebrows more than usual now because the hair growth seem to have increased.
- I haven't had any adverse skin reactions to this oil
- One day my cuticles were completely dry and out of control. I kept picking on it at work so when I got home, it was in really bad shape. Before I went to sleep, I applied argan oil to my fingers. When I woke up the next morning, the cuticle I kept picking at looked almost normal. I was so shocked!


There are a variety of all natural oils available to use as moisturizer, but I highly recommend Argan oil for anyone tempted to indulge in some all natural skin care. Since Argan oil is produced strictly by the women of the Berber tribe in Morocco, your purchase will support the financial empowerment of women.  Good for your skin and your conscience! what could be better?

When you buy, be sure to purchase only pure 100% ORGANIC argan oil. Argan oil is used in food as well, so be sure to buy ones formulated for use on the body not your salad. Don't be cheap with your skin. Some brands I recommend:
- Josie Maran
- Amal oils
- 100% pure
- Shea Terra Organics

On a lighter note, the argan nut is apparently so good, goats are willing to climb the argan trees to eat them!
