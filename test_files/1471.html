Gandhi: The Man and My Musings

Posted on February 8, 2014 by urmishm

As the train leaves,
On another journey routine;

My brain together weaves,
Fragmented dreams courtesy nicotine;

Sinking into a slumber thick,
I see a bespectacled man with walking stick;

A dhoti-clad old man unwizened,
Weaving khadi on Charkha wooden;

‘Kem chho Bapu,‘ I inquire gayly,
‘Maja maa,’ comes reply heartily;

And so the conversation begins,
Ranges from Ahimsa to gins;

I ask him about lifes’ secrets,
Does he have any regrets?

‘One and None,’ he tells,
On his face a smile dwells;

I give him an incredulous stare,
He shrugs as if without a care;

‘Patience, patience don’t you hurry,
Son, next station is only Andheri;’

‘You don’t understand Mumbai’s life’s pace,
Bapu its a city where rats race;

We have no time to lose,
Every second fate tightens noose;

Dreams I have many,
Few zany some uncanny;

Have to achieve a lot,
Fame, power, name, plot;

Not to forget woman’s love,
And a huge treasure trove;

An important chair in corner office,
A chauffeur always at my service;

Want to travel the globe,
Every day in a new robe;

Alas, I doubt it ll ever be,
Ergo I turn to thee;

Fear, failure forever me haunts,
Past always on my Present daunts;

Everyday chores make me numb,
To feeble desires I succumb;

Bapu, I may sound like Cassandra,
Soon next station will be Bandra;

I have heard of your deeds,
How do I throw out the weeds?

Help me get all I need,
Your advice I will definitely heed';

Bapu gave an approving smile,
The train covered another mile;

‘Relax relax Mr. Speedy Gonsalves,
Be calm like sea-shore of Maldives;

I shall answer all your questions main,
No doubts in you shall remain;

Will take you to your goals nearer,
Outside the station you see is Dadar;

Listen to me with attention rapt,
Follow advice if you deem apt;

I reckon you have scientific vent,
Let me show you My Experiment;

Consider the States of Matter three,
Solid-Liquid-Gas are names of thee;

Now associate them with Tenses three,
Past-Present-Future are names of thee;

Let me your life exemplify,
You oft let your Past Solidify;

Thereby creating Present to Liquefy,
And puff! It causes Future to Gasify;

Thus you are left with a void,
Which is it possible to avoid?

Wonder why you become tense,
Is it ‘coz of  living in wrong Tense?

Now we come to part integral,
The train crosses Mumbai Central;

Can a solution be found?
Let’s change the variables around,

Experiment I will a little modify,
Boom! First let your Past Gasify;

Try causing your Present ever to Solidify,
Inside sublime mould Future than shall Liquefy,

Your living you shall ever simplify,
It’ll cause happiness to magnify;

This has always my mantra been,
Helped me through thick and thin;

Had replied earlier ‘One and None’,
Let me summarise it in short son;

The Secret is ever not to Regret,
Never allow the Past to regurgitate';

‘Its amazing Bapu the Gandhigiri you teach,
Will always practice what you preach;

Bole toh thank you Bapu,
For guiding apun jaisa maamu;

Aavjo Bapu, its getting late,
For next station is Churchgate’

And soon the scene dissolved,
Out of mists a new me evolved;

Chemical Locha in me had started,
Light-hearted from train I alighted;

Around Bapus‘ words my heart revolved,
Ever to live in Present I resolved;

As I was walking the phone rang,
‘You are late’, the stern voice sang;

Hurriedly the rats’ race I resumed;
To one of Mumbais’ ratholes I zoomed;

‘Let’s meet again’, longed my chords vocal,
‘FYI Bapu I return in 7.20 Borivali Local!’
