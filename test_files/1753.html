Summer Fling: DIY Two-tone Pleated Necklace



 
Keeping up with the latest trends, we have prepared a simple Summer-themed DIY activity that you and your friends will surely enjoy! We've seen it in from your favorite fashion and accessories brands, so we're stirring it up and making our own version of the Pleated Necklace.

  
WHAT YOU'LL NEED
  • Thick chains
  • Two (2) Crochet Balls of any color: three (3) sections with (40) 40-inched strands per color
  • Two (2) Triangle hooks
  • Chains
  • Needle
  • Scissors
  • Tape
We got most of our materials from a local craft shop called Carolina's. 

PART I: PLEAT BASE & PLEATING

Prepare your pleats. For each crochet color, make three sections with 40 pieces of 40-inched strands. Combine both colors to form three (3) two-toned sections for pleating, each section should now have 80 pieces of 40-inched strands, having 40 strands per color.

Put all three sections together and tape the ends in order to put it through the triangle hook.

Once you've inserted the crochet strings into the hook, secure it with another string, and start coiling the end with crochet until the lose ends are covered.

To close the coil, insert the crochet string into a needle and puncture into the coil as seen above.

Start pleating. When you get to the end, secure with another string and repeat the coiling process with another triangle hook.

You are supposed to end up with something like this:


PART II: CHAINING

This part is very quick and easy. Simply get your needle and thread it with crochet. Start sewing the triangle hook with the chain. Repeat this process with the other end, and voila!

We hope you enjoyed this simple DIY. We would love to see your creations, so don't forget to send us a picture to knock@atthegirlsroom.com, or share a photo via Instagram and tag us @thegirlsroomonline!

Have a great Summer!
