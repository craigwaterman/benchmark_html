Recap: SMU enters the NCAA Tournament

March 23, 2015 by Abundant Journeys
It's only fair to share...40020

On Friday, March 20, Southern Methodist University aka SMU made their 1st NCAA Tournament debut since 1993. It was such a phenomenal and emotional moment to watch those young men take the floor for the 1st time in 22 years. Fighting with all of their might to move to the next level in what we affectionately call ‘March Madness’. With the SMU cheerleaders and die-hard fans, who traveled all the way from Dallas, Texas, cheering them on.

The second and third round of this year’s games were hosted by the University of Louisville at the KFC Yum Center in stunning Louisville, Kentucky. With a view from our box seats, you can see the stadium was pretty full for such early games in the tournaments.

The game itself came with its usual ebbs and flows between the two teams, SMU and UCLA. However, it ended with a call that would send players, fans, coaches and sports analyst across the country into a state of pure confusion. With only 10.7 seconds left on the clock, the referee the furthest distance from the net, with the least vantage point visually, called goal-tending on SMU’s Yanick Moriera.

<<moment of silence please>>

We now know it wasn’t so! The call was wrong in all ways! It was a jaw dropping moment as the crowd seemed to stand still. Nic Moore attempted to make it down the court for one last ditch effort for a 3-point shot. No basket. Leaving SMU robbed of a win, losing 60-59 to UCLA. Lesson learned:

  • Never leave a game down to only a few points and
  • There is always next year!

No time to rest on your laurels. Lace up and hit the court prepping for 2016. And to the seniors, we must say you made history. You were the first team in nearly a quarter of a century to take the big stage. We are proud of you. #GoMustangs
