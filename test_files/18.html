Recipe Blog Post #3 – How do you like your eggs in the morning?!

Hey everyone!

Just checking in here with a little recipe blog post before the next monthly blog go’s up with the new member of the month! Todays recipe blog post consists of a breakfast meal that I personally love! Its basically a bulked up flavoursome version of scrambled eggs but its extremely versatile meaning you can experiment and add a few more ingredients if you wish! A few that I would suggest would be grilled baby tomatoes, mushrooms cooked in a little coconut oil or maybe some smoked salmon! With just 4 base ingredients (5 if you choose to add the balsamic glaze) this doesn’t require huge amounts of effort to make and for those taking part on the upcoming macro challenge its an easy one to input in to MyFitness Pal! . . . Lets have a look at the breakdown of the meal!

To start with . . . The main aspect of this meal; eggs/egg whites! Both the white and yolk of an egg are rich in nutrients. They provide you with a great source of protein, vitamins and minerals. The yolk of the egg contains cholesterol, fat soluble vitamins and those essential fatty acids which we all need! They are a great inexpensive form of protein and more than half of that protein is found in the egg white! The white containing vitamin B2 has lower amounts of fat and cholesterol than the yolk so for this particular meal I personally opt to have 2 whole eggs and then 5 whites just to get that high protein boost! You can of course reduce this depending on how much protein you are taking in and the distribution of your protein sources throughout the day! When cooking the eggs I have some brilliant non stick pans so personally don’t use oil; you can however go for some coconut oil as from previous experiences this tastes great!

Moving on we head to the spinach. A superfood powerhouse that helps add a little substance to the eggs and a little more volume! Spinach has been previously discussed on my smoothie recipe blog post! In short it has always been regarded as a plant with the ability to restore energy, increase vitality and improve the quality of the blood! Its hugely rich in iron which plays a huge role in the functioning of red blood cells; these as we all know are responsible for the transportation of oxygen around the body! It contains vast amounts of vitamins and minerals that all contribute to good health and well being!

The final two ingredients . . . wholemeal bread and half an avocado. The wholemeal bread acts as the base for the eggs and is just a good solid source of carbohydrates. I personally need a little bit of carbohydrate in the mornings to get me going and give me a bit of energy hence why I have 2 slices. You can change this though if you are on a lower carb diet or just don’t want to have it included! The avocado alongside providing a great taste and texture adds some brilliant and further nutritional value to the meal. They are full of vitamins and minerals, act as a great source of fiber and contain some well sourced healthy fats! The flavour combinations and the textures of all the ingredients are excellent; if you do want to add a little touch to finish go for a light drizzle of balsamic glaze to really set the flavours off!

Let me know if you try this one out!

Macro Breakdown

Calories – 575

Protein – 55g

Carbs – 33g

Fat – 28g

Fiber – 10g
