Will My Heart Be Big Enough?

April 14, 2015
By lifeofaministermom

Can I handle 2 children? 

How will my toddler react to the baby?

What will become our new norm?

Will my first trip to the grocery store with both girls be a disaster?

Will I ever get out of the house on time?

Will I ever want to leave the house again?!

How do I get my little darlings up those stinkin’ stairs??

Am I ready for this?

Will I EVER be ready for this?

These questions were a sample of the slew of thoughts and concerns that swirled around my head when I was pregnant with my youngest daughter. Although I could now say I had baby experience and was confident in my ability to care for my #2, in many ways I was still entering into the unknown.

I had no idea what this new little one’s personality would be like, and with my toddler and I doing absolutely everything together, I also had to adjust to the fact that it would no longer be just the two of us enjoying our quality time while daddy was at work each day.  I’d finally settled into a nice rhythm and found a good balance, but there was going to be a new girl in town. Her much anticipated arrival meant there’d  be a lot of changes made to our daily operation, but I knew we’d be alright. The truth is, a change in our schedules, or plans for the day paled in comparison to the deeper uncertainty brewing beneath the surface.

Would I have enough love to go around? Would my heart be big enough to fully embrace this precious gift, in the way I had her big sister?

As her mom, the obvious answer was “Yes, of course!“, but still I pondered.

I’ve seen the fanfare and excitement dwindle from family and friends when baby #2 or 3 or 4…is announced. I’ve heard parents go beyond observing the differences in their children to making blatant comparisons of them, listing the reasons why one child was clearly better than the other. I have witnessed the harmful and damaging effects of favoritism, and wondered if I too would be susceptible to any of these behaviors.

The love for my babies was there from the very moment I knew their sweet lives were growing inside me, and I prayed and prayed that I’d be able to equally and adequately express that deep love to both my girls.

On the day I met my darling Lily, I had no doubt that this blessing was just as special as the first gift I’d received, and that Our Heavenly Father had certainly hand-crafted her just for me.

I will praise You, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made; Marvelous are Your works, And that my soul knows very well. (‭Psalms‬ ‭139‬:‭14‬ NKJV)

She was perfect in every way, and my heart embraced her without a single doubt or hesitation. 

7 months later, she enriches my life in more ways than I could have imagined. I get lost in her eyes, and hold her close at every opportunity. I cuddle with her, smell that baby smell, and stroke her soft skin whenever I can. I love on her with every fiber of my being, and that same love, that profound, unconconditional, and ever-growing love, is given freely to each of my daughters.

I will do my best to model after God, who loves all his children, and gives so much to each and every one of us.

For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life. (‭John‬ ‭3‬:‭16‬ NKJV)

He who did not spare His own Son, but delivered Him up for us all, how shall He not with Him also freely give us all things? (‭Romans‬ ‭8‬:‭32‬ NKJV)

As they continue to grow and mature, my prayer is that, as their mom, I too will grow and mature. This is an area that I will constantly be seeking direction from the Holy Spirit, with the hope that the day will never come when a child of mine wonders if she’s good enough to receive all her mama’s love.

Where are you in this motherhood journey? Is this topic of favoritism or ‘enough love’ something you’ve ever wrestled with?

They Call Me…

September 3, 2013
By lifeofaministermom

 

Patricia Anne is my name.  I’ve always known the meaning behind it (both in definition and personal significance), so after seeing yesterday’s daily prompt, I thought it would be fun to share!

First and foremost, I know that I was named after 2 incredible women who have since gone on to be with the Father.  My Auntie Evelyn Patricia (my daddy’s eldest sister who passed after a valiant fight against breast cancer), and my maternal grandmother, Anne. 

Patricia is of Latin and English origin, and means “noble, noblewoman”. 

Anne is of French and English origin, and means “favor, grace”.

I believe my name suits me and I was always taught to take pride in it!

Here’s a fun fact:  My dad never wanted me to have a nickname, but that idea went out the window once my kindergarten T.A. began calling me Patty.  I guess he was okay with that ;), but I still remember being told that I didn’t have to be called anything I didn’t like. 

I did not, do not, and will NEVER like being called Pat (hint, hint), but I liked Patty.  I was happy with that nickname as long as it was spelled with a ‘y’, and for years (even now) I remember correcting people who insisted on spelling it with an ‘i’. 

It’s funny because my best friend since the second grade is named Tiffani, and I always found myself saying “I’m Patty with a ‘y’ and she’s Tiffani with an ‘i’.”

That, along with explaining that my middle name is Ann with an ‘e’ (like Anne of Green Gables!) became second nature to me.  Looking back I see that it was one of the first lessons I learned about asserting myself, and there is always value in that!  While I let some of the misnomers slide today, I do my best to call other people by the name they prefer, and spell it correctly too! :)

As far as my daughter, Alexandra Simone, naming her was pretty easy for my husband and I.  We did not know the gender of our baby until she was born, so we had a boy and girls name picked out in advance.  Only a few people knew the options, because a. I’m big on surprises, and b. We didn’t want to be swayed by the opinions of others.  This was our child, and the names we selected were important to us. 

Once our daughter arrived, there was no doubt that what we chose was the perfect fit!

Alexandra  is of Greek and English origin, and means “defender of men, mankind”.

Simone is of Hebrew origin and means “hearkening, to hear, listening intently”. 

Our constant prayer is that, among other things, she exhibits the characteristics of her name.  One verse that we stand on for her is the following:

Proverbs 31:8-9 (NLT)

8 Speak up for those who cannot speak for themselves; ensure justice for those being crushed. 9 Yes, speak up for the poor and helpless, and see that they get justice.

 

We know that she will have to do her own share of lovingly correcting others (there’s no ‘i’ in her name people; It’s not Alexandria! ;)), but we hope that she will do so with the confidence of knowing that her name is special, and that she is special!

What about you?  What’s the story behind your name or what you chose for your children?

An Emotional Journey (and the News We Refused to Accept)

May 26, 2013
By lifeofaministermom

 Daily Prompt: Tell us about a journey –whether a physical trip you took, or an emotional one.

My Godparent’s cabin

My husband and I recently enjoyed a weekend getaway in honor of our 3rd wedding anniversary (click here for pictures).  We had an absolutely wonderful time, but there was one part of the trip that was especially significant for us…

The last time we visited Lake Tahoe and stayed in the house you see pictured above, I was in the first trimester of my pregnancy. I was experiencing an array of unpleasant symptoms, and I remember wondering when the disgusting taste of metal in my mouth would fade and allow me to enjoy the little bit of food I was able to keep down.  Leading up to the trip I was doing OK, and it made me feel bad that at that point in time I wasn’t my usual ball of sunshine. ;) We kept our activities to a minimum (movies, window-shopping, etc.), but I was determined to make the most of our low-key weekend.  Unfortunately everything came to a screeching halt when I began experiencing heavy bleeding that Saturday evening. 

Lord, what’s happening?!!  Don’t panic, don’t panic.  You’ll be ok.  The baby will be ok…

With tears in my eyes I tried to remain as calm as possible as I emerged from the bathroom.  I told my husband that something wasn’t right, and (God bless that man) he immediately began speaking God’s Word (the same verse we’d shared with a brother in Christ earlier that day) over our situation.

No weapon formed against you shall prosper,
And every tongue which rises against you in judgment
You shall condemn.
This is the heritage of the servants of the Lord,
And their righteousness is from Me,”
Says the Lord. Isaiah 54:17 (NKJV)

Yes, Jesus.  No weapons, NO WEAPONS.  Everything is fine.  Right?  Everything HAS to be fine.  Thank you, Holy Spirit, for this reminder.

But the Helper, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in My name, He will teach you all things, and bring to your remembrance all things that I said to you. John 14:26 (NKJV)

So here we are in an unfamiliar place, with a pretty serious situation on our hands.  We knew we had to act quickly, and glory to God we remembered something else.  We had been told that a doctor lived across the street, and without hesitation, my hubby ran over to see if anyone was home.  Knowing the hectic nature of a doctor’s schedule, and the fact that some stranger would be knocking on her door, I waited with bated breath to see if help was on the way. 

Help did come, and ‘Dr. C’ was truly a Godsend.  She was kind and reassuring, patient and helpful, and assisted us with everything we needed.  She called ahead and informed the medical staff at the emergency room that we were heading their way, and even offered to drive us if we thought her directions weren’t enough.  She was our angel that night, and we praise the Lord for her!

Once at the hospital we held each others hands and waited.  Our hope remained in Christ, and we did all that was within our power to focus on the most positive results.

Finally, brethren, whatever things are true, whatever things are noble, whatever things are just, whatever things are pure, whatever things are lovely, whatever things are of good report, if there is any virtue and if there is anything praiseworthy—meditate on these things. Philippians 4:8 (NKJV)

After I was examined I was told that I needed to see my doctor as soon as possible, because without running (expensive) tests there was no conclusive way they could tell me if my baby was alright. We were given pamphlets about coping with a miscarriage, and without even glancing at them we tossed them in the trash.  We were determined to stand firm on God’s report and believe that all was well.

So after driving home in the middle of the night, and waiting a day until my doctor could see me on Monday, it was finally confirmed that I’d experienced a threatened miscarriage.

“It could’ve gone either way…imagine walking along a fence and the wind blows.  You lean one way and it’s a miscarriage, you lean the other way and it’s a healthy pregnancy.  Well as you can see from the ultrasound the heartbeat is strong, and although you should take it easy it appears your baby is doing well.”

HALLELUJAH!!!!!!!  I didn’t know anything about the wind blowing one way or the other, but I knew all about the mighty God I serve!!  He had answered our prayers, and placed the right people in our path to help us through it all.  This is why we paid ‘Dr. C’ a visit during this last trip (the first since that unsettling night), and gave her a “thank you” card with Alexandra’s picture in it.  I couldn’t stop the tears from falling as I told her what she’d meant to my family and I, and as we hugged, a certain weight was lifted with our embrace.

I know that there is so much more I could say, but simply put, gratitude is what I feel every single day.  Although this particular journey was an unexpected one, the outcome couldn’t have been better.  God is SOOOOOO good!!!! :)

First Came Marriage…

May 8, 2013
By lifeofaministermom

3 years ago today, my hubby and I decided to get married. We were in the midst of planning a fairly big wedding which was to take place on August 27th, and everything was coming together nicely. There was just one problem. We were ready to be husband and wife before our “scheduled” date.  We were ready to make things official. 

Why the big hurry?  Why were you in such a rush?  You guys couldn’t wait just a few more months?  The truth is, yes, we could have waited, but we couldn’t be certain of what may or may not have transpired during that time.  Both of us had made our fair share of mistakes before the Lord led us to one another, and we knew this time had to be different.  The sexual temptation we experienced was very real, and while we were pretty sure we could’ve stayed strong, the last thing we wanted to do was think too highly of ourselves and fall victim to the lust of the flesh.

Therefore let anyone who thinks he stands [who feels sure that he has a steadfast mind and is standing firm], take heed lest he fall [into sin].  1 Corinthians 10:12 (AMP)

He who leans on, trusts in, and is confident of his own mind and heart is a [self-confident] fool, but he who walks in skillful and godly Wisdom shall be delivered.  Proverbs 28:26 (AMP)

So we prayed and sought wise counsel, and even though we knew this was the best decision for us, there were still those nagging voices in my head:  You are so weak.  How embarrassing.  You’re supposed to be a Minister of the Gospel and you’re not even strong enough to overcome this desire?!  What will everyone think if they know you went ahead and got married.  What about your family members and friends who won’t be there to witness it the first time around.  Will they respect our decision?  Will they be angry with us? Will the wedding still be special? 

And the voices went on and on, until one voice came through loud and clear:  Who are you doing this for?  Are you going to allow your pride to keep you from doing what you think is best to honor me?

Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall.  Proverbs 16:18 (AMP)

Ouch.  That conviction from the Holy Spirit was felt and received, and all I could do was say ‘I hear you God, I hear you!’  His words were the absolute truth.  I knew what decision to make, but my pride was getting in the way.  I still wanted to have my magical wedding day, and I was afraid that by jumping the gun it wouldn’t be so special.  I was afraid that my family and friends wouldn’t understand, and I was afraid that our decision would somehow expose my weakness.  For a moment in time I allowed these fears to overcome me, but then I returned to my sound mind, and released any and all anxieties.

Do not fret or have any anxiety about anything, but in every circumstance and in everything, by prayer and petition (definite requests), with thanksgiving, continue to make your wants known to God.  And God’s peace [shall be yours, that tranquil state of a soul assured of its salvation through Christ, and so fearing nothing from God and being content with its earthly lot of whatever sort that is, that peace] which transcends all understanding shall garrison and mount guard over your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus.  Philippians 4:6-7 (AMP)

Our first and foremost desire was to honor God in our actions by not living together or engaging in pre-marital sex.  We simply wanted to be obedient to Him, and our reverence for Our Almighty Lord and Savior is all that mattered.  Everyone didn’t understand, and to this day, some people still don’t.  But you know what?  We’re more than okay with that!!!  We made the decision that we felt we needed to at the time, and we have absolutely no regrets! 

3 years ago today I put on a department store dress, went to my Pastor’s house, and became a wife.  There was no bouquet, no music, no extravagant white dress.  There were only a few witnesses, and my husband still wishes he had done a better job of ironing his shirt that day. ;) However, what we lacked in pomp and circumstance, we more than made up for in love, and I wouldn’t have it any other way!

We went on to have the most beautiful wedding in August, and between both of our memorable days, it was everything I’d ever hoped for…a God-inspired success!

I wanted to share a handful of photos from our very private ceremony, as a testament to our faith in Jesus Christ.  When I look at them I can’t believe how time has flown by, but I’m also reminded of a very important lesson I learned that May, 2010. Standing up for God is ALWAYS the right decision, and I’m grateful that He gave me the strength to do just that. :D

HAPPY ANNIVERSARY TO THE MAN WHO HAS MY HEART. 3 DOWN, FOREVER TO GO!  I LOVE YOU!!!!!

I encourage anyone reading this who may be struggling with lustful temptation to repent, seek WISE counsel, and remember that there is no condemnation in Jesus Christ! You can make the choice TODAY to honor Him!!

Happy Love Day!!

February 14, 2013
By lifeofaministermom

Being that it’s Valentine’s Day, I wasn’t surprised to see that the daily prompt was asking us to write an ode to someone or something you love.  There would even be bonus points for poetry! :)  Well, I’m not a poet, lol, and if you’ve read any one of my posts it’s clear that my heart belongs to Jesus, my amazing husband and our incredible little girl.  So instead of dedicating an ode to anyone in particular, I simply want to tell you ALL that I love you!!!

I know there are mixed emotions when it comes to celebrating this particular holiday, and that’s ok.  Maybe your funny valentine is your sister, friend, parent, or child, and like most of us you try to show them your love daily and not just when everyone else is putting emphasis on it.  Whether or not you think today is too commercialized, feel like it puts too much pressure on couples (especially men) to be extravagant, or you’re single and would rather maintain that it’s just another Thursday, ;) I want you to know that God’s love was the first and greatest love of all, and we should be excited to rejoice in that!!

It was on this day, 11 years ago, that my “girls only” celebration with my bestie in college became the night I received the infilling of the Holy Spirit, and I knew then that my status had absolutely no bearing on the unconditional love He has for me!  This same love has seen me through ups and downs, break-ups and make-ups, the loss of my dear daddy, and my marriage to my best friend.  This same love sustains me in a way that I can’t fully express in words, but it’s one I hope to share, even in it’s smallest measure, with each of you!

Regardless of where you are and what you’re doing (or not doing) this evening, let the love of Christ shower you with warmth, peace, and joy.

Love and blessings to you all, and HAPPY VALENTINE’S DAY!!!!!
