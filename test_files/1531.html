Throwback Thursday – Fashion Q&A with Rob Norman of Planet Brooklyn Academy

Rebecca Designers We Love, Fashion Design fashion design, fashion designer, fashion q&a, fashion week, planet brooklyn academy, rob norman, throwback thursday 0

This feature on Planet Brooklyn Academy first appeared on our blog in September 2014. We liked it so much, we’re sharing it again!


Q&A with Rob Norman of Planet Brooklyn Academy

How did you get your start?
I started by one day sitting at my drafting table at my parents’ house sketching. I came up with this logo for Planet Brooklyn. I liked it so much that I decided to print a few shirts and try to sell it.

What’s your creative process?
I usually start by first focusing on a idea or theme. Then I research it. I also like to see what is currently trending in the market. I might be able to merge my concept with what’s going on.

What inspired you to start your business?
The fact that there was no brand out that represented Brooklyn. There were random T-shirts out on the market but no one was stepping up to claim and represent the borough. Its one of the most well known cities in the world. Being born and raised here and having a graphic design background, I thought I’d be the best person to do it.


What is the best part of your job?
Being able to create whatever I want without having someone else tell me what I can or cannot do. Working for other companies in the past that was always the struggle. Designing things that you knew were on trend and having someone else tell you no you can’t do that. But you knew better then them what would work. No more of that. No more restrictions.

What is the hardest thing about running a fashion business?
Knowing what the customer will or won’t like. Sometimes I’ll design something that I’m sure will blow out and it will do just ok. Then I’m create something that I don’t think many will like and I’ll produce a small amount of it and that item will sell out instantly. Its hard to gauge your customer especial when we live in a second changing society.

Where do you gather inspiration for your designs?
I get inspiration from a lot of places. I can get from a hardware store to a supermarket. But a lot of my inspiration comes from people-watching. I enjoy watching people in the city. I’ll just walk around looking at things people wear. That will trigger ideas for me. Naturally I get inspired by magazines as well, but mostly Japanese ones.

What is your advice for someone who wants to start their own line?
Have a good concept and make sure you build on it. Your brand should not be one dimensional. It should be able to bend and expand, not limit you for the future. Many brands never think long term. They only think of the present. That fine if you only expect to be around til tomorrow.


How did your company get its name?
Growing up as a kid in Brooklyn we mostly did everything with our family in Brooklyn. My parents worked in the city. So once they came home especial after a long hard week the last thing they wanted to do was come back to the city. So we stayed in BK. We would go shopping, to the movies, everything. Brooklyn was self-contained. Everyone called it “The Planet.” We were also different. We did things differently here. So the day I sat down thinking of a name for my company, “Planet Brooklyn” was the first name that popped into my head.

Where can customers purchase your products?
They can purchase goods from our flagship store in Brooklyn (517 Atlantic Avenue) or online at http://planetbrooklynacademy.bigcartel.com.
