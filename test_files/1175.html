MY DIY REJECT SHOP VASE COST ME $6!

10 People heart this

I’ve never been an outrageously good DIY-er. It’s all about patience isn’t it? DIY projects take time and definitely patience. And a slight bit of creativity thrown in.

I found these metallic sequinned trimmings at The Reject Shop for $3, and decided to wrap them around a tall jar I’ve always used as a vase for tall flowers.

I liked it plain but I like to even more now it has a fleck of metallic goodness around it!

I’m an absolute sucker for fresh flowers tucked into nooks, crannies and corners of my house so I can’t always afford new handfuls of expensive vases, which is why I decided to create my own.

This would also work with mini jars too. Imagine a family of them? You only need these supplies:

  • Scissors
  • The $3 Metallic trim “Krafters Korner” packet from The Reject Shop (available in stores now)
  • Good adhesive super glue that is ok to use on glass.

So I gave it a red hot shot (and some patience so the lines of sequins measured up) and here’s the result. I used the gold sequins, but the silver would look gorgeous too.

I paired with a copper jug (because, darling they’re all the rage) but you could place at your front door table or as a centre piece for a dinner party.
