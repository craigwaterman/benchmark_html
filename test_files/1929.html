AL FRESCO
From The Bride…Brett and I met 8 years ago at a New Year’s Eve party in New York City. After many years together in New York, we decided to move across the country to start a new life together in Santa Barbara, California. When we got engaged and started thinking about wedding locations, we knew there would be no better way to show our new hometown to our closest family and friends.
We toured a few properties and fell in love with the beautiful gardens at the Butterfly Lane Estate. The ceremony was held in the fern garden where guests were greeted with champagne and invited to gather herbs and flowers for tossing from a vintage potting bench. Instead of a traditional guest book, we used a stack of vintage Santa Barbara post cards and Lenny, our Goldendoodle, was the dog of honor. Following the ceremony, we danced our way back down the aisle to a bluegrass version of the Beatles “I’ve Just Seen a Face.”
Guests enjoyed cocktails and hors d’ouevres in the garden before making their way to dinner and dancing. Escort cards were clipped to an eclectic mix of hanging white frames to guide guests to their tables. String lighting and chandeliers were hung over the reception area, and dark wood tables were decorated with natural runners, cream and green floral centerpieces, and lots of candlelight. Guests danced all evening to an 8-piece band and after dinner, confetti cake was passed and a bourbon bar opened. We finished the evening on the upper deck of the estate with late-night fried chicken sliders.
Our wedding day was truly the best day of our lives. With an incredible team of vendors and our fantastic coordinator, we were able to relax and enjoy every moment.

Elegant Garden Wedding at Crossroads Estate


One of my favorite wedding locations ever (long, ago I managed the property) is Crossroads Estate in Santa Ynez. This bride got ready in the historic craftsman house on the property, and donned a delicate lace and tulle wedding dress by Vera Wang. The bridesmaids wore long flowing dresses in shades of pink and mauve and carried garden bouquets by Hogue and Co. The couple said I do with the dramatic Two Oaks of Crossroads as the backdrop. For dinner, long feasting tables mixed with round tables were decorated with lush green and white florals. Smilax vine trailed down the center of the tables making an airy garland. Cake and cupcakes were made by the always delicious, Enjoy Cupcakes.

Indian Fall Wedding at the Santa Barbara Historical Museum

This colorful Sri Lankan wedding at the Santa Barbara Historical Museum featured a stunning gold embroidered sari and a bright ombre palette featuring reds, oranges and yellows. For the ceremony, Anna Le Pley Taylor created a stunning floral altar and friends of the family decorated the aisle with an ombre rose petal treatment. To bring in a little spanish flavor to the wedding design, spanish tiles with guests names served as the place cards. A mix of long and round tables made up the reception and were decorated with bright flowers like dahlias, orchids, roses and changing fall leaves all arranged elegantly in gold footed containers.Photographer: Michael & Anna Costa | Location: Santa Barbara Historical Museum

Elegant White Wedding at San Ysidro Ranch's Upper Lawn





























































This modern white and cream wedding was held at San Ysidro Ranch's elegant upper lawn. The bridesmaids donned cream dresses to go with the theme and carried white bouquets. The ranch's arbor on the upper lawn was decorated with lush white roses and hydrangea by the floral designer, Toast. For dinner, long wood tables were set with long, and low, white centerpieces mixed with tall candle holders decorated with sprigs of greenery. A round bar featured a large orchid tree and a variety of colorful glasses.
