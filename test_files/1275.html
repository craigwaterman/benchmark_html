How a university presents its course...

Plymouth university:
As i go onto the Plymouth university website it greets me with a welcoming image, the sea, and a natural blue website layout. To the right hand side there is a quote from someone who visited the open day, showing that they help put people in the right direction (helpful for a student confused on their career ... Another addition to why i find this an accessible website is that all of the things what a student would need to know first are laid out in the front page: Courses, prospectus (entry), accommodation, library and 'visit' which links you to the open day.
On the open day page, the fist sentence tells you that you will get both welcomed from the students and the staff. I can see why this is helpful because the stuff will show you the campus, the information and advise on the university, but the students will show you what's its like in their perspective, which is more important on a personal level.

T&A for interviews and making portfolios 

http://career-advice.monster.co.uk/job-interview/preparing-for-job-interviews/top-5-job-interview-tips/article.aspx

I found this advise helpful, for example didn't know how important it is to ask questions. It shoulds your interest and enthusiasm for the job because they want to see how your relevant to the job and not just about yourself. 

http://www.theartcareerproject.com/starting-your-art-career-things-to-remember-when-creating-your-perfect-art-portfolio/3890/

I found this advise helpful because it shows you how to assemble a portfolio that is appropriate to the given situation. To include work that uses all the resources you can get and picking your work that shows the most development though your years of being an artist.



In lesson activities:

The activities about comparing peoples work/exhibitions was helpful because i was able to understand what I need to do when i exhibit my work. It also helped my analytical thinking and why i think some works are better than others, according to my taste. The idea of the money being involved to buy paintings (if only hypothetically) was helpful for decision making. Going around and looking at business cards was inspiring, if we're to design my own business card: understanding different styles is important.



Artist statement.

Schofield was born in Cambridge, moving to Plymouth when he was about 5 years of age and grew up there.
There, he attended Hooe primary school, understanding his interest in art from an early age, and developing it later in plymstock school. After looking at some common artists, developing skills in interpreting artists work, Sam is now intending Plymouth college of art, where he is learning skills in observational drawing, experimental drawing, and inductions into 3D workshops, and can use these skills. His style is influenced by the abstract and expressionist work, using impressionist, 'honest to the medium' backgrounds with layers and abstract forms. The plans for the future are to experiment with and many types of art as possible, to keep the and branch out for different opportunities to learn and present his work to interested audiences.
