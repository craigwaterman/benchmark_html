The Public Tasting of VERITAS Award-Winning Wines: CTICC Ballroom

The Companion In My Hand!


“In Vino VERITAS!” I exclaimed, as I got back home after such a great wine tasting experience in the Cape Town leg of the 2014 public tasting of double gold and gold Veritas Award-winning wine - the excellence!
 
Still Sober: You Could Say :D


The wine tasting took place on 15 October 2014 at the CTICC from 17:00 – 20:00, and included the tasting of Qualité Cheeses. Some light snacks were also served. For an entrance fee of R150.00 wine lovers were greeted with a big wine glass branded with the VERITAS, and the sponsor or the event, Deloitte. Here we were, about to taste some of South Africa’s best wines - double gold and gold Veritas Award-winning wines. 

For a start, I randomly walked about, just to get a feel of the scene and here and there tasted wines without paying particular attention to who the winemaker was. Discourteous,you could say. But it was just a start so I did not wish to drown myself in the names of the so many, and unfamiliar, wines before I get a bit of a feel of the crowd, the chilled music in the background, the wine snobs around, gauging the demographics (well, wine tasting also means being social, isn't it?). Of course, some names were familiar - especially the big wine farms of the Western Cape that "every" wine lover knows - e.g. Spier (I enjoyed their Creative Block range!), Nederburg, KWV, etc.  but finally, I automatically merged with the setting and I was part and parcel of it. 

I must have been tipsy at one point. That quiet, diffident me had evolved in no time and I found myself having conversations with the sommeliers, and asking the right questions, well I believed. But I had to save me time so mostly, I first asked to taste the wines and if it tasted really good only then I would ask for more information about the wine. Like, how the wine was made – grapes, aging, region, etc. I also wanted to know who stocks their wines around so I would know where to go and grab a bottle should I need to in future.   

It was there and then, and perhaps after not just tasting but swallowing (I think I swallowed way too many times than I spitted. Or should I say of over 50 different wines I may have tasted, I do not imagine more than five sips left my mouth! 

And of course a place with many people cannot be without some little nuisance, unfortunately. As I noticed, some folks stand in front of the waiter and talk non-stop when other people are waiting to taste wines from the same table! Things one must avoid during wine tasting, needless to say. 
And before I forget to mention, I was at this event all by myself. The initial friend I should have come with could not make it, and the next friend could not make it, and before I knew it none of my friends could not. Not so understandable. One realises, so many people in the African communities are not into wines – untapped market? Maybe, but that is a subject for another day. In any case, before I left it would seem I met a few people and had great conversations with them.

I stopped every now and again, whenever it seemed convenient and had a small chat, mostly with the senior people that attended. As I discovered, these are “walking universities”, if you will. They have a great deal of knowledge about wines, and because I am learning about this savoured drink(s), it was very exciting to hear about some of their best experiences for the night. For instance, I met one old man, walking with two glasses of wine and their stems miraculously intertwined with his fingers of his left hand while his right had had a small plate with chicken nuggets, with sweet chilli sauce on. So I asked him, why is he holding two glasses of wine? Before he could answer he put his "load" down on a table and said to me, “I am looking for my wife. If you see an old woman looking lost and hungry, please let her know I’m here looking for her”. We both burst into laughter and before I knew it, a chat on some of the best wines we both tasted had started.
I had just tasted port wine from Landskroon. Not so sweet, as I'd expected  - dryish rather, and very good! One of the best I tasted on the night. So I told him, I am usually into dry red wine, especially carbanet sauvignon but this port wine from Landskroon I had, was fantastic. In fact it would be great for a party at my place when I have visitors with palates that cannot handle my dry red wines, such as carbanet saugvinon. And that's when he mentioned to me that carbanet saugvinon is his least favourite wine, and he prefers shiraz rather. In his words, shiraz in an old man’s wine. It is “drinkable” and quiet…That I guess I'm yet to find out.
Landskroon Port Wines

On port wine, it was then that he told me that if I am that impressed with Landskroon (Cape Vintage) port wine, I must try Booplaas wines as they have been making the best port wines for over 25 years! I did not waste time, and dashed to the Booplaas table. Man, I have never had such wonderfully made port wines. If you can manage to, try them and come and thank me later.
2012 Boplaas Cape Vintage Reserve
The Boplaas Cape Vintage Reserve must have been the best port wine I have ever tasted!
Boplaas Port Wines

Another old man I met, while on the look out for best carbanet sauvignon reffered me to Simonsig wines, stressing that I should not leave without trying them - "they make some of the best red wines", he said. This would be my second last table, before a man of the microphone announced it is 5 minutes to 8:00pm and they will be closing.
Simonsig Dry Red Wine
Before that, it was mentioned that there is a brandy made in 1964 at the KWV table so I dashed to their table so I could get a taste before I could leave. This is where I met Matt Knight. I, kind of, randomly stopped by him - I commented that I saw him at a couple of tables and he had been asking relevant questions, is he in the industry and if so, what does he do? Very friendly, Matt told me that he was in the industry before and he is working on coming back. And then it had to be my turn to answer the question, and that is when I told him that I simply love wine, wish to learn more about wines, and because I love writing I’d update a blog I have started on my journey to discovering wines, one sip at a time. As a result, you’re reading this!

You almost by now can see that I had a great time. Unfortunately time and space does not allow for one to tell everything - but these were some of my highlights for the nigh. I look forward to the 2015 installment and I hope I mange to convince some friends to come join.
The Unlikely Wine Snob?

What, wine blogger from South Africa, North?!





Tzaneen, a small town in Limpopo, the northernmost province of South Africa where vineyards are not so much heard of, is where I’m from. However I’ve been living in Cape Town for four years now. Yes, the home of wine as you know it. Let me tell you the shortest story about myself. I love wine. Especially the red. Dry red, fine. I'll however, reluctantly, also tell you that I’m not an expert (yet). I just enjoy wine, and this blog is about the journey I have taken to date and my experiences with wine, from experimenting with rose to being a semi-wine-snob. I kid, on the latter. 



That is, the blog is a diary (Yes, call it that, or something similar) about this journey. What inspired the idea for the blog is simply the love of writing and exploration of (from time to time J), fine wine.  



I could have well titled this blog, “From Tzaneen (to Cape Town) With Love For Wine”.



Another glass of the Cabernet Sauvignon, please!
