Gothic Grandeur: Spooky Halloween

I decided to have a Gothic inspired Halloween bash this year, so I have compiled a blog of creepy inspiration.

New blog every week (usually Thursday).

These photos are from Urban Ghost Blog, a wonderfully spooky blog featuring deserted mansions and their stories.

Polish estate home.

Craig Finlay deduced this was the home of Takieddin el-Solh, Prime Minister of Lebanon from 1973-74, and again briefly in 1980.  The binders were full of political documents and voter lists.  Takieddin clearly abandoned his home swiftly after Syria demanded that he live out his days in Paris.

Two above photos by: Opacity

Etsy

If you want to buy your own creepy Victorian mansion…this McIntosh home is for sale in Florida.

The first known records of the McIntosh area was described in a 1851 diary. The passage was written by George Houston who described it as a plantation owned by Col. John Houston and was destroyed during the Second Seminole War in the early 1820’s.  Records and deed confirmations date back to the same time frame where a Col. McIntosh lost his sugar mill also to the destruction of Indians.

Vintage brass dragon griffon candle holders. Etsy

Photo by Past Glory. Take a look at his site, many more beautiful images of this strange building.

Bette Castle history (via Urban Ghost Blog): Built in the 16th century, the castle remained in the family Bette until the 1800′s when it was used for the local gin distillery, a sugar refinery, a potash refinery, and a tobacco factory, which was housed in the caves of the castle. In the 1900′s the castle was sold to a religious order who bult an impressive neo-gothic chapel. After the First World War an institution bought the castle and established a school for the local children.

I am a bona fide design junkie who has traveled the world in search of beautiful things.

I created and ran a design studio and home furnishings boutique store in Napa called "La Vie Belle Interiors". The stores eclectic inventory included unique finds from England, France, Italy and India.

Six years later I packed it up to move to Finland with my husband where I fell in love with Scandinavia modern design. I was attracted to the playful spirit of this European style; sophisticated with a funky edge.

After three years in Finland the sprawling vineyards and rolling hills of Napa beckoned me back. We returned to the states, buying and gutting a 1950's house. The new spacious floor plan has a clean loft like feel, perfect to fill with my flea market finds

Currently I run Life Style Interiors, a home design business and Style & shoot a staging and photography business
