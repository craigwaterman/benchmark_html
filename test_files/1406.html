Comfort Food and Your Inner Child

The bf was going to have a tough friday with a big presentation at his internship and 2 interviews. So I decided to surprise him with a treat. This is a rare ‘sweets’ blog but there are a few desserts I do have a weakness for. And old school ice cream sandwiches are definitely one of them. Living in the bay area, there are a ton of ice cream options to choose from, but Three Twins is quickly becoming one of my favorite! So what better way to start then with 2 delicious pints of cookies and cream.

Let that soften and pile the creamy mess into a waxed line tray.

Mash it all flat till you get a beautiful sheet of ice cream and throw that back in the freezer and let it harden. Now the cookies. I followed this recipe and only used the cookies. Sounded so good! Got the dry ingredients all happily sifted in a bowl, and man does it look promising. Whipped the butter and sugar and worked all the ingredients till it became a lovely dark brown ball of dough. Throw that into the fridge so it’s easier to roll out. Eventually, I got this..

To look like this!

 

I was so excited! Popped it in the oven and waited expectedly. 16 minutes later..this came out.

 

The shapes did NOT hold. Totally disappointed in the recipe! Taste however was pretty rockin’, so at least I had that going. I’m a stickler for visuals so lopsided cookies were going to make the cut. I figured while they were hot, I would use my cookie cutter and trim of the access. Not as pretty, but def got the job done!

Now, more waiting. Cookies have to cool. Multiple batches are being made. So. Much. Waiting. Good thing my sweet tooth had gone to bed by then. Finally, everything is ready! Now time to cut the ice cream and assemble!

 

Home made cookies and cream ice cream sandwiches. Really hit the spot after a tedious day of interviews! Just for fun, I wrapped them up individually in waxed paper! I wished I had stickers I could personalize that with.
