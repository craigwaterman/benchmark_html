December 31, 2014

So long 2014…

This year has had some amazing moments- finding out I was pregnant, and with a girl no less; my mom beating cancer; I fell into a job that I absolutely love; I launched a business that is making me money and making me happy; I found my obsession with Pure Barre and realized I actually like exercising; I found out angels are real; I have spent more time with my grandmother this year than in the last five combined. But this year has had some incredibly low moments too- my mom had cancer; I found out that crazy odds can happen to you; I learned what thanatophoric dysplasia was and how to say it; my dad got staples in his head; I said goodbye to my oldest living family member and saw my grandmother loose her other half; I learned that our country’s medical system and insurance is super broken; I learned what a broken heart really feels like. I said it a few weeks ago, and gave the world wide web notice that I was over this year and that 2015 was going to be different.  Well the purging has begun- it started with a huge donation pile that got hauled off right before Christmas. I also think I have purged my body weight in mucus the last week… sorry TMI!

But in my quest to have a bigger, brighter, more self focused and successful 2015 I turned to one of my oldest and dearest friends from high school. Catie is the living breathing embodiment of how I want to feel- she is brave, she is confident, she rocks red hair, wears heels without apology (she towers over most people barefoot), she has impeccable taste and style yet spends most days in waders fishing… she is my ultimate girl crush! She is one of the very few people on this earth that I trust implicitly- even knowing how wicked she can be sometimes (and those are some of my favorite memories of her!) If she tells me to buy and album, read a book or see a movie- I will; no questions asked. So when she told me about The Desire Map- I bought it… granted I’m reading at a snail’s pace, I’m obsessed. It is the voice I was looking for to guide me through 2015. Danielle Laporte says it like it is, doesn’t muss and fuss with a lot of flowery words, she uses the f-word a LOT, but more than anything she makes sense to me.I’m not going to give you the whole sales pitch because that’s not what I’m here for- I’m telling you about it so that some changes in my blog will make sense to you… Last year I started posting on Thursdays about what I was thankful for that day. It was a great exercise- it made me acutely aware of the many blessings, big and small that I have in my life- even when it felt like life was circling the drain. But it has had a side effect that I don’t really care for- being so keenly aware of my many many blessings has left me with zero patience for people who complain… all I want to do when someone complains is flip it on them and say but you have all these amazing blessings! And maybe I should, instead I kinda tune out and hear this ugly voice in my head that says “this is not a REAL problem…” I don’t like that because I know that to that person in that moment it is a REAL problem and I need to be more empathetic- how lucky they are that their REAL (at that moment) problem isn’t all that terrible. I need to loose that ugly voice in my head and so in 2015 Thankful Thursdays are going by the wayside. I will still continue my habit of counting my blessings but it will be more private. Instead I am going to start sharing my Desire Map inspired trust ritual…

“Focus on the present. The whole point of this exercise is to access the trust that already lives in your being. Unwavering. Multidimensionally verifiable. True for you. It’s not about generating new trust, or visualizing outcomes, or affirming your way into positive thinking. We’re concentrating on the now that is already working… Trust in the Now.”

But I guess I’ll keep with the Thursdays (because Trust starts with a T too!) but they will be Trust Thursdays where I will share with you what I trust in at the moment. It may be short one week and long another. I have no idea where this exercise will take me but I’m going to trust it!

In 2014 I spent a whole lot of time praying,asking, begging, bargaining with God to make things different/ better/ fixed/ healed whatever… it hasn’t really worked out so well for me. I’m closing the books on this year having lost two babies (yup- you read that right, I kept the most recent loss to myself and still am not really ready to share those feelings) and nothing has gotten better/ fixed/ healed. I haven’t given up on my faith- it’s gotten stronger if anything but it isn’t really working so this year I’m giving up on the requesting… Ala Danielle Laporte I am instead going to try a year of declarations- Declarations of desire- I want 2015 to be a great year! I want 2015 to be a year of positivity and creativity! I want to be healthy and strong in 2015! 

I also want to come up with a daily creative exercise but I haven’t quite fleshed that out yet- I need to find something that I can actually do daily… most likely will tap into my old photography roots (did you know I started college as a photography major…learn something new everyday!) as that makes the most sense but I can’t wrap my head around what I want to photograph 365 times… so stay tuned for that one! and I’m totally up for suggestions…

I told you she liked to drop F-bombs!

My mother is probably appalled by the F-word but it really does make the point much more than just “Be a Super Star.”

So there you have it… that’s where I am at this moment- as 2014 fades into the background- I’m ready to take on 2015 (or at least I think I am!).

 

Let’s do this 2015!

Xoxo, The Mrs.
