Sayonara, 2013, You Hot-Mess-of-a-Year!

Apologies for going on a bit of a hiatus for a few months. My career is way more important than keeping you folks entertained with my dating shenanigans (Just kidding, I love you so much)!

2013 was quite an interesting year.. I learned A LOT about myself, mainly that I make horrible decisions when it comes to love. BUT, don't you worry, I'm getting better at it. That's how I learn best: I make awful, life-altering decisions, and then am forced to live through my mistakes. The outcome is usually: "HA! Wow! That was a close one!" or "MAN, I am NEVER doing THAT again." So, to pass on this gift of life's lessons I've accumulated this year, here is a new segment I like to call:

"Alison's DO's and DON'T'S: Life Advice So You Don't Fuck Up Like I Did"

DO! Meet a guy who is older than you. (In most cases) a man's maturity level = his real age minus 2 years (This doesn't matter quite as much when they start to close in on 40).
DON'T! Think for a second that just because he's older means he's mature.. because he does, unfortunately, still live with his mom.
DO! Meet a guy who is thoughtful enough to come pick you up at work when it's late and cold.
DON'T! Immediately blurt out that you love him.
DO! Make plans to move in with your stable boyfriend.
DON'T! Make plans to move in with your stable boyfriend of 3 months.
DO! Take your new boyfriend to a Red Sox game.
DON'T! Buy matching Red Sox shirts. He won't tell you it bothers him; instead he'll just drink himself into oblivion.

DO! Say you understand that relationships take a long time to develop and you don't expect a boyfriend to pop out of the sky.
DON'T! Just SAY it, you dummie.
DO! Realize that you need to lower your expectations and be more realistic about what you can actually afford to do in your dating life that won't compromise researching for your thesis.
DON'T! Date two guys at once. A bunch of casual relationships > the sum of the individual encounters. It's called "synergy." Look it up.

DO! Meet a guy that your friends set you up with at their wedding!
DON'T! Think for a second it's going to work out even though he's handsome and nice and has amazing dance moves; he lives over 500 miles away! (I said don't do it!)
DO! Agree to host him for a weekend that he's in town for work, because A GIRL GOTTA EAT. (And by eat I mean have sex).
DON'T! Agree to accompany him on his family vacation which perfectly coincides with the one week of actual break from school even though the thought of lying on a beautiful white beach with not a single care in the world and getting all that vitamin D you've been deprived of from sitting in front of a computer screen for 4 months in a room with no windows.. Wait, what were we talking about? Oh yeah! NO! DON'T DO IT! Because you will buy your ticket and he will get cold feet and change his mind. Just don't.

DO! Surround yourself with quality friends. You know, the kind that slap you over the head for being dumb and then turn around and give you a hug because they know you need it. Buy them lots of Chinese food and red wine, because you're lucky they stick around to listen to your ridiculous problems (I think secretly they find it entertaining).

DO! Try to enjoy the time that you have to yourself while you're single. You will probably look back on it someday and go "Man, those were the days.. I never got knocked up, and I could fart in my own bed."

DO! Look back on the mistakes you've made and know that one day you'll laugh about it. Because OH MY GOD CAN YOU BELIEVE I DID THAT HAHA!

DO! Find humor in as many situations as possible. That is why I write this blog. When shit happens, it sure does suck, but writing about it in a way that gives people a chuckle is probably the best medicine I can think of. It helps to know that someone else is struggling with the same, or similar, problems. It reminds us that we're human, and it's okay to make dumb mistakes (Just not too many, okay? Don't go crazy).

What Becomes of the Broken-Hearted?

I think it's safe to say that break-ups are never easy.

Some are horribly blind-siding, leaving you feeling like the shell of a person you used to be. Some you find yourself having to do the breaking. And some are mutual or just fall apart.

I've had my fair share of every kind, and in my experience - as it is in all kinds of human interaction - communication is going to be your best friend. And to be honest, that's really all the advice I think I am qualified to give. Every situation is going to be different, but it never hurts to be honest and up front about your feelings.
In the end, it's really all about #1. Are YOU happy? When you're in a relationship for any amount of time, you (hopefully) start to put yourself and your needs somewhat aside to please the person you're with. They have a schedule. They have likes and dislikes that are different from yours. They live in another town. Sacrifices are made in order to spend time together - albeit little ones at first - but they have to happen, and you want them to happen because it's worth it.

But sometimes, it's not worth it anymore. Some people are bad at drawing that line. Some don't even know it can be drawn! Which makes me sad, but it's true. I have always been good at drawing a line for myself. I almost never drink too much. (Food is a different story). I just don't like the way it feels when I'm hungover. I'm completely useless. Sue me, but I like to be able to wake up in the morning and actually be functional/not feel like shit. I know that part of the difficulty in relationships is finding that balance with someone. Figuring out how to work together and what things you're willing to give up in order to be together.

For me, my career is first. I've obviously been unlucky with love, but as soon as right after my first failure, I decided right there that it wasn't going to drive my life. I knew I needed to find something that I could do every day that would make me happy. A career in interior design was/is my idea of a life that I would enjoy no matter what (or who) happened.



I'm not saying everyone should have or should take her advice, but it's an option. I know many of my friends who have not necessarily actively decided not to get married or even date, but happened to choose to make their career/job/livelihood a priority instead. Eventually, you can have it all, but not all at once.

I have never been great with money. I'm probably going to be in a crapload of debt when I graduate. But I will have accomplished something for myself, by myself (with support of my family and friends, of course), and I only have a year left, so I might as well keep doing it by myself! When I started my program almost 2 years ago, I sincerely thought (or really really hoped) I would meet someone. Someone who I could come home to. He might even be in school, too, and we could make dinner at nine- or ten-o'clock and tell each other about the latest project or what koo-koo classmates said or did (This is basically what I do now with my roommates, but they won't cuddle with me afterwards). Someone to just be there.

I didn't think this was much to ask, but I guess it is. The truth is real, meaningful, lasting relationships take time, and I've always been a rush-into-things type. Perhaps this means I need a man who will slow me down and encourage me to take a step back and just fucking THINK about things for a SECOND, will ya?! Sometimes you meet a guy who is not this type. Sometimes you meet a guy who is your exact type and also rushes into things. Sometimes it feels right and you just go for it because you've been waiting so long and all the pieces fit together on the surface, but you don't even wait to find out what's underneath because you're under some magic honeymoon-phase spell. It happens. To some people I know.

Obviously, I'm human. I make mistakes. I try to remind myself that everyone else is human, too, and not to have too high expectations. One of the best parts of being single is you GET to be selfish. You get to work on yourself. Being in a relationship opens you up, you see all of these little flaws and then you break-up and all you're left with is the opportunity to fix them. If you don't break-up? Great. Then hopefully you're with someone who understands and helps you become a better person anyway. It's just a decision you have to make. Are these problems too big for two people to juggle? Are you better off juggling on your own?

I still have so much to work on with myself. Hell, I haven't even cleaned my room today! But I'm okay with it. I don't know if I'll jump into the dating pool anytime soon (summer classes start this evening and I still technically have two jobs.. and a cat), but I will certainly keep you posted.

My Worst Date Ever

Before I meet up with someone, there are a few "musts" that have to check out. Height, age, weight (I don't do skinny), sense of humor .. but obviously the first thing I look at is their photo(s). Now I don't like to judge solely based on photos. I read the whole profile and if it's mildly entertaining and we have a lot in common, then I'll send a message.

So sometime last summer, during my semester, I sent a message to a guy who checked out with all of the above. The only hesitation I had was that he was kind of a "pretty boy". But I thought, "Hey, his profile seems smart and witty. There maybe more to him that meets the eye." So I set up a date with him on a Sunday afternoon at a tapas place around the corner from where I go to school. I did some school work and met him around 6:00. Let's call "him" Mike. Mike is 29, a lawyer and lives in Back Bay. 

I get there a few minutes early and grab a seat at the bar. I ordered a glass of red wine. Mike texts me he is almost there.. Shortly, a tall man with dark hair (Yep, that's the guy. Kind of too good looking, but let's give him a chance, Alison!) walks up to the bar wearing a bright teal polo .. popped collar. 

Okay, a little bit of a red flag.

Introductions were made and he sits down. The bartender asks what he wants to drink. Without picking up the menu, looking at my drink or around the bar for the taps, he simply states "Sauv blanc."

Yep, red flag.

To begin our conversation, he explains to me he couldn't meet up until now because he drank heavily Thursday, drank heavily Friday and had to physically remove himself from the city and stay at his parents' in Wellesley in order to not drink for a third night in a row.

Hmm.. Double red flag.

We continue chatting about this and that and all I can remember from the short conversation to follow was that he hated on Philadelphia for some reason (I think the Eagles were being highlighted on Sports Center) and he tells me about how awful it was to live in Delaware for a few months once when he got transferred for his job. He actually admitted to whining to his superiors to get him transferred back to Boston.. and obviously it worked. Lucky us! Also, I mentioned (in context) a restaurant in Southie and he said he doesn't frequent that area because it's "too far".. from Back Bay.

Next he told me about the three gyms he goes to - Is this for real? - one in his office building, one in his apartment building and the one he actually belongs to. I tried to relate and told of a friend who had a pool in her Chinatown apartment building.. "Oh! Chinatown, I love Chinatown! There's this place that serves cold tea until 6:00 am!"

"Cold tea?"
"Yeah! Cold tea, get it?"
"No, I don't get it. Please enlighten me."
"Cold tea.. BEER. They serve beer in tea pots after 2:00.. It's so fun, it's like a giant dance party with like 5 floors and everyone's doing blow in the bathroom!"

A million red flags flying everywhere!

"Blow.." (pause) half-chuckling at the ludicrous turn our conversation has taken and half at the fact that I am actually stuck talking to this douche I say, "I guarantee you, not only will I never do blow, but I'm usually in bed by midnight."

"Well you're no fun."

HA!

I turn to him, smile and say "Maybe we should just chalk this up as a loss."

His first expression: mild shock. Second: shrugs shoulders. "Yeah, sure.. Check!"

I offer to pay, he refuses. We sit in awkward silence for a few minutes, both with almost full glasses of wine (he on his second SAUV BLANC), distracting ourselves with our phones. I try to drink my wine quickly without chugging it. I make a comment about a dessert I had there once. He interrupts me and says "Yeah, I've been here. I know what the food's like."

His phone rings and he answers it. "Yeah, what's up? No, I'm not doing anything. What are you up to?"

At this point, I'm so fed up I don't even care. I down my wine, push back my chair and say "Nice meeting you!" into his phone and walked out.

I get outside and I CANNOT STOP LAUGHING. I am laughing so hard I look like a crazy person. I think mainly in disbelief that there are actually cliches like him roaming the interwebs of OKCupid. I immediately call my roommate and recount the event, mostly excited because I can tell her such a hilarious story.

So if I ever change my lifestyle completely and become a druggie alcoholic 'roid-head.. I know who I'm gonna call : )
