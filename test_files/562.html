Writing and Travel

   Writing is used in all aspects of life, whether people realize it or not.  Travel involves writing in several ways.  Examples of writing include travel brochures, blogs, and books.  Travel brochures are created to inform prospective travelers about the background information of the destination, tourist attractions, dining, hotels, and much more.  Travelers write blogs while they are on their trips so they can share their experiences with family members, friends, and other travelers.  Some travelers write blogs after they have come back from their trip.  World famous travelers may write books about their adventures.  These famous travelers include Marco Polo and Richard Burton.  Books have been written about the travels of famous adventurers and navigators, one of them being Christopher Columbus.  Books have also been written about travel within the United States and different regions.  For example, Charles Kuralt a famous journalist, wrote about his travels across North Carolina in his book titled, On the Road with Charles Kuralt.  
   The field of travel is impacted by the travel brochures, blogs, and books. because these publications persuade people to travel to those particular locations that the publications are centered around.  They inform people about the destinations, which makes people fall in love in the destination and want to go there.  Blogs and books written by travelers have a great impact on the field of travel.  The writers put their thoughts and emotions into their publications, which causes the reader to relate to them.  The reader is able to see that the writer is so passionate about the places they have been, which may cause them to want to share the same experiences as the writer.
   Travel is similar to the writing process in several ways.  Before people go on a trip, they brainstorm of places they would like to go and then research their destination , which is similar to the brainstorming component of prewriting.  Planning the trip is similar to the first draft of a paper because you decide your destination and method of travel, which may change along the way.  After things are set in stone, reservations may change, flights may be cancelled, or people may cancel, which causes the plans to be revised.  After the revision, new reservations may be made, new flights may be booked, and new people may be added.  This is similar to the second draft.  After the plans are revised and the trip begins, the travelers will be ready to enjoy their trip, which is similar to the final draft of the paper.

Ireland

My Scottish ancesters lived in the Scottish lowlands and later migrated to Ireland to start a new life before migrating to America.  Since I am Scot-Irish, I would love to go to Ireland to see where my ancestors lived.  Also, I heard that Ireland was a beautiful place.  There is so much history there and many things to see and do.  One of the things I would like to do is go to is the Clifden Traditional Music Festival, which includes traditional Irish music and dancing. When I was little I enjoyed watching the dancers perform at the highland games, so I would love to watch dancers from Ireland.  These festivals allow people to listen to music, watch dancers, and also learn how to dance. They allow people to learn more about the Irish culture.


Also, I would like to go to several Gaelic sporting events.  These events include the Leinster GAA Hurling Championship, the Leinster GAA Football Championship, and the Rugby Festival.  I've always enjoyed watching sports, so I would definitely enjoy watching sports from another country, especially live at a stadium.


I would also like to explore some of Ireland's most well known cities, one of which includes Dublin.  Dublin is known for it's luxurious shopping and it's arts and crafts.  The Powerscourt Townhouse Centre consists of Dublin's finest boutiques.  It's a great place to go if you're looking for designer clothing.  Whichcraft, in Old City, contains Irish art and jewelry.  It's definitely a great place to buy souvenirs to take back home.

Monday, March 19, 2012

Scotland

   Not only would I love to go to the hottest vacation spots, but I would love to tour countries in Europe.  The main country I would love to visit is Scotland because I have a Scot-Irish heritage.  Ever since I was little my dad has encouraged me to learn about where our family came from and to be proud of it.  He has taken me to several Scottish festivals where I was able to learn more about my Scottish background.  At this festival, I learned that each family in Scotland belongs to a clan.  My ancestors belonged to Clan Crawford which is a sub-clan of Clan Lindsay.  I used to think it was so cool that there was a clan that was the same as my last name.  Our clan crest is shown below.

The motto "Tutum Te Rebore Reddam," translates to "I will give you safety by strength." 

If or when I go to Scotland some day, I would like to visit of Castle Crawford which is in ruins.

I would also like to take a tour of Edinburgh Castle, which is one of Scotland's most famous castles.  It serves as an active army base and is home to the Scottish National War Memorial and two Scottish regiments.


Another castle I would like to visit is Dunstaffnage Castle and Chapel, which is another well known castle in Scotland.  Castles are a great way to find out about Scotland's history.

One thing I would love to do while visiting Scotland is go to the Highland Games.  The Highland Games began hundreds of years ago when clan chiefs encouraged their clan members to take part in competitions to see who had the skills for battle.  Events include caber tossing, tug of war, track and field events.




I think it would be fun to visit Scotland because there are a lot of tourist attractions.  It would also be cool to see the country where my family originated from.

Thursday, March 8, 2012

Baltimore

   During the summer before my Freshman year of college, I went on my first mission trip to Baltimore with my youth group.  I loved serving people, so I was very excited! I was a little nervous since we were staying in West Baltimore, which is a dangerous area.  The night we arrived, there were four murders that occured.  It's a good thing my mom didn't know about it or she would've freaked out!
    As soon as we got to the church we were staying at for the week, we unloaded our luggage into the sanctuary.  After we finished unloading, we helped out with the children's camp that was going on.  We helped them make crafts and played games with them.  Most of the kids did not have opportunities to make crafts or play games at home, so I was glad to be able to help them have a good experience.  Each day that week we hosted the kids camp.  First, the kids would learn a bible lesson, then they would have snack time, and then they would do crafts and games until it was time to go home.

One of the main service projects we did painting murals and repainting the walls in the local elementary school.  One afternoon, our whole group went and the next day and repainted the walls.  The next day, only a few people, including myself, went to paint the murals.

That night, our youth group put on a benefit concert for the people in baltimore.  It was similar to a block party.  We had food, games, and music. We had a table set up for people who wanted to donate clothes.  People who needed the clothes were encouraged to look through the piles and take what they neeeded. It was a very successful night and the locals enjoyed it!

On one of the nights, during dinner time, we cooked food for homeless people.  The church has a program where they cook dinner for them once a week.  I was able to talk to a few of them and here about there experiences.  It was very eye opening.

During this trip, I saw a lot of things that I was not used to seeing such as prostitutes, drug busts, and SO many homeless people.  I was glad to be able to help the people that have witnessed or have been a victim of all of these things.  It made me realize how blessed I am and to not take anything for granted.  I enjoyed this mission trip and I am hoping to go back very soon!

Wednesday, February 15, 2012

Sneaking into Chicago

      During the summer of my Senior year of high school, my youth pastor, his wife, a youth leader, three of my friends, and I went on a special trip called, "Senior Sneak." It was called "Senior Sneak" because we didn't know where we were going until we got in the van to leave.  Our parents had to keep a secret from us for about five months. When we got in the van, they told us we were going to Chicago.  I was so excited because I had been told that there is so much to do there and it is a fun city to visit.  I would say it was one of my favorite trips.
      One the first day, we spent a lot of time shopping and walking around the surrounding area of our hotel.  The next day, we visited all of the tourist attractions.  We woke up really early and walked to the store that sells day passes for the bus.  We bought our tickets and then walked to Lincoln Park Zoo.  The Zoo was so awesome! We had so much fun looking at all of the animals and taking pictures.  Since the zoo was huge, we were so worn out by the time we finished, and it was only twelve o'clock.  After we went to the zoo, we ate lunch at a Giordiano's, a pizza place that I recommend everyone going to!  If you have never had Chicago style pizza, you must try it! It's delicious!  After lunch, we decided to walk off all of the pizza we ate, and walk down the "Magnificent Mile." If you don't know what that is, It's a mile of shopping in downtown Chicago, that is practically across the street from Lake Michigan.  We went in the coolest souvenir shops that you wouldn't find anywhere else.  It was hard shopping for a souvenir, because there were so many great choices!






After we walked down the "Magnificent Mile," we went to Millenium Park, which is a place that has huge sculptures.  The sculptures were so cool.  The sculptures included a dragon, a dinosaur, a huge reflective jelly bean, and many more.  My favorite was the jelly bean.


After we went to Millenium Park, we took the bus to the Art Institute of Chicago.  They have one of the most well-known museums in the United States.  Their admission is free after six o'clock on Thursday nights, which was definitely a plus.  The museum had some interesting art and some of it, I would've never considered art before going to the museum.  They had so many rooms, I felt like I was in a maze.  The museum was huge and we were so worn out by the end of it, but it was fun!





  That was my first time going to an art museum, and I would definitely recommend you go if you get a chance.  The next day we went to the Navy Pier.  They have a lot of shopping and it's cool because it's on Lake Michigan.  After that, we went to Lake Michigan's beach.  I never realized that Lake Michigan had a beach.  It was too cold to go in the water, so we just played cards and chilled.  It was definitely a great place to relax and get away from all of life's stresses.  We then, went to a cool pizza place called, Gino's.  They let you "graffiti" on the walls, which made for a fun atmosphere.  Sadly, lunch marked the end of our trip.



  Although we weren't going home yet and just driving to Indiana, we were all sad to leave.  Chicago is a great city, especially if you love sight seeing and pizza! I would recommend it to everybody!

Trapped


Have you ever felt like you were trapped in your hometown and just can't get out? Well, that's exactly how I feel.  I am an only child who's parents want to stay by their side forever.  I have lived in the same house in Charlotte, NC my whole life and have been to eleven states, eight of which are in the South East.  I love Charlotte and Boone but I am ready to see new people and new places.  Up until my Senior year of High School, the south was the only part of the United States in which I had been exposed.  As I have gotten older, many of my friends have been going on mission trips and vacations to different countries.  Every time they come back from a trip and tell stories it makes me want to go even more.  My goal before I graduate is to at least go to one foreign country.  What better time to do that than your college years, right? Call me crazy, but I would love to go on a mission trip for several weeks to places such as Africa, Haiti, Costa Rica, or Honduras.  Many of my friends have gone to these places and when they came back, their lives were touched.  My heart breaks for people who are under privileged and I want to serve them and show them that they are loved.



Besides underprivileged foreign countries, I want to visit other countries for leisure such as Scotland, Australia, France, and Italy.  These countries are beautiful and have so much history.  I have Scottish heritage and I think it would be cool to see where my ancestors came from.  Not only would I love to travel internationally, but I would love to just go somewhere in the United States.  I am a very Chill person and I love the beach, so I would like to go to California or Hawaii.  I've heard that both of these states are beautiful and there is to much to do and explore.  Whether its for leisure or missions, I want to see the world, share my experiences, and be exposed to other cultures.  Stay tuned for my next post as I share where I have been as well as more of my aspirations.
