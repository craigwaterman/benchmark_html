Scent from Above - Layering Gucci

For christmas I received a new scent - Flora by Gucci. I was a bit wary because I rarely stray from my usual Seductive (Guess) but I can safely say i've found a new favourite. I'd recommend you go and try it but no doubt you'll fall in love as soon as you try it and the last thing we need is everyone walking around smelling the same. Flora has (you guessed it) a strong floral scent, but it's perfect for wear both day and night I've been surprised by how many comments I get about it.

Though generally a bit more expensive, I generally try and buy the eau de parfum (EDP) rather than the toilette (EDT) as it lasts a hell of a lot longer. In addition, what few people seem to know is the art of 'layering'. Flora, like many scents, are available in shower gels and moisturisers. When each of these products are used with one another, it helps the scent to stay for a few hours longer, so there is no need to worry about reapplying it throughout the day.

If you're a fan of floral scents - next time you're in a department store and see Flora by Gucci give it a go, I promise you'll be hooked!


RRP:
EDP 75ml $164AUD
EDT 75ml $139AUD

Let's Talk Numbers


Recently i've begun getting serious with a new guy. Finally! The dreaded 'does he like me?' and 'what exactly are we?' type questions are no longer taking over my brain and inhibiting me from acting like the relatively normal person I claim to be.

However, celebrations don't last long - I now realise that i'm now faced with a whole new set of dilemmas - at the forefront of these is what I like to call the numbers game.  When is it the right time to discuss the number of sexual partners we have had? Is it ever right to ask or is it best kept to oneself? Finally, does it really even matter? 

Though i'd like to think that i'm a firm believer in the age old 'don't ask, don't tell' philosophy, I find myself beginning to stress about this imminent conversation. It's bound to come up sometime - right? When I consider this, my head still feels like it's exploding with a million different questions. I have by no means bedded so many guys that I can no longer keep count on my fingers and toes, but what if my number is 'too low' or even 'too high'? And if it's higher than average (what even is 'average'?) or, worse, higher than his?

I once found a statistic that the average Australian woman has 11 sexual partners in her lifetime (whilst the average Aussie man has 24). It's a little reassuring to hear that Australians, in comparison to other figures from around the globe, seem to be on the higher end of the scale. Does this small fact about our personal lives even matter, or can it have the potential to be a deal breaker in a new relationship?

More importantly, does a person's number really even  matter - it is in the past after all. In a time where one night stands and 'friends with benefits' are so common, does this number even really make a difference? I understand a person's possible reluctance if their significant other is 'less experienced' - on the flip side they may be intimidated by the other if their number is significantly higher (not to mention the possible health issues associated with racking up a number into the stratosphere). 

In the end though, does it really matter? For every person this is different - but as long as you're safe, happy & healthy I believe that's all that really matters. Let me know your thoughts in the comments below - I'd love to hear your opinions and if your in a similar position as I am right now!

Champagne Taste on a Beer Budget


As a student juggling a full time uni course and two jobs, many would think the last thing I have time for is finding time to spend anything i've earned. Though, like any normal-person-with-a-job, come tax time I ask myself where all that money has gone. Seems to have just disappear seconds after I've received it, really. Poof. 

Helpfully, a colleague of mine constantly reminds me that the reason for this is probably due to my 'champagne taste on a beer budget'. Thanks for that. No really. This post is dedicated to a few of the finer things in life that I find myself to be admiring and, though somewhat unrealistically, wishing for. 

Like any woman, there's a special place in my heart for the perfect  bag. On the left is Givenchy's Antigona. Retail price in Australia is around $2500AUD. I first saw this in a magazine photo layout and fell in love. If I've fallen in love with this Givenchy creation, it has nothing on what I feel when I lay eyes on Prada's Saffiano (pictured right). I first saw this in the Prada store window on King St in Perth a few years ago andI could not take my eyes off of it. Retail price in Australia is around $2600 (it comes in a few sizes, and even better every colour of the rainbow!). Classic, simple, yet sophisticated.



Let me know in the comments below which one you prefer!
Sigh. One day.

Review: Royal Danish Hospital Diet

Let's go back to January 1. Like most of us, my new year's resolution centred around a certain 'healthy lifestyle'. Though motivated by all the Christmas-binges, I'm ashamed to say I fell off the bandwagon after a pathetic ten days. 

A month later I stumbled across a diet referred to as the Royal Danish Hospital Diet (also known as the Copenhagen Diet). This diet, though difficult, promises to shed large amounts of weight and boost the metabolism all in just 13 days. Seems worth a shot. Click here for a link to the diet. 

February 6th marked Day 1 of metabolism-boosting goodness. 
When I look at what I have to eat today my stomach turns. 500g of spinach. REALLY?! Believe me when I say 500g is more than you think. All I can say is I have a newfound respect for pop-eye - props to you because I hope I never have to see that much leafy green for as long as I live.

First few days were tough - constant feeling of hunger and thinking about what I was allowed to eat next. But with determination to make it through the full 13 days I power ahead. 

By Day 4 I feel as though I can already begin to see results - though only having lost around 2kg. Still, 2kg in 4 days isn't bad, it's motivating to see quick results.

I begin Day 5 tomorrow - I'll keep you updated!
