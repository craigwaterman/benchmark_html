A copy and paste of an old blog I did on POZIAM

As the title says this is a copy and paste from an old blog post from 2012 I did on POZIAM. I was on there and saw it and felt the need to share it again. I get so busy that I forget to update blogs that I have. I can only say that I will try to remember more often.

 

Questions I asked friends and family.

  • Posted by Jenn Price on July 28, 2012 at 10:40pm
  • View Blog
I decided to ask some of the most important and supportive people in my life some questions. This has taken me some time to do because I’ve added people to it as I’ve thought about it so this has evolved on me.  When I asked them if they would do this a couple didn’t want to answer the questions because they thought it would hurt my feelings. Some didn’t ever get back to me to let me know if they would be willing to answer them, and I’m not mad at all at that they lead very busy lives. I let the ones who did answer know that I would be sharing their answers in a blog post. I’ll give a little background of each person and how they are involved in my life before sharing the q&a with you.

First is my wonderful mother who has stood by me throughout my whole life no matter what has happened. These questions and answers actually got a dialog started with her and I and we were able to better understand one another. Here is her q&a with me:
How did you feel when you first found out I was HIV positive?
 At first I was angry.  Angry with you for not using protection & thinking that
you were indestructible.  Then I was angry with the guy that gave it to you.
I was really angry with him.  He shouldn't have lied!

What concerns did you have?
 I was afraid that you would die right away.  I had always believed that HIV was an automatic death sentence.

How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?
 You are as healthy as most people without them being poz.  You were always honest with me about the entire thing.  I am proud of the way you have handled your disease and your willingness to help others to deal with the disease.




What did you think when I got pregnant?
 I thought it was dumb to bring a child into the world when you had no idea how long you might live.  Also not knowing if the child would be positive or not.


How did the news that my daughter was negative make you feel?
 What can I say?  The relief was overwhelming.

What advice would you give families who are just finding out a loved one is poz?
 Be patient.  Educate yourself about HIV/AIDS.  Most of all be honest about your feelings and discuss it as a family.  Don't make them feel like you cannot touch them or even be around them.  The risk of contagion is not as great as some of us may have believed.  People with poz HIV are not lepers!

The next set is from my wonderful and beautiful cousin Denise. She’s been super supportive of me and I couldn’t ask for more from her.  As she has said we aren’t just cousins we are friends and that is very important to me.
How did you feel when you first found out I was HIV positive?

Devastated, I didn't want to lose another member of my family.


What concerns did you have?

The first thing I asked when I was told was how advanced was the disease?  At the time you were battling pneumonia which had me even more concerned. However deep down I know where you came from and I knew you would give the disease the fight of a lifetime and you wouldn't let it win either.


How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?

What difference should that make to me it does not change who you are on the inside. That question would be just like asking me if it made the difference if you had cancer or any other disease. It changes nothing

What did you think when you knew I had been pregnant?

Let’s be honest here because there was several different emotions going on at the time I learned you was pregnant. First I was mad as hell cause I couldn't understand how someone could bring a child into this world knowing that they would be sick and die early in life. Second I was very proud of you for wanting to live a normal life and have a family to call your own. Then I got inquisitive and started reading and learning that it was possible to be HIV positive and have a child that was free of the disease.


How did the news that my daughter was negative make you feel?

Relieved and thrilled for you.

What advice would you give families and friends who are just finding out a loved one is poz?

Treat them no different than you treated them before because nothing has changed other than the fact they are infected with a disease. If you was there for them before they find than you should stand by them now no matter what. If finding out that you are positive does nothing else it does show you who will be there when you need them and who won't. This disease is a true test of ones humanity and it is up to each of us to pass it with flying colors.

The next person who agreed to answer my questions is one of my best friends in the world her name is Rose. She’s been by my side basically since I found out since I met her barely a month or two after I was diagnosed. She’s always been very supportive and is always in my corner.  She and I have become so close over the years that it feels like we are more like sisters than just friends.
What concerns did you have?
Truthfully I was concerned about my kids until I got schooled in the matter.


How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?
I am grateful that god has kept you on this earth this long and I know he has special plans for you


What did you think when you knew I had been pregnant?
 I was so happy I cried and prayed for you and the baby everyday


How did the news that my daughter was negative make you feel?
Relieved and over joyed


What advice would you give families and friends who are just finding out a loved one is poz?
Don’t turn away from them and do your best to help them and to support them in any way you can.  There is a difference in being scared and being careful. Being POZ does not mean that your life is over
 This next person is another good friend of mine and is more like a brother than just a friend to me to be honest. His name is Ben and here are his answers.

How did you feel when you first found out I was HIV positive?
Well it was wow and the person that we were both friends with at the time knowing that she knew before me and knowing what she was doing or who she was doing I should say I was even then wanting to get involved. And I wanted to smack her very hard.

What concerns did you have?

 Lots and first but you were so cool to teach me everything I asked about HIV and other STDS
How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?

 Big deal I know I shouldn’t say that but to me it is a big deal because you have taught me not to be afraid of you or anybody else with HIV
 What did you think when you knew I had been pregnant?

 Well you know that answer but I was very concerned about your baby, but being her "uncle" I was concerned. I know I drove you crazy with the calls and everything.
How did the news that my daughter was negative make you feel?
 Great

What advice would you give families and friends who are just finding out a loved one is poz?
 BE SUPPORTIVE and love them just like I love you

Dani is my next friend I chose to ask to answer my questions. No matter what happens I know Dani is one of the people I can count on to have my back. We were in high school together and she’s one of the strongest, meanest (said with total love), and honest people I know. She’s one of my dearest friends and I know that even when fell out of contact we were still in each other’s thoughts and hearts. I’m glad though that she and I are in contact again and I love her dearly.
How did you feel when you first found out I was HIV positive?
 When I first found out that you were HIV positive, I was stunned.  i know that these things happen but u never expect them to actually happen to urself or to people u love.  After I got over that I was very sad...I’ve known u for so long and it felt like u had just been given a death sentence.

What concerns did you have?
 The only real concern that I ever had about this once u told me was that I would have to watch, be it thru Facebook, or any of our other communications, die.  I know that it is something that u have to live with, but it's hard for me knowing that the girl who turned into a woman at my side would not be growing old with me.  I’ve known ur spirit for 12+ years and just the thought of that spirit slowly burning out just breaks my heart.

How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?
 The fact that u've been pos for several years gives me hope.  I know this may sound stupid or ignorant on my part, but I keep telling myself that if u can just keep living and staying healthy then maybe the cure will be found in time for u!!! It may be a fleeting hope, but it gives me hope none the less.

What did you think when I got pregnant?
 When I found out u were pregnant, of course there was concern that she would be born positive. It seemed like some kind of cruel joke being played by God or Krishna or Tom Cruise...whoever u believe in.  All one can do is hope and pray that things turn out okay.

How did the news that my daughter was negative make you feel?
 When I knew that she was born negative I was so happy for u. Because no matter what happens in life u get to enjoy the wonderfulness that is motherhood.  It turned out that finally something amazing was able to happen to u!!! And of course behind all of that happiness there is again worry for her.  It’s hard to lose ur mother, or any parent, even as an adult.  It makes me hope that your daughter won’t have to go thru that at a young age.

What advice would you give families who are just finding out a loved one is poz?
The only advice that I can give anyone out there is 2 things.  Number 1, never ever give up hope...if ur loved one can have the strength and courage to live with this illness that at least we can do is hope.
Number 2, always be there for them no matter what...they are people going thru something that u can't even imagine so there will be bad days, and horrible moments, they will cry, scream, and sometimes feel like giving up..but always be there for them..and never give up on them.

I’m going to end with answers from my husband and my stepson. They have both been through a lot with me over the years. I love them so very much and I’m happy they agreed to answer these for me. First is from my husband Christopher.
How did you feel when you first found out I was HIV positive?
It didn't really affect me that much.
I knew the basics of how the disease is spread and precautions I should take.
Being born with Legg-Calve-Perthes disease I learned at a very young age you do not judge someone by what they have.





What concerns did you have?
The only concerns I had at the time were if you would like the real me.
I tend to come off harsh as I generally am brutally honest.





How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?

 The only effect of your status is that I give my all to make sure you do not get sick.
I do my best to ensure you get enough vitamin C, protein and are not out in bad weather.
I also would much rather have an ER visit for something that turns out not bad rather than just waiting..




How did the news that our daughter was negative make you feel?

 The news that our daughter was negative was a giant relief.
It's bad enough that I have to watch you suffer with your status.
I would have felt bad for you because I know you would have felt guilty every time you saw her.


What advice would you give families who are just finding out a loved one is poz?
The advice I would give, is HIV is not the death sentence it was 20 years ago.
Never lose hope and never give up, no matter how bleak things look.
Never ever forget, they could get even worse.
My stepson is the last person whose answers I will include.
What concerns did you have?

 None



How do you feel now that I have been poz for several years?

 Well I am concerned that you get sick very easily but other than that I don’t really think that much






How did the news that your sister was negative make you feel?

It made me feel happy that she wasn’t





What advice would you give families who are just finding out a loved one is poz?



Don’t just freak out about it stay calm it doesn’t mean it’s the end of the world they are still the same person just a bit different

So basically what I got from all of this from my family and friends is this you should be supportive of those you love in everything they do and everything that happens to them, and above all love them unconditionally. I love my family and my friends with all my heart and hope they all know that I support and love them no matter what life throws their way.

**Also a little note from me for family and friends who are reading this if you want to answer the questions and have me add them at a later date just let me know.

A Cross post from one of my other blogs.

I haven't been around much because I've had a lot of things going on. First I'm still doing work for my favorite author so that's a terrific thing and it is a great job that I love. Back at the end of March beginning of April I had an appendectomy. Well then a few weeks later I was having pain and we found a wound on my tummy just above my hysterectomy/C-section scar and just to the left of my appendectomy incision.I didn't notice it because of where it was until it became infected. I then started at a wound care center to get it healed up. Everything we have tried seemed to work at first then it would revert back to how it was when we started or worse. During all of this I found out I have an allergy to silver which was news to me since almost all of my jewelry is silver. Now they got me approved for a wound vac and that is the course of action (8 months later) that we are on currently. It seems to be working but only time will tell, and it is very painful and I feel for others on them.

This summer as well my other half had to have a hip replacement surgery due to a birth defect. He was released three days after his surgery and was home for a total of three days before having to go back into the hospital because he was throwing up constantly. Turns out the nurses hadn't been monitoring his bowel sounds like they should have been. He wound up spending another week and a half in the hospital and having another surgery. Turns out his lower intestine got caught in some old scar tissue from his appendectomy when he was 8 and go kinked up. He's healing nicely now though and still doing physical therapy.

Also this summer my stepson moved in with us. He's been such a huge help and I don't think I'd have made it through his dad's surgeries without him. We are very happy that he's now living with us it is something we've wanted for a very long time.
My daughter will be turning 8 soon and is very excited about it. She's loving having her big brother around all the time. We just wish their big sister and her baby boy were around more. The grand baby just turned a year old back in October and is the cutest little guy and we love him tons.
Ok I that's what's been going on in my life in a nutshell.

Tuesday, September 2, 2008

Ok like I said I suck at keeping this up to date.

We are having some car issues (joy!!) so are going to have to get a new one ugh. The car we have we haven't even had for a year, but the issues its having are more than we can afford to fix. So because we still have to pay the person who helped us get this car and will wind up with another car payment as well I am looking at getting a part time job. I am so not sure how I will do with a job still as far as my health goes, but I guess we shall soon see. I do know that I will not put myself in the danger that I did before (i.e. working while being sick and not being able to see my doctor) and therefore hopefully will avoid winding up in the hospital again.

I really wish that I didn't have to do this but eh life isn't fair which sucks. I am also struggling with finding out how I am going to be able to get my meds after this month since the assitance I am getting is dropping me at the end of the month. I do think the state will reinstate me on the assitance I was on previously, but I just don't know how long till they will do so. Meanwhile I'm trying to figure out how I'm going to afford freaking $1441 for one month of one perscription. Ever wonder why it seems like people with HIV/AIDS need more assistance??? Well now you know it is because our medications cost more than all our monthly bills combined. I am one of the lucky ones since I am only on one medication at this time, but man it makes up for it in the cost of that one medication. Why not just skip till you can get the assistance again you may ask. Well because when you have HIV/AIDS if you skip your meds then the virus becomes immune to it and then you are back at square one and having to start new meds. Now call me selfish, but I love the med I'm on now and don't want to change again. I have already done three other combos that didn't do anything but make me sicker, so I think I'll try to stick with the one I'm on as long as possible.

Add to all this that I am going to be doing homeschooling for my daughter, for at least her Pre-school, this year and getting dental stuff done, man my plate seems rather full right now ugh. No wonder I'm always stressed out it always seems like there is something to stress about lol. Then again as my wonderful, adoring other half points out to me that even if everything was going our way I would still find something to stress about. Yeah telling me not to worry about things is like telling a fish not to swim lol. Ok well that's enough of this boring stuff that no one reads lol, but it sure does help me not blow up at everyone else when I vent this stuff on here.

Ok decided to start one of these lol

Ok most who know me know that to be honest I suck at keeping blogs up to date. I will try to do better and so far from what I've seen of this I might like it better than some of the other ones I've had. Just be forewarned that I am pretty boring as nothing much happens to me anymore lol. Well unless you count my health escapades and things my daughter does. I got the honor recently to be a part of a great project with MTVU and The Kaiser Family Foundation along with POZ magazine. It is a website called www.posornot.com. Basically you look at a small portion of info that we gave and a picture of us and decide if you can tell if we are HIV pos or not. Sounds simple huh lol don't you wish. Go check it out if you get the chance I think it will give people a bit of a reality check. Oh and you will have a gimme if you can figure out which one is me lol that yes I am HIV+.

Ok other than that I'm a SAHM who happens to be disabled to a beautiful little girl who is 4 and a 1/2 years old. I also am an avid reader of lots of different books. Though usually Urban Fantasy, Paranormal Romance, and Sci-Fi tend to be my genre's of choice with some Horror thrown in there lol.
