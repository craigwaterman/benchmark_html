America's Next Top Model: She Just Wasn't Going To Win
Recap by RTVC Senior Staff Writer Jacob D.
May 7, 2009





This week's episode of America's Next Top Model will see the girls taking flight with Tyra Banks.

Previously, the five remaining girls hit the streets for their Brazilian go-sees. Celia and Fo managed to meet with all five designers, but were late getting back to the starting point and were disqualified. Allison's look and pictures went over well, but not her lack of confidence, while Aminat had the reverse problem, confidence over pictures, and Teyona ended up winning. For the photo shoot, the girls modeled bikinis in a mock beach scene with Nigel Barker serving as photographer for a second time.

At judging panel, Teyona's photo was deemed the best while Celia and Fo were left as the bottom two. Both were plagued all episode long by their age and height, respectively, which are both seen as detrimental in the fashion community, but Fo was eliminated when the judges felt her body of work didn't stack up to Celia's. Four girls remain and this episode will give us our final three. Who will make it? Who won't? Let's find out.

After the most recent elimination, Teyona feels she's getting stronger and stronger in the competition. She says that she's gone from being the under-the-radar threat to just being, "the threat." Celia recalls that it was her second trip to the bottom two and was absolutely shocked when her name was called. Celia feels her problem is in seeming too stern at photo shoots and she knows she needs to just relax. Aminat isn't so happy about having to put up with Celia for another week while Allison is happy she's still around. Allison points out that there seems to be cliques forming, with Aminat and Teyona being one pair and herself and Celia being the other.

Usually, I write out the Tyra Mails for all the readers to see, but this next one is in words I don't recognize. The best I can make out is, "Tchiqui, Tchiqui, Toum," which is repeated three times. If anyone knows Portugese and this show doesn't explain the Tyra Mail itself, please help me out! The next day, the girls head out to a place called, "Aguia de Ouro," which appears to be a dance studio. Many couples are cutting a rug inside and the girls immediately guess that they'll be taking dance lessons. Paulina greets them and informs them that they will be learning some samba moves. She elaborates by saying the point of these lessons is for the girls to learn how to move their bodies and make it look easy. Juliana Leite will serve as the instructor.

Juliana takes the four girls through some basic steps and Paulina asks them to do the dance, but to be aware of how they look as if Paulina were Nigel shooting them with a camera. Celia is first and though she has a partner, she stays focused on Paulina and looks effervescent. Paulina says she tried too hard and wants her to just let go, have more fun. She says Celia's age works against her and acting desperate isn't the key. Aminat is next and she plays up her sex appeal. Paulina feels she took to the dance like a duck to water. Allison tries and she just looks lost. Paulina says she was stiff and weak. Teyona is last and Paulina is surprised to see she isn't as graceful as she, Paulina, expected. Afterwards, Paulina tells the girls that the real lesson was in how to fake it with confidence, that is to say, performing even when you're not sure what to do.

Tyra Mail: "Whatever you do, don't look down. -Love, Tyra"

Ooh, I love that sparkly purple outfit Celia's wearing! Each girl begins practicing their dance moves and head out for their challenge the next day. They come to a roof top where a guy by the name of Eduardo Ramos is twirling Paulina around in a spicy dance number. The two finish and Paulina reminds the girls of yesterday's lesson which is to create the illusion of a good time, a.k.a., "faking it with confidence." Eduardo will serve as their partner in a dance routine and the winner, plus a friend, will get jewelry from Ara Vartanian.
