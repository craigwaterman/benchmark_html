30 Minute Mock Restoration Hardware Mirror Makeover

I’ve had this sweet but very boring mirror sitting in our warehouse at the store for months and with 30 total minutes I was able to give it a little makeover. With leftover Sherwin Williams paint in Shoji White, I loved being able to update this frame in three steps! Shopping for items at Restoration Hardware I stumbled upon this $595 mirror of the same size and knew I could make it for 1/100th of the price! Plus I wanted to be able to control how much of the beautiful original wood would sneak through to the surface.

1. Wipe down the frame with a damp rag to remove any dust or dirt. Don’t worry about the glass yet, you’ll use a razor blade to remove excess paint when you’re finished.


2. Paint all sides of the exposed wood in any color you’d like. I chose Shoji White but this could have been precious in a light pink, dark turquoise or lemon yellow. Don’t worry about being perfect- that helps give you a basis for where you start wiping in step three!  
3. When the paint is still tacky to the touch but almost dry, take a fine steel wool and wet it and start removing as much or as little of the paint as you’d like. I love the look of the hazy, Restoration Hardware look so I left most of the watered down paint on the surface but you can also remove almost all of the paint you’ve applied.


When you’ve gotten your frame to the color you like- let it dry for 2-4 hours to insure it is sealed and then clean your glass! The easiest method to get the dried paint is with a straight razor carefully used along the side of the frame. Enjoy your new Restoration Hardware inspired frame!

Surgery, Sleep & Solutions

Over the last few months I’ve taken some time away from my blog as most people who follow can tell. After being told I’ve had a chronic sinus infection for 3-5 years, my team of doctors decided to try a newer surgery that could provide some relief. The constant pressure in my forehead and cheeks left me miserable at to the point of migraines up to 5 days a week putting me under the bed. I was miserable but didn’t know what the cure was. 

After lots of allergy testing, MRIs, Cat Scans & prayer, in February I had the surgery followed by a secondary procedure the next week. For the next almost five weeks I was recovering with lots and lots of sleep and some hard days of pain. 
I am seeing some relief, but the headaches have started to come back. I have a follow up this coming week to see what next steps we will take!   
I am beyond thankful for my friends and family who called, sent cards and flowers or just sweet messages to check up on me! 
Let’s get back to working on fun projects and recipes!
