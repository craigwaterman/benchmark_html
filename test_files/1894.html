Gonna Change It Up A Bit

So, my first few blogs have pretty much been me venting about the idiot people I come in contact with on a daily basis. I'm gonna change some things up a bit. I am trying to get everything tied together into my Blackberry (my BFF) and plan on posting about my day. "As seen through your groomers eyes" type thing. Hopefully I can post pictures! You wouldn't believe what I see. Ohh before and after pics!! How awesome!!

The one thing I really wish would happen, is for like, the "non groomers" to sit in the shop with us and really see what we go through. Grooming is NOT easy. I hate the fact that I work for a major retail/grooming store. The people in the retail part think we have it SOOOO easy. All we do is play with cute little puppies and style their hair!

How about this....

This lady calls and wants to get her dog groomed. I talk to her about what kind of haircut she is wanting, a puppy cut on a labradoodle. We agree on a day and time for her to come in and i go on with my day. The next day.... the NANNY shows up an HOUR late! One of our bathers was checking the dog in and asking the standard questions. Any special shampoo? Teethbrushing? She takes the dog into the back and waits for me to arrive at work. When I start feeling on the dog for mats, I realize there is NO way that I am going to be able to do a puppy cut on this dog, she is going to have to be shaved. I call the owners cell phone and leave a voicemail. I call the house phone and leave a message with the nanny. THREE hours later she returns the phone call. I inform her that I will have to shave her dog. She doesn't want the dog shaved and says, "What a complete waste of my day, I will just come get her." She then hung up on me. What a BITCH! So the nanny comes and gets the dog. A few weeks later, she made another appointment for her dog to get shaved, guess she changed her mind about the haircut! I already made up my mind that I didnt want to groom this dog. I have the right to refuse service to bitches! Ha! I get to work and see that shes on the books. I suck it up, and prepare for their arrival. Appointment is at 9 o'clock. Ten o'clock rolls around and the husband comes strolling in, "We have an appointment for 9" he says. To which I reply, "Um, its 10 o'clock, we've given your spot away." After a long pause I then tell him that we really dont want to do his dog anyway because his wife was rude on the phone and they have a lack of respect for our time. He storms out. About an hour later, the bitch calls. She said she has no idea how she was rude to me. When I informed her HOW she was rude, she laughed and said "oh you thought THAT was rude?" At this point I was really amused at how the "upper class" think they can walk all over us "common folk". Thats exactly how she was treating me. She said she was calling my supervisor. Big whoop.

>sigh<

Yes we provide a service. Yes we have to wait on our paychecks to be mailed to us. Yes we get covered in dog hair. Yes we clean up poop. Does that make us any less than you? Absolutely not. Please respect your groomer and their time.

Toenail triming

So, I get alot of this, "Im afraid im going to hurt Fluffy". If you follow these simple steps you will not hurt Fluffy and his toenail trim will be a wonderful experience.

If the nails are WHITE this process will go by very quickly. It is easier to see the quick from underneath the nail. No, Im not telling you to bend in a yoga position to see it. Just pull the dogs leg (in a natural position) behind him. Flip the paw back so you see his pad. At this point you should be able to see the white nail and a pink vein (the quick) you want to cut just in front of the quick. You should be able to cut the "hook" off of the nail safely. After you cut, look closely to see of there is a pink or white looking wet spot in the center of the nail. If you see it, that is the quick and you dont want to cut any further. If you dont see it, you may can trim off a little more.

If the nails are BLACK, this process may be a little more stressful on you. Repeat the same process to look at the bottom of the pad. After some experience, you can cut the "hook" off easily and be done. But, until then just take a little off at a time, looking for that pink or white wet spot.

If by chance you do quick the dog (make him bleed) and you dont have any of that fancy schmancy powder the professionals use, DONT PANIC! Simply go to your pantry and get some flour or cornstarch. Touch your finger to your tounge, dip that finger into the coagulate and apply pressure to the dogs nail. If that isnt enough to stop the bleeding (because you got a little cut happy) stick the whole nail into a small dish filled with the flour or cornstarch. Viola!

Just remember to be patient! Only take off a small amount if you are nervous. Sunday, April 6, 2008

Be Patient

When you drop your precious little Fluffy off at the groomer, keep in mind Fluffy isn't the only dog in the salon! When we say we will call when ready, we aren't saying that to entertain you! Do you know how hard it is to groom a dog who has just seen his mummy peeking through the window at him? He goes insane. He assumes that he is going home because you are there to pick him up. This is why we say we will call, to prevent this. If Fluffy sees you, you may as well just take him home right then. Groomers HATE to be rushed. Bad things happen to a rushed groomer. Don't expect bows in their hair if you show up early. Their nails may not get cut either. You have made our brain zone out and our stress level jump sky high. Just remember, a stressful groomer isn't a happy groomer!!

Tuesday, January 1, 2008

just to help me out...

Here are a few tips for a happy groomer and a happy healthy dog.



  • Start bringing them in when they are a puppy. Twelve weeks is a great age to start. They have been started on their puppy shots and are of the age to get a flea bath (if needed). Puppies that are introduced to the grooming atmosphere become good groom dogs. Dogs that get groomed regularly grow to become helpers. We have a giant schnauzer that comes in and hands you each paw so you can trim them. AWESOME!

  • Teach them at home that grooming equipment isnt scary and bad! If you have a razor (or anything else) that vibrates, put it to their feet and face. Let them get used to the vibrations.

  • BRUSH THEM! No, they most likely wont like it. Who cares, you are the parent in this situation. You have got to have the upper hand when dealing with a puppy. Too often we see dogs that completely run over their owners. The only difference between a human child and a fur child is you dont have to buy them a car for graduation! You have to discipline them. They wont hold a grudge, I promise. Sure they are going to show you those eyes and whine and cry, didnt your baby do that also?

  • Walk them before you bring them in. Dogs get excited and when they have a full bladder, that is NOT a good combination. Plus, do you know how annoying it is to have a fully groomed dog ready for mom and dad to pick up that just took a huge crap in his kennel and is river dancing in it?
  • Bring shot records when coming to a new grooming salon. Like I said in the past, we cant take your word, we need the proof. Time gets wasted when we have to call the vet. Vets can also lose your records (uncommon yet happens). You just know Fluffy is current but you lost the paperwork. So, we call Dr. Fourpaws and his secretary cant find it (likely shes too busy to look). Just be prepared for everything. If you know Fluffy isnt current, dont waste our time by dancing around the fact you have failed to keep your dog current. We dont want to hear that Fluffy has been to 6 different vets and you just cant remember where Fluffy got his rabies shot.
  • Kennel train your dog. No matter what your best friend says, it is NOT abuse to put your dog in a kennel. Dogs are put into cages at the groomer. They are pretty spacious but can still seem small to a dog who is used to roaming the house. Dogs who arent used to a kennel become "kennel shy" or "kennel brave". Shy meaning when I stick my hands in the cage to grab him, hes going to bite my hand off. Brave meaning as soon as the kennel door is open hes jumping. Either situation is not a good one. We want your dog to be happy and SAFE!

Sunday, December 30, 2007

I swear he doesnt have rabies!

Yeah yeah we know, Fluffy doesnt have rabies and he isnt going to bite, RIGHT! There are just too many people in this world who are dishonest. That is why we cant take your word, its nothing personal against you, I promise. So dont act like we just ran over your cat. Rabies is a serious thing. When we tell you we need your proof of rabies before we can groom your fur baby, we dont mean your word! We need written proof from your vet, and NO you cant give the rabies shot to your dog. It HAS TO BE administered by a veterinarian. So say your dog isnt current on rabies and have to get his shot. WAIT AT LEAST 48 HOURS AFTER THE SHOT BEFORE YOU GET HIM GROOMED!! It isnt very common, but it happens, dogs have allergic reactions to the shot. You need to monitor them! Also, dont expect us to bend the rules for you. You are not worth losing our job over. I dont care if his rabies expired 2 hours ago, expired is expired. The word expired doesnt mean "expired unless you look at the groomer with puppydog eyes until they cave". Once again, it isnt anything personal, but you arent getting Fluffy groomed today. It isnt your groomers responsibility to keep your dogs shots current.

oops, i forgot!

In this post I would like to address the infamous "no show". I am sure by now you are aware of the fact that we pay the bills by depending on you to live up to the promise of bringing your fur baby in for their appointment. We dont make appointments for our health. We make them because we have learned through the years we get more done by taking appointments. It helps us pace ourselves and plan our day. When you call and set up a time to bring little Fluffy in, please, put it in your Blackberry. You are being held accountable for this. When you decide to not show, that puts us behind. For example: I have 3 dogs scheduled for 11:00a.m., Fido and Fifi show up, now im waiting on little Fluffy. I dont want to start on Fido because I will just have to stop what im doing, put Fido up and check Fluffy in. So I wait.... 10 minutes, 20 minutes, 35 minutes. At this point I assume you have forgotten and will not be showing up. I have now wasted 35 minutes. At this point I pray to the grooming gods that Fido and Fifi are well behaved so I dont get even more behind. I cant stress enough how important it is for you to be on time. If you showed up to "Super Cuts R Us" to get your hair done 35 minutes after your scheduled time, do you think they would still take you in? What about if you had reservations to some fancy schmancy hard to get in resturant, and decided to show up 35 minutes late? Do you think your table would still be reserved? Negative, Mr. and Mrs. Johnson just got seated. Would you buy tickets to a movie that was already 35 minutes into playing? Why of course not, that doesnt make any sense! So what makes Fluffys haircut any different? We depend on you as much as you depend on us. We notate these things on your account, we remember these things. What happens when you are having some unexpected company come over and Fluffy decided that he wanted to go swimming in the newly created mud hole in the backyard and needs to see the groomer? Wish you would have been a little more courteous to the groomers huh? Chances are, if you would have been, we could squeeze you in. Instead you have to hear, "Oh sorry, were booked up." As horrible as it may sound, yea we hold grudges. Who doesnt?

Also, when you decide that getting your nails done is a little more precious then our time and waltz in an hour late for your appointment, dont get mad because you are turned away. Your seat has been given to someone else. Please remember this when you make an appointment.

Friday, December 28, 2007

It costs how much??

Something we hear a lot of is, "I don't spend this much on MY hair!" There are reasons that it costs as much as it does to groom a dog.

1. Dogs don't sit in a hydraulic chair and chit chat with you while you cut their hair.

2. Dogs bite. I dont want to hear, "Oh Fluffy has never bit anyone." If a dog has teeth, it bites.

3. Groomers work hard, we work in blocks. It will take at least 3 hours. We precut your dog, bathe them, then let them dry while we repeat the process with another dog.

4. We listen to barking dogs CONSTANTLY. Those high pitched yappers in your ear tend to take a toll on you!

5. Your dog may be matted. Im sure you dont want the first thing said to be, "Oh I am going to have to shave Fluffy." It is part of our job to work out mats (to a degree) that takes time. We are pulling on poor Fluffy and he tends to get a little cranky!

6. It isnt just a cut and a shampoo. We express anal glands (yeah its nasty). We pull ear hair. We shave pads and butts. Might I add it isnt easy to get the hair from around the eyes when a dog absolutely does NOT want you anywhere near his face. Would you let some stranger hold 7 1/2 inch scissors to your face?

7. While having to deal with Fluffy, we still have to deal with you. Although most of the time it is pleasant, you have to realize we deal with IDIOT pet parents on a daily basis. Those people who are never satisfied. People who want something for nothing. People who dont realize what all goes into grooming a dog.

So with all that being said, please have patience. Please be nice to your groomer, they work hard and most of the time only get 50% commission. Also, we do accept tips.
