Chocolate Rich

We all deal with many things....I deal with 3 kids, their activities, my full-time job, and on and on. We all deal with stuff. I deal with this by having secret stashes of chocolate. Don't judge. But, I've got spots all over that I hide chocolate. It wouldn't be beneath me to hide chocolate in brown paper in the freezer marked "liver". I have in recent times tried to subdue my sugar loving, but I miss the sweet stuff. I don't want the trashy chocolate that tastes mostly of wax, but I love the slightly bitter real chocolate that makes the storm of activity come to a stand still while it melts in my mouth.  Love, love, love it.

As the years have gone on the husband is on to me, and asks, where is it? So, as I lock the door, do the safe locks, undo the padlock and tear away the paper I share a tiny bit. The kids are out of luck. I could throw a handful of chocolate chips in the air and I've got about 20 seconds to disappear.

Chocolate. I love you. Never leave me.

Birth day is Here

It is my 39th birthday today. I am seriously good with turning 39. I like this age a heck of lot better than 20 years ago.
My daughter was asking me a few questions about when my mom died. And she asked me a question that brought me back to being 19 which had to do with my father remarrying. I had a hard time during that year because I felt so lost after my mother died. I wanted my dad to be happy even though I was not. Not because of his new wife but the longing for my mother. The pain and emptiness it caused in my heart. I didn't know my mother. As years went by that hurt remained. I wanted to know who she was.
Years have gone by now and I have a better understanding of who she was and what her purpose was here on earth. She has spoken to me many times through the years. Her voice clear and loving. She looks out for me and my children. She has a constant presence. And when I need to be told she finds a way to speak through situations. Or the blackbirds that just soar through or dance on my roof of my house. Or even nattering at my friends.
So over 20 years have gone. But driving home with my kids and telling my daughter the pain that it caused me then has made me realize how precious life is and that to put more effort into what matters. I thank the universe for those lessons over and over again.
Happy Birthday to me.

10/29/2014

Start of the Writing Challenge....

I needed a new challenge. I could use a fitness challenge, but the universe tells me that I need to write. It really could be empty words on a vast space of internet, but it's MY WORDS. I have deemed them important. I will write everyday for a year. It could be anything. A poem, a haiku, an essay, or a political analysis. I need to flex my writing muscles. This will be my first post on this subject. My reason is simple for this, spirit does not leave this alone. I am told to write and I need to honor it, or it will go away.

Writing #1

I am going to confess. I really do believe in spirit. I believe in God. I believe in the soul. I believe that I am a soul with infinite possibilities. I think that we are all here to serve a purpose. We are here to learn and be challenged. Our soul will find evolution every time we are here on earth. Being human is life altering for our soul. And that, is what makes me get up every day.....

I will confess as well, I struggle. I want to be a soul that is full of light, love and compassion. I fail at times. I cannot get it right, or what seems to be right. I say yes to things when it isn't what I really want. I deny love when I am annoyed. I hold my tongue when I should be screaming out what I want. I fail to be honest with myself which in turn is a lack of truth to others.

Fear is what stops me. I am scared to just say it. If I slip the words past my lips, then I have to face the consequences. I have realized that all that compiling "holding in the words" causes me pain. Physical pain. I would then have to say, "I don't want to do that." or "This is what I really want to do." It seems simple, but I really struggle to be honest with everyone around me. The hardest people to be honest with are the closest ones to home. It is funny, I never thought I was a yes girl, but I am.

Will it make you stronger if you have little support for the decisions you make that stir the soul? I sure hope so.

04/01/2012

One Year Later

April 1st 2012.  The year is up.  I have not blogged about my running for most of February and all of March.  I just wasn't able to keep up to it, and then with other things going on in my life, I decided to just write a final blog.  So, here goes.

The final two months of running had its moments.  No matter what, I just tried to keep running.  It wasn't until the near end of March that I had a week off of running.  Vegas.  I wish I could have counted all the walking that we did. My legs and feet could not have handled a few miles on the hotel treadmill.  No regrets.  Also, I had a huge distraction with a big project I was working on that rendered me useless blogging, but did in fact help my running.  What takes your mind off of stress and anxiety better than running hard down the road?  Nothing. 

I reflect back on this whole year.  I weigh less than when I started.  I have more energy.  I had some downs with losing some wonderful people this past year.  My kids all got the chicken pox.  I had tons of stress.  I had some great things happen to me as well.  I have a wonderful group of friends and I would say we have gotten even closer this past year than we had ever before.  I have surrounded myself with people who have lifted me higher and I hope that I do the same for them. 

Running.  It was the sweetest moment that my last run be shared with my family.  They have had to deal with my absence when I went for runs.  They continued to support me and understood that mom was needing her time to go.  We went for a run last night.  The girls were on their bikes and Bubby was in the stroller.  He begged me to push him on his bike the whole way, but that was a negatory!!  I jogged along with my Hubby.  I loved it.  They were the perfect someones to be with to end this whole year of my running challenge.  So, I give thanks and all my love to Hubby, Bee, Honey and Bubby.  You are what makes my heart skip and my soul brighter.  Thank you all for being part of my life.

The conclusion.  I ran 703.5 miles for the year.  A far cry from 1000 miles.  I am not disappointed.  I could mix this all up with an excuse or I could say I could have tried harder, but I am not disappointed with my result.  My result was a result of my actions.  I am completely okay with it.  I ran 700 miles.  That is so awesome!!  I am proud of myself.  It was a tough challenge and it got tougher not easier as the year went on.  I had no idea what I was getting into in 2011, but I did arrive in 2012 with a smile on my face.  Bee asked me, "What now?"  I don't know, I replied.  She said that I should keep running to get to my 1000 miles.  And that is what I am going to do.  I will see how long it takes me to get there.  Happy trails!

Running Nose

I went for a run after 2 days of really sick.  I will admit I am still stuffed up and coughing.  I realize that others would scoff at my willingness to go for a run at all.  I am not under any medication and I feel more like myself. 

I ran 2.9 miles and had to blow my nose numerous times.  I had to have a few coughing fits.  Yes, it does start to sound a bit stupid.  The truth, after running and getting all the mucous to move, I did feel better.  I did take it easy, and continue to take extra vitamin C and my pro-health pills.  I am getting extra rest.  I may not be able to run as much these next few days, because I want to monitor my situation and keep from putting myself into harms way.

Rise and Fall

Mama is sick or atleast starting to be sick.  I have been taking extra vitamin C, anti-cold pills (which I renamed pro-health pills) and drinking lots of water.  I feel a bit run down and as always can usually fight it. 

I saved my running for the evening when I would be in town alone after dropping off the children.  I started running and was thinking that since I felt run down that maybe I shouldn't be doing this.  I did some positive affirmations about my good health and kept going.  I got a call from Hubby and he asked about giving some medicine for Bubby.  Bubby has a cold and had been sleeping when I left for town.  He had woken up and was coughing, so Hubby thought he'd give him some Tylenol.  Bubby had other problems because he took the medicine and ended up throwing up. 

As a mom, I am thinking "Poor baby is sick.  I have another hour 'til I have to deal with it.  I will enjoy my time."  I didn't say I was a good mother.  I would like to point out I did ask Hubby if I should come home and if he wanted to come back and pick up the girls but he said he could handle it.  I asked. 

I continued running.  I was thinking about poor Bubby.  It was a cold night and I had forgotten my gloves.  In retrospect, I should have just sat and read a book, but at the time I felt my body could handle it.  So, I ran 2.1 miles.  I stretched and grabbed a warm drink and waited for the girls to be done. 

It was a difficult night.  Bubby did go to sleep, but woke up every hour for 3 hours in the middle of the night asking for something to drink.  It nearly killed me.  I was so done by morning, but Bubby was feeling good except for his cold.  I am glad he bounced back so quickly.  I did not.  I fell hard and didn't get up for 2 days.  No running.  No nothing.  I was really sick with runny nose, sinus pain and a bad cough.   I stayed heavily medicated and would not run until I felt better without any medication.  It was the worst cold I had in a long time.  My whole family just came to a stop and gathered around my bed.  It appeared to be a candle lit vigil, but maybe that was a hallucination.  I did finally recoup from my illness.  I am continually grateful for good health and for really good drugs.
