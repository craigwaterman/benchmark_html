My Packing List

Leave a reply

Hey all!

The First Years received their housing information last week, which makes me feel so nostalgic – I still remember getting my housing information before coming on campus at the end of August. Figuring out exactly what room I was going to be in made it so real! And that’s when the dorm shopping began…

As someone who has experienced the terrifying feeling of making sure you pack ANYTHING AND EVERYTHING that you may possibly need for five seconds at some point during your first year away from home, I’ve compiled a list titled “The Things I Wish I’d Packed” for First Year students to reference. In my personal experience, all the items on this list were super helpful and necessary in my dorm. Do with it what you will!

 

1. Surge Protector:

You will never have enough outlet space where you want it. Especially in a dorm in which furniture organization options are limited, it’s nice to be able to capitalize on outlets where you can in your dorm.

2. Ethernet Cable:

You never know when TrinAir (the WiFi network that blankets all of Trinity’s campus) may decide to take a brief hiatus from operating. Furthermore, it’s nice to be able to have an uninterrupted, reliable internet connection for those late-night Netflix binges. (Did I say Netflix binges? I meant homework!!!)

3. Mattress Pad:

Gotta love college mattresses! Actually you don’t… They’re not pleasant. A mattress pad of any kind will transform your plastic-covered dorm mattress into a memory foam heaven that will be comfier than your home bed. I have a memory foam mattress pad on my bed, and highly recommend it – my friends are all jealous.

4. Lots of easily-compartmentable storage space:

The furniture that Trinity provides each student definitely contains a lot of storage space, but I am the type of person who loves to bring way more than I need, which means that I need extra bins, shelves, drawers, etc… This will also help you maintain organization in your room.

5. Desk or Floor Lamp:

Additional lighting can really make a college dorm room feel 100 times homier. Furthermore, some fluorescent lights seem to not be enough light for me, so if I want to do homework in my room I need additional lighting.

6. Floor-Length Mirror:

This item should go without saying, but I’ve decided to include it on my list because I did not need one during my first year. My first year dorm had a floor length mirror nailed to one of the doors, so I didn’t have to worry about it during my first year.

SO I hope this helps some potential First-Years with their packing!

Ta Ta For Now,

-Marie

My Caffeine Fix

Leave a reply

Coffee is a large enough part of my life that it – along with my certifiable caffeine addiction – deserves its own blog post. And I’d argue that most Trinity students would back me up on this statement, based on the frequent presence of caffeinated beverages in their lives as well. Thus I’ve decided to rank the three coffee options on campus in the order of my own personal preference. No hard feelings, baristas…

3. The Underground:

The great thing about The Underground is that there are tons of comfy couches and chairs to sit on and do work. The baristas like to create really creative and quirky beverages to serve. For example, the “Hot-n-Frosty,” which is a winter seasonal drink consisting of eggnog, espresso, and peppermit syrup. Yum!

2. Goldberg’s:

The coffee at Goldberg’s is FANTASTIC and very consistent from day to day. Plus, because Goldberg’s is located in Vernon Social Center, there’s ample seating in which one can sit and enjoy his or her hot beverage.

1. Peter B’s:

Peter B’s is hands-down my favorite place to be on campus. The environment is great, with lots of natural light and open space. It’s conveniently right in the library, so I don’t even have to leave my studying to get my caffeine fix. Also, in my opinion, the coffee there is the strongest, which definitely comes in handy. As an added BONUS, you can get large-sized iced beverages. A large iced “Chai Charger” from Peter B’s is pictured above, which is what I’m drinking as I write this post.

A Cappella

Leave a reply

So I sat down today to start arranging Trinitones music for this coming year, and then got really excited for the fall semester to start so that a Capella could start up again! My a Capella group is the thing I miss most during the summer, because it’s such a huge part of my life at Trinity and because the Trinitones are my absolute closest friends on campus.

Its surprising how how huge a presence a Capella is at Trinity. There are five different groups – Trinitones, Pipes, Accidentals, Quirks, and Dischords – and each group has its own personality and identity. The groups are all really supportive of each other; we go to each other’s concerts and perform together all the time!

I still remember my audition weekend like it was yesterday… After watching all of the groups perform, I walked from audition to audition like a deer in headlights, hoping my voice wouldn’t crack! After auditions, there were callbacks, which were even MORE nerve-wracking! all weekend I was super nervous and jumpy, and I’ll never forget how happy and relieved I was (though still super excited) when the Trinitones surprised me at my dorm and sprayed me with silly string to let me know that for the next four years, I would be a Tone!

What being a Trinitone has meant to me for the past three years is having a built-in family of 12 other girls who are always there for me when I need them. Not only can I always count on them to be there for me, but also I can count on our rehearsal time to be a great way to relax and recharge at the end of the day. The bonds that I have formed with my fellow Tones are ones that I will value for the rest of my life, and that I CAN’T WAIT to get back to in the fall!

Until next time,

Marie

From PA to TC

Leave a reply

Hi there!

I’m so excited to be a brand new Bantam Blogger! As this is my first post, this seems like as good a time as any to introduce myself and give you all a bit of a background to my own Trinity experience. I’m from Devon, Pennsylvania. For those shopping mavens out there, such as myself, that’s about 10 minutes from the King of Prussia Mall. I am also a rising senior (YIKES) about to take on the extraordinary challenge of living my last year of college life to its absolute fullest.

I am double-majoring in English literature and German language, which is a combination that I somewhat stumbled upon. I’ve already begun work on my English thesis, which will be about James Joyce (my favorite writer). This will be a year-long endeavor, and I’m sure you’ll hear me mention it A LOT if you continue reading my blog posts! During my first year at Trinity, I was a member of the InterArts Gateway Program, and have had the opportunity to take a lot of other music courses as well. Obviously, Trinity’s flexible curriculum has allowed me to explore many interests.

Another endeavor I am taking on this fall is becoming a First Year Mentor – a student who provides academic and social guidance within a First Year Seminar. The seminar is called “Battles of Faith and Reason,” and will be a course about the conflicting views of philosophy and religion. It’s definitely going to be a fun course, and I’m so excited to be able to act as a support network for new students as they navigate the first months of their Trinity careers.

Outside of my academic life I am heavily involved in different areas of the extra-curricular scene at Trinity. I’m a member of the Trinity College Trinitones, Trinity’s all-female a Cappella group, and I have just been named the Musical Director for the coming academic year, which is something that I’ve been dreaming about ever since I auditioned my way into the group during my first week at Trinity! For the past two years I have been a Writing Associate in the writing center, helping students improve their writing at every stage of the process. I’ve recently been named one of the Head Writing Associates in this office, so next year I’ll be able to take part in more of the behind-the-scenes work in the Writing Center as well!

I’ve also been the Finance Secretary of the Student Government for the past couple years, as well as an active member of the SGA Budget Committee. This coming year I will serve as the Vice President of Finance for the SGA. Before coming to college I never thought I would get involved with Student Government, but now that I’m here I’m SO happy that I took that leap and tried a new activity, because it’s so rewarding and has been a really great learning experience.

Looking at this list, it’s obvious that I am taking full advantage of everything that Trinity’s extra-curricular and academic worlds have to offer. Writing all of this out just instilled in me a real excitement for the fall—I can’t believe I have to wait two more months before my senior year officially starts!

Goodbye for now,

Marie
