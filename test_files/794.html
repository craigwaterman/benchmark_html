ENGAGED TO MY LOVER

On March 7, aka BEST DAY OF MY LIFE. no seriously. best day ever. The boy I am madly in love with proposed to me. I am so beyond excited. He proposed up near the Alpine Loop in Sundance right after it had snowed and it was so beautiful and perfect. I may be bias but it looks like it was off of Pinterest. Seriously Aaron's creativity never fails me. He hand-made everything and it was so cute and everything just looked awesome. I couldn't have asked for anything better.

I HAD NO IDEA it was coming that day. I was not prepared in the slightest... haha. I knew he was proposing soon but I did not think it was going to be that Saturday.

On Friday night, Aaron and I went to dinner in Salt Lake. He wasn't being weird or anything. We went to the Cheesecake Factory and then saw the movie The Duff. When he dropped me off, he asked if I wanted to go take pictures the next day in Sundance. I really didn't think anything of it since Aaron and I take a lot of pictures together.

Saturday morning came around and I really did not feel like taking pictures anymore. Haha I kept telling my roommate Daphnie that I just wanted to lay in bed all day and watch movies and sleep... However, luckily, her and my cousin Maddie made me feel all bad and so I never said anything to Aaron about not wanting to go.

Funny side note... Aaron had a specific shirt he wanted me to wear and so he told my roommate to make sue I was wearing it. Props to Daphnie because she got me in the shirt and I was never suspicious.

Aaron picked me up around 4'oclock and it seemed like a casual afternoon. We were jamming out to Hilary Duff with the windows down... life was good. The only weird thing that I can remember was that his shoes were wet. Hahah I was so confused and I mentioned it to him but he laughed and brushed it off.

We approached Sundance and he kept saying "oh my gosh, the lighting is so bad." That's when he started acting strange... haha we were literally just driving through a shaded area... I was like what?? Since when have you been concerned about the lighting?

After a few more minutes of driving, we arrived at an abandoned field. He grabbed my camera bag and we got out of the car. He closed, then reopened the door and put the camera bag back inside.. At this point I was like HUH?? Then he said, let's go for a walk... At this point, I kinda could sense what was going on... We pulled up and the rest is history. I said Yes! I cannot wait to be married to the love of my life for eternity on August 15 in the Gilbert, Arizona Temple.


Pictures are worth a thousand words right??

Life is Perfect


The Lord's Perfect Timing
&
My Perfect Freshmen Experience


This post has taken me so long to write because I cannot even find the words to express the gratitude and happiness to describe my life right now! On March 7, 2015 at around 4:30 p.m. I got engaged to the LOVE OF MY LIFE! 

Backtrack.... 
While I was in high school, I kept having this feeling (and begged my mom) to let me move to Provo right after I graduated. Well, my dream came true and I moved in to the Isles with three of my best friends for the summer. (I kinda did the whole live-off-campus thing backwards. I was suppose to live in the dorms that summer but my parents agreed to let me live off-campus my first semester of college if I agreed to move in to the dorms and not argue with them so I could be sure to have the "freshmen-experience.") Aaron and I met on my first day in Provo, June 6, 2014. I can still remember the day like it was yesterday, Mackenzie and I were pulling out of my newly found parking spot around midnight for a late-night ice cream run at Sonic. As we were pulling out, a super cute guy came up to the window of my car and quickly introduced himself. I was flattered... but didn't think too much about it. 

The next day, Mackenzie had everyone up at her cabin in Park City for the weekend. As we were all getting in cars to go up, my friends advised me that the boy I had met the night before, Aaron, "liked to ask out a lot of girls and would probably try to ask me out." Again, I didn't think much of it since it was still my first 24 hours away from home. As I got to know a bunch of people up at the cabin, I was immediately drawn to Aaron. However, the boy who had seemed so friendly was suddenly mysterious to me...  I was dying for Aaron to talk to me but HE WOULDN'T!!!!! 

After a few hours of me casually stalking him and staring him down, I finally told my friends that I SERIOUSLY wanted this guy to ask me out. My friends talked to him and he still showed no interest. Then, we played a game of Heads Up and he started talking to me... talking led to cuddling... and then we went home for the weekend. That was Saturday/ Sunday. On Monday, Aaron asked me out on our first date. We went to Swig and then made his world-famous salsa. I had the BIGGEST crush on him but I was so afraid to kiss him.... 

Hahaha but ya that didn't last... the next day, Tuesday, only 4 days after we had met for the first time we had our first kiss. The rest is history! We have hung out everyday since June 6, (except for times we were out of town).... I was in love with Aaron the second I saw him in the parking lot of the Isles.... It is so cool that I met the boy of my dreams my first day of college.
Right before our first kiss!

haha this is the super high-quality photo I sent my mom before my first date with Aaron...

I was so against getting married at a young age. I was the person that made fun of girls that got engaged in the dorms. I always told myself I wouldn't get married until I was at least 22. 

So fast forward a couple of months to January. This was the first time Aaron and I talked about getting married. I was super afraid of marriage before then, I would just laugh and be like ya haha I'm 18, I am not getting married any time soon. But, after Christmas Break I realized that I honestly cannot live life without Aaron. He is the most positive, uplifting, creative, spontaneous, social, handsome, friendly person I have ever met. I knew that I had to marry him. I prayed about it, went to the temple, fasted, and read and reread my patriarchal blessing. 

Well, some complications arose... In November, I applied for a study abroad and got rejected. I was devastated when I found out I didn't get in. Well, in January, right around the same time Aaron and I decided we wanted to get married, I found out that I actually got accepted to the program. Getting accepted to this program meant two months away from Aaron. There was a lot of back and forth, and a lot of tears (from me haha) and a lot of prayers. I ultimately decided to decline the study abroad.. but to be honest I had no idea why I didn't want to go anymore. I could have gone and been engaged... nothing really made sense. 

Aaron and I started ring shopping, and then WE GOT ENGAGED (see separate post).  About a week after we got engaged, I found out that I got my dream internship in fashion. I am pleased to announce that this summer, I will be working for the newly formed pajama brand Sleepy Jones in New York City. Ever since I was a little girl, it has always been my dream to go back to NY and work in the fashion industry. Looking back, I know that if I would have accepted the study abroad, I would not have been able to participate in the internship. Seriously, the Lord has a plan for each and every one of us. This internship is so perfect because Aaron also has an internship during the same months for Senator Flake in Washington D.C. 

As I come to the conclusion of my freshmen year, I have realized something... there really is no one "freshmen experience." Never in a million years did I think that I would be engaged as an 18 year old, or receive my dream internship. Every one's situation is unique and different. No matter where you go to college, if you keep your old friends, get a boyfriend, get a 4.0 (props if you can do this.. seriously) make completely new friends... whatever... COLLEGE WILL BE THE BEST TIME OF YOUR LIFE. Don't get me wrong. I have mental breakdowns on the daily, but if there is one thing that I have learned, it is to listen to heart and follow your dreams. Everything works out in the end. Heavenly Father knows each and every one of you and listens to your prayers. His timing is perfect. Everything will be okay. 

Stay tuned for some fun adventures coming to you from the East Coast this summer.

xoxoxoxoxo,
Sammycashh

Saturday, February 21, 2015


 Hi everyone who is reading this... thanks for taking the time to come and look at my blog! Last March, I completed one of my life time plans: start a fashion blog. Well, I obviously have not posted on sammycashh.com since then so…what happened? To be completely honest, I was afraid after I put up my first post. For real scared out of my mind. I was nervous that my friends were going to make fun of me, or call me narcissistic. As an insecure high school student, the last thing I wanted was to be judged. But here I am today, almost a year later, with the new and improved sammycashhfash.com! I am still new to the blogging world so if anyone has any suggestions for me please text me or email me at samanthacashclark@gmail.com!

I know there are a million fashion blogs in the world, so why look at mine??? Well I am super passionate about fashion and want to pursue a career in fashion journalism. I am currently majoring in journalism at Brigham Young University and I absolutely love it!! I am also planning on minoring in graphic design. I just created the logo that is on my website a few weeks ago. So what makes my blog different and why should you keep coming back? I am a college student and I love high end fashion, and designer brands... don't we all? Well, designer brands and high end fashion are EXPENSIVE! The goal of my blog is to provide you with head-to-toe fashion with the best possible prices. 

 Thanks for checking me and supporting me on my journey to become a fashion journalist!
