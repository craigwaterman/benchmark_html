How to Shop for Statement Necklaces

9/26/13


Statement necklaces have been one of the jewelry must haves for a last couple of years. The tricky thing about them is their tendency to look tacky when they are not made well. So if you plan on adding one to your wardrobe, do not hesitate to invest in it. Since most of us do not wear them daily, statement necklaces are used mainly for special occasions, so it is crucial that these necklaces do not look like custom jewelries. 
1. Good craftsmen-shift - Ensure pieces on the statement necklaces are sturdy and put together well without any loose wires and pieces. It should not feel like it is going to fall apart, unless it is meant to be a delicate piece.   


2.  Size - Avoid too large necklaces for your body frame. Necklaces are supposed to accentuate an outfit and your features, but they should not overwhelm your appearance. 

3. Color - Pick a color palate that is either mostly neutral or encompasses various contrasting colors. Neutral color palate is extremely versatile for any outfit, on the other hand contrasting colors in a necklace is gives you more flexibility to pick outfits of different colors.


4. Silhouette - Make sure it sits comfortably on your body. It should look like it belongs on your body, not in a glass display at a gallery. 
5. Price - $150-$200 is a reasonable price for a statement necklace. More is definitely excessive and less will most likely end up being waste of money.

Fall Wardrobe Essentials' Guide

9/23/13

Scarf: H&M Crinkled Scarf // Sweater: J Crew Oversize Merino Pocket Sweater // Coat: Burberry Double Wool Twill Trench Coat // Ankle Boots: BCBG generation wallis buckle bootie

During colder weathers, only a few pieces frame our outfits the whole season. Thus, they have to encompass everything you need from a clothing item. It needs to be classic, comfortable, warm, versatile, and chic and most importantly represent who you are. I do not wear clothes that do not seem like me, and this is the case for most of us. Remember that beautiful shirt you bought a year ago you never wear, because it just does not feel right... Yep, that is a type of purchase you must avoid when you shop for your fall essentials.  Here are a few tips for you when you shop for your fall essentials. 

Price: Invest in your fall essentials, because you will wear them daily for a few months and possibly a few years if they are classic pieces, which is worth the investment. You do not want to look for a new coat in the middle of the season, when your bargain priced coat rips on you. However, I do not encourage spending money that you do not have. Invest in a way that your wallet still has some room to breath.

Color: Pick colors that are neutral or earth toned such as army green, caramel, black and cream. Bright pieces might look beautiful once in a while, but you do not want to be that girl in red for the entire season. 

Fit: Ensure your fall pieces fit comfortably. Give some extra room for layering, so forget about that adorable skintight sample size coat you found.

Makeup Review: Neutrogena Healthy Skin Foundation

9/14/13


Neutrogena Healthy Skin Liquid Makeup is the first and only drug store foundation that I loved so far. Natural finish with medium to full coverage that lasts at least 4-5 hours, preferably 8 hours is what I expect from my foundation. It is nearly impossible to find one that meets my expectation. However, this one did amazingly well. 

Texture (1/3): It is rather runny and not thick at all. 

Coverage/ Pigmentation/ Color (2/3): It is medium coverage. Colors have more of pink undertone than yellow, so people who have very yellow undertone might have trouble finding a shade. 


Application (2/3): It is very easy to apply. Makeup sponge gives more natural finish, but it gives more dewy finish with foundation brushes. Overall, it is naturally dewy finish that is just perfect alone, but with finishing powder, it could be mattified a little. However, it is not matte at all, even with finishing powder.

Longevity (2/3): It last at least for 4 hours during the summer which is impressive. In colder temperature and with finishing powder and primer, it could definitely lasts 8 hours. 

Packaging (1/3): It has a glass bottle without a pump. So it is not travel or pour friendly. I always end up with more than I need, and lack of a pump is not sanitary and user friendly. 

Price (3/3): I paid around $13. It is affordable and quality is good for the price.

Overall (2/3): Neutrogena Healthy Skin Liquid Foundation is a great affordable drugstore foundation with natural finish and reasonable coverage. The color selection for those people who have more yellow undertone and inconvenient lack of a pump are the only problems I have. But they are common problems for drugstore foundations. If you like truly matte finish, then I would not suggest this product. However, it does not break me out, which is great for people who have acne prone skin.

Beauty Blender is Different From Regular Makeup Sponges

9/11/13

I wanted to know what the deal is with this little hot pink sponge. Normally I apply my foundation with my hands on daily basis for more natural look. For heavier coverage, I use kabuki brushes. I noticed that using any sponges result in the most natural looking coverage, but I hated how it sucked up most of my foundation. 

I wetted the beauty blender thoroughly which only took a second and squeezed the excess water out. It is pretty flexible, but compared to the general sponges, it is bit denser. I dotted my foundations on the usual spots and used the beauty blender to blend everything out. Everything applied evenly that I was wondering if it was even applying anything to my skin. Being a doubter, I thought that the pink blender was eating all my foundations instead of putting it on my face, but surprisingly most of my foundation was really on my face being blended beautifully like my second skin.

When I used the second layer of foundation as a concealer at areas that have breakouts and dry patches, the dampened blender did an amazing job covering the imperfections without emphasizing the dry patches and blemishes. I think the secret to the beauty blender is the denser composition and its round shape. So maybe if you try cheaper beauty sponges that is slightly ticker and round, you might be able to get the same result if you don't want to spend around $20 for this hot pink mess. I do not regret spending the money on it, but if I can find something else for cheaper, I would go for it.

Fontaine Saint-Michel


The picture was taken in Paris last year. I loved my stay in there and really would like to be back for a casual trip without running around the city trying to see everything. The city is simply huge and there is so much to it that you can never finish exploring little streets that have full of life on their own.

A month ago I realized that there is not any interest group or club type of organization for female graduate students in our institution. Graduate school is long and difficult journey to pursue for anyone, but it is longer and more difficult for women especially because they tend to have a set of unique needs and circumstances that do not quite go with the career pathway. For example, most graduate students in natural science field in the United States take 4 years for college, 6-7 years for PhD, and 3-5 years for post doctoral fellowship. Then they take another few years at a research institution to get a tenure track position, which tends to be very stressful and competitive. So female graduate students are between 31 to 35 on average by the time they are done with their training. By that time, their biological clock is ticking if having a family is something to be considered. The problem I find is that nobody talks about this topic at all through out their training and I am not sure how they really handle everything at the end. I believe that since having a family is unavoidable for most women and have to plan somewhere along  the way, it is important for female graduate students to have some understanding of how people figure things out in general, because sure many female scientist in academia make it through at the end, though it is relatively small percentage of the total population.

So I decided that I will start that organization. I had no idea where to begin. The first obvious step was to contact the campus activities organization regarding information on how to start a new club. I got an immediate response and got an appointment with the director for 15 minutes. From what I gathered, the student government organization decides whether or not the club deserves to be recognized. But what I got in general was that he did not seem to think that women in research/science can be an organization on its own, because students in research club (already existing) should be able to cover the same topic. I think it is understandable that he is trying to streamline everything so there are not bunch of clubs that have the same focus, but I did not get the impression that he thought the issues that I brought was important. I guess maybe I did not do a good job explaining what I meant to say. Afterwards, I talked a female coordinator of the college regarding the topic, she thought it was a great idea and it is something that can be helpful for many students.

Now the question I left with is that, how two people perceived the same idea very differently. Obviously they can not be directly compared, because their jobs were different. Another thing I thought was that maybe I came across as a crazy feminist who hates men (I recently realized that when you fight for any female related cause, you get branded as a crazy man hating feminist in the United State lol). I guess I do not care what people think since there really seem to be a need for such an organization for the sake of other women, but it does not mean I am a bitter, crazy, insane man hating feminist lol.

It is very funny how things get interesting when you put your head out of the world of pure science and lab work and step into social issues in the field. Things seem to get more relevant to everyday life and farther away from science. My words get more subjective  when there is not any figures and legends to support my point of view, while others' opinions seem to matter more even without any supporting solid evidence. I am not sure how people in humanities are able to manage to get their point across, but it seems to be more difficult when there is not any funny graphs and blots like in natural sciences.

All About Clean&Clear Deep Cleaning Astringent


What does it do?
Clean and Clear Deep Cleaning Astringent has been one of my skincare staples ever since I was a teenager. It contains salicylic acid, which is one of the two most common over the counter acne medications and also it is a gentle chemical exfoliator.  Thus, it is perfect for teenage or acne prone skin type. Salicylic acid gently exfoliates, unclogging pores at the same time peels dead skin layer, preventing clogged pores. Also that fancy word astringent indicates that it should close pores as well, which is must after opening pores to deep cleanse. The point is that salicylic acid is good for your skin and these guys have it in them.

Are the generic brands as good as the brand one?
You will find store or brand version of this product everywhere. I have used pretty much all of them at some point of my life. My verdict is the clean&clear one is more effective for some reason, though the active ingredient is the same for all of them. I would personally buy the brand version, but if I did not have a choice, I would survive with the generic versions.

What is the difference between the red and blue one?
That is a great question. The blue one has only 0.5% salicylic acid while the red one has 2%. So in short, the red one is stronger and the blue one is gentler, which is recommended for sensitive skin. I personally like to use the blue one, because it works for most skin types. The red one is pretty strong and can irritate skin pretty easily. Unless you have serious acne issue, I would stay away from the red one. However, if you are breaking out all the sudden, go for the red one and use it as a spot treatment. It will do wonders.

Is there any cons?
They peel skin, so do not over-soak the cotton balls or pads, because eventually it will start to peel skin of your fingertips, which can be pretty annoying. Also they tend to lose their effectiveness after extended use.

How much do they cost?
Somewhere between $3-5 depending on where you get them

Strawberry Picking: Can We Live Without Technology?


Development of internet, technology and television really made us people into a whole another "species". Though if we put someone from today with someone from 20 years ago, they can definitely mate and produce viable children, which makes the use of the word, species, inappropriate in this context biologically. However, the mere fact that, they will probably have such a horrible time communicating and understanding each other is the reason why I used the term. 


As a person who spent my childhood during the start of the technological era, I can somewhat understand the lifestyle before technology, those who are born during 90s have no idea what it is to be like without internet, cell phone and constant TV. Looking back, it might have been more pleasant time before technology, because you get to do what it feels to be natural as a living organism. 


However, now it is almost like we are trying to be the part of the technology rather than trying technology to be part of us. We want to have a google brain and do not want to sleep anymore. The truth is, we will never be a technology as we are just a part of a nature that found a way to be unnatural in this world. As I spend my precious free time staring at the computer screen and TV at the same time on a coach that makes me forget what it feels like to run and be outside in fresh air, I wonder why I feel so exhausted and uninterested when I am absolutely not moving at all. 



The funny part is, I do not realize that all I need to feel alive is stop staring at screens smart phone, laptop, tv and what not and start being an active living organism that I am for real. Feel the air and wind, let the brain process wilderness is what I need rather than artificial light glaring at me from some screen of a kind. As I walked down the aisle of a strawberry farm, I realize how beautiful it is to just do something natural like strawberry picking and enjoying the view. Yet I can not leave my technological life behind, bringing my camera and trying to capture the view and moments. 



Yea yeah... it will make me remember these moments from years to come, but the reality is nothing comes free. While I am playing with my camera setting and lighting and photo composition, I am losing moments that I should have truly enjoyed with my full attention, strawberry picking. I do not think I will live without any technology rest of my life. To be honest, I don't mind it, as it is the reason why living standard gets better in all respect including medicine and convenience. The only catch is, I, you and everyone should relearn to enjoy nature and be ourselves--human beings in wilderness--without or at least minimal amount of technology that we can definitely live without for a few moments.
