About Coleen Naylor, RN, BA, HNB-BC

Coleen Naylor, RN, BA, HNB-BC

My vision of Healing to the Power of You is the product of my journey across 20 years of patient care from a hospital setting to an independent holistic practice. It is with great enthusiasm and joy that I begin my independent practice, and I am delighted that you have stopped by.

Just out of nursing school, I began my journey caring for cardiac patients in a step-down unit.  These patients were recovering from heart attacks, coronary artery bypass surgery, and other serious heart conditions. I gave the patients their medications, monitored their vital signs, and fulfilled my nursing tasks, but noticed significant positive changes in their overall health when I was able to rub their backs at night and allow them to express their feelings. I wanted to learn more about relaxing people, as I could see that it was very healing. I took a class in foot reflexology to help relax my patients and improve their health. As I performed my new skill on my patients, you could see immediate results; their stress just melted from their faces. Often, they were sleeping within minutes of a treatment. That was the beginning of my holistic journey.

As I continued to work as a cardiac nurse, I expanded my holistic knowledge base, taking classes in relaxation techniques, holistic nursing, and energy modalities. This course of study led me to become certified by the American Holistic Nurses Association.

Through my holistic approach to nursing, I gained the trust of my patients, the respect of my colleagues, and the support of the hospital leadership. In this way, I was able to pioneer the position of Holistic Health Nurse at the hospital, which I held for 9 years. While I enjoyed the opportunity to provide holistic nursing care to the patients at the hospital and teaching holistic classes for the nursing staff, I had a vision to expand the scope of my holistic practice.

Healing to the Power of You is my vision to empower people to achieve balance in their lives, perhaps even before they become patients, and to help healthcare practitioners care for themselves as they care for others.
