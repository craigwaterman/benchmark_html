DIY Juice Cleanse

August 28, 2015 by Annika Leave a Comment

A few months ago my fitness levels were at a all time high which resulted in me letting go a little bit telling my self ‘I’ts ok just go ahead and eat whatever you like, you work hard enough’ which I think is important every once in a while, however, I got a little carried away. A two week holiday which generally involves stuffing my face didn’t really help either.

I still managed to get in a few workouts but at also noticed my pants getting tighter. Not surprisingly, I put on 4 kilos. The best way for me to get back into eating healthy is to reset completely with a juice cleanse.

My ultimate goal with the juicing was not to loose weight as its usually water weight that is lost and you will probably put it back on when you start eating solids, I like to do it to reboot my system, get rid of toxins which play a part in food cravings and to start over.

There are plenty of companies in the UAE that do juice cleansing packages but you’re going to have to shell out the big bucks and why do that when its perfectly easy to do at home if you have a juicer.

The last time I did a cleanse I was living in Melbourne and not working a full time job which gave me plenty of time to squeeze my juice and have it right away. However now between work, working out and everything else in between doesn’t leave much time to squeeze as I go.

I started the cleanse on the weekend and for the last day, squeezed the juice into mason jars to take to work. I filled the juice up all the way to the top of the jar before sealing and refrigerating. This keeps the juice from oxidizing which would usually happen in 20 minutes if exposed to air, which is said to diminish nutritional value of the juice.

Result: 

PROS

Many people seem to complain of headaches however I did not have any issues.

It’s amazing how much time in a day we spend on eating, cooking and talking about food. I found myself having plenty of time to kill which wasn’t such a bad thing compared to my usually busy day.

I did a session of circuit training on day 3 and was worried I would pass out but you’re stronger than you think so long as you are hydrated at all times.

I felt hydrated and light. It felt good to de-bloat and though I didn’t check my weight before and after I’m pretty sure I lost a kilo or 2.

Spent a total of AED 125 for all that fruit and veg. A juice cleanse usually costs between 500 to 1500 AED which is just bizarre. I’d rather spend my money on actual food.

CONS

With all that free time I spent a good couple of hours liking food photos on Instagram and Pinterest. Why torture myself you ask? It’s very hard to stop myself from thinking about food. I’m one of those people who will talk about what to have for dinner while still having lunch.

I found myself fantasising about food and wanted to sink my teeth into something, anything meaty.

Chewing is totally underrated. I really missed chewing!

Cleaning the juicer is just annoying

No social life for 3 days because lets face it everything involves food

Tips for an effective juice cleanse

1. Fruit Juice only in the morning as it is high in sugar and your body will burn it during the day. Do not have it later than breakfast. Before you have fruit juice ensure you drink a lot of water so it doesn’t lead to a spike in insulin.
2. Do not mix fruit and vegetables as they require different enzymes for digestion. The only fruit that should be mixed in with vegetables are apples and pears. This will also sweeten vegetable juice.
3. Water is your best friend
4. Try to get in some form of exercise

PS I am not a nutritionist. This is just something that works for me.

Here’s my 3 day juice cleanse plan:

Day 1, Juice 1: Pineapple and cherry
Day 1, Juice 2: Beetroot, Celery, Apples, Carrots, Lemon
Day 1, Juice 3: Rocket, Lemon, Cucumber, Apple
Day 1, Juice 4: Celery, Parsley, Cucumber

Day 2, Juice 1: Grapefruit and Orange
Day 2, Juice 2: Coconut Water, Pineapple, Mint, Celery, Spinach
Day 2, Juice 3:Carrots, Capsicum, Parsley, Celery
Dat 2, Juice 4:Broccoli, Cucumber, Apples

Day 3, Juice 1: Kiwi and Pear
Day 3, Juice 2:Beetroot, Celery, Apple, Ginger
Day 3, Juice 3: Apple, Lemon, Ginger, Carrots, Spinach, Capsicum
Day 3, Juice 4:Cucumber, Celery, Corriander, Lemon,  Rocket
