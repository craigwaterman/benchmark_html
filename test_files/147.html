Wearing my heart on my sleeve; a journey of photographic discovery {Brisbane Wedding Photographer}

June 22, 2012

Lately I’ve been reflecting on where I want to take my business in the future and the kind of work I want to produce for my clients. In a very saturated industry sometimes it can feel difficult ‘standing out from the crowd’ and remaining focussed on creating work that truly reflects your own individual style, rather than what’s popular or ‘in fashion’.

I have always wanted to produce amazing, emotive images for my clients. Part of reaching this point has involved defining the style of my work. Defining ‘your style’ as a photographer is a very personal but exciting journey (which I’m sure other photographers would agree with). It’s a whole lot of self discovery and everytime I get a little bit closer it feels AMAZING! Being a photographer to me is like being an artist, a painter, a musician. As an artist I wear my heart on my sleeve and sometimes this is scary! But the rewards for doing so make it well worth it.

I am a photographer for many reasons, but a large reason is for my clients. I love translating my clients hopes and wishes into final pieces of art. Sometimes I think it’s easy for us {photographers} to get caught up in getting noticed by other photographers, but I was recently reminded by a very special photographer that if we focus on our clients then the rest will follow. If it wasn’t for our clients we wouldn’t be here!

Today I feel like saying thank you. I’m so grateful for the beautiful people in this industry who have made my journey thus far a pleasant one. The last minute text messages, facebook PM’s and countless phone calls which have helped me problem solve my way through starting my own business.

Thank you to the amazingly talented people in this industry who inspire me and push me to continue to learn and grow. I cherish and appreciate what it is to share your artwork with the world and the hard work that goes into doing this not only out of passion, but also as your chosen profession.

I want to say a big thank you to the wonderful Kristie from Ngg studios for setting this shoot up for me and inspiring me to continue to develop my own personal style. Also a big thank you to our lovely couple Alysha and Cameron for being wonderful to work with! (Amazing dress, ring and earrings supplied by Eve Boutique on the Sunshine Coast).
