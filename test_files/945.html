Card Buffet – Confetti Wishes

Posted on 08/03/2015 by Live Love Paper 76

Happy Monday!  I hope you all got a chance to stop by to read Saturday’s blog post about the all new, amazingly awesome product from Close To My Heart – including the brand new Cricut cart Artistry!!!

It truly is amazing – over 700 images like each of the others we offer exclusively through CTMH.  I had a chance to play with it this weekend and I’ll have some projects soon to share!

But today, I am excited for a whole other reason!  I am hosting my very first  ‘All You Can Create’ Card Buffet this month!  YAY!!  I’m holding two sessions on Aug. 22nd – a morning one from 10 – noon and an evening one from 6:30 – 8:30 – so if you are local to me and want to join, go over to Facebook and RSVP for the morning or evening session!  If you aren’t local to me and still want to participate, there’s a ‘To Go’ option I’m offering as well, but more on that in a little bit.

For my ‘All You Can Create’ card buffet, there will be 10 card designs to choose from.  Each will be in a separate tote – the cardstock, papers, ribbon, shimmer trim – it’s all pre-measured and cut for you.  The inks and stamps will be included in the tote as well as any embellishments you may need.  I am not including any instructions per se, but there is a sample card that I created for a visual to go by and, of course, I’ll be there for questions or guidance.  I wanted this to be something fun for the most novice stamper/card maker to enjoy all the way up to an experienced crafter.

This week I’ll be sharing the 10 card designs I created.  I used 5 different paper collections, 2 card designs from each.  Today I’m showing the cards I made using the Confetti Wishes paper.  And since it’s my hubby’s birthday today and we celebrated over the weekend the first card I made was a birthday card!

This was a great card to make because it used up some of the scraps I had leftover from making my hubby’s gift box.  I thought this was so cheerful and I love that Doodled Designs stamp set!

The next card is one that I call ‘any occasion’.  I love to keep these on hand because you can use them for birthdays, thank yous or really just because.  I love to send just because cards.  With all the junk mail and bills that is received in the mail on a daily basis, it’s nice to send something that can put a smile on a loved ones face and let them know you’re thinking about them!

I’ll be honest with you – the Confetti Wishes paper was not my favorite when this Idea book first came out.  But when I finally got the paper in my hands and saw how cheerful the colors were, I fell in love.

Now, earlier I mentioned there would be a ‘To Go’ option for the card buffet.  This is a great option for anyone who lives a great distance from me!  The ‘To Go’ buffet will include all the pre-cut papers, any cricut die cuts there may be, ribbon/shimmer trim and embellishments for each of the 10 cards.  I will also include a supply list for each card so you may purchase the supplies needed to make more if you choose.  What is not included is the stamp set, ink and adhesive.  The stamps used may be purchased from my online shop or you may substitute a similar stamp of your own.  The To Go card buffet kits are $24.99 and that includes shipping.  Just send me an email or message me on facebook letting me know you are interested and I will get your info and send you a Paypal invoice.

Have a great week!!

Happy Birthday – gift box idea

Posted on 07/31/2015 by Live Love Paper 76

Good morning, how’s everyone doing today?  I’m My hubby’s birthday is Monday, and he’ll be working so we are celebrating this weekend!  I love birthdays – I love any reason for a fun celebration :)

I collect ideas through the year for gifts…whenever he or the kids mention something they want or like I write  it down and come back to it whenever I need a gift idea.  I’m pretty confident they will be getting something they want and like and yet still keep the element of surprise!

My husband is a hard-working truck driver logging in the miles every day – he’s also a musician.  Unfortunately, he doesn’t have much time to play or write anymore, but he does have his music to keep him company in the truck every day.  A while back, he asked for a couple of cds – does anyone buy cds anymore?  He has an ipod too, but still comes back to cds a lot.  Anyhow – I went on Amazon and got a great deal on what he wanted but I needed an awesome box to wrap it up in!

This box was sized to hold 2 cds – you could also alter it a bit to hold a single cd.  I cut 2 pieces of cardstock 7 x 7 3/4.  To make the bottom of the box, I scored each side at 1″.  On the two shorter ends, I cut near the corner on the score line up to the score line that crosses it to make the flaps.  Fold everything and glue the flap to the inside to make the bottom box.  I did the exact same thing to make the lid, but scored it 1/8″ shorter on each side so the lid slides over the box easily.

I created a belly band to go around the box so it wouldn’t fall open.  I’m not sure of the measurements of this piece – it was a last minute addition and late last night.  I pretty much just cut a 2″ strip and wrapped it around.  A 12″ paper was a bit too small though, so I cut another piece to bridge the gap and put that on the top so it was covered by the embellishments.  The star border and Happy Birthday were cut from Birthday Bash cricut cart and the Party Hat was cut from Cuttin’ Up cricut cart.  I don’t have measurements – I use Design Space or Craft Room depending on what machine is closest and just fit to size.

The balloons on the box were cut from CTMH Artiste cricut bundle.  I popped them up with some foam tape and added Glacier twine for the strings.  Here it is all put together:

I’m also working on one to fit dvds and blu-rays.  I’m hosting a card class in Oct. where we will be creating Christmas versions of these boxes.  I’ll post them when I have them completed and kits will be available for those who can’t attend!

Operation Smile

Posted on 07/27/2015 by Live Love Paper 76

Phew!  It’s been a crazy week!  My son was in an all day camp last week and the baby is down to one nap a day, but it lasts for a couple hours.  I can’t believe how many projects I was able to complete! I’ve posted some on Instagram and Facebook – I’ll have posts going up here shortly.

Today though, I wanted to talk to you about Operation Smile.  For those of you that are unfamiliar, Operation Smile is an organization that Close To My Heart has partnered with that provides free, life-changing surgery that repairs smiles and reshapes lives, one child at a time.  When you purchase the special stamp sets, Close To My Heart will donate $3.00 of the proceeds to Operation Smile.  Since its founding in 1982, Operaton Smile has performed over 220,000 surgical procedures to correct facial deformities in children.  Since 2008, Close To My Heart has provided support to change the lives of over 1,500 children and their families.  You can read more here!

There are now TWO ways to support Operation Smile – one is by purchasing the special stamp set.  The current stamp set is called Happy Everything and is super duper cute!  It’s also one of the stamps I used on todays project.

Isn’t that so cute!!  Ok – I did mention two ways to help out.  The second way is through these awesome, brand new t-shirts offered through Close To My Heart.  They just became available for purchase yesterday!  They are available in colors gray or red and are available in either men’s or women’s style shirts.  The shirts are only $25.00, larger sizes slightly more, and $10.00 of each shirt is donated toward Operation Smile.  I love these shirts!!

Ok – on to today’s project.  I have a card to share with you guys.  I used a technique that I have never tried before.  I’ve seen it on various blogs and on you tube for years but it’s one of those things on my to do list that never seem to get done.  Well, I’ve decided it’s time to tackle some of these techniques and shake things up a bit!

This was my inspiration for the card.  I started with White Daisy card stock, ran it through my Cuddlebug with the Chevron embossing folder.  I then laid the cardstock down over a piece of scrap paper and lightly ran my Glacier ink pad over it.  I love this!!  I want to use every single embossing folder I have in every color we offer!! Realistically though – I see this popping up in my projects more often.

Here’s a quick pic of all the supplies – they will be listed below as always with links for you. Notice the second stamp set – Pretty Petals, this was for the flowers.  It coordinates with the Art Philosophy cricut cart so I didn’t have to do any fussy cutting.  So easy! And here is the completed card…

I just absolutely loved how this card turned out!  The embossed & inked paper makes for a really cool background!  I can’t wait to use this technique again.  :)

Hope you all have a wonderful week!!

Supplies used:

  • White Daisy cardstock
  • Glacier cardstock
  • Chevron embossing folder
  • Happy Everything stamp set
  • Pretty Petals stamp set
  • Glacier ink (sentiment & embossing background)
  • Thistle ink (main flower)
  • Sweet Leaf ink (leaves)
  • Bamboo ink (flower center)
  • Art Philosophy cricut cartridge
  • paper trimmer
  • bone folder
  • glue pen
