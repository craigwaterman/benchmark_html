Coconut Bounty Protein Bliss Balls

November 11, 2014May 23, 2015 | kbsugarfree

Seriously, who doesn’t love bliss balls? The perfect snack for morning, post work out, 3.30itis, dessert – bit size pieces of absolute goodness.

There is nothing intricate or demanding about them, all you need is a food processor/blender, ingredients and 5 minutes. Bliss balls have the potential to be extremely nutritious, energizing, metabolism stimulating and of course healthy and if done well they are the perfect substitute to a chocolate bar or sweet craving!

Generally, you will be able to pull off a bliss ball with whatever ingredients you have available. I generally dont follow a recipe, but that is always annoying when you make an awesome batch and want some more the exact same! So for the purposes of sharing, I have been scribing and measuring my ingredients for you all, so you can enjoy similar little balls of goodness! (you are welcome ;))

So, what are my priorities with bliss balls?
1. Well personally, I love eating treats knowing I’m getting in some much needed vegetables. Its weird, I know. But vegetables are so important and sometimes when you are constantly on the go, getting your recommended serves can be difficult. Also, vegetables are mostly tasteless, meaning they add bulk substance to the balls with minimal calories and provide extra fiber, minerals and vitamins! Vegetables will generally remain in the raw form, which is always a plus!
2. Minimal amounts of sweetener – this to me is really important. Its pointless to have a bliss ball that is purely comprised of an ‘alternative’ sugar and provide no additional substance or nutrition. That isn’t beating your sugar addiction, its feeding it. Sweeteners can really extend far and my tips to help with this include:
– Using a liquid sweetener – its just my own theory but this helps it to be finely and equally distributed over your bliss ball mixture, that way the sweet taste is consistent and when you test try your batch you wont have inconsistencies and will hence require less
– Making a liquid sweetener – for example soaking dates, breaking them down, and using the liquid that remains at the bottom as well. Or mixing hot water and less coconut sugar to make a liquid. Mixing the sweetener with a nut milk to spread it further etc! There are plenty of ways, but these all end up ensuring the first point – even distribution of sweetener requiring less!
3. Protein – powders, nuts, legumes, beans; all important for a balanced meal/snack and ensuring bioavailability of nutrients
4. Good fats – essential for any snack, more fat = more satiety = less snacking = less overeating (but pls gosh don’t under-eat!)

So there you have it! All that constitutes a good bliss ball in the kbsugarfree books!

The first two really are what I prioritize and I’m feeling that a series of vegetable bliss balls might be necessary ;) Let me know if you would be interested in that in the comments below or via email (kbsugarfree@hotmail.com).

These bliss balls tick all the boxes, and I got the veggie serve in using Amazonia’s Raw Green Powder which is made of nutrient-dense green sprouts, leaves, juices, grasses, algae, herbs and natural sea minerals (all the good green stuff!). I also sourced the protein from the equally incredible Vanilla Raw Protein Isolate by Amazonia, chosen for its pre-biotic, bio-fermented pea and brown rice origins + being GF, DF and the raw enzyme qualities!

So welcome to the collection, you beautiful little Coconut Bounty Protein Bliss Balls:

Ingredients:
• 20g Amazonia Raw Vanilla Protein Isolate
• 5g Amazonia Raw Greens
• 25ml Almond Breeze Unsweetened Almond Milk
• 100g organic oats
• 65g organic shredded coconut
• 50g organic almonds
• 15g organic cacao powder
• 50g organic dates (soaked in 50-75mLs of hot water at least)
• 30g Pure Harvest Rice Malt Syrup
Method:
• Soak dates in boiling water at least 20 minutes prior and intermittently squash during that period (even add more boiling water) it should be equal quantities of hot water to dates AT LEAST, I will usually add more – just make sure it doesn’t go TOO runny and separate
• Blend the almonds first, to get a meal consistency.
• Add everything else!
• NOTE: I kept the rice malt syrup until the end, just as a touch up for flavor – you might not need this, you might need more or you might need less (depending on the sweetness of your dates/sweetener of choice)
• ENJOY YOUR BALLS OF GOODNESS
