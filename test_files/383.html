I may not have a list of favorite games of the Seventh Generation, but I can give you guys a list of my favorite games of this decade… so far. The criteria are as follows:

  • One game per franchise.
  • They have to have been released between 2010 to 2015, at least from their country of origin. That is why Bayonetta is nowhere on the list because it first came out in Japan in 2009.
  • Only original games. No HD remasters.

Just remember, these are my personal picks. If your favorite games are not anywhere on the list, that means I either haven’t played them or reluctantly booted them off the list while picking the candidates. Without further ado, these are my Top 10 Games of the 2010s decade… so far.

Posted by CrashJakFan1994 (CJF94) on May 23, 2015
Posted in: cjf blog. Tagged: CJF94, My Little Pony. Leave a comment

Hooray for an episode centering around Discord. Even after he reformed in Season 3, he is still a lot of fun, being the mischievous draconequus that he always is. Not to mention that he is voiced by Q from Star Trek: The Next Generation, doubling the fun. It seems as though we have yet another great episode in this season, taking advantage of Discord’s comedy, while still providing a relatable situation.

The episode begins with Discord and Fluttershy having a tea party. Fluttershy reveals to him that she is taking someone to the Grand Galloping Gala, the show’s equivalent to a ball. Discord is a little ticked off about this, but tries not to show it in front of her. Yup, it’s a jealousy episode.

Gexup – Top 10 Platformers for the Nintendo 64

Posted by Keith Allen Weber on May 19, 2015
Posted in: Gexup, Gexup Written Countdowns, Gexup's Written Reviews, Written. Tagged: Banjo-Kazooie, gexup, N64, Nintendo 64, Super Mario 64. 1 Comment

The Nintendo 64 is my second favorite Nintendo console ever. Just having the whole trend of 3D gaming was some of my favorite hype ever to video games. It was the new standard of video games to be heading and look at us now, it now the norm! Nearly everything that was released from the Super Nintendo was transformed into full 3D. It was a gimmick that actually worked and it’s always a joy to reminisce about these days. More so than ever is how the platform genre tremendously evolved into the 3D platform genre that’s my all time favorite genre ever! This machine was built for 3D platforming with it’s innovative analog stick and rumble feature that still blows my mind today. I couldn’t get enough of what this system was offering in the platformer category. The genre works so well for this system and it seems so seamless when playing it. And that’s what i’m going to judge here in this list of the best platformers of the N64!
