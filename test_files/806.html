I've looked at the leagues that appear to be out there and none satisfy the criteria I'm seeking. Actually, none are even close. So what's a guy to do? Start a new league, it seems.

So I started working on a set of rules for this new league that would feature a relatively tight salary cap, player contracts, free agency, and a financial system that acted as both a way to pay for your players and a motivator for league participation and service.

I'm looking for veteran league guys (especially commissioners) to offer some constructive criticism. Have at it!

FYI - It's a long read...

The IRONMAN BASEBALL LEAGUE (IBL) is a computer league using the latest version of Action PC Baseball Game and subsequent patches. It is comprised of 24 league members, each of whom will act as Coach & General Manager for their team. The league uses the designated hitter (DH).


ORGANIZATION

Commissioner

  • Shall be empowered to assess penalties, at his discretion, for violations of the Constitution where no specific penalties for said violations are delineated in the Constitution.
  • Is also empowered to impose a lesser penalty than that specified in the Constitution.
  • Shall be empowered to veto any trade that is in his opinion, a significantly unbalanced trade, or not in the best interests of the League.

Rule Changes

  • A 2/3-yes vote of the membership is required for passage of any amendment (except expansion; see below). All Coaches must cast either a Yes or No vote on all Amendments. Abstention is not permitted.
  • Amendments to the Constitution are effective one month prior to the start of the regular season unless otherwise stated.
  • Before the league can expand there must be an 80% approval vote by the membership.

Regular Season Schedule

  • Each full season consists of a 160 game (80 home and 80 road) regular season. Each week involves the playing of two series, either at home or on the road.
  • The weeks including July 4th and Labor Day will be 2-week periods in which the current week's games can be played at any time during those two weeks.
  • Each team's 160 regular season games include the following:
    • Division: 4 home and 4 road games vs. 11 division opponents = 88 games
    • Other Divisions: 3 home and 3 road games vs. 12 non-division opponents = 72 games
    • See APPENDIX A: Schedule Template
  • The current league alignment is as follows:
    • East Division: 
    • West Division: Mohave Hellfire, 

Tie-Breaking Rules

  • Ties for draft position and playoff seeding are broken in the following order:
    • Head-to-head record
    • Division record (if in same division)
    • Run differential (scored versus allowed)
    • Home wins
  • In the unlikely event that none of the above listed criteria is sufficient to break a tie, a one game playoff will be simmed by the commissioner.

Playoffs

  • 2 Division Winners, seeded 1-2, and 6 Wild Cards, seeded 2-8, make the playoffs.
  • 1st Round: 8@1, 7@2, 6@3, 5@4. Remaining teams re-seed to begin each new round with highest remaining seed(s) hosting lowest remaining seed(s).
  • All playoff series are "Best of 7" format, using a 2-2-1 schedule with one day off between each switch of venue and two days off between each round.
  • Statistics will be reset prior to the start of each series. Injuries carry over into the next round.
  • Batters' fatigue will be used in the playoffs, checking ON the option to "Prorate Usage Rules For Each Playoff Series" in the USAGE RULES Tab.

FINANCES

Salary Cap

  • Teams may spend no more than $100 million and no less than $90 million during the regular- and post-seasons.
  • Off-season: Teams may exceed the salary cap until a date set by the commissioner, which will typically fall a day or two before the Unrestricted Free Agent Draft.
  • Increases: After the 2011 season and for each season thereafter, the salary cap will be adjusted accordingly if DKS makes significant player dollar value adjustments.

Expenses

  • Total Salaries, paid in full (deducted from account) just prior to the start of the season, not to exceed $ 100 million or fall below $ 90 million
  • Optional Restricted Free Agent bonuses, paid immediately upon signing player
  • Cash traded

Contracts

  • Range from 1-5 years, with base salary being equal to the player's current DKS dollar value at the time of the signing and is set until the contract expires.
  • Remain attached to the player until he is cut or his contract expires.
  • May not be restructured.
  • Do not "kick in" until the player is carded for the first time.

Free Agent Bidding

  • Each offseason, prior to the Minor League and Unrestricted Free Agent drafts, the league will hold a four-week Restricted Free Agent bidding session via email. During this time, in draft order, each team will nominate a player for general managers to bid on.
  • Each day, the team currently up for nomination will submit a name, team that owns rights to the player, and the player's current DKS value. Bidding is then open and bids are submitted via email to the proper authority. Any team may bid but no team is required to do so. Bidding for the day closes at a time set by the proper authority.
  • An acceptable bid will contain a contract length (1-5 years) and bonus amount (optional). Total contract value is calculated as ((DK$ x length) + bonus). 
    • Ties for highest bid are broken in the following order:
      • Highest bonus
      • Draft order
    • Teams may bid on their own players.
    • Bonuses are deducted from the winning team's account immediately and cannot exceed available funds (account balance minus total of active contracts). While player salary is not deducted from the account immediately, it is smart to make sure you will be able to afford the salary for the upcoming season.
  • The team that owns the player's expiring contract also owns the right to match the highest bid on that player. At the conclusion of the day's bidding, highest bid is either matched or released to the bidding team but must be accepted in its entirety and may not be restructured. 
  • Teams may pass at any time but in subsequent rounds may no longer submit a nomination. The passing team may continue to bid on nominated players and match bids on it's own Restricted Free Agents.   
  • Restricted Free Agents that do not receive a bid during the four-week bidding session must be immediately re-signed to a 1-5 year contract at current DK$ value or released. Restricted Free Agents that are released go directly to the Unrestricted Free Agent Draft pool.  


PLAYERS

Drafts

  • Each offseason the league will conduct a 3-Round Minor League Draft of any player currently listed on a minor league roster but not "carded" in the game, followed approximately two weeks later with an unlimited Unrestricted Free Agent draft comprised of all computer-carded players not on an IBL roster.
  • Draft picks may be traded.
  • Teams draft in the following order of their record from the previous IBL season:
    • Non-playoff teams draft at the beginning of each round (1 through 18), in reverse order of record.
    • 1st Round playoff series losers draft next, in reverse order of record.
    • 2nd Round playoff series losers draft next, in reverse order of record.
    • World Series loser will draft 23rd and winner will draft 24th.
  • Players selected in the 3-Round Minor League Draft shall be placed on each team's unlimited "AAA" roster until he is “carded” for the first time. No other players may reside on a team's AAA roster.
  • Players selected in either draft 
  • Teams may not exceed either the 40-man roster or the Salary Cap during the Unrestricted Free Agent draft.

Trading

  • Trades are allowed at all times except for the period starting at 12:01 AM August 1 through the completion the World Series. Trades must be free of contingencies and must take place immediately upon formal league-wide notification of the completed deal.
  • Draft choices for the "following year's" Minor League and Unrestricted Free Agent Drafts (those after the upcoming Minor League and Unrestricted Free Agent Drafts) may not be advertised for, nor solicited, nor traded, until January 1st each year. Draft choices beyond the current draft and following year's draft are not available for trade.

Cuts & Claims

  • Players may be cut and claimed at any time except during the Minor League and Free Agent drafts. 
  • Player claims are awarded on a 1st come, 1st serve basis, FOLLOWING the full 24-hour league-wide posting of their availability. If two or more teams place a claim on the same player at the exact same time, the team with the worst W-L record (Tie Breakers apply if required) shall be awarded the player.
  • All cuts & claims MUST be posted league-wide (IBLpost@yahoogroups.com).
  • All players claimed in-season, including those subsequently traded and players originally on a Week 1 roster later cut and claimed, are automatically released following the completion of the World Series.
  • All players claimed during the regular or post-seasons are signed to a one-year contract that expires at the end of the season, with contract value payable in full and immediately. 
  • Cuts announced at any point of the calendar year cost 50% of the remaining value of the contract, payable in full when the cut is announced to the league.  

Free Agents

  • Any player on an IBL roster whose contract has expired is considered a Restricted Free Agent and becomes eligible for Free Agent Bidding.  
  • Any player not an an IBL roster is considered an Unrestricted Free Agent and may be signed to a 1-5 year contract on a first-come, first-served basis, during any open claim period.
  • Restricted Free Agents may be traded but remain eligible for Free Agent Bidding.

Rosters

  • Teams must carry exactly 25 players on their active roster and 40 players total (not including those on their AAA roster) throughout the regular and postseasons, except during the final month of the regular season, when rosters expand to 40.

  • Once a series begins, the active roster may not change, except in the event of an injury that extends beyond the number of games remaining in the series. 

  • The offseason roster size shall be unlimited until just before the Free Agent Draft when all teams must be at or under 40 players.


GAMES

Setup

  • While the League File should have all the Rule settings, before starting a game verify your "Game Configuration" are set properly, as follows. All rules are for the regular and post seasons unless noted otherwise.

    • In the General Rules setup, these options are turned ON:

      • Catcher Fatigue

      • Display WC Standings

      • Apply Park Effects

      • Rain Delays

      • Replay Reviews

      • Suspensions

      • Draft League LH Pitcher Boost

      • Sacrifice Rules : Using Modern Rules (1954 to present)

    • In the Roster Management setup, these options are turned ON:

      • 25 Opening Day Roster Limit

      • 40 Player September 1st Roster Limit

      • Use Daily Lineups If Available

      • Injuries

      • Max Injury Length = 30 Days

    • In the Usage Rules setup, these options are turned ON:

      • BATTING

        • Over 110%

        • 50%

        • Based on Plate Appearances

        • Exclude if over 500 Plate Appearances

      • PITCHING

        • No Penalty

    • Prorate Usage Rules for Each Playoff Series checked ON

    • In the Miscellaneous Rules setup, these options are turned ON:

      • Show Steal Success %

      • Show Base Runner Success %

      • Display Pitcher Estimated Pitch Counts (EPC)

    • Coaching Decision Clock is set at 10 Seconds

  • Stock stadiums and stadium settings will be used.

Playing & Reporting Games

  • Regular season games may be played head to head, solo versus the computer, or simmed by the commissioner. Postseason games may be played head to head or simmed by the commissioner.

  • The visiting team is responsible for playing each series during the designated playing period. All game files must be emailed directly to the commissioner by the weekly deadline. Only the commissioner can extend a deadline. 

  • The commissioner reserves the right to request another manager, typically the home manager and only if time permits, play unreported games solo versus the computer. Any games not reported on time will be simmed by the commissioner.

Injuries & Usage Rules

  • Player usage will be regulated by the game's fatigue system, in accordance with but not limited to the following general criteria:

    • Batters with less than 500 Plate Appearances have "Reduced Hits by 50%" penalty set at 110% Plate Appearances. 

    • Pitcher usage is controlled by the game's EPC System.

  • Injuries carry over into following weeks, including Playoffs. No injury extends beyond the World Series.

  • The two-week windows for July 4th & Labor Day are to accommodate those holidays only, and are not considered extra days off.

Thanks for the feedback guys!

Salary cap - This is a number I expect to change a bit but do you really think $100 million is too low? First, keep in mind that I don't want All-Star teams. Frank mentions that using a guy's initial salary for up to 5 years won't work but that's intentionally added to promote drafting for the future. One thing I don't like about cap leagues is the unrealistic roster turnover that happens every year as guys trim down to 10-15 guys and essentially redraft the bulk of their team every year. There are many pitfalls with that type of league, not the least of which is that many guys do not buy into a long-term plan for their team because they don't have to...they can just cut and redraft every year and have a contender. That mentality leads to complacency and many guys simply bail if they don't like how their teams panned out in any given season. A system that promotes long-term planning doesn't guarantee all owners will go that route but my nearly 20 years of league experience tell me that most will. My thought is that maybe the first couple of years the cap will seem low but as teams begin to draft true rookies with low initial salaries, and those players begin to prove more valuable in years 2 and beyond, you are now getting more bang for your salary cap buck.

LH Pitcher Boost - so you don't think many teams will be able to produce a lefty-crusher lineup with 24 teams? At what number of teams would you say this setting becomes useful?

Unlimited use for batters with 500 AB - the thought here is that with a tight cap there might be a need for unlimited use. Thoughts?

Pitcher EPC - Not tied to it, just noticed that several leagues out there use it. I'm really trying to get away from strict PAIP type usage restrictions and let the game smack down any player who is overused. Is EPC not the best way to do that for pitchers? It seemed pretty strict to me but I don't have extensive history using it so I'm no authority.

Visiting team playing the series gives the computer a slight advantage by having last at bat. Over the last 17 years, I've been in leagues that do it both ways and the ones that let guys play solo versus the computer always have some ridiculous home-road splits. Those that give the PC manager the last AB tend to have much more reasonable splits.

160 games or 162? 160 is easier to schedule but I suppose adding in 2 more games would not be a major issue.

Two 12-teams divisions - The main point to divisions in real life is to promote rivalries and add more playoff teams...in other words, generate more money. It's actually a really inefficient setup that creates more competitive problems than it solves and causes very good team to miss out on the postseason when a weak division must send someone to the playoffs. Keeping division to a minimum reduces that effect.

Maybe I need to reword the playoffs part because Frank got the impression that 16 teams make it but only 8 make the playoffs.

Starting small - this may seem like the common sense approach but I did that in my football league and the biggest complaint I hear from potential new members is that 16 teams is too small. When we had no cap, it was an all-star league and now that we have a cap, they don't like that alot of "names" are not on rosters. I'm on the fence with this one even now, quite frankly.

Jelicker, Maybe using EPC and curbing results after 110% usage, like with batters, is the better way to go?

Frank, no I don't think my system would undervalue players after five years. The premise, as I mentioned before, is to promote building for the future as a low-risk, high-reward option, thus making the opposite approach (redrafting 25 players to your team every year) less attractive. Again, the option is there should you choose to do it that way but I don't want a league where the "mass redraft" option is the chosen way for most teams. So I'm setting up the rules to promote a long-term approach and reward teams that invest in good, young talent.

Of course, that comes with it's own set of challenges because no young player is ever guaranteed of reaching all-star levels, no matter how hyped they may be as a minor leaguer. So a team GM that "overdrafts" these types of players is going to have a hard time winning on a consistent basis. A reasonable projection for most young players on a five-year contract would be that you would only receive 1-2 years of play at a level that far exceeds the contract value, then the player hits Free Agency and is due a huge pay raise. Guys like Pujols and Mauer are the exception, not the rule. Of the players you mentioned, most, if not all, will not reach a high level of production in the first 3-4 years of MLB service. I think you overvalue them.
