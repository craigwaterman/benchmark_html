shake up your routine menu with a foil pack recipe

Food in foil…it’s just plain fun. The kids will love it and the even bigger bonus (in my opinion) is that fact that cleanup is so easy! No scrubbing pots and pans–just take the food out, then the foil goes into the garbage. These recipes can be made on the grill, over a campfire, or in the oven and we’ve got everything covered–from the main course to snacks and desserts.

Wake up your tastebuds tonight with a delicious dinner of Grilled Barbecue Chicken and Vegetables. This tasty recipe is courtesy of Katerina from Diethood.

Now’s just the right time to enjoy Jaclyn’s Salmon and Summer Veggies in Foil recipe. Be sure to load up on your favorite vegetables of the season. This meal is full of nutrition, not to mention very tasty.

Try these Foil Packet Sweet Potato Tacos for a new twist on a Mexican meal. And don’t forget to add your favorite toppings…cheese, sour cream, salsa or whatever you can imagine.

These Grilled Banana S’mores will certainly satisfy your sweet tooth! For the ultimate experience, Nicole notes to make sure that you get both the banana and toppings in each bite. We found this yummy dessert on 365ish Pins.

Ever tried grilling steak in foil? Jill’s Lemon Herb Steak Foil Packet recipe looks like a great way to try it out. The fresh, lemony flavor is especially perfect for this time of the year.

Burritos for dessert? Of course, when it’s a Chocolate Peanut Butter Campfire Burrito. I must admit…this one had me at “peanut butter”! Make these up ahead of time then throw into the oven or on the grill or campfire for smiles all around.

Click here to find out how to make this scrumptious Grilled Caramel Apple Crunch. Don’t forget to top it off with your favorite flavor of ice cream.

7 secret hideaways made just for kids

As a child, I remember draping a blanket over our kitchen table and making my own little hideout. It was magical–my own space…my own world. If you’ve heard “I’m bored” at your house one too many times since school got out, one of these projects may just be the solution you’re looking for.

They range from a simple draped bed sheet to a more sophisticated PVC pipe construction project. Don’t forget to let the kiddos get in on the action. They’ll love being part of the design crew!

Helen says that her Simple Tent is the perfect place for impromptu tea parties and we couldn’t agree more. An old bed sheet works great for this fort.

We love the beautiful festive look of the Outdoor Pavilion that we found on Ikat Bag. This creative hideaway is made from old edging from her yard that was headed for the trash…recycling at its best!

Carla constructed the perfect place to cozy up with a book. She took her son’s old crib mattress along some sheers to make this comfy reading nook. This little oasis may even encourage a little more reading in your household. You can see all her instructions over on Small + Friendly.

Laura transformed her daughter’s bottom bunk into a fort by simply using a folded twin flat sheet and a tension curtain rod. This No-Sew DIY Fort can easily be reversed by just taking down the rod.

Got a closet that’s not being used? Then with some plywood and a few power tools you can design a warm, inviting play space for your little one. Find out how over on 551 East.  Ultra cute white lights are optional (but totally recommended).

Speaking of magical spaces…click here to find out how you can convert a cardboard box into an awesome Twinkly Light Cave. We found this innovative project on Homegrown Friends.

Holly came up with this DIY Fort Kit to keep her kids busy. The PVC pipe is cleverly color coded making it easier to construct the perfect hideout. Once you’ve put your design together–just drape with blankets or sheets.

homemade granola to enjoy by the handful

Wholesome and delicious…granola is a satisfying treat all by itself but can also be sprinkled on your favorite foods (think yogurt or ice cream)–or pour it right into a bowl for a filling breakfast, the possibilities are endless.

 

Here are a few recipes to get you started on creating a scrumptious snack. The beauty of these recipes is that they are a great starting point for devising your very own custom interpretation (add a favorite ingredient or leave one out–let your tastebuds lead the way!)

 

Stephanie’s recipe for Ginger Peach Granola comes with a warning~you will want to sprinkle this tasty mixture on everything (and be ready to make another batch as soon as you finish the first).

Kelly concocted her version of a favorite cereal and came up with this Banana Coconut Granola–it’s vegan as well as being gluten and nut free. We found this gem on The Pretty Bee.

Chocolate lovers will really dig this Mocha Hazelnut Granola–and by dig, I mean they’ll want to grab the nearest spoon and dig into a bowl of it.

Next up is Joanie’s Butter Pecan Granola…it’s full of flavor boasting mouthwatering ingredients such as roasted pecans, butter, and vanilla.

Calling all fruit fans! It’s time to try the Cranberry Orange Granola recipe that we found over on Cookie + kate. Real orange zest is a key ingredient in this delicious dish.

If Kristy’s recipe for Lemon Blueberry Granola looks like your kind of snack then you’ll want to head over to her blog Keepin’ It Kind.

Here’s an example of a great base recipe–Vanilla Almond Granola. Michelle mentions on her blog that you don’t have to use almonds…you can substitute any nut or fruit that you like.

7 savory homemade salad dressing recipes

You can always tell when a salad dressing is homemade…the difference is amazing! Gone are the preservatives and other (sometimes weird) ingredients in bottled dressing from the store.

These delicious dressings are VERY easy to make and packed with lots of flavor.

Impress your family and friends with Marzia’s 2 Minute Caesar Salad Dressing (they’ll think they’re dining in a restaurant).

Going gluten-free? Then you’ll definitely want to drizzle this Avocado Green Goddess on your next salad. Erin’s recipe is also dairy-free.

Love lemon? Check out the Grilled Lemon and Basil Vinaigrette recipe on Culinary Ginger. Grilling the lemons not only releases the juices, it adds sweetness and a slight smoky flavor.

Jennifer from Crockpot Gourmet believes that life is better with sauces and dressings (by the way the second that!)…and her Zesty Lime Jalapeno Ranch is no exception. You have complete control of the heat factor by adding as much or as little jalapeno as you’d like.

Creamy, tangy, yum overload…just a few choice ways to describe this Creamy Feta and Sun Dried Tomato Salad Dressing.  Head over to Mississippi Kitchen to get the lowdown on this delicious dressing.

In less than 5 minutes you could be enjoying this Toasted Sesame Asian Dressing–it’s full of flavor and minus the added sugar found in many supermarket versions.

Talk about double duty…Ari’s Fat Free Thousand Island Dressing can be used as a dressing, drizzled on sandwiches and burgers, or as a dipping sauce. Her healthy adaptation replaces mayo with greek yogurt.
