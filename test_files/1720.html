What Do Post-Workout Supplements Do?

Whether it’s an hour at the gym, a new record on your daily run, or a really good yoga session, the physical exercise you engage in pushes your muscles to their limits, and it’s only as they rebuild and repair themselves that you get stronger, faster or more flexible. The Bias of the Prohormone Ban – Restricting Some Drugs and Not Others

As many of you may know, in December of 2014, a prohormone ban was signed into law restricting the use of certain substances and the supplements containing them, and having massive implications for the bodybuilding world at large. The legislation restricts the use of prohormones with a similar structure, derivation, and (more importantly) purpose as anabolic steroids. What this means in layman’s terms is that any supplement that is meant to increase muscle mass and testosterone the way a steroid would went off the market as of the moment the bill was passed.

The health and fitness community has been understandably outraged at the news and is questioning the rights of lawmakers to determine what they should be putting in their bodies. After all, what kind of standard allows people to imbibe alcoholic beverages that led to the deaths of 88,000 people a year from 2006-2010 according to the CDC, but doesn’t allow a substance which cannot be positively tied to mortality?

But this isn’t the first time that the government has used flawed logic and bias to make its decisions. Lots of substances are illegal for a reason, but the criterion by which lawmakers classify them and thereby outlaw them has always been somewhat sketchy.

Take marijuana, for example: while the substance obviously has certain side effects, they are generally considered to be somewhat mild (even by medical professionals), especially when compared with other schedule I drugs with which cannabis has been classified. Imagine receiving the same penalty for possessing marijuana as for possessing heroin! The government’s reasons for classifying the drug so inaccurately? Pressure from a bunch of paranoid parents in the ’70s and ’80s and the political agendas of certain lawmakers. Never mind the science.

The same thing can be said of the prohormone ban. While listed as a Schedule III substance, indicating some potential for abuse, some known medical uses, and moderate to low psychological dependence, prohormones have mostly suffered from political pressure and bad press. It’s easy for lawmakers to generalize about the dangers of steroids and act like they are passing laws in the interests of the people, but what about those people’s rights?

Prohormones arguably only pose a threat to the people who use them – unlike legal substances like alcohol and tobacco – and are perfectly safe when used correctly. If you used these prohormones intelligently and followed the appropriate cycles, they only enhanced your body and helped you to achieve fitness goals. The pills carried no greater risk than you might otherwise face just by working yourself so hard. It begs the question: shouldn’t you be able to decide for yourself?

So what does all this mean for you? Well, at present, it’s illegal to buy or sell some of your favorite prohormones and there’s not much to be done about it in the short term. In the meantime, you can do three things:

  1. Keep working out!
  2. Find alternatives that are legal.
  3. Educate people and raise awareness on the topic. Perhaps with some deft campaigning and some well-directed petitions, popular opinion will sway politicians in the right direction.

What to Look for in Fat Burner Supplements

Looking to lose some weight in a safe and natural manner? Fat burner supplements may be the choice for you, but how do you choose which ones to trust? Unless you’re a nutritionist or doctor it can be hard; however, there are some key signs that indicate an effective, useful supplement that will really help you shed some pounds. According to us at Black Diamond Supplements, these include the following:

1.  No unrealistic promises – We all know there is no such thing as the fabled “miracle pill,” and yet many people continue to fall for unrealistic claims anyway. These claims take advantage of our hope that there is an easy solution to our weight problems. There are solutions, but they will generally work gradually and they always work best when paired with sustainable healthy diets and at least moderate regular exercise. In other words, a fat burner supplement that says it can help you lose 20 pounds in 4 weeks is quite possibly correct—as long as you understand that you’ll also need to eat healthy and work out. Fat burners are meant to boost the results of diet and exercise, not replace them.

2.  Ingredients proven to work – Over and over we see ineffective fat burners with unproven or even disproven ingredients being touted as the real thing. In the fitness industry, it can sometimes be hard to see past the marketing hype. But if you look closely, the best fat burner supplements will eventually get down to brass tacks and talk about their individual ingredients and the research behind them. It’s worth reading up on the studies in question or even just visiting the Wikipedia page for each ingredient. This can give you a sense of whether the ingredients in a given supplement really have led to weight loss in controlled studies, and what dosage is needed. Remember, a supplement that contains too small a dose of an effective ingredient is, basically, an ineffective supplement.

3.  High-quality ingredients – The quality of an ingredient is different from whether it works or not. You’ve probably noticed a difference between, say, fresh ground black pepper versus pepper from a plastic shaker. The potency of natural ingredients like herbs and berries depends on many factors, including how they are grown and harvested. The best natural fat burner supplements will include information on their ingredient sourcing among their marketing materials to help you make an informed choice.

4.  A blend of multiple effective ingredients – Remember, lots of fat burner supplements choose one trendy ingredient and stick to it. While this is fine if the ingredient works, there’s no reason you can’t blend several proven winners—and some of the most trusted names in the fat burning industry are those that contain a blend of several powerful ingredients.
