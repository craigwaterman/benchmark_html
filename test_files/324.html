Baking Fails

March 1, 2015March 1, 2015 | camillal33

What do you do when you try to bake a cake to impress your new man, your friends, family or your colleagues, and it fails? 

Try to eat what is, I hope, salvageable, and let it go… (and try not to burst out into song, Idina Menzel style).

I tried to bake an Indonesian layer cake yesterday and disaster descended upon me. Now, it’s not like I’ve never made this cake before. I had, once, at my mother’s home. So, I’m beginning to think that it only worked last time because of my mum…! And I think what’s worse is I felt like one of those annoying contestants on My Kitchen Rules that always say “…but I’ve made this a thousand times before! Why isn’t it working now?!” Yeah.

Normally, my baking fails aren’t that disastrous, I can usually still eat and enjoy them. Take cookies for example. Sometimes I make cookies and they look nothing like the pictures on Pinterest but they taste pretty good. The cake I made yesterday… well, let’s just say that a layer of ‘egg’ as part of a cake isn’t that delicious.

I’ve tried to troubleshoot with my mother about what went wrong, and I still haven’t worked it out. This frustrates me. I want to know where I went wrong, so I don’t make the same mistake. The recipe called for 14 eggs. 14!!! That’s a lot of waste. Too many variables, and ultimately, too many opportunities to make mistakes when making that particular cake.

But out of curiousity, what do YOU do when something you bake doesn’t quite work out? How many goes do you do before you get fed up?

I now have a mild fear when I think of that cake, or any cake where the ratio of butter to eggs is high (to eggs)… so, that’s pretty much any sponge cake! While I may tackle again sometime in the future, I think I’ll go back into the kitchen this afternoon and bake something no-fail, like a chocolate cake or something.

Homemade crumpets

February 16, 2015February 16, 2015 | camillal33

What do you do when you want to put off doing homework on a Sunday, for just a little bit longer? 

Bake of course!

Now, if you came to my home, you’d see firstly, that I have a lot of shoes (I’m sure I’ll talk about that in another post soon enough), and then you’ll notice that I have a lot of craft books, cookbooks and food magazines. I’ve been trying to cut down on what I buy (mainly because I don’t have the physical space) and for some subscriptions, I’ve switched to the e-Mag (Donna Hay and Feast magazine mainly) but I must admit, the e-version isn’t the same as the print version. To be honest, they are two completely different experiences, and until I can afford to buy my own house and have a built-in library, I’ll have to stick to some e-Mags.

When it comes to cookbooks though, I love having the hard copy book. Nothing beats flicking through the pages of a cookbook, salivating at all the images of food… glorious food! Last year, I picked up a copy of Phillippa’s Home Baking. It’s a beautifully presented book. I knew I wanted to buy it before I’d even flicked through it. I’m lucky enough to have sampled some of Phillippa’s goodies, and let me tell you, her baked goods are AMAZING. So I knew that I had to have the book. Plus, it was signed by her (bonus!). I’d encourage you to check out Phillippa’s Bakery.

I’ve always wanted to make homemade crumpets but when it comes to cooking with yeast, I am a tad bit afraid. I’ve baked with yeast before (mainly breads and hot cross buns) with reasonable success so I’m not sure where my hesitation with crumpets came from. Now, Phillippa promises that crumpets are “entry level yeast baking” so how could I continue to resist? I gave them a go, and was pretty pleased with the first go. I decided to make mine a little ‘healthy’ by substituting half of the flour with wholemeal flour, and they tasted just as good as any other crumpet. I think my batter (at first) was a little thick, but I added in a little water, as per Phillippa’s tip, and the crumpets towards the end of the batch were definitely more ‘holey’, as good crumpets should be. Now, before you go and make these, just some forewarning – you will need crumpet or egg rings (I think it’s better to use crumpet rings because they’re taller than egg rings).

I will definitely be making these again, I think they’re going to be part of my special breakfast repertoire!

Crumpets with Honey Butter

makes 6 regular sized crumpets or 12 small ones

Crumpets
175ml milk
175ml hot water
1/2 teaspoon dried instant yeast
225g plain flour
3/4 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon baking powder
butter, for cooking

Honey Butter
125g cultured butter, softened
160g strong-flavoured honey

  1. In a warm bowl or wide-necked jug, mix the milk with the hot water. The resulting liquid should be lukewarm, around 28 degrees C.
  2. Dissolve the yeast in the liquid, then add the flour and whisk together to form a smooth but runny batter the consistency of thick cream. Make sure all the flour is blended through.
  3. Place the bowl or jug in a plastic bag and leave in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours or until the surface is quite bubbly. It may take longer, especially in winter, it will depend on the ambient temperature.
  4. While you wait for the batter, make the honey butter, by creaming together the softened butter and honey in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate until needed.
  5. Take the crumpet or egg rings and grease well with oil or a little butter. Heat a heavy-based frying pan over medium heat. While you wait for the pan to heat up, finish the batter by whisking in the salt and baking powder until well blended.
  6. Grease the pan with a little butter. It’s a good idea to make a test crumpet before proceeding with the rest. Place one ring in the pan and pour in enough batter to reach the two-thirds mark. After a minute or so holes should appear on the surface. TIP: If holes are not forming, the batter could be a little too thick, so whisk in a few tablespoons of warm water. The consistency should be fluid but not runny.
  7. After about 5 minutes the crumpet batter should be set and ready to turn over. Flip with a spatula or egg flip and cook for a further 2-3 minutes or until golden. Remove from the ring and place on a plate. Cover with a tea towel to keep warm.
  8. Repeat with the remaining batter, making a few crumpets at time.
  9. Serve immediately with lashings of honey butter, or just a good quality butter and honey, or any topping you like.

Crumpets will keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week and can be toasted if not eaten fresh. I had one toasted this morning for breakfast and it was delicious!!!

The Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

February 6, 2015February 7, 2015 | camillal33

C is for cookie, that’s good enough for me.

When I’m sick, I crave junk food. Potato chips, ice cream, cookies, chocolate, you name it. Which I know, is not great. In fact, being diabetic, it’s actually diabolical (ok that’s a bit of an exaggeration but you get the drift).

So I thought to myself, what can I make that’s sweet? I keep telling myself that if I make it myself, it can’t be as bad as the cookies you buy – you just don’t know what’s in those… (It’s amazing how I can trick myself).

Lately, I’ve been craving for chocolate chip cookies. I asked myself, what makes an exceptional choc chip cookie? For me, it can’t be too sweet, but it has to have the right amount of crisp and chewiness. Like Mrs Field’s cookies but with a bit more of a crisp to the edges. Oh and a bit of a nutty flavour doesn’t go astray either. Now I haven’t had a great success rate with choc chip cookies. Taste wise, no problem, but texture and shape wise, i just can’t quite get it right. So I’ve been doing some reading, and it turns out that you should let your cookie dough rest i.e. chill it and put it in the fridge or flash freeze it before baking. I was a bit put-off by what I read. I read that for best results, you have to chill the dough for a minimum of 24 hours. 24 hours?! You mean, I had to think and prepare in advance? I will not lie, I am lazy and when I’m sick, the last thing I want to do is prepare cookie dough in advance. Nevertheless, I was determined to make the perfect cookie.

So, putting my rational hat on, for someone that hadn’t given this much prior thought, this makes total sense. Think about cookie dough you can buy from the supermarket. That’s chilled. You just slice and bake. Well, I thought I had to give this a go for myself.

Now I’ve made these cookies before, and while they tasted fabulous, they didn’t look that great the first time round. Now equipped with new information, I thought to give these another go. The result? The best, choc chip cookie I have ever made and tasted (in my humble opinion).

Here’s the danger – when I’m bored, there’s a very good chance that I’ll make cookie dough and just leave in the freezer. You know, just in case. Uh oh…!

With the recipe below, please note that I’ve reduced the sugar amount but I find that they are sweet enough. If you have a real sweet tooth, just add 20g to each of the sugar amounts below.

Bake and enjoy! And try not to eat the cookie dough raw, although, I myself was tempted.

Choc Chip Cookies

makes about 24 cookies 

80g caster sugar
90g firmly packed light brown sugar
115g unsalted butter at room temperature
1 large egg
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
175g plain flour
1/2 teaspoon flaky sea salt or 1/4 teaspoon salt
200g chocolate chips (I used half / half of milk and dark chocolate)
100g walnuts, toasted and chopped

  1. Adjust the oven rack to the top third of the oven and preheat to 150C (fan forced). Line your baking trays with baking paper.
  2. Place the nuts on a tray and toast until golden. Chop up and let them cool for a bit.
  3. Beat the butter and sugars together until smooth. Mix in the egg, vanilla and baking soda.
  4. Stir together the flour and salt, and then mix this into the batter. Mix in the chocolate chips and nuts.
  5. Place the dough in the fridge (at least overnight) or in the freezer until chilled.
  6. When you’re ready to bake, scoop the cookie dough into 1 tablespoon balls and place about 8 balls on each baking tray.
  7. Bake for about 15-18 minutes (depending on your oven and how crispy you want them) or until pale golden brown. Remove from the oven and cool on a wire rack
  8. Store them at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 3 days (if they last that long).

Shake it off, Triple J

January 30, 2015January 31, 2015 | camillal33

And so another Triple J Hottest 100 countdown is done and dusted. Though, I have never seen so much controversy around the countdown. Until now.  

You might have heard that here Down Under, we have a radio station that is ‘not commercial’ and is known to play new, or up and coming artists that may not be featured on mainstream radio. When I say it’s ‘not commercial’, I mean that it’s part of the ABC Network, though it is an FM station. I think it’s quite poignant that I should point out that many songs played on Triple J are also featured on mainstream radio, which I’m sure diehard Triple J fans will argue with me here. Go on, I dare you to.

But I digress. So every year, Triple J holds a countdown to the hottest 100 songs of the year. How this is determined depends on the voters. You can vote from a pre-determined list of songs or you can nominate your own. There was article on Buzzfeed that asked “Why isn’t everybody voting for Shake it Off in the Hottest 100?” stating that it is arguably the biggest song of 2014. There has been a lot of pushback against #TayforHottest100, with haters saying “She’s American”, “it’s pop music, Triple J doesn’t play pop music”, etc. etc. Ok, while you can’t deny that the song is pure pop and the little darling is American but as much as I hate to admit it, it is a damn catchy song. And quite the anthem. Haters gonna hate, fakers gonna fake. You just gotta shake it off. Yes, it’s a damn catchy pop song, by an American but hey, not all the songs in the Hottest 100 are Australian.

So the campaign went viral and I loved participating in it. And I thought, yes, here’s our chance to show those music snobs a thing or two. Come on hipsters, get off your music high horse and accept that potentially, tay tay will make it into the countdown. Well, guess it wasn’t meant to be… So it turns out that Triple J ‘disqualified’ Taylor Swift’s Shake it Off so it couldn’t enter into the Triple J Hottest 100 Countdown. Not cool, Triple J.

I don’t understand what all the fuss was about. Yes, Triple J supports local artists by providing them with a platform to get their music out there, and by letting voters vote for Taylor Swift apparently goes against this very idea, but let’s take a look at the top #10’s of the Hottest 100’s of the past three years:

I did it. I booked my flight to London. 

It’s been a long time coming. I haven’t been on holidays (as in an overseas holiday) since 2011, when my visited my good friend in Shanghai and attended my cousin’s wedding in Jakarta. So you’ll forgive me if I’m just a little bit ecstatic.

I’ve always thought that I’d be the type of person to prioritise travel in my life. Clearly, it’s not, having revealed that my last overseas trip was in 2011. I won’t provide you with a list of excuses… but I will say that when you change jobs every couple of years, it’s challenging to take long periods of time off… let’s just say I never really had a lot of annual leave saved up! While some of my friends will argue that you don’t need a lot of annual leave saved up, I beg to differ – if I’m going to travel halfway across the world, I’ll want to be away for at least 3 weeks. But maybe that’s just me.

So! London! I’ve always wanted to live and work in London, though have never really had the courage to look into it in detail. Maybe that will change this year… maybe not. I’m just really excited about all the adventures I’ll have.

But that’s not all… I’ll also be attending my friend’s wedding on a Greek Island! I’ve never been to Greece or a Greek Island for that matter. But I’ve done a lot of googling (what did we do before google?!) and the pictures of the islands are phenomenal. I’m not the type of person to lie around on a beach but I just might, this once.

I know I have a lot of planning to do but I’m looking forward to it. New places to see, old friends to catch up with, make new memories, what’s not to love? Thankfully, for my birthday, I received a London 2015 guidebook, which will come in very handy. I’ve already dog-eared pages… watch this space as I update you on my planning!

Working from home

January 13, 2015January 13, 2015 | camillal33

Working from home… The most wonderful privilege (or so I thought) one has in their working career. 

I used to think that working from home was only reserved for who I always thought were ‘the A Team’. You know, the ‘favourites’ of the office, the ones that were trusted to actually do work at home. Or perhaps they were the ones that were disciplined enough to do work… That was the way I thought, until one day, it was bestowed on me, “Camilla, maybe you should work from home tomorrow, you get interrupted a lot at work.”

HOORAY. I had finally joined the elite – the trusted, the inner circle… Actually, at the time, I had a really great manager and team that embraced flexible working hours.

I actually really enjoy working from home. Despite the following:

  • You end up working more than a 7.5 hour day… but then you can work around your schedule
  • You end up working ‘straight away’ – I love working in my pj’s, in my bed, but then I find it challenging to actually get up, shower and look somewhat presentable
  • You end up eating more (or maybe that’s just me)… I go looking for snacks to ‘reward’ myself “oh well done me for getting that strategy out, go get a biscuit”
  • You end up working while watching TV (again, maybe that’s just me)… and get a little distracted at times. Cartoons in the AM are just so stimulating for me
  • You end up with a sore back / tight shoulders because your ‘workspace’ isn’t quite as ergonomical as your space in the office

Having said all that, I do like the flexibility of working at home. I like wearing my pj’s while working, I like that no one can see me, or judge me while I play Country Grammar (that’s my jam, man) while writing up a report.

I hope that everyone gets to experience the joy of working from home. While it’s slowly becoming more and more socialised and accepted by our workplaces, this needs to be done at a faster rate. Maybe then I’ll have the courage to ask my manager to work from home more often.

The Globes – it’s about the accolade rather than fashion faux pas

January 12, 2015January 13, 2015 | camillal33

Ah, the Golden Globes, and the most important part of the night – the red carpet parade. 

I’ll be honest. I’ve never been one to watch it, read the news about it, follow live twitter feeds on it. Mainly because of the time difference, and a small part of me can’t be bothered. I do though, like seeing all the pretty dresses the women wear. It fulfills the little girl in me.

What does bother me is how people (yes, I know they are celebrities, but they, at the end of the day, are just people too) are judged by what they wear on the red carpet. This year, I even heard on the news “and xxx sported a beard and xxx wore xyz” (see, I can’t even tell you the detail…) or “there was a lot of bare skin at this year’s Golden Globes”

Who freaking cares if a man has a beard or wore a blue tux? Or if a woman decides to wear a cut out dress, or one that may not ‘be in fashion’ right now. Why are they scrutinised for their fashion choices? Maybe they really liked what they wore. Or was paid a lot of money to wear what they wore. They should be free to wear what they want to wear. Or perhaps it comes with the celebrity tag.

Or maybe it was just a slow news day.

Shouldn’t the awards night be a celebration of talent? Of beautiful, moving or entertaining films? Of people that bring stories to life? Instead, we receive immense pleasure from celebrities’ fashion faux pas.

Guess it’s probably the only time when we see them ‘down’. That leaves me feeling icky.

2014 was so last year. Hello 2015!

January 1, 2015January 13, 2015 | camillal33

I’ve been doing a bit of browsing… mainly on social media, searching hastags such as #NewYears, #NewYearsResolution #NewYearsEve. 

What have I found you ask?

I’m going to be honest. It’s all the same shit. Everyone reflecting over 2014, thinking about ‘what could have been’, regrets they have, things they wish they had done, etc, etc.

While I don’t have an issue with reflecting (I think it’s a very healthy way to lead your life), I do sort of have a gripe with doing it on the 31st December, every year. How come we don’t make the effort to reflect and learn as we go? Now, I’m no perfect peach, because I’m guilty of doing the exact same thing I’m griping about. But that’s about to change. No more reflecting at the last minute, lessons should and will be learned throughout the year. No more regrets, sure, wallow in it at the time, and then move on.

So, instead of me writing about how 2014 was such a massive year for me, and how emotionally and physically draining it was and how, as a result, I’ve changed so much, I’m going to post about how different and amazing 2015 will be.

I will (in no particular order):

  • Be 30 this year (eep!)
  • Make more time to sing, including going to karaoke
  • Practise the guitar more often. Bring on the calluses!
  • Bake more
  • Make time for friends and family
  • Continue my journey towards work/life balance
  • Travel to Greece to attend my friend’s wedding. I’ll tack on Switzerland and the UK too (hopefully!)
  • Graduate from my graduate design studies by September (if all goes well and I pass all my subjects)
  • Work out a way for me to do some sort of free-lance work. Even if I only get paid pennies, I don’t care
  • Be the maid of honour at my sister’s wedding
  • Potentially play ‘tour guide’ when my family from overseas come for my sister’s wedding

So, quite a lot in store for 2015. I am very much looking forward to what this year brings. This list is only of the things that I know for certain. I may not achieve all the above, but I can only try. I’m sure there is so much more in store for me, though I don’t know of all those things… I can only hope that I’ll be ready to receive the amazing things planned for me this year.

Those that meet with me on a regular basis, I throw down the challenge – please do follow up with me and ask me how I’m going with these things. Hold me accountable!
