Inside MFW

Prada
Miuccia Prada
“She likes obsessions”
Though Miuccia Prada was not present at her S/S 16 catwalk this year, due to a family death, her collections spoke volumes on her behalf. Boxed jackets and Prince of Wales wools were partnered with sheer organza and an overload of embellishment, in a range of earth tones and pop art colours. A trend of retro-chic has appeared at Milan Fashion Week, and we are smiling with excitement; Drop waist dresses mimicking the 20’s, matched with the 60’s skirt suit, finished with futuristic, plastic accessories and appliqués. Layering these to create something completely new is something I find particularly wonderful about the collection, and I’d do anything for a pair of those striped go-go boots! Step into Prada’s time machine and transport yourself to wherever you see fit!
Emporia Armani
Giorgio Armani
“A new feminine figure” and “determined form of grace”
Soft palettes and every kind of texture: this is my kind of collection. Emporia Armani have hit the nail on the head, for catching this season’s trends and making it their own. No Pussybow’s were seen in their collection, a trending piece in the SS 16 season, but instead, beautifully embellished neck scarves that presented an elegant sophistication. Bermuda shorts were styled with blazers and jackets, dresses were softly tailored for an easy breezy feeling of effortless chic.
Gucci
Alessandro Michele
“Treasure chest of aesthetic references”
After taking over from Frida Giarinni this year, Michele followed the success of his A/W show. This collection looked to further define the Geek Chic aesthetic, whilst still embracing the trademark Gucci glitz and glam and principle colours red and green. This season everything was amped up- colour, sequins, embroidery and accessories to die for. Pussy bow blouses were a big feature with berets and big glasses to match. Still embracing the 70’s trends, the brand gave it an update introducing 80’s glamour to the mix- an interesting hybrid which further showcases Michele’s dynamic work for the brand.
Dolce & Gabbana
Domenico Dolce, Steffano Gabbana
“Italia is love”
Let me take a selfie! Dolce & Gabbana got a 21st Century Update as they paraded the runway; phones in hand, taking selfies with everyone and everything. A celebration of one’s culture and heritage is always welcomed especially when it is adorned so beautifully as D&G showcased. The brand has become known for it’s homage to Italy and we never tire of seeing their latest update on the trend. The collection celebrated everything Italian with Peruvian pom poms and rivera chic featuring. Embroidery was detailed and colourful rejoicing the bright colours and fast paced lifestyle of Italian culture. 50’s sundresses and tunics were in keeping with a very feminine structure. It has us getting itchy feet for Tuscan landscapes and summer walks in Sicily.
Fendi
Karl Largerfeld and Silvia Fendi
“I just wanted to create a soft-lined silhouette to sit against the concrete garden (of the austere grey catwalk).”
Fendi has an eye for detail, and their S/S16 collection presented this in yet another masterful way. The tailoring was inspired by Tudor and Victorian dress, with beautiful strength, femininity and modern twists. Ballooning pantaloons and shirts were presented with snake skin outerwear and handbags, which were beautifully crafted in a wonderful combination of rich and vibrant colours. The coral high neck shirt and deep red woven leather mini skirt epitomised this combination perfectly, giving a soft but defiant feel. Similarly, this idea of the women appearing very feminine was contrasted, as Largerfeld mentioned, with the ominous grey catwalk. Once again, designers are reimagining retro-chic.
Versace
Donatella Versace
“This is a collection for the way women live their lives today mixing tailoring with sportswear and effortless glamour”.
Forget the Balmain army it’s all about the Versace brigade. Versace is known for embracing all that is sexy and confident but this collection got an edgy update. Think chiffon dresses in camouflage, jackets with animal print and a LOT of military jackets. Donatella’s show was centered around equality and we are ready, clad in our Versace armour, to follow them into battle.
