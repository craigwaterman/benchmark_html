Welcome Home, baby!

May 20, 2015 by kbridgwood

So after 9 long months of carrying your little angel, eating for 2 (or more), you finally have your bundle of joy all wrapped up in your arms, ready to go home! Uh-oh!!

On the very first day we brought our baby home, we must have sat in silence for about an hour with a look of trepidation on our faces; in fact for the first two weeks, my husband and I felt as though we had been given an egg to tend to. One wrong move and the baby would crack and fall to pieces.
I have to admit that before I got pregnant I thought that I would handle things completely differently, with a pinch of salt! Err… Yeah right! For the first week I had the paediatrician on speed dial and must have called him every time the baby so much as farted. To be honest, I was completely neurotic. Let’s get real, less than 7 days earlier, a baby had been plucked from my nether region, I was waddling around like a duck and I was an around the clock food dispenser running on batteries.

Babies are smart, don’t let their small stature and wrinkled up mushy faces fool you; it’s almost as though they have inbuilt sensors that detect when you’re just about to….fall……asleep…..and then…… WAAAAAAAAA! The same goes for when you want to eat or go to the toilet! In fact, if you manage to squeeze in a shower that lasts longer than 3 minutes, consider yourself lucky!

I have to admit that for the first two and a half months, it was extremely trying. Nothing we had heard or read could have prepared us for the responsibility of taking care of this precious life that was completely dependent on us. Our whole lives had shifted and was now revolving around this 3kg mini human. It certainly brought out the best and the worst in us at times (What is it with men and their inability to hear a baby crying at night? Or is that another symptom of selective hearing?).

I guess what I am trying to say is that, raising a child is HARD; yet so rewarding. If there is one piece of advice I can give to my fellow new mummies (and daddies) which is something that I am also learning now, is to try and enjoy every moment of being a parent. I feel as though we (women mostly) worry too much, we constantly scour the internet and always come across an article that makes us believe we are doing something wrong! Let’s not forget that we were born in the age of no internet (aka the Stone Age!) and our parents seemed to do just fine without all the gadgets, rules and regulations that are imposed on us today.

Sooooooo, have patience, and if singing a lullaby puts your husband to sleep instead of the baby, kiss him goodnight, cover him over and enjoy some extra cuddle time with your little one.

Keep smiling!

Lots of love,
Kiki xxx

You’re not sick, your pregnant!!!???

May 16, 2015 by kbridgwood

If you’re  anything like me, those words will grind right through you. For over the first half of my pregnancy I had to remind myself that it was all worth it, every stomach churn, every nauseating swell, and every inevitable undesired date with the toilet bowl; would result in the birth of our gorgeous baby.

Now, like me, for those first time soon to be mums who are reading this and thinking ‘’Morning sickness, will it ever end???’’ I can honestly tell you that everything I read about the 12 week mark was, for want of a better word….bullshit! I felt sick well into my 5th month, I was starting to think that the feeling would never go away, I was destined to have vomit exiting through my nose (yeah, admit it, all sorts of stuff comes out of you!) till the very end.

Being pregnant is hard work! (Must be the reason why all those Disney Princess’ marry Prince charming and BAM the baby miraculously appears in the sequel, with said Princess dancing around singing; birds and butterflies fluttering around, without so much as a stretch mark or a bag under her eye!

I must have tried every remedy possible; nothing worked 100%, after all you are growing a baby and that’s going to take a lot out of you, these are the things that worked for me

  • Seasickness bands (I know, weird, but they actually help alleviate the nausea and can be purchased from any chemist/pharmacy.
  • Ginger. Grated into fizzy water, ginger biscuits or even ginger tea worked a treat, especially during the day whilst at work.

At the end of the day, there is no ‘cure’ for morning sickness, ‘’you’re pregnant, not sick’’ remember?

When the morning sickness finally subsided, around my 24th week, I thought to myself, it’s all uphill from here, surely that pregnancy glow will settle in (it did!)I was feeling great, my skin was bright my hair was like a Vidal Sassoon advert, my nails were unbreakable; my bump had really started to show, I was like Joan of Arc; Victorrrryyy!! All was well…….and then the constipation set in!

Now, I am about to get graphic; There I was bent forward, sitting on the toilet, pushing and then not pushing (For some reason I was worried I may crap the baby out!) Phone in one hand addressing my Doctor on call (Google) about what I should do (of course baby’s health is number one so laxatives and the like were completely out of the question) when I came across this piece of advice:

‘’Using a sterile glove, insert you index and middle finger gently up your anus and slowly massage the feces, breaking it down into smaller parts’’

WTF?? I’ve had about 3 massages in my 30 years and here you are telling me to sensually massage my poop? Charming! Needless to say, that wasn’t an option for me and thankfully after consulting my Gyni I used an over the counter stool softener, it took a while to kick in, but once it did…let’s just say my singing was not restricted to the shower only!

Well folks, I think I will end it there for now, I hope you enjoyed reading my first blog! To all those of you who took the time to read it, THANKYOU!

Make sure to follow me on Twitter  like my Facebook page.

Till next time!

Love,

Kiki
