Small Business Insights: Use Finance to pay Outstanding ATO Debts

Posted by Ella Bryan in News | 0 comments

If you didn’t already know it, ATO debts are commonplace within the small business community. It may not be something business owners willingly disclose, but those of us working with SMEs know it is more common than you might think.

And no, the majority are NOT due to bad management or fraud.

There are countless reasons why businesses get behind with the ATO, from personal (e.g. key family member in the business gets critically ill and things fall through the cracks while s/he is treated), to business (e.g. a strong business has a dispute with a large customer, despite winning costs, the cash flow impact is significant), to human error (e.g. the accountant submitting the BAS significantly over-states revenue), and the list goes on.

In most cases, a payment arrangement is agreed with the ATO and the business has a path back to stability. But these payment arrangements can extend for long periods, and until you are free of the debt, your bank will be very unlikely to approve new finance.

But what if your business needs finance to take advantage of new growth opportunities? Or you just want to repay the ATO debt and move on?

There are a number of non-bank finance providers that have experience in funding businesses with ATO payment arrangements in place. This type of finance can generally be used for any business purpose, so it is the business owner’s choice whether to use funds to repay the ATO debt, or invest directly in the business.

The most common type of funding used in these situations is short-term unsecured finance. This is usually in the form of short-term Invoice Finance or Factoring, and Unsecured Business Loans. These types of Alternative Finance can be very useful if you have ATO arrears and need funding, but there are a few things to consider when choosing what’s best for your business.

Flexibility

Do you have to use the finance to repay the ATO debt? If the finance provider is concerned about your ATO position, they could make this a requirement. Make sure you understand this. In most cases, assuming you’re up to date with the payment arrangement, you should have flexibility to use the funds for any business purpose. Most importantly, if you want funding to invest in a new business opportunity (e.g. that will significantly grow profits), tell the funder that’s your intention and be clear about what restrictions or oversight they might have.

Cost

The reason why many businesses have ATO debt is because they think it’s a cheap form of finance, which it is until (a) the ATO decides they want their money back or (b) you need more funding and suddenly can’t access your bank credit line. When considering Alternative Finance, be aware that if you have an ATO debt, your business is considered to be more risky and pricing will reflect that. Choose a finance product that best suits your business and try to avoid fees and charges you don’t need to pay.

Timing

Ideally you will only need Alternative Finance for a short period. You either repay your ATO debt and get the business back on track, or put the money into growth and repay the ATO debt through increased sales. Try to avoid taking on new finance that will create long term obligations, for example, don’t sign up to a 12-month invoice finance facility if you only need it for a couple of months. Your business will benefit from using Alternative Finance selectively. Ideally within 12 months, your business will have reached a point where it can refinance with a bank, or even better, doesn’t need finance at all.

Key Takeout: For businesses with ATO payment arrangements in place, there are finance options available to help your business grow, manage cash flow and even repay the ATO debt. Your bank is unlikely to want to help, so don’t be surprised if that’s the case. Investigate other options – the Alternative Finance sector in Australia is growing, and there are a number of providers who are experienced in funding businesses in these situations.
