9 Powerful Comedian Ali Baba Inspiring Motivational Words That Will Change Your Life

Atunyota Alleluya Akporobomerere, stage name Ali Baba, is a Nigerian stand-up comedian.
Listed below are some of Ali Baba’s most popular selected message . See further below for more info...

#1. "Your body radiates and is taken over by certain body heat. These are some of those things that sophisticated spy gadgets can pick up miles away. However, some of these can be sensed by some wild animals before they strike. The bulk of these contains a good quantity of Adrenalin. You can see the neutral guy... That's why they are called cold blooded people... So if you want to know what you are feeling like... Look at this picture. Your body vibes tell all."
#2"One interesting novel back on the days was THE BEAUTIFUL ONES ARE NOT YET BORN. The title would fool you. But the novel will enrich you. One of my uncles in AT&P, Sapele, told me it was a very good book. That it balanced out one of the fourth legs of his unbalanced table. What about you do you think qualifies you as beautiful? If you know it, enhance it. Continue to increase the number of those other USPs... Social intelligence. Courtesy. Hygiene. Dressence. Temperament. Understanding. Home engineering. Emotional intelligence. Sense of humor. Skin bleaching, not. Trained voice tone and texture. Independence. Tolerance. Self grooming. Education... That's all what count in the overall scheme of things. Remember, those beautiful things that draw people and may hold anyone, initially, are called FIRST IMPRESSION... The other very beautiful things that follow that are the beautiful anchors that create the LASTING IMPRESSIONS. Same thing applies for men... Broad shoulders don't make a caring man. Six packs don't mean generosity. 7' 2 inches don't pay bills unless in NBA, even then, it's not a given. So, let's just say we are all beautiful and handsome... We all try in every way to upgrade to be better than we used to be..."
#3"BREAK UPS AND FLARE UPS When you break up with anyone, the way they handle the break up is a true reflection of who they really are. There is nothing like it was the break up that brought the worst out of them. Trust me, It was always inside that person. The capacity to anger and consequently do harm by some people, is not as a result of love gone sour. It was always a covered up rot and disaster waiting to happen. It's been revealed by several researches. The way some people handle separation often shows why you left them. Some men do not know how to handle "NO". In fact they can lose it if she adds "What part of NO do you not understand?". I heard of a man who bathed his ex with acid because she refused to reconsider. Another posted a lady, newly married, from Lagos to Bayelsa state. She was lucky to have met an ED of another bank who had been looking for her. One lady, in Lekki Phase 1, went down stairs after her bobo said he couldn't continue with the relationship. Broke the windscreen of his G63 and cut the seats of his Porsche Cayenne with a razor blade. By the way, she had been driving the Porsche for nearly 5 months. (By now some of the Lekki people know who I am talking about) Then left with the key and threw it across the Lekki bridge. I know some stupid people will quickly say, hell hath no fury like a woman scorned.... But hey! The way you react to a break up not only defines you, but could foreclose on whether you guys get back together or make up. I am one of those who does not believe that your jealousy is enough reason to go do something that you think will show you ARE MADLY IN LOVE. Because what it really does is give the other person more reason to stay away from you. Regarding this post, Terrence's Ex just justified his reasons to have left her. No one should try and tell me it's because she is hurt. You can not force people to love you. It's no crime to be pained by a break up. But to begin to do things to pull down your Ex, deform, abuse, rubbish and generally cause harm to the person you once claimed to love is absolutely unacceptable. It just showed you were not in love. If it's over, get over it, grow up and move on."


#4"Father Lord, I pray, my evening shall be greater than my morning. My efforts in life shall not come to nothing. Wherever there is a casting down, I will experience a lifting up. Not by my power but by your grace Lord! I accept my weaknesses and pray for your strength in my moments of vulnerability. And to all women I say, I believe in Luke 1:42, and pray your joy shall be full. Forgive all our forgivable weakness and human failings.... Only the forgivable ones. May our (men) story that will make our face make the front cover of an iconic magazine not be one that will make us cover our face in shame. NO is NOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO! Even if it is whispered. Even if she is your wife. Even if she is your lover. Even if you spent so much on her. Even if it looked like you THOUGHT you saw a green light. Even if you think you are justified, just know you are not. And just in case you need to be told, trust me, if you ask nicely, some sisterhood will welcome you to their neighborhood. Just ask... Nicely. And if you ask and it's a NO, then it's not your Neighborhood. Breaking and entry is still a crime."

#5 "Saw this picture yesterday and as I was looking at it, my wife said "this your head go burst one day! What are you seeing again in that picture?" So I told her like 3... She said share it before you will now say you forgot. So on behalf of my wife, these are the things this picture tells me... 1. We all have one. 2. We all have a life span. 3. You can have it work in low current or full current. 4. When it's not powered by NEPA, you either find alternative power or wait till NEPA restores your power. 5. You can use it to light the path for others 6. You can increase it from 20watts to 1000watts. 7. You can have many switches that turn it on, but just ensure its on. 8. If your source of power is irregular and unreliable, you will blow your mind (in a bad way) 9. Your ideas, dreams, expectations, all dwell here... Your heart is just a regulator. 10. This is your CPU, your heart is just a monitor 11. Many things will come in here, till when you turn it on, that's when people will know how bright or not so bright you are! 12. Some bulbs are very bright but don't shine far. Some are big but shine less. Some may look good but won't last. 13. You can see through some bulbs and tell whether they are ok. That way you don't waste your time thinking if you power it up, you will get some light. Some you have to shake to know they are gone!!! 14. No matter the capacity of the bulb, if you do not switch (empower) it... It's just a bulb... Not a light. So empower it to enLIGHTen! 15. Sometimes you need a Chandelier to reflect the light you generate, so it can reach a lot more places... (twitter, Instagram, YouTube,...) just spread the light. 16. When your light is brighter than some people's own they will ask you to dim yours, why should you? You are not on de same lane naaa? Ok, dim it, let them pass and turn it UP!!! 17. If the bulb can not handle full current, it will burn out. Only engage in what it can manage. (tbc) 18. The Bible says let your light shine... NA your brain be that! 19. Because it's your brain. No one will use it for you. 20. "It's a thing of the mind"... Is a phrase that says you have a thought process."
#6"If you think saying either of this makes it a done deal, you will continuously have problems in any of your relationships that has any of both elements. In fact, when you claim to have any of those, you either continue to prove you do or give room to make one party in that relationship doubt the authenticity of your claim. I hope you know the green (LOVE) makes you grow and the red stops you in your positive emotional development. The funny thing is we all know these things... You don't need me to tell you. I battle everyday to see that my confession of love stays that way. But I can not control what the other person will do to make me stop showing the love I confess. Guess that is why it's called a two way street or better still 50/50 love,,,"
#7 "It takes two to make love work. But the work takes a lot of things. To some, what will make them fall head over heels in love is the same thing that will turn off another person. Check this out... Emeka is a banker... He loves Mabel. She is decently dressed. Would be a wonderful mother with how she cares for kids that are not hers. She is not in his face all the time. She is quiet yet makes very educated and informed comments. Gainfully employed. Very mature. She doesn't like it but he keeps spending on her. Sadly, Mabel doesn't like, not even love Emeka. Because beyond the money, he can't give her the Big O! Tosin on the other hand loves Emeka, she has done everything you need to do to get him to make her his wife. She cooks. Romantic. Let him have his way. Buys him gifts. Calls him always... But Emeka knows she's in it for the money. She has no source of livelihood yet Lives large. She is loud. She is flippant in her utterances. Doesn't show she had core family values. Relationships like these abound everywhere. A lot of people think. ONCE SOMEONE SAYS "l love you" THEN ITS ALL THEY NEED TO DO. Sorry to disappoint you. I stand to be corrected, when anyone says I love you, it's an offer on the table... From then on, the offer, if accepted has to be proven, defended, amended, compromised, reexamined, upgraded,... And if it stands the test of time, can be cemented into a marriage. And even that, as we all know, marriage is a higher level of trials. And if the "i love you" is tested and fails on different platforms, it can not be forced. The problem with us is that, even when we sometimes see that there is no miracle that can justify the "I love you" anymore, we just hang in there till it becomes hate. I have tried to talk to a couple I thought SEEMED PERFECT FOR EACH OTHER, so as usual i was pained to hear they could not work things out. Up until, I heard the details of the relationship, did I realize my hope for them was a pipe dream. Her parents made her marry him because he got her pregnant. And beyond sex, they had nothing in common. Love can't be forced. It's either natural or you manage it as best as you can. "I LOVE YOU" IS NOT CAST IN STONE."
#8"I have over the years observed that many people who EARN N30 will want to enjoy the lifestyle of someone who HAS N300,000. Please note that I purposely put EARN & HAS, in caps. In the the use of the word EARN, I also did not say "earned"... I said EARN. As for the HAS... It is to make you know that it's already in the kitty. These are the Rioke whobare not looking to have. They already have. These are the owners of wealth, that some are looking to have. Guess that's why it's distinctly called the haves and have nots. Sometime in 2010, one very rich man in Nigeria, was called by a new account officer in a bank acquired by one of the top 5 banks, to inform him of regularizing his account that had been on sundry for over 1 year. He had lodged a N35m cheque through an account officer and FORGOT about it. So I told him how can he forget he had that amount in an account. And he told me, the true mark of wealth is to have and not feel a loss of any, except life! Everyone should work to achieve wealth and comfort. We do not all have the same capacity and opportunities. I will not ask Dangote today why he has 2 private jets... He has EARNED IT! That's the goal for all of us... To be continued You earn 120k a month, because the lady next door who is a chartered accountant & manager earning 2m monthly wore human hair, you SEF wan wear am. After all wetin she get wey you no get abi?... Dey there oooo! YOU GO SOON THIEEEEF! Finally, you saw a guy in the club wearing a 250k jeans and soon as you received your 300k salary, you bought it. You had better call Dbanj... #kokoGarriThingz. Okpolo eye no be open eye!!! If you are new in the game, make you go pay your tithe!!! Cause you dey dey there dey hate me while I'm making my money!.. These days, you see a graduate Fresh from NYSC camp, jobless, brimming with hope, and he is looking for (not thinking of)?a Duplex in Lekki phase 1 and an SUV... As the Instagram picture posted shows, everything NA step by step. Joshua took over from Moses! Period!"
#9 "It is not a crime to ask to be helped. The problem with us Nigerians, is that we think we are all that. Learning has no age limit. There are many people who learn one thing or the other from people half their age. If you are going into a project, that you necessarily have to ask a younger person who has been in the business before you, and knows where the bodies are buried, don't be ashamed to ask for the expert advice that will help you grow in your own business. I was talking with an aburo yesterday and it hit me. Many young ones don't know they inspire us. And I am one person who says it to them to encourage them further. Adebola, Wildchild, sorry, Ik, BankyW, OscarInThecity, Chude, TejuBabyface, Ay, Abinibi, (in spite of his craze), Toke, FlashSultan, Yaw, Ayo Animashaun, Lolo, Timi, Basket, Noble, N6, McPrince, Helen Paul, Ushbabee, Akpororo... These are a few of the many who inspire me daily. Get off your high horse, if you need young people's help, ask for it. It's your load. As the Italians say, "Eleru gberure!" Everybody will answer their father's name. But someone must call your Father's name for you to answer it. I see nothing wrong in asking for young people to help when I think they can. I have been called by Professors to come teach topics in marketing, self development, business management, career enhancement... in their classes, and I don't even have a PHD! It's not a crime to be helped by a younger person. Don't let the chip on your shoulders rob you of the value proposition the youths of today have. Let me rephrase, don't let the chip on your shoulders rob you of the value proposition that SOME youths can bring to the table. I had to say some... Because many are just.. Never mind. Think of it like you are 65, and on a flight to Abuja. The pilot is 32... As long as you get to the destination, his age doesn't matter in this circumstance. Caveat Emptor: Senator Yerima this does not include you!"
