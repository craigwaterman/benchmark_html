Organix argan oil Vs Macadamia oil

I have been hunting down the perfect hair oil to stop my hair from getting dry and brittle. Oraganix renewing argan oil of Morocco was suggested to me by the hair company which I order my hair from. I have tried a few different hair oils but this has been the best by far. After washing and conditioning my hair over night I used the Organix renewing argan oil of Morocco the following morning I could see I difference in my hair instantly, my hair was no longer frizzy and the ends was not so dry anymore. The texture is quite thick and over use will leave your hair greasy but after finishing the bottle and going back to get some more I discovered the Organix Macadamia oil for dry styling, this oil has just as good of a result as the argan oil, however the texture is a little bit lighter so it is better for a day to day use.

Garnier body, Intensive 7 days 

 The smell of the mango seed oil in this body moisturiser is so beautiful and refreshing, I use this every morning after showering. It also contains L-BIFDUS which was " inspired by probiotics found in yogurt, studies have revealed its capability to reinforce the skins protective layer" claims Garnier (2014)
 The consistency is light and is easy to spread, it isn't very greasy so when you leave the house you would not attract every particle of dust in the air which I sometimes find with heavier creams. It also leaves a nice shimmer on the surface of the skin, leaving you with a glow.

 This is on special offer at the moment in superdrug for £2.32 instead of £3.49.
Bio Oil 

All hail Bio Oil, this is good for scars, stretch marks, uneven skin tone, ageing skin and dehydrated skin. I use this head to toe every night from my face to body. Bio Oil contains:
  1.  Calendula oil, this is not only soothing for the skin but it is also helps to protect and restore damaged skin.
  2. Lavender is an essential oil which does not get enough credit, when someone thinks of lavender they are automatically thinking relaxing and old ladies, but lavender is a multifunctional oil, it is also great for acne, insect bites and scarring. 
  3. Rosemary oil is probably the most stimulating of the essential oils in there, rosemary is also a great essential oil for acne and skin irritations. 
  4. chamomile is very healing and calming to skin.
Bio oil also contain Vitamin A and E. Vitamin E is in just about everything on the high street, vitamin E is good for the skin and can help prevent signs on ageing. Vitamin E is not synthesised by the body so using it directly onto the skin could prove to be beneficial.
Vitamin A is all over the beauty industry, in Bio oil they have used Retinyl palmitate which is a skin conditioning agent.

 Bio oil is also on offer at the moment in boots.

Masters Colors - Nomad Pen

 Last but definitely not lest is  for a part of the body which we all forget to take of, are nails. This Cuticle pen from Masters colors, is easy to carry around in your handbag. It softens you cuticles and encourages nail growth. it contains sweet almond oil which protects the skin from loss of hydration and it also is extremely nourishing. It also contains Shea butter which is a rich emollient and it absorbs easily into the skin leaving your cuticles soft.
