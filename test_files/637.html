WHAT YOU NEED:

Bone-in chicken breasts (we made about six pounds)
Salt and pepper to taste
Mexican chili powder
Vegetable Oil — about 1.5 cups for 5 pounds chicken
Onions, diced
Garlic, minced
Corn tortillas
Shredded cheese

Toppings:

More shredded cheese
Lettuce, finely chopped
Tomatos, diced
Sliced avocados
Lime, sliced

WHAT TO DO:

Boil the chicken in water with a pinch or two of salt for about two hours, until chicken is fully cooked and tender. Â  Let cool, and then shred finely by hand, then toss in chili powder, salt, and pepper to taste. Â Preheat your oven to broil.

In a large frying pan or wok, sautee the garlic diced onions until translucent. Â Then add the shredded chicken to the pan, mixing vegetable oil according to appropriate texture (you don’t want the chicken to be too dry or too oily). Â Use salt and pepper to taste, and toss in dried oregano. Â Afterwards, transfer the chicken into an oven-safe pan, like a casserole dish, and drizzle shredded cheese on top. Â Broil in the oven for ten minutes, until the cheese is bubbling.

In the meantime, prepare the tomatoes, lettuce, limes, and avocados for toppings. Â Heat corn tortillas over the open flame on your stove, two at a time, as if they were one tortilla. Â Flip them with caution until the bread puffs slightly, and wrap them in a warm towel until all are complete. Â Remove chicken from the oven, and serve.
