Is It Worth It To Make Almond Butter Like PB2?

By Jen in Food October 1, 2015

If you’re not already familiar with PB2, I strongly recommend adding it to your clean & healthy eating arsenal.  PB2 is a peanut butter …concept.  It’s a bit weird, but if you add water to peanut powder (and nothing else – no oil, no sweetener), you’ll get something like peanut butter but is much lower in calories and fat.  Meanwhile, you’ll still get the same amount of fiber and a manageable amount of sodium.  You do lose some protein, but if you’re trying to get your protein needs met by peanut butter alone you’re doing it wrong.

Since I’m not 12, I don’t eat too much peanut butter.  But I do keep the powder around for the occasional nut butter and toast with banana that has been a snacking godsend.

Peanuts, however, aren’t a power food like almonds.  They’re one of the least healthy nuts around.  However, I pick up a giant bag of almonds every few months and toast them up whenever I like.

The other day, I was ready for my PB2 toast with banana and looked in the pantry to find nothing but beans, tortilla chips, and questionable soup.  And almonds. So I got to thinking… what if you could make an almond powder like PB2 and get the same perks without the commercial processing? I set to work on this yesterday.

It started, of course, with the Vitamix blender.  The ultimate blender that can puree an iPhone (untested).  I got this thing as a gift because kale wouldn’t liquify in our old bullet-style blender.  I tossed in some raw almonds, powdered them up, added some water and poof!

Raw almond powder.

It was revolting.  This disgusting raw almond butter, I decided, had too much moisture to handle more moisture.  It might have worked had I added a drop of honey or something, but instead I scrapped the raw idea and toasted up the almonds.  Even if the experiment failed, I’d at least get some toasted almonds out of the deal.

Nom, nom, nom.  I tossed them into the blender and created a fine toasted almond powder that was satisfyingly dry.

A later version was even more powdered, so you might want to kind of ignore what you see here and run that blender until your powder is as fine as flour.  Or… you know… as fine as you can get it.  You can always take a mortar & pestle to it and grind it down, too.

Here’s a before and after the mortar & pestle so you can see the difference (before = left). Pardon the blur. But you should be able to see how the almond powder is more fine.

After I added water, the consistency was like so…

Okay, but not perfect.  So I toasted some more almonds and left them in the blender longer.  I got the powder I was after this time (not shown).  The flavor was kind of dull, so I added the tiniest amount of salt and, for shits and giggles, a drop of honey.

The flavor is spot on. So I put it on toast and did it up right.

I found that I needed more powder to have enough Almond2 to cover the toast.  It also need a bit of flavor because by itself it was rather bleh.  Here’s the recipe.

Homemade Almond Butter like PB2

Ingredients

1 c Raw Almonds
Salt to taste
1/4 t honey

Directions

  1. In a blender, grind up the almonds into the finest powder you can manage.  Use a mortar and pestle if needed after blending.
  2. Store powder until needed in air-tight container.
  3. Put 3T of almond powder in a small bowl and add 3 teaspoons of water, or however much you need for desired consistency.  Add a drop of honey (equivalent to about 1/16 of a teaspoon) and a teeny, tiny pinch of salt.
  4. Serve.

As for the nutrients, here’s the breakdown.

Nutrient Homemade Peanut Butter (2T) PB2 (2T) Homemade Almond Butter (2T) Almond2 (2T)
Calories 190 45 190 85
Fat 16g 1.5g 16g 8g
Carbohydrates 8g 5g 7g 7.5g
Protein 8g 5g 7g 3g
Fiber 2g 2g 3g 0g
Sodium 10mg 94mg 0g trace

 

First of all, this nutritional info is an approximation.  Don’t hold me to it.  Secondly, I’m not sure how PB2 gets all the fat out. They say it’s “just plain peanuts”, but peanuts have fat without the oil added.  Anyone care to solve that mystery?

Either way, the Almond2 is lighter, though not as light as PB2.  And for me, for as infrequently as I have it, I think I’ll take the questionable simplicity of PB2 over the arduous process of making my own Almond2.  What about you?
