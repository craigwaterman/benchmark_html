Allow Yourself….

27 Nov

Hello Beautiful People!

I’m writing to you from the comfort of my cozy room, the scent of jasmine in the air, a white candle flickering, listening to my Blue Six pandora radio station letting hershey’s chocolate kisses melt in my mouth. I am here to assist in your growth. During the holiday season we can be anywhere from bursting at the seams excited to super depressed wanting to retreat to a tropical island until mid January and anywhere in between. Allow yourself to do what makes you happy. For me, it’s makeup, hair and a glass of wine here and there. To help scoot through these upcoming weeks I want to empower you to try something new. Something small that you’ve been putting off. Pick up  that new bright shade of lipstick you bought for yourself and wear it! 

 

 

You may be surprised at how a little pop of color whether it be in your lipstick, nail polish or even a scarf can shift your mood.

(I know this chick above doesn’t look very happy, but trust me, she was!)

Something else that’s a lot of fun is to allow yourself some time to play with new makeup or hair techniques. Do it when your about to wind down at the end of your day. Lock the door, turn on your favorite music and go for it!

This way you won’t feel in a rush to hurry and get yourself out the door. It’s like learning a new dance. You’ve got to practice a few times before the performance!

 

Good luck my friends, have fun and allow yourself the moments to do what makes you happy!!

Fearlessly yours, Lena

Embracing the Season

12 Nov

As we creep slowly into the holiday season I get a lot of my clients asking me to help them update their look to embrace the holidays. An easy way to do that is to add a little color or bling…in the industry we call it shimmer, sparkle…or even glitter! Personally I love a touch of shimmer in a chosen place on the face…not all over, unless you’re going for the ice queen look, which is fun sometimes! I’ll share a couple of my favorite tricks and products with you.

The first one is a cult classic I’ve realized. Urban Decay’s heavy metal glitter eyeliner. I love to apply just on the tips of the lashes so it’s not too intense but just enough to make a statement. The way it catches the light is fun and not overwhelming like glitter can be sometimes.

The second one is Laura Mercier’s baked eye shadows. She came out with a couple sets for this holiday season, this is my favorite one.

To keep a more sophisticated, elegant look I like to choose one of the three to be the main color on the eyelid. Choose more matte colors to go with your chosen shimmer to keep it under control. You can also use the lightest color from the trio, and with your ring finger pick a little bit up and lightly dust it on the highest part of your cheekbones.

Finally we come to lips! Laura Mercier just came out with these incredible new lipsticks called “Creme Smooth”. They are super luxurious, moisturizing with vitamin e and olive oil as well as long wearing. I’m addicted. (I’ve been wearing lipstick for about seventeen years, my first lipstick was Wet n’ Wild #521) This new creme smooth formula is to die for. Wait til you try it! She has EIGHTEEN new shades that you can incorporate with any holiday look, whether you want to do a shimmer smokey look with pale lips or bright bold lips with a subtle glitter eyeliner.

Here are some of my favorite shades so far:

In case my hand writing is challenging for you to read here is a list of the colors and lipliners, all Laura Mercier:

Peche- a pale peach to wear with a smokey eye, or if you’re feeling a bit demure

Plum Orchid with Crushed Berry lipliner- a beautiful bright magenta lip when you’re feeling sassy

Red Amour with Ruby lipliner- a classic red with a blue undertone to wear when you’re feeling bold and you want to be heard. Classy, bold, polished.

Plumberry with clear lipliner- a gorgeous deep berry color to wear when you’re feeling like you want color, not too bold, not too bright, but just enough of a confident statement.

Remember, a little bit goes a long way. We want to embrace the holidays without leaving glitter everywhere we go! Choose one place on your face, eyes, lips or cheeks. Empower yourself this season to try new colors, see how they make you feel, feel that extra pep in your step. You won’t regret it! Most importantly, have fun! Makeup is fun and a great personal expression, embrace that.

Fearlessly yours, Lena

Tips Your Mama Didn’t Tell You

7 Aug

I come from a Mother who wears makeup. She is the beautiful, natural type of makeup wearer. When I first started working in the cosmetic industry, I asked her if she’d like for me to get her any specific makeup. Her reply was, “Brown eyeshadow please.” The first thing out of my mouth was, “But Mama, your eyelids are already brown…” she immediately responded to me with saying my full name loudly and telling me she actually has been wearing brown eyeshadow every single day of my life…hence the beautiful natural type. I couldn’t believe it! So I got her the brown eyeshadow and have since introduced her to different colors and makeup techniques. Of course she has taught me quite a few tips and tricks along the way as well.  

Now, it is part of my job to teach the tips and tricks she’s taught me as well as the ones I’ve learned doing my own makeup since the age of twelve, from my beauty school teacher’s and being in the beauty industry for a little over a decade. So, I’ll fill you in on a few tips I think you’ll benefit from the most.

First of all, take care of your skin. If you don’t your makeup will not look as good as it has the potential to. Make it a ritual for yourself. Night and morning. At night think of the dirt and car exhaust you’ve walked through just by walking a short distance down the street. That stays on your skin until you clean it off. Not to mention the makeup you’ve worn that day and the oil your skin has produced. You don’t want to go to sleep with all that on your face, smear it on your pillow and rest in it all night…hello, that’s the perfect recipe for pimples. So cleanse, moisturize your face and use eye cream. If you’re like me then you like to use a bit of concealer under your eyes. If that area isn’t hydrated the concealer will look dry and flaky. In the morning you can splash some water on your face or cleanse it again if you feel oily.

Next tip, this shocks some people, you need to replace your mascara every six weeks! The reason why is because bacteria builds up in that tiny little magical tube. Also, when your mascara is old it flakes down onto your face when you wear it. Not cute.

This final tip is a big one for me. I get clients all the time talking about how a certain mascara or eyeliner smudges. Well guess what. It’s probably not the product. This is why. Do you use eye cream? It’s probably getting too close to your eye. Apply it to the top part of your orbital bone, pictured below, and just a tiny bit during the day.

What will happen is the eye cream will move on your skin, so applying it to the orbital bone is sufficient for during the day. Now at night time, you can use a little more eye cream and get it closer to your lashes. Using eye cream will also help your under eye concealer from looking dry and flakey because the skin under your eyes will be hydrated.

Now you have a few tips to use so your makeup won’t end up looking like this…

ok I know that’s a bit much, but you know what I’m saying! Until next time my friends!

Fearlessly yours, Lena

My Flying Kit

10 Jul

 

I officially got the job five days before I started. A three day lifestyle shoot in Phoenix. Frantically I booked my flight and started to prepare my tools and myself. He we go buddy, my kit is going to fly! There I am, my entire makeup kit spread out on my bed as I scale down and figure out what I truly need and don’t need. Deep soulful house music playing in the background. The sun shining through my curtains. Checking the ounces on all my magic potions and sprays. Packed my tweezers and scissors in my check-in bag along with my hair kit. There it is. All packed up neat and organized!

 

The smaller bag is my hair kit and the backpack had all my makeup in it. I put my hair kit in my suitcase and I’m so grateful my backpack has wheels. Since it was a lifestyle photo shoot, I knew exactly what I needed because I’ve done many of these types before. Lifestyle, people living life, having fun, not too glamourous not too boring. Pretty and polished. So I knew exactly what to bring. 

Arriving in Phoenix was good. Arriving in Phoenix was HOT. It was about 105 dry heat when I got there. I came from 68 degrees San Francisco weather. Mind above matter in a major way.  Everyone from my cab driver to the young kid that brought me room service (because I wasn’t going outside unless I had to!) was so nice and accommodating. I felt very taken care of. I stayed at the Hotel Palomar. http://www.hotelpalomar-phoenix.com/ Talk about comfort and incredible design. I asked one of the models who was born and raised in Phoenix how they did it with it being so hot. He said, “air condition hop.”

I got all the models ready all three days in an air conditioned hotel room. Here’s my set up after one of the days.

On the third day we shot outside in the morning. It was 97 degrees at 9:30a.m. As a makeup artist you not only help to get the talent ready, but sometimes offer a hand to help the production run smoothly too. Here I am on the third day helping the photographer, Cris Molina with his light. http://crismolina.com/

After all was said and done, Phoenix was great. The entire crew was fantastic. The scenery was pretty and my meals were delicious. I learned a lot in my first travel experience with my kit. Like how to pack it, I can make it through security with 98% of my makeup kit, and that people are really nice and willing to help in all kinds of ways. Getting back to San Francisco went smoothly too. I’ll tell you I’ve never been so happy to be home, only because of the weather. Which is always a huge deal to a lot of people. I noticed a while back that the weather is always a hot topic. I’m usually pretty unaffected. Until Phoenix! Here’s me dancing, happy to be back in my city by the bay.

Until next time my friends! Keep smiling.

Fearlessly Yours, Lena

Age 7 Years?

5 Jul

Attention please Ladies and Gentlemen! You MUST wash your face at night. Especially people who wear makeup. If not, you’ll age SEVEN years. (ok maybe not seven years, but it’s horrible for your skin) My estetician school teacher Mrs. Lassiter, who resembled a blonde Lucille Ball and wore her black rimmed glasses on the tip of her nose while teaching us the importance of skincare told us. An old tale I’m sure, point being, it’s really, really,really bad for your skin. The reason why is just by walking outside your skin is subjected to free radicals, which are little particles flying around in our air from car exhaust, people’s second hand smoke etc. Not to mention if you’re talking on your phone and the build up of bacteria on that little thing…gross. If you’re wearing makeup all day, have been outside even just a little bit and go lay your head down on your comfy pillow without washing your face, then you’re marinating your skin in dirty oil from your face that collects on your pillow case. Hi pimples, how’ve ya been? Even if you just use a face cleansing wipe, that’s fine. Keep your wipes by the bed or your toothbrush. Just get the yuckies off! One of my new favorite face wipes are these.

I’ve found them at Whole Foods and Ulta.

Ladies, drag queens and female impersonators, when you take care of your skin by cleansing AND moisturizing your makeup will look better, more natural and even. If not you can go from this…

 

To this…

 

You owe it to yourself to make a nightly ritual of self care. Just do it, you’ll love it. Don’t get me wrong, aging is not bad. Not taking care of you skin is bad! Just the basics. Cleanse and moisturize. (exfoliating and doing a mask a couple times a week is great too, but I don’t want to overwhelm you) There’s a whole slew of advanced skincare, but if you at least do the basics that’s great. Standing tall together as we embrace the aging process and take care of ourselves! 

Fearlessly Yours, Lena

Testament Experience

11 Jun

I got the call on a sunny Tuesday afternoon. Was I available to do some male grooming on a two day photo shoot with a local metal band? Ummm, ya! I guess I had a bad phone connection because both times talking with two different people I thought they said I’d be working with “Pessimist”. In the middle of getting the guys ready on the first day of shooting on Mare island I learned that was not the name of the band. I saw Tiffany a.k.a. Mama Tiff, one the bands managers pull out this leather vest that had TESTAMENT embroidered in white on the back of it. As I’m finishing up with the lead singer, Chuck, I asked her, “Oh is that the other name of the band?” She answered me, “No, that’s the name of the band. Testament.” Greeeeeaaat. This whole time, about a week or so I was researching a different metal band! I told them and we had a good laugh. Of course I know who Testament is. Being a child of the San Francisco bay area and having a slew of different friends, some metal heads, I KNOW who Testament is!! Awesome.

Day 1: Call time 8:45a.m. on Mare Island. We arrived and set up shot. There was a huge winnebago with a trailer attached to it. Dean Karr, director/photographer was shooting. After I got everyone ready we had a lot of down time. On every shoot I do, lighting is always the reason. It trips me out, so technical. We wait for the magic of the lights to happen. While we were waiting guess what movie was playing…Goodfellas! One of my all time top three favorite movies. After that Talladega Nights played. Mama Tiffany had provided a ton of fresh, healthy food for everyone. There was also Paul the owner of the winnie barbequing some sausages which were also delicious. It felt like we were set up in the middle of nowhere.

Finally, it was time to shoot. Last looks, the guys looked great. Hardcore. Tough. With the smell of fennel and the sound of mini blinds flipping in the wind from a nearby abandoned building, I stood behind Dean the whole time making sure they stayed camera ready. It was really wonderful to be around such creative energy. To see the process and see how his photo assistants worked their butts off was inspiring. There I was, watching Dean’s creative process while he was smoking a cigar, snapping photos and making us laugh. He also took some shots with a polaroid camera. We were instructed to put the negatives in some water. Old school.

Day 2: A redwood forest in Guernville, Ca. We all met up in the east bay and hopped in the winnie. Drove about 100 miles. On the drive I’m standing up, bracing myself against a table, taming some wild hair. Getting the job done. We arrived in the gorgeous redwoods, with the smell of nature in the air. Paul skillfully pulled the huge trailer into a spot.

It’s time to shoot. Me, the five guys in the band, Mama Tiffany, Dean, the first photo assistant Antonio, 2nd assistant Scott, and owner of the winnie and our faithful driver Paul go hiking into the lush redwood forest.

Antonio was lugging a super heavy battery thing that I’m going to guess weighed about 60lbs. That super heavy battery thing was essential in making the flash go off for the fancy lighting…in the redwoods. We only spoke to the forest ranger once, she was cool about it. Mama Tiffany made sure we all behaved and respected the forest. I had a blast as part of the brigade. After about an hour of so of some spectacular shooting it was time to head to the second location of the day. West Oakland. We all hopped back into the winnie and Paul drives us about 70 miles.
We arrive at American Steel Studios in West Oakland, Ca. Where a community of artists reside. Here’s a couple snapshots of artist Karen Cusolito’s work that astounded me seeing up close.

Venus was in transit. Passing in front of the sun. One of the artists from the studios had a plasma welding mask he shared with us so we could safely see it. It was incredible. For me, magical. Several hours later we were wrapped! Done! Two full days of technical lighting and raw inspiration fueled me. You’ll be able to see the photos in Decibel, Guitar World and Guitar Player magazines. Testaments 10th studio album, “Dark Roots of Earth” is scheduled to be released July 30, 2012. It was definitely an incredible, family style two days. Until next time my friends!!

Fearlessly yours, Lena

A Lovely Recap: Something Blue Bridal Show

6 Jun

We did it. My team and I. By team I mean my first best friend ever/ soul sister, and my uber supportive boyfriend. It’s times like these when I call on the people I trust and respect. Lena Chavez Makeup Artistry’s first bridal show was a success. I had Celza in charge of public relations and Louie was my tech support handling the slide show I had playing with all the complicated wires and plugs. We had a guestbook to sign in, vouchers and pens to give away, a slideshow of my work playing, and me doing touchups on the lovely ladies who attended the event. All the events and shows I’ve done for retailers etc. thus far have prepared me to rock out my own booth. We were prepared, handled business and had a blast.

Doors opened at 6:30 with the first 100 people through the doors receiving a fabulous bag from Bloomingdale’s stuffed full of goodie’s. The deep soul house music was awesome, people were dancing in a corner the entire night, tasty treats including sushi, cupcakes, kabobs and a signature tropical inspired blue cocktail were a hit. We were set up in between the henna lady and Halo blowout bar. With fantastic bridal table settings in front of us, candles lit, blue balloons floating about and cameras capturing the nights events. It was a cozy boutique feel. A great bridal industry community coming together celebrating love. I felt so grateful the day and night went smoothly and was such a success. Salud to sharing our craft and doing what we love. Enjoy the video from Compass Cinema!

Fearlessly yours, Lena
