Why Tall Women Should Wear Heels

I am 5’9 and a half, 5’10 on a good day.

I want to share with my fellow tall sisters why they should, no MUST wear heels.

Before I do though, a little about myself and my height woes. I was 5’9 when i was in grade 7. Awkward, gangly (you now that time before you discover hair straighteners, good make up and style?) it’s fair to say I looked like a boy. When my friends would stand around at recess I would actually bend my knees and hunch over so I would not stand out. Do you know how ridiculous that looked?

Not until the ripe old age of twenty- freaking- two did I march to Brown’s (shoe store in Canadian shoe store) and buy a pair of heels.

How did I sum up the courage? I STOPPED CARING WHAT OTHER PEOPLE THOUGHT

  • Flats look ridiculous with my dresses, skinny jeans, interview clothes. When a tall girl is wearing flats with a gorgeous dress I am 99% sure she is only wearing them becauseshe is self conscious, not because she thinks the flats look better.
  • If you are already tall, then what does it matter if you add a few inches? You will always be tall so you might as well embrace it, not try and fight it.
  • In my grad program we had to dress professionally for presentations. Heels can make a drab business outfit look amazing. Did it look strange that everyone else in my presentation group was a head shorter? Maybe, but I looked elegant and professional, and oh yes I DIDNT CARE.

Bottom line is: Wear what makes YOU feel good. Do not underestimate what a difference a pair of heels can make to an outfit. You will find a new found confidence and you will feel great! Height is a blessing, not something that you should feel ashamed about and be banished to a life time of flat shoes (unless you love flats and you are tall, or short- this pep talk is for those who are afraid to try heels).

Warning: Don’t buy the tallest heel you can find if you are starting out. Like anything, it talkes a little bit of practice to develop a solid walk. Take smaller steps than you normally would and put you weight evenly on your heel first.

Happy Strutting!

Smoothie + Cereal = Dessert !

I recently unearthed a blender that was hidden deep within the depts of my kitchen cupboards. I’ve been having fun creating all kinds of smoothies, and after I read what was in a Tim Horton’s “Real” Fruit Smoothie I swore I would never buy a processed smoothie ever again.

If you are bored of eating regular cereal with milk, try throwing a handful of healthy ceareal into the mix. It becomes a very tasty dessert style treat!
Blend:
1/2 cup of low fat soy Milk
1 cup of mixed berried (strawberries, raspberries and blueberries)+ a handfull of ice cubes
Add:
1/2 cup of Kashi Go Lean crunch cereal
Enjoy!
