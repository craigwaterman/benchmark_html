What Is Your Money Story?

April 29, 2015 By Katrina Marie

We all tell ourselves stories all day every day.
Unless you are an advanced zen master and have learned to silence the mind chatter.
Unfortunately, I am not one of those. Sad face.
The stories we tell ourselves are on repeat in the back of our minds.
About money, about each other, about life.
In fact,  I just told you one of mine – ‘I’m not a zen master’. mmmm interesting…
The funny thing about these stories (that we are often not even conscious of) is they become true for us. Our stories are our beliefs. Our beliefs form our experience of life.
What is your money story?
What do you tell yourself over and over in the deepest recesses of your mind about your financial situation?
Does your story go along the lines of

‘I always have plenty of money. 

I give and receive money with joy and ease.’

Or is it the more common:
‘There is not enough money’ 
Other versions of this story go:
‘I don’t have enough money’
“I’ll never have enough money’
I’m always broke’
‘Money is hard to get’
‘I need more money’
‘It’s hard to make ends meet’
‘I have to work hard for money’
‘I might not have enough money later’
The good news is you can change that story now.
You can find a way to change your internal story from ‘not enough’ to ‘I have enough money’.
Do it gradually, do it consistently, do it by setting a budget and living within your means.
Repeat after me:
I have enough money.
I have enough money for today.
I have enough money in this moment.
 No-one is demanding I pay up right now.
 In this moment I have enough money.
Make this your mantra. I have enough money for right now. 
or, if that is too far fetched for you to believe just yet, try this instead:

‘I look forward to having enough money’

This may take some practice and awareness. You may find all sorts of other thoughts crop up to defend your belief in not enough-ness.
Your thoughts and beliefs really do create your reality.
But don’t worry, changing your beliefs can be a gentle, gradual process.
Start by noticing as often as you can that you have enough money for this exact moment.
Unless you are scrabbling in your purse or your card is being declined, in which case I doubt you are reading this at the same time!
You have enough money in this moment, in this moment, in this moment.
Do not think about the next five minutes or the next hour or next week.
In this very second you have enough money.
This will shift the energy of your repetitive thought to enough-ness.
Do this often enough and you will soon find you do have enough money.
Then you will have more than enough money.
Then you will discover you have plenty of money!
This is why budgeting is such a powerful exercise. You plan a way to live within your means so that you know (instead of trying to convince yourself) that you have enough money.
Your beliefs in not-enough start to crumble and lose power. You can confidently say to yourself I have enough money for that! When you go shopping for clothes that you have budgeted for or receive a bill in the mail.
The fact is you do have enough money. You have enough money right now for everything in your life. You just need to learn to channel it so that you can have the physical and mental experience of having enough money.
Say to yourself on repeat : 
I have enough money. I have enough money. I have enough money.
Soon you will start to believe it and then it will morph into ‘I have plenty of money’. And the best part is it will be true! Whooppeee!!
now…for my zen master training……
Lots of love!!
​Katrina xoxo

Debt-Free Living Is Do-able

April 23, 2015 By Katrina Marie

 

Ok, I’m gonna say it to you straight. You have to get good at this money thing if you want to be debt-free and propserous. The first step is to believe that debt-free living is do-able.

Then manage your money like a boss!

Money affects every single person. We all need it to pay for things we can’t (or don’t want to) make or do ourselves.

Most of us interact with money daily. It is often on our minds.

There is no point hoping or wishing your money situation will improve someday. That day may never come unless you take action today to improve it.

As much as I personally love the idea of living in peace, love and harmony exchanging vegetables and sweet wishes for goods and services it just isn’t practical in our present society.

Alas, I don’t think my children’s orthodontist would appreciate being paid in pumpkins. With potentially four kids needing braces in the next few years – that’s a lot of pumpkins!

Thankfully, there is an easier way that doesn’t require a truck and a forklift.

Money is a very efficient exchange system and the only system that is recognised the world over. But you have to know how to work your money to your advantage.

If you don’t master your money, it will master you. Usually in the form of debt.

​

You can start by doing the exercises in The Art Of Debt-Free Living Guide and Workbook.  Where else can you get all that information in one place for free??

It is a tragedy that basic money skills like budgeting, saving and investing are not taught more in schools. Most of us have to learn as we go and usually only get the lessons when we are in financial pain. At least that was my story.

But it is not too late!

Managing money is a skill that can be learned and mastered at any stage of life. Once you get the hang of it it is sooo thrilling to turn your finances around from debt or struggle to abundance!

It’s time to rise up to the challenge!

It is not luck or the government or even your partner that will decide your financial future. YOU decide your financial future starting today.

No matter how much you earn, the skills remain the same. This is why 70% of lotto winners declare bankruptcy within a few years of winning big money. They have never learned the skills to manage money.

Remember it’s not about how much you earn, but about how much you keep that counts. 

Managing money well is a daily practice that, like any other life skill, will affect every other area of your life. I see it as much of a spiritual practice as yoga or meditation.

Yes, it does take courage, determination and discipline to face your financial situation. But setting sail in the direction you want to go is a heck of a lot more rewarding (even if it is a bit choppy sometimes) than staying stuck in an endless cycle of bills and repayments, going nowhere.

So make your choice now. Will you choose freedom over debt? Financial empowerment over struggle? No complaining or whining or blaming. It’s up to you!

Take it one day, one dollar, one debt at a time. You can do this!

Bon Voyage!!

PS: You can be all peace, love and mungbeans and be outrageously wealthy! Why not?

How To Make Money From Your Hobby

April 8, 2015 By Katrina Marie

Do you have something you do for fun? Why not earn some extra cash doing what you love? You can make money from your hobby to supplement your main income. All you need is an idea and the self belief to follow it through. Who knows! You may even choose to turn it into a lucrative business that supports you full time! Anything is possible.

As you may know, I love to grow food, not so much for money but because it is fun and it means my family eats fresh, organic produce.

We sell our excess produce from a small farm stand at our front gate.  We also sell our fresh free-range eggs to neighbours and friends. This covers the cost of the chicken food, seeds, tools and other things we need for our farm. It also provides a nice bit of pocket money. We put all the money into a jar and when it’s full I count it and take it to the bank. Last time it was over $3000!

If getting a second job is not possible because of family, time or health constraints – consider using your creative skills to supplement the family income.

Not only can your hobby earn you money but it may also save you money if it is something you can make or do instead of buy from someone else. Growing your own food is one example of this. So is sewing clothes or soft furnishings. Remember the old saying “A penny saved is a penny earned. ”

Whatever you do, here are some tips to make the most of your time and energy when making money from your hobby.

Believe in your skills. Self-belief is critical if you are putting yourself out there in any way. Know that you are doing the world a favour by offering your creative skills and services for others to pay for. You are earning money AND you are offering real value in return. It’s a win-win situation.

 

There is nothing wrong with starting small. Even if you are only earning a small amount of money, it all helps. From tiny things big things grow, it just takes a bit of time. If you keep at your craft with consistency and reverence – soon you will build a loyal following and customer base and your earnings will increase too.

 

Dream A Little. My friend Jackie makes gorgeous girl’s clothing out of upcycled materials such as doilies and tablecloths. She has been doing it for a few years now and started it for pleasure after a difficult time in her life. She has built a loyal customer base so that every time she finishes a piece it is snapped up in minutes. You can check out her facebook page here.

 

Spread the word of what you do. Do not be afraid to self promote. How else will people know what you are up to? Everyone is so distracted these days so don’t be shy to repeat yourself and consistently promote your products and services.

 

Make a Start. For services such as cooking or photography – start by offering your skills at a reduced cost to friends and family to build your experience and start spreading the word.

 

Tell all your friends and family what you are doing and ask them to refer you to others.

 

Use social media to build a following. Start a facebook page. It’s free and easy to set up. Also consider other social media platforms such as instagram, pinterest, google plus or youtube.

 

Treat it like a mini-business and keep track of your expenses and earnings. A simple notebook will do to start with. Record what you earn and what you spend. This way you can ensure you are making a profit. The last thing you want to do is go backwards financially.

 

Have a plan for the extra money you make so it doesn’t get frittered away on everyday life. Set up a separate account or put it into a special envelope if you are paid in cash. Use it to pay off debt, save for a family holiday, save for a large household item or renovation or even better put it aside to save for retirement.

Here is a list of hobbies and some of the ways you could make money from them.

Arts and Crafts

Run classes in your area of expertise. Even if you don’t think you are an expert you could teach the basics to beginners. For example – Quilting, sewing, painting, drawing, pottery, ceramics, mosaics, crochet etc…

Start an online store on Etsy or Facebook. Post pictures of your creations and sell them.

Sell your products at local markets and craft fairs.

Spread the word that you design and sell pieces by commission.

Enter competitions that offer cash prizes.

 

Animals

If you love animals and are good with them you can use those gifts to earn money.

Start a dog walking service in your area.

Offer dog grooming services.

Offer pet sitting/feeding services.

Breed a litter of puppies or kittens. Check your local laws and regulations first. Also consider breeding and selling chickens, quails, ducks, guinea pigs or rabbits.

If you have the space, consider adgisting horses on your property for a fee.

 

Photography

Sell your photos online to stock photo websites such as Shutterstock, Fotolia or Dreamstime.

Turn your images into notecard sets or postcards that you can sell. This could work well in scenic areas that attract a lot of tourists.

Do wedding or event photography.

Cooking

Teach cooking classes in your home or hire a kitchen in a local hall or venue. You could do general cooking skill classes or specialised classes in your area of expertise and interest. Such as gluten free, paleo, whole foods, vegetarian, vegan, Chinese, italian, vietnamese, thai etc..

Make jams, chutneys, pies, cakes, cookies etc and sell your creations at farmer’s markets and fairs.

Offer yourself as a personal chef to cook for people in their own homes for special occasions.

Make birthday cakes, wedding cakes or cupcakes for sale.

Cater for events.

 

Gardening

Sell your extra produce at your garden gate (if allowed in your area) or to friends and neighbours. Most people are more than willing to pay for fresh, local and homegrown produce.

If you have animals such as horses, chickens, cows or sheep, collect their manure in bags and sell to others for their gardens.

Hire yourself out to do gardening for other people.

Run lessons in sustainable gardening or permaculture.

 

Music

Hire yourself out to give private or small group lessons.

Perform at restaurants or events.

 

Writing

Start a blog

Offer freelance writing services to local newspapers, magazines and websites.

Offer your services through sites such as Fiverr or Elance.

 

Do you have a hobby you could turn into some extra cash? What is it? Write down some ways you could earn money from your hobby. Choose the one that sings to you the most. Then go for it!

Who knows where it could take you!

Lots of love
Katrina

7 Ways To Improve Your Relationship With Money

March 18, 2015 By Katrina Marie

 

 I’m gonna come right out and admit it. I’m in love.

 

With money.

 

Yup! If money was a person I would lovingly stroke it and stare into it’s eyes all day.

 

You know, I am a country girl at heart and not into fancy brand name clothes or a glitzy lifestyle. That stuff doesn’t interest me. But I do love money and the freedom it provides.

I love to tend money, spend money, save money, invest money, count money, watch money grow and channel money into the avenues that bring me the greatest fulfilment and joy. I love money.

 

Does that shock you that I say that out loud?  Hey, I’ll happily shout it from the roof tops.

 

I FREAKING LOVE MONEY!!

 

What’s that? “Money is not that important to me” you say?

 

Really? Can I have yours then?

 

Nahh….. Just playing.

 

If me openly admitting my love for money ruffles your feathers,  try this little exercise just for kicks….

 

Bring to mind a beloved friend or partner. Someone very important to you and that you love with all your heart. It could even be a pet.

 

Now replace the word money with their name in the following sentence.

 

‘Money is not that important to me.’

 

‘(Name of Loved One) is not that important to me.’

 

Whoa! Yikes.

How do you think they would feel if they heard you say that? Pretty hurt and upset, I bet. Do you reckon they would hang around you so much? Or even want to be with you? How would your relationship be if that is how you felt?

My bet is ….not great.

I for one would not stick with someone who said that about me.

(But it’s Ok I know it’s not true. We are just doing a little exercise to prove a point here.)

The truth is we all want to be loved. We gravitate to the people who make us feel loved. Money works in the same way.

If someone makes you feel special and loved you are more likely to do nice things for them.  You would go out of your way to help them when they need you.

As much as we’d all like to be magnanimous unconditional lovers, be honest, who would you prefer to hang out with – someone who couldn’t care less about you, or worse, is ashamed of you and won’t even admit you know each other OR someone who genuinely loves, honours and takes care of you?

 The same principles that work for loving, healthy relationships with people also work for a healthy relationship with money.

To show you love someone and genuinely want them in your life you do the following things (and they can also be applied to the way you treat money):

 

  1. Pay Attention.

    No-one likes to be ignored, especially by loved ones. To love someone is to look at them, notice them, acknowledge them and smile at them.

Do the same with money. Look at your bank statements, acknowledge what money is doing for you – putting food on the table, a roof over your head, clothes, education or travel. Notice where your money goes, look at your bank balance and receipts.

 

  1. Invite Them Into Your Home and Heart.

When you love someone you invite them into your home. You open the door and ask them to come in. You make them feel like an honoured guest.

Honour money in the same way. Invite it into your life. Open yourself to receive money. Set up structures such as a savings account, investment portfolio or a business and invite money to flow into your life.

 

  1. Appreciate Them and Be Glad To Be With Them.

We all love to feel appreciated. For someone to tell us how much they love what we do for them. To feel like someone is proud of us and our achievements. Doesn’t that make us feel good and want to do even more?

Money is no different. Appreciate every dollar you do have, even if it is not that much. It is more likely to grow and multiply when you honour it with gratitude and appreciation.

Money will disappear fast from your life if you treat it with contempt and let it leak out everywhere. When you look at your bank balance try to be grateful for what is there, rather than fearful because it may not be as much as you would like.

 

 4. Make a Nice Space For Them.

Have you ever stayed with someone who has gone to a lot of trouble to create a lovely bedroom or space for you to sleep? Tidy space, clean sheets, comfy pillows, extra blankets…. Whatever you need to be happy? Doesn’t that feel good? Don’t you just want to snuggle in and stay there?

You can create a beautiful space for money in your life too. Unfold the notes and place them neatly in their own compartment in your purse or wallet. Don’t shove a fistful of crumpled notes in amongst the old movie ticket stubs and receipts.

Keep your purse and wallet clean and tidy. Organise your paperwork, bills and statements into an easy system to make the flow of money in and out simple and stress free.

 

  1. Be Proud of Them (and not ashamed of them).

Can you imagine being in a new relationship with someone that you adore but they are embarrassed to be seen with you? They won’t even talk about you or tell their friends that you exist.  How awful. You’d want to get out of that relationship fast for your own sake. You deserve better treatment than that.

Hang on. Are you doing the same thing with money? Are you embarrassed to admit you are working on your finances. That you are building a healthy relationship with money and paying off debt. Do you pretend everything is fine to your friends and not talk about it? Or are you embarrassed by how much money you make? Because it’s too much or too little?

 

  1. Be An Advocate

You would never stand by and watch someone you love be bullied or taken advantage of. You would be an advocate and help them get their needs met and the treatment they deserve. You would encourage them to get out of toxic or dangerous situations which are not helpful to their physical, mental or emotional health, wouldn’t you?

Do you do that for the money in your life? Do you ask for and expect to be paid what you are worth? Are you an advocate for your own financial wellbeing? Do you move away from the things that are bad for your finances and do more of the things that are good for your finances?

 

  1. Set Boundaries

Every healthy relationship needs healthy boundaries. As much as we love someone we all need a certain amount of time apart doing our own things. You also would not want to do things that go against your deepest held values for anyone including those you love.

The same is true for money. As much as you love and respect it, it is not healthy to focus on it 100% of the time. Or do things for money that go against your deepest held values.

Relax and spend time with loved ones doing things just for fun. Focus on other ways abundance shows up in your life. For example you might notice the abundance of nature or the abundance of joy when having an outrageously fun time playing games with friends or family.

How can you improve your relationship with money? What can you do today to make money feel like a valued and honored guest in your life and so open the door to more money flowing in?

Much Love

Katrina xxx
