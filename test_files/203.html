An Introduction to Gerber Technology

Gerber is the name of the software used to create apparel pattern designs with CAD. In 1967 the industry saw the first Gerber plotting machine, invented by H. Joseph. in 1968 the first Gerber cutting machine was introduced, this design is now used in apparel industry globally and has improved countless techniques in speed and cost.

“An intelligent pattern design software system for the apparel and fashion industry.

For decades, the world’s leading apparel brands and manufacturers have relied on AccuMark® software to satisfy their most demanding production requirements” (Gerber, scientific. inc, 2014).

[http://www.gerbertechnology.com/en-us/solutions/apparelretail/productdesign/accumark.aspx]

Modelling a Cowl Neck

A technology that’s been around for a long time, but is still highly innovative, is modelling on a stand to find a garment. I modelled a Cowl neck on the stand, I didn’t use a working drawing for this however as I find it can be a great way of drawing in 3D. Once you find the shape you are happy with, be sure to mark every single fold and arm hole etc. because this will be very important when the shape is transferred onto pattern paper.

Laser Cutting Research

Laser cutting technologies are a modern innovative way of creating different styles of aesthetics in furnitures, apparel, accessories and more. The technology was theorised by Albert Einstein in 1917 “when he described his theory of stimulated emission” (Morgan, R). The 1940’s saw the beginning of Laser cutting technology, and Bell Laboratories was one of the first companies who started to find ways to utilise the technology. The technique is used to cut beautiful patterns and images into apparel, or even cut to create garments by up and coming designer Elvira T’ Hart [http://www.elvirathart.com/index.xhtml].

The Art Of Illusion

Illusion is becoming an epidemic for future design in clothing technologies. These designs use contrasting colours and ‘ideal’ body shape to create the illusion that the body looks that way. For example the dress above from Alexander McQueen’s 2009 Resort collection. This particular dress highlights the beautiful hourglass shape women so typically desire and have, whilst dark grey contours the outlines of the body, portraying the shape in white to be the models body shape. The fabric is a jersey of some kind, probably a mix of elastine and cotton.
