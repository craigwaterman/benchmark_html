Bear Stories: Wednesday, November 18

November 18, 2015UncategorizedMrs. Hunter

Today and tomorrow in Math, PK4B students are being introduced to the concept of joining (addition) number stories.

In small groups, Pre-K students built little homes for the bear manipulatives using blocks.  We left the houses open on top, so they could be used for our joining stories.

The key mathematical concepts for this lesson include:

1.  counting objects to solve simple problems

2.  solving addition number stories while using concrete objects and modeling

3.  recognizing that a collection of objects can be made larger by adding objects to it

Wow, lots going on in this math activity!

 

3 Bears are in the house.   They invite 2 more bears to come play in the house.

2 bears are playing a game.  2 more come to play.  How many are there now?

2 bears are eating a Thanksgiving dinner.  They tell 3 more to come to dinner.  How many are in the house now?

 

I added 2 more bears!  3 bears and 2 bears together makes 5.

I’m going to make the number of bears in the house bigger.

2 bears plus 2 more = 4 bears

When joining numbers and manipulatives together we get a larger numbers.  This activity is a great way to start learning the skill of adding.
