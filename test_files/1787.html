Dark horse: Ministry of Sound MOS006 headphones review

What is this doing here anyway?

 Once I was at an airport and I had to choose a headphone in 2 minutes with a budget of 50 euro.
I hated every second of it, since I could not do a research beforehand, what I always do.
Thank God, I was able to try quickly some and the MOS006 was the only one what sounded much more clear to me than the others within the price range, easily some Sennheisers and Sonys.

Since I am a big fan of rock and metal music, after a bit of EQ-ing on the smartphone I find immediately the right settings and I enjoyed my flight.

But what is this brand?

 Ministry of Sound is active within the dance music, its a label and club, as well. Hm... Definitely not cup of tea. However, they made a great job designing the MOS006 headphones.

Invitation for dance...

Decent metal ear cups with a little mash on the side (probably we can call it semi opened), leatherette cushions where the sweaty ears are returning. Two metal headband on the top, nicely wrapped in black foam. Unfortunately after some time the pressure feels heavy on the ears and became uncomfortable to wear it. Detachable cables is a positive, however its really thin cable. Sound isolation is average, but its leaking some noise out above a certain level of volume.

And the sound???

 Well, we are not short of low end, that's for sure. Listening Skrillex's world famous song, Right in, I got a great listening experience. The bass is almost shaking the ear cups, clear and heavy, going really deep with fast response, but not boomy, not harsh. Mid-range is muffed and dull, possibly this can be the weakest point for today. Highs are quite OK, clear enough, not pushed or processed, feels great for the music. Separation is nice, detailed sound. Since its an on-ear construction, it takes out a lot from the soundstage, but this cannot be a really big problem on the go.
To be honest, I enjoyed listening the whole Bangarang EP with these headphones, for a better result I set the EQ a bit up on the bass, take off some from the mids and add a little to highs. Perfect match for the sound. I have no complaints, even tough I have really little experience in listening electronic music.
If you tend to listen rock music on this headphones, what is not as bad as it looks like for the first time, the mids can be a problem for some people since its too pushy and muffed. Better stay with Skrillex.

Bottom line:

Ministry of Sound made an excellent quality built headphones, really attractive and a must-try for only 50 Euro. If you are electronic music fan, the MOS006 is for you.

Low cost IEM... Shure SE 112 review

I expected more...

 I needed a new earphones, since my long serving Sennheiser CX250 started to fall apart after 6 years of extensive use. While I was doing a research for a new one (as I always do if I want to buy an electronic product, I came across the new Shure SE 112 IEM, what stands for in-ear-monitor headphones.

Prestige, history and fame...

The Shure SE 112 came in a really nice looking package, with 3 rubberish eartips, a small bag and a lots of supplementary instruction papers. I was quite happy. The cable is thicker than on a regular earphones, the housing is from plastic, but quite nicely made. You will recognize that the printing is upside down. It indicates that it was designed to wear over the ear, but its fine to wear it on the normal way. too. The Shure SE 112 easier to wear than other, more expensive IEM monitors.

Personal issues...

Non of the 3 supplied pair of eartips were fitting into my ear perfectly, but I have an odd size of head and ears. With the medium sized it was a bit big, pushing much my ear canals from inside (probably thats the right fit for me) and the small was almost any time could fall out.
However, with the medium I got great isolation of the outside world, you fall into a state where are only the music and you, nothing else. This can come handy in stage or noisy traffic.

Shut up... How does it sound?

 The Shure SE 112 sounds really flat and dull for the first time and thanks to the great isolation you hear great details. The lows are a bit too much, we have lots and lots of mids and highs are also present in some way. Average soundstage, but its really mid focused sound. I was quite dissapointed still, after trying every EQ settings on my smartphone and PC to get a comfortable sound from Spotify. However, I like rock music, the mids were way too much for me and I got this dull overall sound what I could not get rid of.

But lets just stop here and think: what is this headphone is made for? What is its purpose? What Shure is famous for? Yes, the answer is the stage.

The flat, mid-centric sound is great if you are in a band and you use the SE112 for monitoring your own instrument or the others. Personally, I am a drummer in a rock band and it worked for me very well.

Bottom line:

The Shure SE 112 is the cheapest  item within the range of the company's earphones, but its does the job what it was designed for. With its outstanding build quality and 50 Euro price range, you cannot go wrong if you are looking for a monitoring IEM on a tight budget.

The free horse: t.bone HD880 Studio monitoring headphones

Do not check the teeth of the free horse...

(What a great Hungarian proverb and its still true.)

I got the t.bone HD880 with my electronic drum kit as a free gift. Thank you thomann.de.
After checking the value on the website and had my first listening, I put it back to the box and left it in the basement. Your right, I was not expecting anything special and that's what I exactly got.
After some years and getting more experience with headphones, I have tried it again and I was surprised. I liked it it.

Maybe it worth something?

 T.bone is a home brand for thomann.de, Europe's biggest music store (20.000 outgoing shipments a day, only takes 28 minutes from ordering on website until your item gets into the delivery truck, just incredible), basically you don't hear about it until you came across with some strange sounding brand names on Thomann's website.
I was always careful with home brands, not always leading to great results. But the HD880 changed my perspective.

Enough... How does it sounds?

 In short: it sounds flat. Probably because its written  'Studio Monitor' on the headband? Come on, it cost only 25 Euro...

I started to use this headphone for mixing my rock bands recordings and I was quite satisfied. You get that overall flat line in the sound, where you can adjust the volumes of each instrument, translates great and points out incorrect volumes. Detailed enough to hear every instrument, even when you have a lot of them in one time.
Well balanced sound, OK sound stage, enough bass and highs, but a bit too harsh and pushy middle. Almost like you stand next to a full tube half stack guitar amp and you feel that typical scratchy and harsh sound of the metal riff from the cabinet, what we all love. This can be the biggest negative, as well: after some time it can be tiring to listen, welcome fatigue effect.

The leatherette ear cushions and the headband are comfortable, for longer use also, not pressing too much on the head. Stated as a closed headphone, still it leaks some noise out and the black mash on the sides gives an idea about some possible holes.

Bottom line:

Thomann's home brand t.bone surprised me with an entry level studio headphones and even if its not for audiophile listening (not at all), but it comes handy for small project/home studios.

The first review: Superlux HD681 Studio monitoring headphones

Favorite, but not...

Everybody knows about Superlux already, who is involved into a bit more advanced audio listening than the average guys. Superlux, a company from Taiwan, offers great products for almost nothing. And I mean it.

I am always focused on great quality/price ratio, I really hate to give a much more money for something what I can get cheaper. Most of the times its only the company name, prestige, logo ect.
That's where the Superlux has its advantage. You pay for the item itself and not for the brand.

I was looking for a new headphone, as I mentioned before great quality/price ratio is a must, and I came across with the Superlux brand and some dude who was telling great things about it.

Showdown...

 Picked up one pair of HD681 at the local music store and I was quite surprised what I got for 20 Euros only. Great tonal balance, clean sound, jackpot!

After some burn in time, the sound didn't really change, the headband is still quite pushy, the leatherette ear cups are comfortable, but after some time it just irritating, you want to take them down (modding for a soft ear cushion is an option). Other than that its a solid structure.

But how does it sounds? Well, V shaped. Its a pleasure to listen different kind of music, the sound is not pushing, not harsh. The bass is cut in the bottom zone, starting from around 60 Hz, then detailed, not boomy. Midrange is quite laid back, but a bit flat at the bottom end. We have a lot of highs here, bit over tuned, too shiny, can be harsh and muddy on loud volumes.

Mostly I am listening rock and metal music, but often I turn the listening session towards classical, pop and alternative, and its my favorite headphones so far for listening.

Soundstage is great, detailed enough for the value. The HD681 is a semi-opened headphone, with some sound leakage to the outside and inside, as well.

I was naive and tried to mix and master my rock bands recording with this headphones, Superlux states that the HD681 is a studio monitor headphone, it was a bad idea. Because of the V shaped sound, the first mix was sounding great on these headphones, but anywhere else not. You should get a proper flat headphones for that.

Bottom line:

The Superlux HD681 is a great sounding headphone for a value, regardless its weaknesses, definately a must-buy around 20 Euro price range. Can we ask more for that? I don't think so.
