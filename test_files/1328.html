Halloween Costume FAQ

Above is a GIF. Below are answers to your questions about my Halloween costumes.

Can I watch a 1-minute video about the making of this costume that has dramatic music in the background?

Yes.

Can I share your costumes on my blog/TV show/back tattoo/etc?

Yes please. And please link my website and/or tag me.

You are officially granted permission to share or broadcast anything from this post, my website, the making of video any of my other videos.

So IHOP paid you for this, right? You sold out! The internet curses you! Hashtag unfollow!

In fact, IHOP neither paid me nor were they were aware I was making this costume. I don’t even eat there (no gluten free pancakes). I do, however, applaud their support of Childrens Miracle Network Hospitals.

Where do you get the ideas for your costumes?

I give motivational speeches for a living. They are funny. Sometimes on purpose. So while you are at work every day, I am sitting on the couch at home or in an airplane en route to a speech thinking of amputee jokes.

But here are the specific sources of inspiration:

  1. Gingerbread man—from Shrek. Not the gumdrop buttons!
  2. Leg lamp—My girlfriend fiance  wife Ashley suggested this idea, a reference to the movie “A Christmas Story.” Frah-gee-lay.
  3. Flamingo—I was just at the zoo one time and I noticed that flamingos look like me doing a crutch handstand. So yeah.
  4. Foosball player—A YouTube subscriber suggested it to me at VidCon. I thought it was appropriate since last year I was also named to the US Amputee World Cup Team.
  5. IHOP—It’s a classic amputee joke. This year I decided to anthropomorphize it.

What does anthropomorphize mean?

(X)

Have you always approached your disability this way?

Wow, who is asking these questions? You seem to be really smart and insightful and probably cool to hang out with sometime.

I’ve always dealt with the social discomfort of my disability by having a sense of humor. But I used to be much more self-conscious. 

In high school I wore a prosthesis all the time and didn’t want anyone to find out I was missing a leg. But now I’m more comfortable with who I am and what I look like, and I guess with these Halloween costumes you could even say that I celebrate what makes me different. Which may be either weird or refreshing, depending on your perspective.

Wait, is this the same FAQ that you posted here last year?

Um, well, yeah some of the answers are the same. But wait, things are also different! Significant milestones of the past twelve months:

  • I published a book about my dating failures (We Should Hang Out Sometime)
  • Those dating failures officially ended when I got married last month
  • I played soccer in the Amputee World Cup in Mexico in December

What happened to 2011?

WE DON’T TALK ABOUT 2011.

(But if you really must know)

How do you make these costumes?

They are a joint-taskforce creation between my assistant Lisa and me. We toss around ideas for a few months about different costumes and how they could be constructed. She does all the heavy-crafting. She’s a licensed carpenter, among many other talents.

Was Halloween a big thing in your family growing up?

No, for religious reasons my family didn’t celebrate it at all. We’d literally lock all the doors and turn the lights off so people would think we weren’t home and wouldn’t come trick-or-treating.

I didn’t dress up for Halloween for the first time until I went to college. 

Is the foosball costume mobile?

Like most things in our smartphone-driven era, it does have a mobile version. See it move here.

But how did you float in the air in your foosball costume photo?
Check out this video on my YouTube channel.

How long can you balance in the flamingo pose?
Only long enough to take a picture or kick a soccer ball.
