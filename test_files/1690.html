Dames at Play #2

August 29, 2011 By Karen

Jenna started us off the other day, talking about her interests/hobbies outside of her writing life. What exactly do Deadline Dames get up to when they’re not, well, on deadline?


Dame Kaz (geeky and PROUD)

I collect comics. Comic books. Whatever you call ‘em, I buy ‘em.


In fact, I’m sort of starting my collection again because – like many people of a certain age – I sold my entire collection several years back when I desperately needed the money. Do I regret it now? Hell, yeah! But… hey, it had to be done at the time. These things happen. And I get to experience the ‘fun’ of starting all over again. *cough*


Having sat down to write this post (very late in the day – sorry about that), I genuinely don’t know what to say or where to start. Comics are serious business to me and I am totally passionate about them! I know it makes me a huge geek, but there you have it. I used to keep my comics obsession a little quiet, and people just knew me as a Wonder Woman fan. But it goes way deeper than that. I bought my first comic at the age of about seven:


This one, in fact.

(Click on pics to embiggen – may need to click thru twice.)


After Wonder Woman, came Dazzler:





I’m not going to link to all of the names/titles on Wikipedia or wherever. You can Google them if you’re interested, and really… who doesn’t know Wonder Woman? I’ll forgive you for not having heard of Dazzler.


So, yeah… I’ve bought and read comics since a very young age. I’d spend my pocket money on them, along with my brother, and we’d collect our favourites and swap issues every month. I’ve read so many different titles over the years, although of course there were times in my life where I was more interested in other things and comics would take a backseat.


But I always, always go back to them.


Comics can be cool.




Another favourite...




There’s so much variety out there, too. If you’re not into superheroes, then there is definitely a comic book or graphic novel for you. Into YA (sub)urban fantasy and modern tales of Faerie? Try Jamie McKelvie‘s SUBURBAN GLAMOUR:





Want something with no fantasy elements at all? I could recommend a ton of stuff, including Brian Wood and Ryan Kelly’s critically acclaimed LOCAL:





I have so many favourite creators, too many to list here. It would be silly to even try because I love a ridiculous number of writers and artists. I go out of my way to get my hands on anything Greg Rucka writes. Also Marjorie M. Liu – a name most fans of urban fantasy or paranormal romance will be familiar with. Marjorie writes some great comics for Marvel, I particularly enjoy X-23:





For anyone who thinks that superhero comics (of which I admit I’m still hugely fond) are just filled with fighting and superpowers, try reading something featuring my favourite ‘super couple’ – Luke Cage and Jessica Jones. As written by Brian Michael Bendis, we get a realistic relationship struggling amid the crazy life of superheroes:



From New Avengers #47


I’m not sure I can even put into words why I love comics so much, because there’s a huge mixture of things: nostalgia, adventure, escapism, realism, fantasy, drama, fun… magic. I think that must be it – there’s just something truly magical about the medium of comics. It’s the unique alchemy of words and pictures combined that makes story and meaning come alive. That’s the thing that calls me back, time and time again.



(Kaz is reading Captain America and the Secret Avengers written by Kelly Sue DeConnick.)
