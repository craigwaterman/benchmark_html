Painesville students do not currently undergo drug testing, but that could change

September 24, 2015 · 12:01 pm

Painesville stu­dents do not cur­rently undergo drug test­ing, but that could change. Super­in­ten­dent John Shep­ard said the North­east Ohio school dis­trict cam­pus is con­sid­er­ing revamp­ing school pol­icy to allow ran­dom drug tests this year, par­tic­u­larly for stu­dent ath­letes and those involved in extracur­ric­u­lar activ­i­ties. The move fol­lows the appar­ent suc­cess of nearby school dis­tricts who use the test­ing pro­ce­dures. The Men­tor School Dis­trict has used ran­dom K-9 sweeps as well as breathalzy­ers at school dances and extracur­ric­u­lar events, but say their zero tol­er­ance pol­icy focuses on pre­ven­tion. Shep­ard said the school board plans to exam­ine the pos­si­bil­ity of imple­ment­ing a ran­dom­ized test­ing pol­icy at an upcom­ing meet­ing.
