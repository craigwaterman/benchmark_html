Welcome to Halfway to Human

Halfway to Human isn’t just about zombies, it’s about humanity and the struggle to stay free from the brain eating hordes that lurk just out of arms reach.

Boston isn’t the only city in danger of being over-run by bitey things, oh no.

Maybe your cursor is hovering just over the back button on your browser right about now. We’d like to think that perhaps we’ve intrigued you.

It’s not about zombies. Or, not entirely.

It became hard to ignore in the winter of 2006, that we were really bored. We were caught up in routine: commute, work, gym, bars, movies, clubs. Even our “freetime” had started to feel rote. The short version of the story is that we decided to organize a Zombie March and encouraged people to show up in Davis Square dressed as the undead. Over a hundred people showed up in Davis one April afternoon – most as zombies, some as zombie hunters, some as pirates and still others as Texans.

Few moments in recent years rival the hour we spent ambling down Mass. Ave. with a hundred strangers covered in fake blood. For weeks afterward people emailed us with stories from the day, people they met, reactions they received on the train ride home, how they were still finding dried up drops of fake blood around their apartment. And nearly a year later we are in touch with many of the people we met that fine April afternoon – including the incredibly talented Yuppie Zombie who has created this website.

Halfway to Human is about creating a network of folks who want a unique citylife -one that can be bold and irreverant, campy and carefree or meaningful and inspiring. So look around, post your events, write a rant or ask a question. Hopefully, you will use this site to connect with other people in town and create a great event of your own. Cheers.
