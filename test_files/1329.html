About Alla Goniodsky


I am a professional artist performing oil/mixed media paintings, inks, paper sculptures and decoart. B.A graduated from the Academy of Theater, Music and Cinematography in St. Peterburg, Russia. Former member of The National Association of Russian Artists. Number of my artworks are purchased by Backrushin State Theatrical Museum (Moscow,Russia) and State Museum of Children’s Theaters. My works are in numerous private collections in Russia and Israel, Canada, and the USA. Since 1998 I have been working in Seattle, WA, exhibiting my artwork every year at some best art venues in Northwest.
Here is my story.


“Destined to be an artist…” No, it’s not about me. I wasn’t destined to be an artist. I had no choice but to become an artist.

My parents were well educated, good hearted, loving people, but both of them were neither especially interested in art, nor they directed my attention towards art. I began drawing when I was four. Actually, since then I’ve never done anything in my whole life but draw, paint, create. At a very early age I came to realize that it’s the best way for me to express my feelings and to communicate with the world around me. Now that I am a very grown up person and highly experienced professional artist, it is still is.

As for my primary education, I got the best schooling whatsoever in terms of art: it was the Specialized Art School by the State Academy of Art in St.Petersburg, Russia. For the graduates of this school the future was predetermined. At that time already my way of life was… art. At the age of 22 I found myself to become a professional stage artist, having 5 years of academic training from St.Petersburg Theatrical Academy. Staging after staging, first in provincial theaters and puppet shows, and then in the established Moscow puppet theaters in Russia turned me into a well known professional when I was still quite young.

A stage artist creates in the most exciting world, world of theatre, “living” in different ages- ancient, medieval, modern; being utterly absorbed in different atmosphere – comical, tragedy, drama. The play characters in puppet theatre - invented, made and dressed up by me - become my good acquaintances and friends. They become my team. And as team members they drift from play to play never letting down their leader, the one who gave them life.

Working on each staging, an artist uses to make hundreds of sketches and drawings. 2-3 of these, the best ones, are left for the shows, and gradually turn into artwork. That’s what happened to me. My paintings grew out of my theatre draft material, and become artworks of full value. I am tied to the theatre, soul and mastership. The bonds are so tight, that in time my theatre characters moved to my paintings and drawings, found new fantasy environments there, and I was blessed with faithful “companions”. They change costumes and play new roles. I put my own thoughts, feelings and emotions into their postures and gestures.
