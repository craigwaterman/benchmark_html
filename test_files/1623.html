5 Reasons Why You Shouldn't Quit Your Job To Travel

Now, don't get your panties in a wad...Why you shouldn't quit your job to travel JUST YET. ;)

This post is for everyone itching to file that resignation letter to travel.

I have been seeing numerous beautiful posts on social media about folks who have finally gone out, dropped everything, and packed their bags to see the world. I personally admire vagabonds who have found the courage to quit their Corporate careers to brave unknown roads all over the globe. I follow these rad souls with bright child-like eyes looking through the screen of my laptop hoping that one day, I too, can explore the world just like them.

Anyone who works 8 hours a day and 5 times a week will understand how disheartening it is to read about this dream while you actually want to be living it instead. I have browsed through various blog posts on why you should quit your job to explore the world... and trust me, I won't deny it at all, but I actually contemplated on working on that resignation letter myself hoping I can go on with packing my bags to get out and start off early with my journey.

Now, don't get disappointed. I did not quit my job. This post will not be about my story on finally bidding the Corporate life goodbye. I still work 8 or even 9 hours a day from Monday to Friday. I pretty much travel familiar roads during weekdays, see the same people, tackle my way through busy concrete streets, and as much as I want to be in my bikini 24/7 I actually follow the Corporate dress code religiously... BUT I AM NOT MISERABLE. I travel more than the usual employee, I have backpacked, I have walked unfamiliar roads, I have explored numerous new places, I have financed my trips with what I get at work, and most importantly... I feel as alive and amazing as these beautiful beautiful (yes, I said that twice because I just can't stress how gorgeous their souls are) souls that inspire me everyday through their posts that share generous stories about adventure and travel... about self discovery through experiencing the world.

No, I don't think it's because I'm a coward. I'm not scared to leave my job nor do I belittle those who were courageous (I salute you) enough to do so. I have experienced having nothing left after a wondrous trip and have sacrificed more than the desirable to finance each and every one of them... but as you go through these experiences you'll slowly realise that there's more to that resignation letter and passport. 

I think it's time I shed some light on 5 things you might want to consider before sending out that resignation letter:

1. Not everyone is a traveler (Well, not yet at least)
As ridiculous as this may sound, it is true. During my trips out of town and visits to unfamiliar places I have seen these backpackers and have admired them immensely for the amount of sacrifice they put into what they do.. from settling with eating instant/processed noodles... to spending the night at airports and bus stations... to wearing the same shirt for 2 days and skipping showers.. man, do they sacrifice! Having witnessed these legit vagabonds, I truly believe that not everyone is a traveler but everyone SHOULD travel. Everyone should experience the world because there is a certain awakening that an unfamiliar place does to the human soul... it is a phenomena that should be cultivated in everyone. 

Now, being a traveler is different. I think it takes a great deal of will and sacrifice. This isn't something someone can just jump into. You have to be mentally, physically, and emotionally prepared to embrace whatever your journey will entail.  You'll have to asses your willingness to let go of everything you want that will put everything you need to travel at risk. Travelling is a leisure one can prepare for periodically... while being a traveler is a lifestyle. You will need to eat, sleep, and breathe anything that will provide resources to fuel your journey wherever you are. Asses your willingness to let go of a great amount of comfort you currently have.  - Something I'm not prepared to take on just yet.  Ask yourself, are you? Because being a traveler isn't just about pretty photographs on Instagram.

2. You have to be prepared
I mentioned above that you have to be prepared mentally, physically, emotionally, and most importantly financially.

Mentally and Emotionally
You have to be prepared to endure the stress that travel could and will bring to your mind. As much as travelling is an avenue for self discovery... it will constantly require you to use your mind and heart. You have to make sure you are mentally and emotionally stable to take on whatever comes your way. From missing home to figuring out how the hell you're going to pay for food and shelter. This is going to be a roller coaster ride... from being absolutely, without a doubt, happy... to suddenly dealing with sadness and fear... you'll have to be ready.

Physically
Travelling is exhausting. You will constantly be moving from one place to another. You have to make sure you are ready to take on the physical stress it brings with it. You'll have to make sure you can physically endure the long walks, climbs, and sprints that the journey will bring with it.  Can you imagine climbing hills and walking long distances while you're physically unfit? In addition to this, rest will become a privilege. It will get difficult to get. I have seen backpackers find rest in the most peculiar places and in the most awkward ways! I once saw two backpackers brushing their teeth in Edsa because they just woke up from a nap they had to take on the streets of Manila. You ready for that? I don't think I am.. just yet. 

Financially
You can no longer be a material girl living in a material world. You have to be financially ready. This means no more 5-10 pairs of shoes, new gadgets, and constantly adding outfits to your closet (which you won't have).You have to save whatever you have to make sure you can pay for your physiological needs and your immediate needs. Aside from saving, you need to make sure you have a sustainable source of income wherever you might end up at. You have to make sure that you're equipped with the necessary certifications and licenses that will allow you to earn and save in different places. Remember, you will be in transit and mobile most of the time. Read and research on certifications and courses to take. Have you done that? I'm currently getting certified as a dive instructor so I don't think I'm ready for this.. just yet.

3. You will be leaving a lot of people and things behind
This is why you have to be emotionally stable. You will be leaving home. You'll have to prepare yourself for the amount of miss and homesick you'll be feeling on a daily basis. You'll be out there experiencing things you wish you could share with the ones you love.. the ones you left back home. If you're currently in a relationship, are you ready to be far away form your partner? are you prepared to only hear from them whenever possible? You have to be.

4.Respect your Responsibilities
Let's not fail to acknowledge that you must  not step on anyone's toes. You have to take in account the responsibilities you will be turning your back away from and your willingness to do so. 

5. You can see the world even when you're employed
Traveling is not synonymous to being jobless (as mentioned above). I am not a traveler... I go to work on weekdays but make sure I spend my weekends on the road. For now, I can safely say I get the best of both worlds. I use my weekend getaways as my source of strength to do even better at work and a way to motivate me to prepare for the next adventure lined up. I take advantage of days I can take that are off work. Most importantly, I am happy. I am making the most out of what I have and what I can do right now. No matter how tempting it is to just drop everything and  go for what I want to do... I acknowledge that I am not ready to do so, just yet.. but when the time comes that everything falls into their proper place, I'll know when to do the needful and it will be the same with you too.

In the meantime, as I am at peace with the fact that I love my job as much as I love my short lived trips. I am currently travelling. I am currently exploring. I am currently seeing the world. I am establishing soul connection in every place I go, and I am currently in awe of how much lessons I get out of these short trips. 

It is not true that you HAVE to quit your job to travel. You see, I am scheduled to fly to Kota Kinabalu this May, London this June, and numerous other local destinations until December. Your job will not keep you from doing what you love... Travel and work can go hand in hand and you can make it work.. but when the time comes that your uneasiness gets uncontrollable, that your bed no longer feels like it's yours,  that your home feels like it's just a house, when your mind wanders more often than it doesn't,  when your soul craves for much more than you can give with where you are... you'll know what to do. This is when you'll know you're ready.



Mahalo!
