Run sweet run - my life as a diabetic runner

Tuesday, December 8, 2009

A new PR that's setting the tone to keep on going :)

This weekend I ran the St. Jude Memphis half marathon again. I was apprehensive about it though. Although I've been running quite a bit for the last several months now, something always happens to me on race day. A few years ago, I just didn't finish after stepping into a pot hole, last year I cramped bad at mile 9 in Overton Park (same place of the pot hole incident... go figure) and it aggravated my plantar fasciitis issues... anyhow. I was afraid.

Afraid? Really? Running 6 miles every other day for the last 2 months? Running a 3-mile race with a pace of 8:30 minute/mile??? Really???

Yep. I was scared. So much that after meeting with my team, the AutoZone Extra Milers, I left the office several minutes before them and walked to the starting line, to "get acclimated" I said. The truth is, I was trying to get myself into a racing mindset. There was so much at stake, personally at least. So I walked to the corrals and chose one that had a comfortable pace: corral 11, with a 10:40 - 11 minute/mile pace... and I stood there, in the cold Saturday morning, loosening up my legs, watching everyone getting ready, covering my fear behind a pair of sunglasses... I couldn't see anyone from my team... and we had pretty colorful shirts :) it made me feel a little weird, but better I guess. And then, it begun.

One by one, each corral began taking on the race course, and slowly, mine was heading towards the starting line. I kept saying to myself "this is it. you can do it." -- I removed my gloves and took my phone out of my belt to text my wife: "I know I can"... a phrase that's become my mantra for so many years now... she replied not 30 seconds later "I know you can". It was a boost of energy, and so I spent the next two minutes walking to the front of the pack... and then, the starting line was there, and so were a couple of my team mates... I got kind of scared I admit... panicky... I felt like I was going to have to either keep up with their pace or keep them far enough behind me so that I could concentrate on running... and then, without any more time to worry about it, we began running.

I quickly began running and moving to the front of the pack, realizing that my group was actually too slow... one by one, I began weaving through the crowd of runners in what felt to be a slow race. I passed the mile 1 marker at 9:30 min/mile... I smiled :) and kept going... I decided to let go and enjoy the race, so I began absorbing each step, each wave and cheer of support from the bystanders, and it felt great. I passed the first one of my team mates. Then I turned to Riverside drive, a very crowded stretch of the race and I kept weaving through traffic, calling out loud "coming through on your left (or right)"... water stop? nah... let's keep going... I reached Beale street and climbed the steep incline effortless and that made me feel really good as I passed more runners and another one of my team mates :)

Third street was ahead of us, the photographers and bystanders cheering were absolutely inspiring. As I was getting close to AutoZone Park, the crowd was such that the road narrowed to less than 20 feet and that made me emotional. I actually cried for a second, so excited and enjoying each and every step of the race.

I continued running and passing more runners, left and right. The next landmark, St. Jude Hospital campus, was the most emotional of all. The children, hairless, standing with the crowd and cheering, was another emotional moment. There were tears on my face for the entire segment of the race, until we left the facility... and I refocused.

North Parkway was a breeze. I passed the 10K mark at under 59 minutes and I was feeling great. But then I saw my nemesis in the distance: Overton Park. I entered that segment with caution. The roads there are not the best and it's easy to miss a step if you don't pay attention. The traffic was bad. As I passed two more of my team mates I noticed a girl trying to weave through the crowd and I caught up with her... we both were struggling with the number of runners in such a small space, so I said to her "do you want to play fartlek?" -- she smiled and said "yeah!!" and so the game of chase begun... it was a very fun 1-mile chase weaving through the park... once we left the wooded area and saw the Memphis College of Art, we parted ways, she actually took off at an amazingly fast pace and said "good luck!". It was cool. Not one minute later I passed another one of my team mates, who seemed to be struggling and was walking. In an effort to encourage him I asked if he was ok and then said "c'mon, let's keep going man!" :) and took off... He tried to keep up with me but fell back right before leaving the park... and so I took Poplar with renewed energy...

I ran miles 10 to 12 effortless and was feeling great :) after the mile 12 marker I had a small tweech on my right leg, so I looked at my watch and decided to slow down my pace. I was cruisin' :)

With AutoZone Park ahead of me, I felt invincible! I was jogging to the park. My original goal was to finish under 2:40 hours... I was way ahead of that goal :) less than 100 yards from the entrance I looked up to the sky and said "thank you God for letting me do this without any cramps or any such shit..." I guess He didn't like me saying shit... I cramped 15 yards from he entrance to the park :) just a reminder of how humble we should be...

I stopped. Stretched for a few seconds and began running again... joggin I should say... my leg was hurting and was stiff because of the cramp :) I can't wait to see the pictures!!! :) LOL

But so I finished in 2 hours 4 minutes and 46 seconds. 1 hour and 2 minutes under last year's result and 1 hour 6 minutes under my last half marathon (Germantown last March).

Today, I feel great. No pain. And regained confidence that I'm on the right track, diet wise, running wise :)

I'll keep on running :)

- M
