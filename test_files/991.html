Let’s celebrate National Small Business Week!



At 99designs, we’re passionate about helping small businesses succeed. So while National Small Business Week 2014 officially starts next Monday (running from May 12 to 16), we thought we’d kick off the festivities a little early with free designs to help you celebrate.

We partnered up with 99designer PinchStudio to create this awesome poster for you to print out and display in your storefront, thanking your community for supporting small businesses by shopping local. No storefront? No problem. You’ll also get a matching Facebook cover image to share your small business pride.

Celebrating since 1963

In 1963, President John F. Kennedy proclaimed the first National Small Business Week. Every U.S. president since then has signed a proclamation to continue NSBW, now coming up to its 51st anniversay. The numbers are striking: More than 50% of Americans either own or work for a small business and each year, small businesses create two out three new jobs in the U.S.

A variety of events leading up to, and during, National Small Business Week will feature national level speakers, expert panels on entrepreneurial subjects – like financing, or business innovation – as well as networking and business matchmaking opportunities.

Making it big

This year’s theme – “Making it big: Small Biz Success in a Mobile World” – isn’t unfamiliar territory for our loyal blog readers. We’ve helped creative entrepreneurs take advantage of the latest technologies by sharing how to develop online brand recognition and showing the way to beautiful mobile app design. The folks at U.S. Small Business Administration have many more of these insights to help you take your business to the next level. National celebrations will be held in San Francisco, Kansas City, Boston, and Washington D.C.
