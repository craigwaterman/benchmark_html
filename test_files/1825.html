Bio

Frederic J. Holzberger & Fredric’s Corporation

Frederic J. Holzberger is the Founder, CEO & President of Fredric’s Corporation and owner of the Aveda Fredric’s Institute in Hyde Park, Ohio and Indianapolis, Indiana. While working as a full-time electrician, he received his Bachelor of Science Degree in Marketing from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio. After graduation, Frederic worked as a sales and marketing specialist for Thomas & Betts Corporation, a leading electrical supplier. During his tenure at Thomas & Betts, he married a Licensed Cosmetologist and supported her in opening hair salons in the greater Cincinnati area.

Frederic began operations of Fredric’s Corporation in 1983 with a strategic focus on the beauty, health and fitness industries. In 1985, Aveda Corporation granted Fredric’s Corporation exclusive distribution rights in the State of Ohio for Aveda Corporation’s line of Environmental Beauty Products. From 1985 through 1992, Fredric’s Corporation continued to experience growth and success as an exclusive distributor of Aveda serving Ohio, Indiana, Michigan and Kentucky. Fredric’s Corporation was honored and proud to celebrate 20 years of service to the salon and spa industry in 2005. On October 31, 2005, Aveda Corporation began servicing the Fredric's Corporation network directly and Fredric’s Corporation began its focus on expanding the Aveda Fredric’s Institutes.

In 1994, Mr. Holzberger entered the retail industry with the opening of the Aveda Experience Center at the Kenwood Towne Centre in Cincinnati, Ohio. From 1994-2001, Mr. Holzberger opened 10 Aveda Experience Centers. The majority of the Aveda Environmental Experience Centers operated by Mr. Holzberger ranked in the top 35% in sales of all Aveda Environmental Experience Centers located in the United States and Canada. In February of 2003, the Aveda/Fredric’s owned stores were acquired by Aveda Corporation allowing for more support to the consumer and salon network.

In 1999, Frederic, along with his wife Julie, opened the Aveda Fredric’s Institute located in Cincinnati, Ohio, offering courses to become licensed in Cosmetology, Esthiology and Manicuring focusing not only on high standards in beauty, health and education, but on healthy lifestyles and environmental awareness. Students focus on theory, demonstrations and extensive practical, hands-on experience. Frederic believes that cosmetology is not just about cutting hair, it is about caring for people and the environment. It allows individuals to explore their creativity through a remarkable range of careers while earning a great living. This 6800 sq. ft. state of the art facility was built using the most environmental and ergonomic materials available from the natural flooring to the energy efficient lighting and promotes a healthy non-smoking, cellular phone free environment.

The Aveda/Fredric’s Institute has received numerous awards including the Ohio State Board of Cosmetology Award of Excellence which has been given to the Institute each year since the awards’ inception in 1999. This Award of Excellence is given to schools that have placed in the top 20% of all schools as measured by passage percentage and recognize that excellence is a result of the effort and energy of the staff and students. The Institute was a Cincinnati Better Business Bureau Torch Award for Marketplace Ethics finalist in October of 2001. This award is given in recognition of a business’s outstanding commitment to ethical standards in their relationship to their customers, employees, suppliers, competitors and surrounding community. The Institute’s goal is to provide quality education and services while helping to fill the growing demand for licensed cosmetologists in the beauty industry. They have also been recognized by Cincinnati Magazine and City Beat as the Best of the City and most recently by CinWeekly publication. A second Aveda Fredric’s Institute opened in the fall of 2005 in Indianapolis, Indiana. The grand opening celebration of this 16,000 square foot environmentally friendly facility is planned for March 12, 2006.

On the surface, many things do not always appear as they seem. The opposite is true of Frederic Holzberger and Fredric’s Corporation. It is his, and the Corporation’s, daily mission to appear and be an environmental, socially conscious corporation striving to make a difference that is visible and tangible. It is evident that the path that Frederic Holzberger has chosen to take for himself and his corporation, “. . . to put back more on this earth than [we] take away,” has led to a natural progression of building blocks for Fredric’s Corporation. Frederic has shown that as a person or company grows, what you give and take from the roots you establish will nourish not only oneself, but others around you for years to come.

Project Daymaker is a non-profit organization Frederic Holzberger founded in 1995 to service and teach the less fortunate proper hair and skin care with the hope of enhancing their employment opportunities. Project Daymaker is a successful traveling salon housed in a Winnebago. The vision behind Project Daymaker is licensed professionals donating hair and beauty services for those in need and with the aid of over 800 volunteers, Project Daymaker has touched over 10,000 people in Fredric’s Corporation’s four state distribution region.

Taking his environmental commitment one step further, Frederic and Fredric's Corporation has joined the US Green Building Council. Their mission statement reads: The U.S. Green Building Council is the nation's foremost coalition of leaders from across the building industry working to promote buildings that are environmentally responsible, profitable, and healthy places to live and work. He is proud to be a part of an organization that is leading the way in providing alternative resources in environmentally conscious building materials.

In 2002, Frederic partnered with Cincinnati Habitat for Humanity to build one of the first Habitat Homes in Hyde Park, an upscale Cincinnati suburb. It was a unique partnership allowing an avenue for the Fredric’s team, families, salons, Experience Centers and the Aveda/Fredric’s Institute staff and students, to participate in helping build a future for a local father and his family. It continues to be a rich, rewarding experience; construction was completed on a second Habitat/Fredric’s Corporation home in 2003 and rehabilitation of a fire-damaged home was completed in 2004. A fourth home is being rehabilitated and will be ready for a family to call it home in 2005. A fifth home will be renovated in 2006 in Cincinnati and Fredric’s is now partnering with Habitat for Humanity Indianapolis and will partner in renovating a home in Indianapolis


Fredric’s Corporation’s socially responsible activities include employment of Fairfield High School students through a CO-OP Student Program. Time and money are contributed annually to the Cincinnati Children’s Theatre, University of Cincinnati School of Fashion Design, Cincinnati School for Creative and Performing Arts and the Haven House of Hamilton (a family shelter). Food and gifts are donated continuously to the underprivileged in Butler County. The Earth and Humanity foundation was created to further the efforts of AIDS and Breast Cancer research and awareness, Project Daymaker and The Upledger Foundation Dolphin Assisted Therapy Program. These organizations were chosen by vote of the Aveda/Fredric’s team. Aveda products are donated to St. John Project Daymaker in Over the Rhine, Cincinnati, Ohio. This salon is the first of its kind and was the beginning of a relationship between Frederic Holzberger and Sister Bonnie Steinlage. Fredric’s Corporation has also been involved in Aveda’s Give to the Earth Foundation and has received support for Project Daymaker through the Give to the Earth Foundation and the BBSI Maximizer’s grant program.
