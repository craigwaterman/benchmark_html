Top Tip #3 - Selling Your Home

5 Nov 2012


At DesignBuild Homes we often come across clients needing to sell their home. There are many reasons, mostly so they can start their building journey with us. Selling your home is a significant and important step and here are a few hints and tips from our experts.

Before you put it on the market

Declutter and Depersonalise

Clutter within the home can make it seem less spacious and untidy. Declutter by putting all excess furniture and personal items into storage and depersonalise the space of photos and trinkets so potential buyers can envisage themselves in the home.

Upgrade

Minor upgrades of fixtures can make all the difference, especially if you're on a budget. Upgrading plastic door handles in kitchen and bathrooms to inexpensive chrome fixtures will modernise outdated bathrooms.

Repair

While you might be able to live with that missing door handle or ripped fly screen, it's something that will quickly put buyers off. Make a list, bring in the tradesman and get it fixed before you put your home on the market.

Street Appeal

The street appeal of your home is the first live impression of the home that potential buyers will have. Spend a few weekends weeding the garden, trimming bushes, washing the outside of the home and giving it a fresh coat of paint if necessary.

Good Photography

Invest in good photography for your marketing campaign. The photos will be the first impression potential buyers will have and may decide whether they attend an open inspection or not.

 

For the Open for Inspection

Fresh flowers on the day will give the home a lift, but don't go overboard with things like fresh bread baking in the oven, coffee brewing or music playing. Buyers will think you are trying to hide smells or noise from the home.

Open all the windows and doors to demonstrate the natural lighting throughout the home. Open curtains, blinds and shutters and ensure they are vacuumed and free of dust. Don't turn on every light in the house as buyers may think you are trying to hide a dark home.

Tidy wardrobes, pantries and cupboards. Buyers will snoop in storage spaces and open wardrobes and doors, so tidy and stack items neatly for presentation.

Remove pets during the open for inspection. Take them to a friend's place or out for a walk and make sure any pet odours are removed from the home.

Use a plastic storage tub to take very personal things from the bathroom cupboards such as wet toothbrushes, hair brushes and other toiletries out of the house and with you during the open for inspection. Remove wet or used dishes from the sink and dishwasher as well.

The Kitchen Triangle

23 Oct 2012



The Triangle Rule is a commonly used principle when designing a kitchen. The triangle is a conceptual triangle from the centre of the sink to the centre of of the fridge, then to the cooktop and back to the sink.

The principle of the kitchen triangle is to create efficiency within the three main functions of the kitchen - food storage, preparation and cooking. The principle was developed in the 1940s when kitchens were simple and while today's more modern and complex kitchens need to take into account some additional features, the principle remains the same.

The food storage function mainly refers to the refrigerator, however today could also include the pantry and butlers pantry. The preparation function refers to the area of the sink, however benchtops and dishwashers should also be included. The cooking function remains as it always has the cooktop and stove area.

The kitchen triangle works for all types of kitchen layouts like the common galley style, U shape and L shape.

The kitchen work triangle is a helpful tool, although don't let it inhibit you from thinking outside the triangle when it comes to kitchen space planning.

Top Tip #1 - Cross Flow Ventilation

8 Oct 2012
 
Cross ventilation is a simple system that will reduce the need for airconditioning by taking advantage of natural breezes to cool your home.
 
The key to using cross flow ventilation is to create openings on opposite sides of the room. Thoughtful placement of windows and doors will promote fresh air to enter and flow through the room and then to the exit.
 
DesignTip: Consider blinds and curtain that allow some airflow. Mesh and woven materials are excellent alternatives to 100% blockout products.
 
Designing with good cross flow ventilation is environmentally friendly, ecologically smart and has the long term benefits of lower energy and water bills. All DesignBuild Homes designers are accredited HIA GreenSmart Professionals.

The Block Challenge

24 Sep 2012



Unless you have a completely flat, traditional sized block of land clear of sewer lines and stormwater, there will most likely be some issues when designing a new home.

It may a sloping site, difficult soil conditions or council imposed restrictions that present a challenge, however it's devising a solution that will dictate the outcome of the project.

This narrow lot also had a sewer line and manhole running directly through the line of the property. Council had imposed an easement over the sewer that meant there had to be permanent access to the manhole at all times.

The clients approached DesignBuild with this dilemma, with a brief to create a spacious family home on an already narrow site. The building designer's solution to the challenge of the manhole was to design a central, covered courtyard with removable decking over the manhole. The solution satisfied council requirements while still addressing the brief from the clients.

The central covered courtyard creates a private, outdoors space for year round living, while isolating the family space at the rear of the home for relaxed, casual living. The upper floor mimics the lower floor, creating a completely private master bedroom suite with walk in wardrobe and ensuite.

Guy Kits from DesignBuild Homes says almost every lot will have a design challenge and it's how a solution is found for that challenge that will determine the outcome of the project. "The design challenges of a lot can be as minor as some buried asbestos and as major as termites in a home in a demolition control precinct. A successful building project is determined by how these problems are overcome. In this case, designing the home over the manhole with removable decking was an innovative and practical solution to the dilemma."

If you have a block of land with design challenges, speak to the team at DesignBuild Homes about the solutions and options available.

Split Level Homes

2 Sep 2012


Split levels homes, commonly designed on sloping lots, present an unique opportunity to create multi level zones, multiple views and interesting design.

Guy Kits from DesignBuild Homes says they have long recognised the value of split level homes as an unique design feature, "In a split level home there are opportunities to have multiple living zones and multiple panoramas which can increase the value of a property."

Split level homes are far more economical solution today to building on a sloping lot as the design works in harmony with the natural fall of the terrain rather than cutting into it. In sharply sloping sites, non-living spaces like laundries, wine cellars and media rooms are located against the terrain while the living spaces are designed towards the outlook to maximise the views and natural breezes.

DesignBuild Homes specialises in designing split level homes on terrains that other builders turn away. The builder has won a number of awards for homes built on sloping lots, and say home owners should look at them with fresh eyes when it comes to design. Getting your Greens

27 Aug 2012

 

 
 
 
 
 
Building green need not mean a tree house in the bush. DesignBuild Homes create homes which are contemporary and eco friendly.
 
All of DesignBuild Homes' designers are HIA GreenSmart professionals with recognised skills in environmental awareness and sustainable design and construction practices. 
 
Home are oriented to capture the cross breezes for natural ventilation, while eco friendly materials that are easily renewable or have long term green values are used.
 
Common green friendly materials include easily renewable bamboo flooring, Expanded Polystyrene (EPS), a lightweight material with high thermal insulating values and fibre cement sheeting, a low embodied energy product with low maintenance. Frames can also be constructed with Forestry Stewardship Council (FSC) certified timber meaning the timber comes from forests that had been responsibly managed.
 
Guy Kits from DesignBuild Homes says building green is about short term and long term benefits. "Using easily renewable materials such as bamboo and forestry timber is a good green contribution during the short term of building, but long term benefits of building green, such as lower maintenance and lower heating and cooling bills, are equal considerations."

The Essential Demolition Checklist

19 Jun 2012


With the limited amount of land available in the inner city, DesignBuild Homes has seen a significant increase in the number of knockdown rebuilds over the past couple of years. Demolition is one (but very important) aspect of this process that needs to be completed prior to construction

If you choose to arrange the demolition yourself, below is a list of items that you need to ensure are completed by either yourself or your demolition contractor

Before demolition
 

  1. Ensure you have a demolition approvals from your local council.
  2. Arrange disconnection of all services such as electrical, telephone and gas. This can sometimes take 6-8 weeks for processing with your service supplier so it’s best to plan ahead.
  3. Arrange for existing sewer connections will need to be capped and inspected by the local plumbing department. Allow sufficient time as your local council may also have long lead times for bookings.
  4. Ensure your contract for demolition includes the safe removal and disposal of asbestos removal (if required) in accordance with Workplace health and safety guidelines. 

After Demolition



  1. Ensure the site is left clean and clear of all building materials including all concrete, timber etc. (suitable for construction to commence).
  2. Ensure the builder (DesignBuild) inspects the site prior to the removal of the machinery to ensure that the site is acceptable.


This is not a comprehensive list and there may be some other aspects specific to your site that may require additional attention. It's advised to speak to your builder, council and demolition contractor directly before commencing the process. DesignBuild Homes are specialists in knockdown rebuilds and can arrange demolition for you. Speak to one of our property consultants today about your project.

Fabulous Facades

The facade of your home makes the first impression and is an integral part of the making your home unique. Whether it's ultra contemporary or ultra conservative, the facade can be completely custom designed to suit you.

Tony Domingo, DesignBuild Homes' building designer, says there are infinite options to creating a facade and understanding and designing a facade comes through close consultation with the client. "One on one meetings with the client are the best way to understand they type of facade they desire and there are endless roof styles, materials and colour combinations to create an individual facade," he says.

Whether the home is completely custom designed or a modified design from DesignBuild's standard portfolio, the facade is a way to give your home unique street appeal. The smallest change to colour or roof pitch on a standard design can give your home a completely different look while a completely custom facade will make your home totally individual.

While there may be some restrictions to the creating a facade, generally a completely custom design can be created, "If the home is a demolition control precinct there may be some requirements regarding balconies and windows, but we're very familiar with these guidelines and have worked within them many times," says Tony.
