Drinks on the house

Details
Published: 20 September 2015

After three days of testing, checking and being cautious, the decision was made this morning for the unpleasant feeding / draining tube via my nose to my stomach to be removed, which I assure you is not only a significant step, it is extremely welcome. Next step is to see if I can drink water without nausea, if that goes ok for 24 hrs hrs then solid food will be gradually introduced over a few days. I get food now by an IV in the neck which sounds and looks a lot worse than it actually is. The first 7hrs of water drinking has gone smoothly.

CT Scan

  • Print
  • Email
Details
Published: 17 September 2015
Ward doctor arranged CT scan today afternoon. The recovery went slow. They liked to see whether anything wrong in the abdomen. The doctor came at evening with the good news - CT scan result is good. The drainage is in place and do not need any intervention. The 'slow' is purely individual recovery situation. We just need to give time to wait for stomach working. And there was bacteria found in the earlier test which means the cause of infection found. They used antibiotics. They will do some blood test tomorrow to see the effect. The ward doctor looked also happy. He gave me some science course per my request - explained to me what they did in Steve's surgery. They cut part of liver, part of pancreas, part of stomach, whole duodenum, and all gall bladder, and then connect the pancreas and small intestine to stomach. After surgery, once stomach works, the bile should flow to intestine. But there is still too much green fluid came out from the nose tube, which indicates the stomach has not worked well. They need to keep the drainage in the nose and watch the volume until it drops to less than 200ml. I left the hospital in happy mood.
