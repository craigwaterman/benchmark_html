Makeup Inspiration: Taylor Swift’s Old Hollywood Glamour Look

Posted on November 18, 2013 by Tina

10

Taylor Swift is one of those celebrities who looks gorgeous in anything. She usually sticks with glamorous timeless dresses for red carpet events. As for her makeup, she follows the 50/50 rule – smokey and pale lips or nude/lines eyes and red lips combo. I guess you could say she plays it safe and I wouldn’t blame her for that. Hollywood can be rude and you don’t want to end up in the worst dressed list. However, she upped her game at the 2013 VMAs when she sported an old glamour look with a classic pin curls plus her signature red bold lips. She looked womanly and confident. Everyone’s jaws dropped. She’d make Marilyn Monroe very proud.

I drew inspiration from Taylor Swift’s look for the wedding I attended last Tuesday. I wore a navy blue dress and it was just fitting to sport a red lip for a pop of color. Also, a flawless complexion and contours are the perfect finishing touches to this glam makeup.

I started off by applying Nature Republic Orchid Foundation #23 all over my face. It’s a shade lighter than my skin tone but it turns into a perfect match once it reacts to the skin because I’m quite acidic. It’ll also highlight focal point later on especially when I’ve contoured my face. I dabbed a little bit of Collection Lasting Perfection Concealer in Medium to conceal all my spots and blemishes and MAC Pro Longwear Concealer in NC30 under my eyes to give me that wide awake look. Once the face looks flawless, I contoured the corners of my forehead, temples of my cheeks and jawline using LA Colors Liquid Makeup in Black Walnut. This liquid makeup looks really dark so make sure to use it sparingly. I also find the Real Techniques Expert Face Brush amazing at blending this product onto the skin without looking overly sculpted. I set my base with MAC Studio Fix Foundation in NW30 and used the Prestige Pressed Powder in Honey for my undereye as it has a yellowish tone and would keep the brightening effect of the concealer. I brushed a little bit of Nars Bronzer in Laguna in the contoured areas as the liquid bronzer might’ve been erased by the powder. I added a hint of flush on the cheeks with Art Deco’s Romantic Rebel Blush. To finish the skin, I used The Balm’s Mary Lou-Manizer to highlight the temples of my cheeks.

Taylor Swift’s eye makeup is quite simple – a little bit of sparkles, liner and full lashes. I primed my lids with Maybelline’s Color Tattoo in Audacious Asphalt. I brushed Pistol from the Urban Decay Naked 2 Palette all over the lids to intensify the gray color and lined my lower lash line with it as well. To add drama, I lined my eyes with Physician’s Formula Shimmer Strips Eyeliner in Black and dragged the left overs to my lower lash line. I coated my lashes with Maybelline The Hyper Curl Mascara* and wore The Face Shop’s Falsies to give me fuller lashes. I just ran a little bit of eyeliner on top of base of the falsies and smudged it to make it look natural. Dabbed a little bit of The Balm’s Mary Lou-Manizer on my inner corners and brow bones for highlight. I filled in my brows with Shu Uemura H9 Brow Pencil in Acorn. I swiped Charmed I’m Sure lipstick from the MAC x Marilyn Monroe Collection. It’s a dark true red lipstick that’s classic and perfect for the look I’m going for. To complete the retro look, I got the Malibu Rum Beachy Waves from The Blowout Bar. (Review soon!)

What I wore: Nature Republic Orchid Foundation #23, Collection Lasting Perfection Concealer in Medium, MAC Pro Longwear Concealer in NC30, LA Colors Liquid Makeup in Black Walnut, MAC Studio Fix Foundation in NW30, Prestige Pressed Powder in Honey, Nars Bronzer in Laguna, Art Deco’s Romantic Rebel Blush, The Balm’s Mary Lou-Manizer, Maybelline’s Color Tattoo in Audacious Asphalt, Pistol from the Urban Decay Naked 2 Palette, Phycisian’s Formula Shimmer Strips Eyeliner in Black, Shu Uemura H9 Brow Pencil in Acorn, Maybelline The Hyper Curl Mascara, The Face Shop’s Falsies, Charmed I’m Sure Lipstick from the MAC x Marilyn Monroe Collection.

Semi OOTD

It was my first time to sport a look like this and I’m quite pleased! The lady who did my hair even asked where I had my makeup done and she was surprised when I said it was just all me. Yay! I know it’s not exactly like Taylor Swift’s but I guess the idea was there. Anyway, let me know what you think! Thank you for reading! :)
