Roasted vegetables and chickpeas

December 15, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

  I’ve been watching Masterchef a lot lately on my commutes to London. I love the part where the chefs have to do an invention test, particularly as inventing dishes is what we have to do everyday with whatever is in the fridge! Seeing as I’m living with my mum at the moment, she tends to do the shopping so I have to work with whatever I can find in the cupboards. This dish uses few ingredients but creates a filling meal which happens to vegan and packed with healthy vits and mins to help beat the winter blues. 

Serves 2 

Ingredients:

  • 2 carrots
  • 1 large parsnip 
  • 1 can of chickpeas
  • 1/2 white onion
  • 1 large tomato
  • 1/4 tsp cumin seeds 
  • 2 tsp dried herbs 
  • 1 tbsp rapeseed oil 

Method:

  1. Put a large saucepan with water on to boil and heat the oven to 200c.
  2. Peel and chop the carrot and parsnip into large chunks and boil for 7 mins
  3. Meanwhile, add 1 tbsp of oil to a baking tray and place in the oven for 2 mins.
  4. Drain the veg (saving the boiling water) and place on the preheated tray. Add salt, pepper and the dried mixed herbs. Bake for 30mins.
  5. Put the tomato in the boiling water you saved and leave for 5 mins.
  6. Finely chop the onion and sauté on a medium/low heat in a small frying pan with a little oil and 1/4 tsp cumin seeds.
  7. Halfway through cooking the veg, add the drained chickpeas to the roasting tray.
  8. Remove the tomato from the boiling water and cut in half, use the knife to lift the skin of the tomato and peel.
  9. Use a teaspoon to remove the insides of the tomato and discard, finely chop the outer flesh and and to the frying pan. Fry for 2 mins. 
  10. Once the veg has had 30 mins, remove from oven and place on a plate, adding the chickpeas on top.
  11. Finally add the onion and tomato mix and the dish is complete! 

The 5 of the best cheap and healthy foods 

December 6, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

5 of my favourite cheap and healthy foods:
Healthy food does get a bad reputation for being too expensive. And whilst this is sometimes true, it doesn’t mean we can’t all enjoy healthy food on a daily basis. Here are my favourite healthy foods that won’t break the bank…

  
1. Carrots and houmous. You can get 1kg of carrots for £1 at most supermarkets and a pot of houmous for £1. Making each portion come in at less than 25p (if you were to eat the pot of houmous in 4 portions).
2. Avocado. No need for an introduction, avocados are delicious! I like mine with a sprinkling of sea salt and paprika. You can get 4 of the small ripen at home avocados from most supermarkets for £1.50-1.75, making each avocado only 38p! They are so good for you as well, loaded with good fats, fiber and vitamins and minerals. They have also been linked with protecting us against heart disease and cancer.
3. My third choice is frozen berries. At only £2 for 500g at most supermarkets, frozen berries come in a lot cheaper than fresh (£2 for just 150g). Admittedly fresh do taste amazing but frozen taste great in smoothies and make them cold and refreshing. You can also defrost frozen berries in the microwave which makes them juicy, great for topping porridge or yoghurt. 
4. Peanut butter. Peanut butter is a favourite amongst the fitness community as its rich tasting whilst being a low carb, high protein and high fat snack. A spoonful of this nutty deliciousness will help keep you fuller for longer and provide you with protein and fibre. It is relatively cheap especially if you buy it in bulk, with a 1kg tub from Meridian costing £5-6.

5. Bananas. Everyone goes bananas for bananas! They are the full package, cheap, tasty, filling, nutritious and versatile. You can use them in smoothies, freeze them and make banana ice cream (check out my recipe for ‘nice cream’), eat them with yoghurt or the best combination – with peanut butter! 

  

Roasted cauliflower and lentil dish

December 1, 2015December 1, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

Here is a great recipe that happens to be vegan and is totally delicious. This was inspired by a recipe in simply nigella but using lentils instead of chickpeas. The light curry flavours from the cumin goes perfectly with the lentils. Try it and you won’t regret it!

  Ingredients:

  • 1/2 head of cauliflower 
  • 80g puy lentils
  • 1 carrot
  • 1/2 a bunch of Spring greens
  • Vegetable stock
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Method:

  1. Preheat the oven to 200c. Chop the cauliflower into small florets.
  2. Mix the olive oil and spices in a large bowl and add the cauliflower. Mix the cauliflower til it is coated in the oil and spice mix. Place on a baking tray and put in the oven to bake for 20mins, turning halfway through.
  3. Bring a medium pan of water to the boil and add 1 heaped teaspoon of stock or a stock cube. Add the puy lentils and simmer on a medium heat for 15-17 mins.
  4. Whilst the lentils and cauliflower are cooking, finely chop the Spring greens and grate the carrot. Prepare a steamer with boiling water in the bottom. (Or a saucepan for boiling if you don’t own a steamer).
  5. Put the Spring greens on to steam for 4 mins (3mins boiling).
  6. When the lentils are cooked, drain and add the grated carrot back into the saucepan. 
  7. After roasting the cauliflower for 15 minutes, add the pine nuts to the roasting tray and put back in the oven for 5 minutes.
  8. Place the spring greens on the plate, followed by the lentils and finally the roasted cauliflower and pine nuts. 

This dish is a great vegetarian/vegan dinner or lunch that is comforting for this time of year. The lentils are a great source of low GI carbohydrate, protein and fibre. And the pine nuts and olive oil provide a great source of healthy fats.  

  

 

Gluten-free: fact or fad?

November 18, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

The craze of ‘gluten-free’ food has gone mad in the UK. Whilst this is useful for sufferers of coeliacs disease, it has left a lot of us a bit confused about whether we should be eating gluten or not. Here I will try to straighten out the facts and dispell a few myths about gluten- free food. 

Gluten-free: As the name suggests this refers to a diet which does not contain gluten. Gluten is the protein component found in wheat, rye and barley. It is found in foods such as bread, pasta, breakfast cereals, flour, pastry, cakes and biscuits (basically every tasty carb there is!). So finding out from your GP that you’re intolerant to gluten can be a devastating realisation. Symptoms of gluten intolerance include bloating, pain and stomach cramps after eating gluten-containing foods. Luckily there are gluten-free product ranges on the market, here are a few swaps that can be made to cut out gluten:

Swap pasta for –> rice, buckwheat groats or quinoa.

Swap breakfast cereal for –> oats, buckwheat groats, chia seed pudding

Swap cakes and biscuits for –> raw snacks such as my homemade nakd bars or homemade granola bars

Use gluten-free flour such as brown rice flour, buckwheat flour or coconut flour.

However it should be noted that the advice for someone with gluten intolerance or sensitivity is to cut out gluten completely. The evidence suggests that even a small amount of gluten within the gut can cause inflammation and ill effects. 

So the question is, for those of us who don’t have a gluten intolerance, should we go gluten-free?

Cutting gluten out of the diet can sometimes decrease the intake of other vitamins and minerals, such as B vitamins, iron and fiber that our found in wholegrain products such as wholemeal bread, pasta and breakfast cereals. Therefore the advice would be to eat whole grain carbohydrates normally, however the consumption of high GI carbs such as white bread, flour, white rice and pasta should still be eaten in moderation. 

Gluten-free products have become popular as people perceive them to be healthier. However this isn’t always the case – after all a gluten-free brownie, is still a brownie and is likely to be just as sugary as a regular brownie. 

Overall, I think it’s important to listen to your body to see how it reacts to different foods. If you do notice bloating or discomfort after eating gluten then you should get a gluten intolerance test. If you’re like me, and haven’t noticed any problems eating gluten, then you can relax about whether your snacks or meal are gluten free. 

Superfood Watch: Shelled Hemp Seeds

April 25, 2015September 14, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

Everyones looking for the next superfood to make us feel amazing. First it was blueberries, then kale, but now the REAL superfoods are taking centre stage. Delicious things such as Goji Berries and Raw Cacao have recently been hot favourites. But now my attention has been turned to a new superfood: Shelled Hemp Seeds.

There has been a big change in what we perceive to be a ‘healthy’ diet in recent times. In the past, the focus was to reduce our intake of fat, as too much fat can increase our cholesterol levels which can increase the risk of heart disease. However, what the media didn’t let us know, is that there are good fats and bad fats. Fats can be good for us, (and actually essential!) as long as we eat the right ones.

This is where Shelled Hemp Seeds really are in their element, in fact they take the crown for the highest source of Omega -3 in the plant kingdom! These essential oils give you glowing skin, glossy hair, bright eyes and can even make you think clearer.

These little gems are also a complete source of protein, giving the body all it needs to build and repair cells, and strengthen the immune system.

I have to admit I was a little sceptical as I have tried hemp seeds before from the supermarket and they were crunchy and a little bitter. But, the shelled hemp seeds from creative nature were completely the opposite, a lovely nutty flavour with a ‘cous cous’ like consistency.

I’ve started added them to my chia seed puddings for a nutritional boost. Here is a great recipe for Coconut and Blueberry Chia Seed Pudding:

Overnight mix:

2 tbsp chia seeds

1 tbsp shelled hemp seeds

1 tbsp coconut flakes (or dessicated coconut)

150ml coconut milk

In the morning:

Top with blueberries and another sprinkling of shelled hemp seeds.

This breakfast comes in at a healthy 280kcal, with 18g Fat (all good fats!), 22g Carbs and a whopping 14g protein! Great for if you’re looking for a low carb, high protein and fat breakfast that will keep you going and provide you with a nutritional start to the day. Also great for gluten and dairy free diets.

Overnight chia seed pudding

April 10, 2015September 14, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

This is a staple breakfast for me as the weather starts to get a bit warmer. Chia seeds might be an alien food for some people and whilst the incentive might not be very high to go out and buy some, considering they aren’t available in most supermarkets and they don’t have a delicious taste.

However, if one thing can persuade you to start using chia seeds it would be the nutritional value. These little seeds are a powerhouse of nutrition and are a great place to start if you want to incorporate a superfood into your diet.

So, what will chia seeds provide you with?

  • Fibre: great for digestive health and for people looking to lose weight
  • Protein: the muscle building macronutrient will help you with recovering from workouts and building muscle to raise your metabolic rate. Also keeps you feeling fuller for longer.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids: an essential nutrient that can aid weight loss and keeps your skin and organs healthy
  • A serving (28g) contains..
  • Calcium: 18% of the RDA.
  • Manganese: 30% of the RDA.
  • Magnesium: 30% of the RDA.
  • Phosphorus: 27% of the RDA.
  • They also contain a decent amount of Zinc, Vitamin B3 (Niacin), Potassium, Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) and Vitamin B2.

The seeds expand in your stomach due to all the fibre so keep you fuller for longer. They’re also low in carbohydrates. 

Overnight chia seed pudding

Ingredients:

2 tbsp chia seeds 

1 tbsp oats

1 tbsp greek yoghurt 

100ml milk (semi-skimmed, almond etc.) 

1/2 tsp cinnamon

Handful of mixed berries

1 apple

Mixed seeds (eg. Pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, flaxseed)

Method:

  1. At night mix the chia seeds, oats, berries, yoghurt, milk, mixed seeds, cinnamon in a bowl or jar and cover with cling film and leave overnight
  2. In the morning grate in the apple and top with extra seeds or peanut butter – if you’re an addict like me!   

Pesto Courgetti

February 26, 2015September 14, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

This ‘Pesto Courgetti’ is a perfect low carb dinner whilst being filling, packed with flavour and full of vitamins to keep you healthy. This vegetarian recipe is a cheap, meat alternative and still has a good balance of protein from the chickpeas and good fats from the avocado. A spiralizer is useful to get a spaghetti-like shape, however if you don’t own a spiralizer you can use a vegetable peeler to make courgette ribbons. Ingredients (serves 2) 3 courgettes, spiralized ½ jar of basil pesto ½ can of chickpeas, drained 1 red onion, finely chopped 2 tsp coconut oil 40g goats cheese 1 avocado Method

  1. Finely chop the red onion and spiralize the courgettes.
  2. Add 2 tsp coconut oil (or other oil) to a wok on a medium heat and add the onion, fry for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the courgettes to the wok and continually turn over with a spatula for 2-3 minutes.
  4. In the meantime, drain the chickpeas and if using the avocado, whizz up the pesto and avocado in the food processor. If you don’t have a food processor you can mash the avocado and mix with the pesto.
  5. Add the chickpeas and pesto mix to the courgettes and warm through, whilst stirring for 2 minutes.
  6. Serve the courgetti and tear the goats’ cheese on top. Also tastes great with a few rocket leaves.

No bloated feeling, no guilt, just pure, nourishing, delicious-ness!

How To Keep To Your New Years Resolutions

January 16, 2015September 14, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

The New Year brings out the best in people with their New Years resolutions. Whilst at first the self-motivation might be quite high, it is easy to slip back into old habits.

So how do you prevent a relapse? We have the answers to make sure your New Years resolution lasts the whole year.

  1. Get organised

You will likely fail unless you have a plan for how you will stick to your resolution. For example, if it is to eat healthier foods, this will involve planning your meals and doing a food shop to get healthy foods in. Think about what you can swap in as healthy unalternatives. And more importantly get rid of the junk food in the house – that includes the ice cream in the freezer!

  1. Create a goal

It has been scientifically proven that by setting a goal a person it more likely to stick to a workout or diet plan. The goals you set must be achievable, and include short-term and long-term goals. For example if your resolution was to get fitter, you might firstly set a short term goal of being able to run 3km continuously, and then a long term goal of running 5km.

  1. Bet on it

This may sound bonkers, but a recent study highlighted in the TV programme ‘Weighing Up The Enemy’ found that people were more likely to stick to a diet if they had their own money on the line. I’m not suggested betting your life savings but it might be a motive if you know that £20 is at stake. Especially if you place the bet with somebody you’d love to prove wrong.

  1. Record your achievements

So a week goes by and you’ve managed to stay away from the chocolate and sweets (for example). But you don’t feel any different and you start thinking, whats the point if I don’t lose weight or feel healthier. Don’t be disheartened, it will take longer to see and feel changes in yourself, but it will be worth it next time you see an old friend and they comment on how great you look. In the meantime, make a record of how many days you have gone whilst keeping to your new years resolution, each day will feel like an achievement when you mark it off on your calendar.

  1. Don’t blow it

Even if you do go slightly of track with your New Years resolution don’t take the easy way out and give up. One day or one week of switching back to old habits doesn’t mean you can’t start the resolution again next week. Pick it back up and you will feel the benefits.

Following these steps will give you an extra advantage to keeping to your New Years resolutions. This could be the year that you eat healthily and start to feel amazing or get the bikini body that you’ve always wanted. Good luck for 2015!

Apple and Pecan Baked Oats

November 24, 2014September 14, 2015 foodiebeeblogLeave a comment

Having been a porridge lover for many years, I was interested to try ‘baked oats’ which is a different way of cooking porridge in the oven, rather than on the stove. Admittedly, it is a little more time consuming, but totally worth the extra effort because it tastes delicious.

You won’t believe that it is just a pimped up porridge you’re eating because it tastes more like an apple crumble dessert.

This recipe is adapted from a deliciously ella recipe, that woman is a genius!

Serves 2

Ingredients

1 grated apple

1/2 cup oats

1/3 cup milk (almond milk tastes great in this recipe – and dairy free!)

Handful of pecan nuts

2 tsp cinnamon

handful of raisins

Methods

1. Preheat the oven to 180c

2. Soak the oats in boiling water for 10 mins then drain

3. Mix all of the ingredients together in an ovenproof dish and bake for 20 mins, or when the top is golden brown.

Tip: Apple slices and a few pecans on top make it look pretty, but don’t add the pecans til the end else they have the tendency to burn in the oven.

I was cooking for one, but just kept the other half in the fridge and had it the next day for breakfast with a dollop of natural yoghurt.
