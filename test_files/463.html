Working Hard While Looking Good

Being a high school student means having a jam packed schedule. Whether it's sports, homework, or different club activities I think many can agree that being a student is like having a full time job. With that said, many teenagers also happen to have a job while juggling all of their extracurriculars.

Around a month ago I became one of those teenagers to join the work force.

It may sound somewhat silly, but it is true that one of my biggest challenges I face before work is picking out what to wear. I'm a hostess so really I am the first impression of the restaurant people receive when they walk through the front door. This means not only acting in a certain way but dressing in a certain way.

Typically hosts have the boring black on bottom, white on top dress code. Fortunately, that is not the case for me. So for the rest of you that hold jobs requiring somewhat dressy attire, this post is for you.

With a fairly lenient dress code comes many choices but also many decisions. Here are a couple of tips to help with that decision making:


  • LAYER- yes, it is in all caps for a reason. You can predict your work environment but you can't predict the weather. 
  • Color to occasion- know when it is appropriate to wear pink and when it is important to wear black
  • Accessorize- no outfit is complete with just clothing- but don't go crazy
    Personal Photo
  • Overdress if unsure- just like with overpacking, you really can't go wrong
  • Closed toed shoes- okay so really this mainly applies to those working in the restaurant or food industry. Who wants spilled food on their freshly polished toes? The answer is no one.
  • Express Yourself- I am a firm believer that what you wear directly expresses your personality, so do it well my friends!

Wednesday, April 22, 2015

Travel Tips and Quips

It is now mid April which means everyone is back to school from spring break. For a lot of us it also means back to places with insufficient weather conditions. I don't know about you but Michigan weather just puts me in a bad mood.

Flash back to a week or two ago and it would be another story. Everyone was having an amazing time soaking up the sun and enjoying the week of no homework and no problems.

I happened to be one of those lucky people.

My mom can be somewhat spontaneous when it comes to trips. For example, around a month ago I walked in the house after school and my mom says "We're going to Hawaii!!"

I know what you're thinking, but it really does work like that with her. No complaints from me because I am the beneficiary of this Type A personality.

So my mom and I took a "mother daughter" trip to the island of Oahu over spring break.

It really ended up being an exceptional trip so I thought I would share some things with you if ever going to a warm or tropical destination.
Personal Photo


  • Use packing cubes- my dad got me these for the trip and I was really confused at first but now I am enthralled
  • Use sunscreen with SPF 30 and up- pretty self explanatory since no one likes to come home looking like a lobster
  • Over pack- unless the luggage ends up weighing over the limit, but seriously I never understood why this was a bad thing. The more options the better!
  • Buy toothpaste when you get to your destination- TSA has very strict policies when it comes to the 3 ounce rule so this will be confiscated if brought in your carry on
  • Take pictures- yes, you should live in the moment but you should try to capture the moments so you have memories to look back on

Don't Be a Fool


In just a few days is the unparalleled day of pranks. Although it may not be recognized as a national holiday, it is recognized by many in the world. However, there are slight variations as to how it is celebrated depending on the country.

 April Fools' Day in Spain is also known as "Day of the Innocents". However, as strange as it may seem it is actually on December 28th. It is the same as America's in the sense that jokes are played but when it has happened the trickster yells "Innocent one, innocent one!"

 In France, April Fools' is known as "Poisson d'Avril." It involves sticking a paper fish on the back of someone as a jest. 

 As I'm sure many in America know, no one is immune to having these sort of pranks played on them. 

The first of April also happens to be my birthday so I like to have a little fun with this holiday. Every year I try to find unique pranks to play on friends and family and Pinterest always seems to have good ideas. Here are some of my favorites that I thought I would share to prepare you.

Sunday, March 15, 2015

Funny Right?

Saturday Night Live just had their 40th anniversary last month and they definitely deserve all of the credit they receive. For years they have successfully made skits full of satirical elements. 

I myself happen to be a huge proponent of sarcasm. As a tradition, every Saturday night that my dad and I are together we watch SNL. Just a few weeks ago Dakota Johnson hosted. The "I Can't" skit stood out among the rest. 
YouTube- Saturday Night Live "I Can't"
Sometimes it isn't so easy to pick up on what is being criticized behind the comedy. However, this skit makes it pretty evident.
I think we have all run into that overly dramatic slightly annoying valley girl. If you haven't, you might just be one.
Nothing against you if you are, but this clip definitely aims at mocking that specific character.
The video accomplishes this task in multiple ways using the three interns:
  1. Exaggerating the weather
  2. Using words repeatedly (literally, I can't)
  3. Whiny attitudes
  4. How they speak
There is literally one category this fits perfectly in; exaggeration. 
Even though there may be some people out there who speak EXACTLY like this, most don't. With that said, we do often say things similar to what is going on in the skit. How many people walk outside when it is 30 degrees and say "oh my gosh I'm going to freeze to death!"? A lot. We all exaggerate at some point.

I truly believe that this SNL skit does an amazing job at satirizing this character. Many of us have often fallen victim to whining and complaining in our everyday lives about silly things. Watching this skit myself I definitely laughed and enjoyed it but also realized I do some of the same things.

So if it ever starts raining and your hair gets messed up, just remember that you literally won't die.

The Divorce Diaries

"50 percent of all marriages end in divorce." It is a sad but true statement here in the United States. Earlier in my life it seemed as though everyone had the "perfect family" but now it seems divorce is becoming more and more prevalent.

This statistic hits home for me in the sense that my parents divorced when I was around 2 or 3. Now I'm not going to say that it was the best thing that ever happened to me, but I can say to all of you out there who are struggling with a split family: it will be okay.

I personally don't remember the details of the divorce because I was so young, but that doesn't mean I don't deal with the same aftermath that all teenagers do resulting from a divorce. And for those of you who don't live in a separated home, here is a little peek into our world.

Personal Photo

Back and Forth.
Especially for someone that grew up with parents having 50/50 custody, this becomes a lifestyle. Depending on how far parents live from each other it really isn't all that bad.

Who Pays What.
Often times parents won’t mind paying for simple necessities, but once bigger purchases come into play so do big problems. It is a constant battle of “I just paid for this, so you should pay for that.” Make sure everything is split down the middle so there is no question as to who does more.

 The Haul.
 This is the most common issue that a child from divorces faces. Carrying all of the things you may or may not need from house to house.


Of course I could list many others, but it is important to remember that with every down there is an upward swing that follows.

Single's Guide to Valentine's Day


Soon approaching is the notorious day of L O V E. Usually when the 14th of February rolls around if you don't have a significant other, you don't have any plans. Well I am here to tell you to NOT give up!
There are so many different ways to spend your day without a sweetheart, and I have some ideas to help.

Personal Photo
Go out and have dinner with friends, or even cook it yourselves. This is a great way to have a fun night filled with laughter and memories in the making.

If you're feeling extra special(it is, of course, all about you) go and treat yourself to a little shopping trip or spa day.

Not feeling up to going out? Get your popcorn and pick out a good rom-com like Valentine's Day to watch with the girls.

Another fun idea for at home is to scout Pinterest for DIY gifts to give to friends and family. From baked goods to a sharpie designed mug, anything you put the thought into will melt their heart.

As spoken by the beautiful and talented Maya Angelou, "If you only have one smile in you, give it to the people you love". Whether it is friends or family, surround yourself with people that love you on Valentine's Day.
