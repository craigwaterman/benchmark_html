Give Up the Goat

June 4, 2015

Today I did something that characterizes the very heart of the startup mentality; I chose risk over stability. I purposefully took a pay cut in exchange for more freedom from my full-time job as a software engineer at Codelation. I did this to allow myself the time and focus to utilize the marvelous resource of something that I didn’t appreciate enough when I was younger, free rent.

I’m already one month into living in the Fargo Startup House and I found myself making silly financial choices with the extra money that I hadn’t been putting towards rent and utilities. I analyzed my behavior and determined that if I wanted to succeed in building a business over the next eleven months then I need to expose myself to more external pressures.

What I found when I analyzed my behavior was that when given too many resources, focus and drive became stale. This may only be a personal trait, but I have a feeling it applies to other entrepreneurs as well. The conclusion that I have come to is that too many resources available allows us to belay the tough choices and hard actions that we need to make to succeed, because we always have our backup.

My solution? Whittle-away my backup. Give up the goat.

Starting tomorrow I will no longer be working Thursdays and Fridays and instead will refocus my time and energy into building my personal brand along with my startup based on ride-sharing.

I hope this brief self-analysis helps you to refocus your efforts like it has me.
