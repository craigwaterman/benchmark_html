5 Foods This Nutritionist Will Never Eat

July 23, 2013factsandflax Healthy Living, Nutrition 29 Comments

Good morning! I hope everyone’s week is off to a great start. It’s raining here, which I’ll see as a good thing because it has been in the 90’s the past week, and I’m sure the poor foliage outside was dying for a drink!

I’m obsessed with reading nutrition articles, and one of my favorites is the Huffington Post’s Healthy Living section. Sometimes they will have articles about how nutritionists eat- how they eat at parties or what they’ll eat for breakfast. I used to be skeptical when I saw the word “nutritionist”, since it is not a protected title in the United States, and anyone can call themselves a nutritionist- but the “nutritionists” in the articles are always Registered Dietitians, which is a protected title.

I thought it would be fun to do my own little spin off on how this nutritionist doesn’t eat! I figure since I post foods I do eat, it would be a nice change of pace to show what I don’t.

*Disclaimer: Even though I have a Bachelor’s degree in dietetics, I am not yet a Registered Dietitian. All opinions expressed are my own*

5 foods this nutritionist will NEVER eat

1) Hot Dogs

(Let’s pretend for a moment that I’m not a vegetarian and I consume meat on a regular basis. If I was not a vegetarian, I would eat chicken, turkey, and occasionally beef.)

Hot dogs may be the most highly processed meat ever created. When I took (and later TA’d) a Healthy Ways to Weight Loss for Women class in college, the professor shared with us a newspaper article about ingredients found in hotdogs, and I’ve encouraged people to shy away from them since. I don’t know who wrote the article she read or where it came from, but I did find one that talks about the hot dog making process at the factory, and what ingredients a typical hot dog contains.

“In vast metal vats, tons of pork trimmings are mixed with the pink slurry formed when chicken carcasses are squeezed through metal grates and blasted with water. The mush is mixed with powdered preservatives, flavourings, red colouring and drenched in water before being squeezed into plastic tubes to be cooked and packaged.”

Other ingredients found in a hot dog:

Sodium nitrite– may increase your risk of bowel cancer

Potassium and sodium triphosphates– emulsifiers. Also found in flame retardents, paper, rubber, and anti-freeze

Carmine– another word for cochineal, a red food dye made from the crushed up shells of small beetles that have been boiled in ammonia

(Article source)

 

2) Pop-Tarts

Ingredients for a frosted blueberry Pop-Tart (source):

ENRICHED FLOUR (WHEAT FLOUR, NIACIN, REDUCED IRON, VITAMIN B1 [THIAMIN MONONITRATE], VITAMIN B2 [RIBOFLAVIN], FOLIC ACID), CORN SYRUP, HIGH FRUCTOSE CORN SYRUP, DEXTROSE, SOYBEAN AND PALM OIL (WITH TBHQ FOR FRESHNESS), SUGAR, CRACKER MEAL, CONTAINS TWO PERCENT OR LESS OF WHEAT STARCH, SALT, DRIED BLUEBERRIES, DRIED GRAPES, DRIED APPLES, LEAVENING (BAKING SODA, SODIUM ACID PYROPHOSPHATE, MONOCALCIUM PHOSPHATE), CITRIC ACID, MILLED CORN, GELATIN, SOYBEAN OIL, MODIFIED CORN STARCH, MODIFIED WHEAT STARCH, SOY LECITHIN, XANTHAN GUM, CARAMEL COLOR, RED 40, VITAMIN A PALMITATE, NIACINAMIDE, REDUCED IRON, NATURAL AND ARTIFICIAL FLAVOR, BLUE 2, BLUE 1, VITAMIN B6 (PYRIDOXINE HYDROCHLORIDE), COLOR ADDED, TURMERIC EXTRACT, VITAMIN B2 (RIBOFLAVIN), VITAMIN B1 (THIAMIN HYDROCHLORIDE).

The bolded words are ingredients that can be found in a standard home kitchen. The rest can be found in a standard chemistry lab, especially TBHQ, a preservative also found in petroleum.

3) Twinkies

I’ll admit I was excited when I heard a few months ago that Hostess had gone bankrupt, but I’m hearing now they will be back in business. If the 37 ingredients list doesn’t scare you, maybe the extended 25 day shelf life to 45 day shelf life will.

4) Fruit Loops

Fruit Loops have been banned in the UK and other European countries due to the artificial dyes that have been linked to brain cancer, nerve-cell deterioration, and hyperactivity in children. These artificial dyes are derived from petroleum (like TBHQ), which is also used to make gasoline, diesel fuel, asphalt, and tar.

The first ingredient in Fruit Loops is sugar, which means Fruit Loops contain more sugar by weight than any other ingredient.

5) Doritos Locos Tacos

Ingredients (source):

Ground Corn Treated With Lime, Vegetable Oil (Sunflower, Canola, Corn, and/or Soybean Oil), Water, And Less Than 2% Of The Following: Corn Flour, Maltodextrin, Salt, Cheddar Cheese (Milk, Cheese Cultures, Salt, Enzymes), Whey, Monosodium Glutamate, Buttermilk, Romano Cheese (Part-Skim Cow’s Milk, Cheese Cultures, Salt, Enzymes), Whey Protein Concentrate, Onion Powder, Corn Oil, Corn Flour, Disodium Phosphate, Natural And Artificial Flavor, Dextrose, Tomato Powder, Lactose, Spices, Artificial Color (FD&C Yellow No. 6, Caramel Color, FD&C Yellow No. 5, FD&C Red No. 40, Extractives Of Tumeric, Extractives of Annatto), Lactic Acid, Citric Acid, Sugar, Garlic Powder, Nonfat Milk, Red and Green Bell Pepper Powder, Disodium Inosinate, Disodium Guanylate, TBHQ (Preservative). CONTAINS: MILK

Again, I bolded the ingredients found in the standard home kitchen. Besides artificial dyes and TBHQ, the Doritos Locos Tacos also contain Monosodium Glutamate, otherwise known as MSG. The FDA has classified MSG as a preservative that’s “generally known as safe“. The FDA has also received reports of headaches, flushing, sweating, facial tightness or pressure, numbness, tingling or burning in the face, neck, or other areas, heart palpitations (rapid, fluttering heartbeats), chest pain, nausea, and weakness from people eating foods that contain MSG. (source)


Despite some of the scary ingredients in these foods, I stick by my mantra that everything is okay in moderation. This list isn’t meant to provide restrictions to anyone’s diet, but is a personal list of foods I will never eat. That isn’t to say that I haven’t eaten some of these foods before, or some of the things I do eat don’t contain these ingredients (because they do), so please take this list with a grain of salt- but no more than 2300mg per day ;)

Have a great day!
