5 Pro Tips For Buyers When Purchasing A Home

SEARCH THE MLS FOR YOUR NEXT HOME!

So, you’re ready to find a home, settle in, and live the American Dream.  Purchasing a new home can be one of the most satisfying experiences, but it can also be one of the scariest if you don’t know what to expect.  Buying a home is most likely one of the biggest financial commitments you will make in your lifetime and can take a lot time and energy as well.  On top of that, there tends to be a lot of emotion invested in the home buying process.  This all being said, purchasing a new home can be downright stressful!  However, with the right real estate agent and a good understanding of what to expect when buying a home, it can be turned into a great experience that leaves you with fond memories.
These 5 buying tips will save you time, money, and headaches.

1. Get Pre-Qualified

Before you can seriously consider purchasing a home, it’s important to know exactly how much you can afford.  This can be the scariest part of the buying process for some.  It’s perfectly natural to be nervous, but a good real estate agent will help put you in touch with someone you can trust to help get you pre-qualified.  A mortgage lender will discuss your financial situation and your needs to help you decide if buying is right for you and what type of loan you may qualify for.  With your loan application and a credit check, they can determine what price range you should be looking in based on what you qualify for and what you can afford.  If you do not qualify for a loan at the time you make application, a good lender will tell you what you can do to begin to rectify your credit and get it back on track.

2. Think Resale

In the US, due to the mobility of our economy, the length of ownership is about 5 – 7 years.  This is important to keep in mind when looking for your next home.  What you think could be your forever home now, could be too small a few years down the road if you decide to have kids.  People outgrown homes, kids move out and homes become too large to keep up with, and  jobs get transferred.  With this in mind, you should always be thinking about the resale factors of your home.  For instance, homes backing up to a busy street or railroad sell for less, and take longer to sell than other homes in the neighborhood.  Likewise, updated homes in a good neighborhood tend to sell quickly.  It is always a good idea to think about resale when choosing a home – if your plans change and you need to sell your home quickly you will want to do so without reducing the price too much.

3. Aesthetics Can Be Changed

A typical home buyer is looking for their dream home, right?  They have a picture in their head of exactly what it will look like, down the color of the front door.  However, not everyone has the luxury of affording EXACTLY what they want when thinking about their perfect home.  It is important to remember that flooring, light fixtures, plumbing, paint colors, and wallpaper can all be changed.  Today’s buyers have an advantage – the internet offers countless DIY (do-it-yourself) project ideas and instructions, so buyers can even choose to do the work themselves and transform their “fixer upper” into their dream home at a much lower cost.  Don’t let the lime green wallpaper turn you off of your potential dream home.  Think of how much fun it will be to make a home yours!

4. Don’t WAIT!

Too often, we have seen home buyers fall in love with a property, wait a few days to make a decision because they are nervous, and the home that they fell in love with SOLD!  Purchasing a new home can be scary – especially if you don’t fully understand the process.  A good real estate agent will talk you through the process and make sure you understand and are comfortable with everything before you begin your search.  This will help alleviate any concerns, uncertainties, and stress.  If you still have questions, or don’t understand something, ASK!  Your agent is there to be your personal consultant and walk you through the purchase from beginning to end.  We want you to be ready so that when you find the home you love, you don’t wait for someone else to purchase YOUR home.  Be prepared to write an offer!

5. Compare Sales

Once you’ve found the home you’d like to purchase, you should be ready to make an offer.  To determine the best price to offer, or to know if the asking price is correct, look at the recent sales of similar homes, called “comparable sales.”  Comparable sales are recent sales of homes that compare closely to the one you are looking to purchase, usually sold in the past few months.  Specifically, you want to compare prices of homes that are similar in square footage, garage spaces, number of bedrooms and baths, lots size, etc.  Your agent will be able to find these and help you understand what they mean for you and the current market so that you can be confident when you make your first offer!

MISSISSIPPI GULF COAST REAL ESTATE MARKET STATISTICS FOR JUNE 2015

August 5, 2015August 5, 2015Leave a comment

JUNE 2015 MARKET STATISTICS

 
In the MS Gulf Coast real estate market, conditions continue to favor both buyers and sellers, even as fewer houses become available compared to this time last year. June found an increase in the number of new listings, active listings, and pending listings for single-family homes, according to information from the Mississippi Gulf Coast Association of Realtors.
 
The 738 new listings in June of 2015 represented a 14.6 percent increase over June of 2014.  Active listings decreased 17.8 percent to 3,585 over the same period.  Pending listings had an even higher jump, with the 411 pending listings in June 2015, indicating a 20.18 percent increase over June 2014.  Actual sales of single-family homes also increased over the same period.
 
 
The Mississippi Gulf Coast MLS reports that in June 2015, a total of 457 single homes were sold in the MS Gulf Coast area.  This represents a 13.4 percent increase over sales in June 2014.
 
Prices in the Mississippi Gulf Coast real estate market began to climb during that year-long period.  The median price of a single-family home in the MS Gulf Coast area increased 7.5 percent between June 2014 and June 2015 to $129,000.  Average home prices rose 3.1 percent during that period to $154,500.  The average price per square footage of sales during the year increased 7.9 percent from $76 to $82 per square foot.
 
Homes spend an average of 156 days on the market on the Mississippi Gulf Coast, which is a decrease from June 2014.  Generally low prices, a more limited availability, and quick sales of those homes that come up for sales make the MS Gulf Coast real estate market a favorable environment for sellers.
 

What does this mean if you’re thinking of selling your home?

If you haven’t had a home valuation recently, chances are your home is worth more than you think! As you can see from the stats above, home prices throughout the MS Gulf Coast area have rapidly increased in the last year alone.

Though the more limited supply of homes makes things easier for sellers, it makes things more difficult for buyers. If you’re looking to buy in this market, you need to make sure you’re ready to act quickly! Chances are, you’ll be competing with other buyers for your dream home!
Now, that doesn’t mean you should wait to buy.  The longer you wait in today’s market, the less home you’ll be able to afford. This year, mortgage rates have remained near record lows.  In May, the average rate for 30 year, fixed- rate mortgage was just 3.84%, according to Freddie Mac! However, interest rates are expected to rise as the year goes on.
