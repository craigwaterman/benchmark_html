Iceland ❤️

Published April 26, 2014 by phoebecupcakes

Last month, I was in Reykjavik, Iceland, which is seriously cold, but I had a lot of fun. I’ve seen the northern lights, swam in the blue lagoon, seen an active geysir, been to a waterfall and slept. A lot.

I’ve actually been able to see the Northern Lights, and I should be able to put a picture and a post up about it.

Girls, visit Iceland just for the Blue Lagoon. It’s a geothermal (naturally heated) pool, with a swim up bar and mud facials. They even have their own range of products, and I love their conditioner. Speaking of conditioner, if you go, you have to have a shower, so wash your hair and leave the conditioner in, then plait/braid it to stop it looking like a scarecrow’s (because of the silicon in the water).

On the last day, I went on this trip called The Golden Circle Tour, where we saw the Gulfoss Waterfalls, Þingvellier (the space between the tectonic plates of North America and Europe), a greenhouse that’s heat is powered by volcanic activity and some active geysirs (underground heated water pools that explode).

Reykjavik itself is a beautiful city, and we stayed in the Leifur Eriksson Hotel, where my mum accidentally booked three single rooms instead of one three room! The hotel was great though, right near the high street and a beautiful church, and you could get free hot drinks throughout the day! We went to two lovely vegetarian restaurants, and we were able to pick up snacks at the local supermarket for on our tours.

All in all, Iceland is a worthwhile holiday destination, not to be overlooked. Yes, it’s cold, but you only need to wrap up warm to avoid that and appreciate the beauty of such a small country (population of 320,000!).

My school bag essentials ❤

Published March 2, 2014 by phoebecupcakes

Recently my school bag ripped so badly that I need a new one, and while I was clearing out my bag and writing a shopping list for what to put in my new bag, I realised that there are some things you need for in your school bag. I keep two small cosmetics bags in my bag, so here is a list of what is in both of them.

Pink bag (beauty)

  • Dry shampoo ~ great for after P.E/Gym or if you’re busy and didn’t wash your hair.
  • Matte powder ~ perfect for keeping your foundation and concealer in place, especially on P.E/Gym days
  • Hairbrush ~ I have a mini TRESemme paddle brush, which is a brilliant size and fits in my bag perfectly
  • Body spray/perfume samples ~ keep an Impulse body spray in your bag at all times, unless you prefer a stronger smell and want perfume samples. In that case, go into Boots/Superdrug and ask for perfume samples
  • Mini deodorant ~ always have a mini deodorant on days you have P.E/Gym
  • Hair grips and ties ~ everyone knows you need these, just be careful who you lend them to, because there’s always that one girl who borrows them and never gives them back
  • Powder brush ~ for applying powder
  • Clear mascara ~ unnoticeable eye makeup at it’s best
  • Eyeshadow crayon ~ preferably in nude, this is optional eye makeup
  • Concealer ~ in case you have a spotty day, concealer covers it up quite nicely
  • Lip gloss and balm ~ just makes your lips look nicer

Purple bag (essentials)

  • ‘Girly things’ ~ if you’re a girl you know what I’ll mean
  • Tissues ~ just in case
  • Wet wipes ~ never know when you might need one
  • Hand sanitizer ~ because school toilet sinks are always gross
  • Hand cream ~ especially in winter, definitely if you get dry skin
  • Mints/gum ~ don’t have gum if you’re not allowed, but both keep your breath fresh after eating

Unnoticeable makeup

  • Concealer
  • Foundation
  • Eyeshadow crayon
  • Clear mascara
  • Lip gloss

Easy tips for keeping your skin healthy

Published February 28, 2014 by phoebecupcakes
  1. Always moisturize – you may have to go through a few before you find the correct one, but I would suggest Simple moisturizers to anyone, as they are kind to your skin and don’t usually irritate your skin.
  2. Keep the makeup minimal – aside from looking horrible, caking makeup on your face ruins your skin. Spots, rashes and redness galore. Also, you get accustomed to the makeup quantity on your face, hate your natural skin and put more on. This doesn’t necessarily apply to just a few times going out, I’m just saying don’t daily/regularly shovel it on. Natural looks better anyway. :)
  3. Don’t sleep wearing makeup – just like the last one, cosmetics are to blame. Sleeping with makeup on is a definite no no. It smears, it dries out your skin, it’s impossible to get off, ugh. It’s a disgusting thing to experience and it basically destroys your face. Stay away from makeup and remove it before you sleep.
  4. Eat healthily – god, this is a bit hypocritical of me to write! I’m not saying go on a lettuce and wheatgrass only diet, but try and include your five a day. I’m terrible at this because I have little self control when it comes to food. Nonetheless I eat quite healthily in that I eat proper meals and I eat all of them at the right time (except sometimes breakfast – who has the time?).
  5. Don’t smoke – not only does smoking wreck your lungs and health, it affects your skin. Smoking actually wrinkles your skin and makes it look visibly older, and narrows blood vessels on your outer skin, decreasing blood flow! It can also damage the collagen and elastin, and they give your face fiber and elasticity. The whole action of smoking (pursing your lips to inhale, squinting your eyes to keep smoke out) wrinkles your skin as well! (Sorry about all those sciencey words)
  6. Keep your stress under control – it greys your hair, it wrinkles your skin, stress is very bad for you! It can also ruin your health and that’s definitely not a good thing!

Fears and overcoming them

Published February 7, 2014 by phoebecupcakes

If you have read the previous blog post, you will know that one, if not the biggest fear of mine is social situations. Even the thought of them makes me feel like screaming. I don’t think fearless is a believable concept, but overcoming them or suppressing them so they don’t ruin your life, now that’s the bravest, and the hardest, thing to do. I have fears of clowns, mannequins, sometimes heights and my physical image.

It’s hard being afraid of mannequins, because people don’t understand it or even accept it. I get teased about it and it can even affect my education, as a lot of school trips are to museums (where they often have mannequins) and I have to tell the teachers and get embarrassed about that, then it singles me out a I have to sit alone with everyone looking at me, probably wondering if there’s something wrong with me, or that I did something bad and have to sit out.

Don’t even get me started on clowns. It’s one of the 3 most common phobias, and to me, they’re terrifying, even thinking about clowns gives me the shivers. Honestly, google clowns and the first five-ten results will be creepy. The psychological idea is that the main reason why people are scared of clowns is because of the painted face and inability to see the man/woman’s real face. Heights aren’t really such a big fear of mine, but I’ll talk about them anyway, as some people really suffer from acrophobia. I think that the best thing for overcoming fear of heights is not flooding, but Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). This is where a person is slowly introduced into their fear, instead of having it forced upon them.

Physical image fears. Wow. This is pretty much dysmorphia and self consciousness, that sort of thing. It’s basically the fear of looking bad and seeing your body morphed into horrible images when you look in the mirror. I am slowly overcoming self consciousness but I am having trouble, just like every other girl in the world. I find that instead of asking everyone how you look and seeming needy, or saying things like ‘I’m so fat’ just to have reassurance that you aren’t and seeming like you’re attention seeking, try to wear and pick out clothes that make you feel comfortable, and if your friends are making you feel like you’ve got something wrong with you, drop them and find new people. Ugh, even typing that makes me feel anxious. It might be hard and make you feel anxious and uncomfortable, but it’ll be worth it in the long haul. Trust me.

Sorry that I didn’t talk about overcoming the first two (three if you count my anxiety) fears, but I haven’t overcome them, or come across any working methods, so I can’t be of much help there. Apologies.

Being alone and social anxiety

Published February 4, 2014 by phoebecupcakes

I’m not sure if I am the only person who suffers this or if it is a common problem, but I hate doing things alone. I won’t go to a club, volunteer for a group or do anything like that without a friend. It probably sounds crazy, but it’s really hard for me to go through. I’m only young but I’m already worried about university because I’ll have to go alone and I won’t know anyone there. A lot of people say that you make friends there, but the daunting idea of entering a room where I know no one makes me feel like they’re all staring at me. Social situations can often make me feel two foot tall (it may not seem like it, but I’m actually five foot. I promise), but it’s always easier with friends, and people who deny that seem really brave to me. I really am quite socially awkward and terrible at making friends. There are many clubs I want to attend at my school but simply can’t as I’m not brave enough to even try alone. The one club I do attend is a drama club which I love and have met many of my other older friends by, but when I first joined, it was with a friend who introduced me to all of the others and went with me, but she soon quit and I was so grateful she’d been there beforehand to help me out. People who can appropriately handle and volunteer in social situations without faltering nor hesitation, I (and a fair amount of the internet) salute you. If you suffer from this or can relate to this in anyway, don’t hesitate to tweet me or in any other form of contact talk to me. There is a form below if you wish to talk to me about sensitive matters and/or feel like you have no one to talk to. Thank you, and I hope this was helpful, inspiring, or you’re just glad to know more about me. :)
