Lights, Candles, ACTION!!

I love lights and candles! It’s romantic, intimate and cozy. Did you know that candles in lanterns create a super romantic lighting effect  and ambiance for any ceremony?

Have you been wondering how you can incorporate lanterns in your ceremony or reception ?  fret not! :-) 

Here are a few tips:

  • lanterns can be placed down the aisle or used as a centerpiece
  • You can choose to place lanterns on a few or all of your tables , this may help to cut down on the cost of flowers (or other centerpieces), while still showing off your wedding or event in a beautiful  light
  • Even if you’re getting married while the sun is still up, clusters of lanterns can create a beautiful ceremony space
  • Be creative! You can put in some decorative/ DIY items in place of the candles

Talents unleashed at the Glitz Africa Fashion Week 2014

After a long week of work, I decided to give in to attending the Glitz Africa Fashion Week which I had gotten free VIP tickets. I needed to release some good amount of stress which had begun to build up. And oh boy,did it help ! I wasn’t the least disappointed though i got to attend only the last day of the fashion week. I ought to have attended all three!! Dammit!

It was my very first fashion show… I am a newbie! I have found myself looking through fashion magazines for trending vogue, but never imagined myself at the front row of an actual runway … it was just awesome !!

The models were so professional and knew their stuff. The runway themes were so creative… From the “Chocolate models” biting off chocolate bars to the models taking selfies… it was just fantastic!

And if you are a soon to be bride, you should definitely hook up with some of these designers! Give yourself a beautiful treat cuz u deserve it. They are amazing talents.

The Islamic Wedding of a Dagomba girl…

Have you ever attended a Moslem Wedding before? Have you been thrown an invitation and you passed it?

Three words for you then… You Missed Big Time!!! (oops that’s 4). My very good friend ( known her since Uni days) recently tied the knot and I was going all gaga over the entire ceremony as well as her stunning costumes. It was truly an elegant celebration. The fooooood was heavenly!! I bet i would have gained a few pounds had i participated in the 4 day ceremony!

The celebrations started on Friday and ended on Monday.

I hope you all learn a thing or two from this post… Enjoy :-)

 

 Day 1:

On day 1 the bride-to-be was bathed with henna which is believed to purify and glow her skin. Afterwards she was decorated with henna tattoos.

Bride-to-be getting her henna tattoo

Henna tattoo on her hands

 

Nana all dolled up!! Lovely!

 

My girl looking RaViShInG!!

 

On this day, the bride-to-be changes a couple of times to welcome all who have come to help her in the preparations. There is a lot of dancing and merry-making.

 

Day 2

On day 2, she had a civil wedding and a tropical themed reception  followed. It was celebrated with pomp and grandeur at the Little Acre hotel in Aburi. I must say Nana, you are one stunning bride ( No exaggeration guys, She is a natural beauty).

 

Civil wedding preparations under way. Beautiful henna tattoo!

 

Perfect Gown!! Did I mention that the bride happens to be a Fashionista too?

 

Stunning!

 

 

Did I mention that she featured in one of my first posts about going natural? Yup she took the big CHOP!!

 

Ravishing…

Don’t they look gorgeous??

 

The ceremony was held at Little Acre Hotel in Aburi. Beautiful place and the scenery is just Spectacular!! Perfect destination wedding

 

 

Friends of the couple

 

 

The Wedding Cake

 

The night just began…

 

 

releasing the balloons…

Day 3

The Moslem traditional marriage ceremony also known as the Nikkah was held in the mosque. After the ceremony, the bride hosted her guests at a separate venue while the groom did same. There was more merry-making and dancing.

The best highlight of the day was when the bride changed into several different costumes in the course of the celebrations.

 

Outfit No 1

Friends of the bride in uniform cloth (yaaye) and gele having a good time

 

Later in the day, the bride and her guests go to the grooms house to continue with the celebration. 

 

The Groom waiting for his bride

 

The groom met the bride in a matching outfit

 

 

Final Outfit of the day…Number 4… My personal favourite!

 

When the celebrations come to an end, the bride leaves for her home and takes a holy bath. The elderly married women in her family take turns in advising her.

She is soon taken away by the aunties of the groom to her husbands house to spend the night ( Oh la la….)

 

Day 4

The following morning, there is an unveiling ceremony of the bride.  The bride’s family mainly the women, are picked up from her family house to her grooms house to witness this ceremony.

 

the Unveiling of the bride

 

 

Is she the one?? lol

 

Yes She’s the One!! The newest bride in town.

 

Nana Mariam, thank you for letting me showcase the beauty of Moslem weddings. Yours was the very first I witnessed and I really had fun as well as learnt a lot of things. Thank you for being YOU!

Mohammed has truly found a gem. May Allah bless and keep your marriage. Ameeen

Lots of love…
