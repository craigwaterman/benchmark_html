Avarie’s Baby Shower

March 18, 2015

Another catch up post for this week. About a month ago I hosted a baby shower for my best friend and her little girl on the way! We ended up having beautiful weather that day despite it still being winter.  The theme Avarie wanted was Baby It’s Cold Outside which ended up being unbelievably cute!

I wish I could take credit for all of the adorable décor but I just can’t. We had blue punch and a hot chocolate bar and two kinds of soups. For dessert I even branched out and made some smores inspired cupcakes which were so pretty but a pain in my butt to make! The day turned out perfectly and little Vayda was spoiled rotten!

Our Third Anniversary Trip

March 15, 2015

It is always a priority for Chris and I to get away to celebrate our anniversary. We have some big expenses this year, so we decided to keep our celebration a little more low key.  We headed to our favorite city, Indianapolis. We spent two evenings in the city. I made sure to book the hotel that we stayed in on our very first solo trip to Indy back when we first started college for nostalgia purposes.

On Friday night we did something new and went to a play.  The tickets were a gift from Chris for Valentine’s Day.  The play was a shortened version of The Giver put on by the Indiana Reparatory Theatre.  It was a pretty cool experience but way different than we anticipated.  The play was put on in one of their smaller more intimate theaters and the cast was only seven or so people. We had a great time and followed it up with some dinner downtown.

On Saturday we slept in and I convinced Chris that the best way to start the day was with Cinnabons in bed. They were as delicious as I remember! We then prepared ourselves for a little wine tour of central Indiana.

The day of wine tasting sort of developed over the course of our drive and got way better than we originally anticipated!  When we started out I had only planned on visiting Oliver Winery and Easley Winery downtown.  On the drive I ended up seeing signs for two other wineries to visit. It turned out to be the perfect afternoon!  Wine tasting is an activity that Chris and I enjoy equally and seems to be able to be done just about anywhere we go. In all we visited Oliver, Easley, Mallow Run, and Cedar Creek.  We walked away with ten bottles of wine. Don’t judge!

Saturday night was our actual anniversary dinner and we went to our favorite splurge restaurant, Ruth’s Chris.  They have the most delicious steaks! Plus we love that we can get all dressed up for each other. Those kind of opportunities are few and far between now a days.  The whole trip was a much needed reprieve from our every day life and some time to relax as a couple. We have a lot of life left to live and weekends like this one allow us to do so with some semblance of sanity. There is no greater company than my husband and its nice o have weekends where the only company I have is his!

Three Years

March 3, 2015

March 3. There have now been three full rotations around the sun since the day that I married my husband.   Anniversaries are just so special.  They symbolize so much love and bring me back to such a magical day in my life.

Its incredible to me how fast time goes each year. Chris and I have had the pleasure of being married for three years and together for nine. Each year gets better. Each year brings us exciting new experiences and always new challenges.  This past year in particular has been a rollercoaster.  We seemed to have picked up a string of bad luck and have been carrying it around in our pocket. I think we are both hoping year four will be the year we can kick it to the curb. Fingers crossed! Despite all the challenges thrown our way though, Chris and I have never been stronger. It sounds so cliché but it is 100% true.  It’s just one of the benefits of weathering storms together.  We are dorikly head over heels for one another.  We are so eager to spend a life time loving one another.

I want to end this post with a letter to my husband. I hope to preserve as much of my emotion as possible. Christopher,

I feel like year three was one of those long, exhausting legs of The Amazing Race.  We’ve reached the mat ready for a reprieve and Phil looked at us and said “Congratulations, you get to continue racing. The next leg starts now!” and handed us the next clue.  So off we go! I want to start by telling you how incredibly proud I am to call you my husband. You have matured so much in this past year and are the man I always knew you would become. In your personal life you have taken ahold of your health and ditched the medications and lost 15 pounds. You are such a hunk!  Professionally you have just been killing it.  Your drive and your dedication to your job is inspiring.  I know you have finally found your niche. VP here you come!  I always knew that we made the right decision in leaving the seed corn industry and 2 years later God is rewarding us for that leap of faith.

Thank you for being my pillar of  strength.  We were handed our fair share of lemons this past year and you and I collected them and made some tasty lemonade.  I’m so grateful to have you as a dedicated partner in life.  You have always been so invested in the success of our marriage and the time to enjoy those benefits is now. We have finally found our “sweet spot.”  I have always loved you, but this is different. This is pure and real and deep. I was discussing with someone else that I think true love is rare. I think that people either settle before they find it, or they find it and let it die.  What we have is true.  I just want to bottle some of it up to keep with me always.  To pull out and open on those hard days.  I can’t wait to gross our children out and teach them what real love is.  I’m looking so very forward to year four as we continue to hone our “sweet spot.”  As Corey Smith sings…

I’ve been scared as hell
Cause I’ve heard how the story goes
You get married and you settle down
And the passion withers like a rose
But I refuse to lose
This thing we have together
And I’ll keep the fire burning baby
Burning both ends of forever

This is the first dance
But it won’t be the last
Timeless moments like this
They won’t ever be a thing of the past
We’ll spark a candle tonight
An eternal flame of romance
When the honeymoon is over baby
I’ll still move you like the first dance

I love you. Happy Golden Anniversary.
