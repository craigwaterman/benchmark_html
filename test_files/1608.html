Close to 15,000 people poured into the Crestwood mall, arriving as early as 6 a.m. Saturday and Sunday, for an open casting call — vying for 2,100 parts to be an extra in George Clooney's new movie "Up in the Air," which begins filming here this spring. They anticipated the rush and were well organized, handing out tickets just to get in line. The tickets were for specific times.The line to get inside that room wrapped around much of the mall, but because the tickets had times on them, no one was forced to stand in line for more than two to three hours.Once someone arrived at the front of the line, they sat in front of the casting crew for only three to five minutes.The movie will take place, largely, in an airport, on airplanes and in hotels, so the casting crew was looking for business-type travelers and for anyone who might have experience with airline or hotel work
But there were all types. Hipsters. Sexpots. Young people. Old people. Squat, and towering. Lots of men in suits and trench coats, looking the part, with briefcases in hand. Once at the desk, the would-bes were asked a few questions: Could they stand on their feet for 12 or 13 hours at a time? Did they have any experience? When would they be available?

The United States Senate voted unanimously Monday to delay the deadline for the country's transition to digital television by four months, despite initial resistance from Republicans and objections from the wireless industry.The delay moves the latest date for the switch from analog television broadcasting to digital transmission to June 12 from Feb. 17.

Consumer advocates and Senate Democrats have said a delay is needed in order to accommodate many of the poor, elderly or rural households who are not ready for the switch. Digital layover the Senate has OK’d a delay for four months, until June 12. (The House still has to act.)

Anheuser-Busch will be rolling out its commercials for this years Super Bowl festivities on Feb. 1. Anheuser-Busch, brewer of Budweiser and Bud Light, is the biggest sponsor again this year, with nine spots featuring talk-show host Conan O'Brien and Clysedale steeds. The price to air a Super Bowl 43 commercial on NBC this year was a record $3m. AdAge argues that this huge price tag is still a bargain even in a downward economy. AdAge describes the success some companies are having with their Super Bowl Ads. Anheuser-Busch for instance says that Super Bowl week is now the 7th biggest selling week of the year for the brewer and it is not in summer.

To help make its case, the company is enlisting its iconic Budweiser Clydesdales for three Super Bowl commercials, the horses' greatest exposure yet.That level of exposure is meant to be a pep talk of sorts for Anheuser-Busch's fans, a signal that the company's values will endure. St. Louis is jaded in the wake of InBev's takeover. But A-B still wants Budweiser to be seen as the quintessential American beer. This commercial, and others, needs to be right.

The minute-long commercial filmed in Los Angeles is a fable about how one Clydesdale came to America. At first, the horse struggles to find his way. But he perseveres and finds his true calling, paving the way for future generations.

St. Louis born singer/producer Akon doesn’t know how old he is. His birth certificate shows he was born in April, 1977, making him a good 31-year-old. However, the singer reportedly refuses to accept that he’s that old, and in 2006, he even told one interviewer that he was 25. Sources such as the AP have indicated he was born in 1973. Legal documents released by The Smoking Gun list his name as Aliaune Damala Thiam and date of birth as 1973-04-30[10] or 1973-04-16.[11] According to the Smoking Gun article, "By all indications, he was born April 16, 1973."[12] When asked his age during an interview with VIBE, Akon reportedly responded, "The only thing I hide is my age... before I lie to you, I'd rather say nothing."[12] Well, we think two people could verify how whole he is--his parents!

WORDZ TO THE WHYs
Seventies disco diva Gloria Gaynor was interviewed on Tom Joyner Mornig Show last week and she said that her disco anthem, “I Will Survive“ was written by Dino Fekaris, This fact brought us to refect on other female anthems that have been written by men and found that many have been written by men. The first popular female anthem was Nancy Sinatra’s “These Boots Were Made Ffor Walkin” recorded in 1966. It was wriiten by her duet partner Lee Hazelwood. Next was Aretha Franklin‘s cover of “Respect” in 1967 (written by Otis Reddng). The next popular one was Helen Reddy’s “I Am Woman,“in 1972 and co -written by her and Ray Burton. In the 1980’s, the one that comes to mind is Karyn White's Superwoman"recorded in 1988 (written by Babyface),. In this decade, Beyonce’s “Irreplaceable” recorded in 2006 (written by Neyo) and her current anthem, “Single Ladies” recorded in 2008( written by The Dream). So we ask, why are most female anthems written by men? Why?
