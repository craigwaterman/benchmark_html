Yoga Under a Rainbow – Shiva- Fasting

March 18, 2015 / soulinteraction

I love that the weather is finally changing. I love taking my practice outside. There is something powerful about having the open sky above me. The limitless space is powerful. I started out by laying back and staring into the sky, and I was surprised to see a rainbow. The colors were pretty brilliant against the white clouds. I joked in my head that yoga was the pot of gold I’ve been searching for all this time. Since stepping a bit more deeply into my yoga journey, life is picking up. I feel that things are coming together, and that’s a good thing. I am still healing old wounds, but that is what yoga allows me to do. Yoga gives me the space to step into my heart, to listen with depth.

“Read a little. Meditate more. Think of God all the time.” -Yogananda

I’ve been reciting the Hanuman Chalisa everyday, I go through it 3-4 times in the morning. I’m feeling a big difference. I’ve started doing regular japa practice as well. Shiva is my go to God. Before I stepped into teaching yoga, Shiva came to me in a vision. He showed me some pretty remarkable things (which I’m not ready to share yet), and that began our relationship. Many of my tattoos are dedications to Shiva. Stepping back into mantra practice has opened a deeper connection in my relationship to Shiva, I’m excited to see where it goes. I’m working on designing some Shiva inspired sequences.

It is time to fast once again. Fasting and dietary cleansing helps me come back to center. Not really sure how I am going to structure this, but I’m starting tomorrow. I’ve been trying to go completely raw for a while, but I’ve always found excuses to step away from it. I think I’m going to do a juice feast, which is basically doing raw juices, water and nothing else for the next couple of weeks. I have all the supplies, it’s just time to do it.

Part of what I hope to explore with this blog, is living my yoga. Rather than just have my practice be something that I do, I want it to be something that I live. Much of my time in DC was spent chasing paths that were not necessarily mine. I got to explore facets of spirituality I might not have been able to otherwise, but in that time I lost a bit of my own path. The past few weeks I’ve felt a fog clearing itself out of my consciousness. I feel I am finally moving with purpose, and am aligning with my great work. It is going to be exciting to see what come from this work. My soul is nudging me in new directions, and It’s exciting.
