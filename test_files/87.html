Nike x Liberty London sneakers

Posted in Inspiration, Liberty, London-Land, My favorite stores, New in, Shoes, shopping, Sin categoría by Alicia in Londonland

                          

 

Nike sneakers and Liberty London: when two of my favorite things are together nothing can’t finish well for my bank account. If you follow my Twitter and Instagram you know how much I love shoes and sneakers, these last ones even before they were “on trend”. Trainers are perfect for walking around, discover cities, dance until you can’t move anymore or just to lay down in the park with a glass of wine. And if they look as beautiful as the Nike for Liberty collection AW 14  it is difficult to resist the temptation.

One of my favorite models is the Air Max 1, the classic but comfortable style, that you can wear with jeans, leggings and with dresses if you feel a bit braver. But I have to admit that when I saw the Air Max 1 Mid in that blue greyish color I felt very tempted to go for this more wintery shoe. So now I don’t know which one to go for. I created some looks with both of them to have more ideas, but I would like to know your thoughts.

 

Which one do you prefer: orange or grey? How would you wear them?

                           
                               

Have a great weekend!

Victoria´s Secret in London

Posted in London-Land, My favorite stores, shopping by Alicia in Londonland

The lingerie brand Victoria´s Secret have crossed the ocean to open the first 2 stores in London (and outside USA) few months ago. My favorite is the flagship branch in Bond Street: a huge place to wander around and feel like an angel in the 40,386 square-foot distributed in 4 floors (they have a smaller shop in Westfield Stratford too).

The decoration is very special: lots of colours, stripes, baroque style lamps with plenty of crystals, flowers, printed wallpaper, huge screens, mirrored walls, beautiful stairs and plenty of pictures of previous shows on the walls (so I am sure  your boyfriends or male friends wouldn´t mind to have a look inside ;)

The pictures speak by themselves, all I can say is that if you visit London and like fashion you can´t miss it. You will find most of the Victoria´s Secret collections there: lingerie, sleepwear, beauty, Pink, etc.. and probably you will leave the shop with one or two of their signature pink striped bags.

To celebrate their openings in UK you can find some knickers and bras with London prints of them…not my cup of tea, but, maybe´s someone else´s is.

If you are a VIP you can go straight away to the fourth floor where there are some private rooms and special services (and I would visit the toilets because they look like if they were from a luxury hotel, no details missing).

More over you can find costumes of the 2011 catwalk show inside the store and in the shop windows. They are full of feathers and swarovski crystals as the ones that Doutzen Kroes, Adriana Lima, Cara Delevigne, Miranda Kerr, Jourdan Dunn and Lily Donaldson were wearing  yesterday at Victoria’s Secret Fashion Show in the Lexington Armory, New York.

Did you like the brand before it arrived to Europe? Have you been already at the London stores?

A frozen Christmas in Harvey Nichols

Posted in Harvey Nichols, My favorite stores, Shop windows by Alicia in Londonland

One of my favorite London stores is Harvey Nichols and of course its windows: they are always amazing, very creative and original.

The Harvey Nichols website explains “that the windows this year play to the concept of an ice workshop, within which lovingly handcrafted Christmas props have been frozen in production. Majestic polar bears, animals and mannequins trapped in giant blocks of ice take centre stage amongst ‘discarded’ work tools cast in clear resin and empty work benches to create an eerie winter wonderland.

Snow drifts, snowflakes and glittering icicles complete a scene that is topped off by the two giant, abstract Christmas trees that flank the entrance to the store. Constructed from giant snow blocks, and swathed in clear plastic, hidden snow machines shower each with gently falling, glistening flakes”

What do you think?? Do you like them as I do?

xx

Una de mis tiendas favoritas es Harvey Nichols, sobre todo por sus escaparates: siempre son impresionantes, muy creativas y originales.

En la página web de Harvey Nichols  explican que “los escaparates de este año juega con el concepto de un taller de hielo, en el que se han preparado con mimo todos los detalles de un atrezzo navideño helado. Majestuosos osos polares, animales y maniquíes atrapados en bloques gigantes de hielo se convierten en el centro del interés entre “deshechados” moldes de herramientas de trabajo en resina transparente y mesas de trabajo vacías para crear un inquietante paisaje invernal.

Ventisca, copos de nieve y carámbanos completan una escena que se remata con dos gigantes y abstractos árboles de Navidad, que flanquean la entrada principal de la tienda. Construídos con bloques de hielo gigantes, causan sensación gracias a su plástico transparente, y ocultan los lanzadores de nieve, que rocían todo el escaparate con una incesante y brillante lluvia de copos“

¿Qué os parecen? ¿Os gustan tanto como a mí?
