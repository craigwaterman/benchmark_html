I Can’t Stand Looking at You, Look at Me, While I Look at Myself in the Mirror

  The biggest hurdle of married life thus far has not been:
A.   The nightly cover-tug-of-war
B.    The ghastly sight of Wonder Bread in my fridge
C.    The adaptation towards an 8:30 pm bedtime with little to no Bravo TV beforehand.
Rather, the confrontation that most sucked the air from beneath my fresh-off-the-alter wings had been born from four words: “Are you ready yet?” Allow me to plead my case. You see, like many other couples, mine and Isaac’s dating life had been virtually drama-free probably wholly due to the fact that we did not live under the same roof. For three years he was absolved of the painstaking pleasure that was watching me get ready for a wedding or a night out. As far as he was concerned, I woke up like this; face fresh as a dewy daisy and hair imperfectly swept into an exaggerated attempt at a look I like to call sexy bedhead chic. While I’ve never been one for makeup or glamour per se – opting for a distressed tee and jeans – I must admit it takes an equal,  if not more amount of time to perfect the “this is my nightgown as well as my nighttime attire” style I had accustomed myself to.
            Imagine the rude awakening Isaac had upon realizing that his ‘minimalist’ and ‘carefree’ girlfriend actually spent hours in front of the mirror, ruminating her sartorial options. In my defense, Gossip Girl and every rom-com ever made had led me to believe that guys actually enjoyedwatching a woman dress and undress, over and over again. What’s an hour better spent than one watching THE GIRL OF YOUR DREAMS slip and un-slip into outfits of little to no comfort? What’s an ego boost more fulfilling than the one gotten from knowing girl in question will likely decide on the outfit you deem most flattering? Said girl is partially trying on a rotation of five different outfits because she wishes to please you and maybe garner a compliment along the lines of “damn Africa, remind me how I snagged you again?” Feminists put your claws away. Of course, we women dress in order to project a certain level of respect and esteem we have for ourselves – but there is absolutely nothing wrong with wanting a nod of approval from your sig-other. Then again, if your guy pageants a proclivity towards cheek-bearing bandage skirts, he can very well take a backseat.
            That being said, if you are:
A.   Not going to revel in the prolonged and private fashion show that is “getting ready” then please leave the room
B.    Going to withhold your opinion on my sartorial options, you relinquish the right to comment on the end result
C.    Making a point of asking me, when I will be ready every 7 minutes or so, I will spitefully curl my hair with my fingers for five extra minutes.

And finally, a note to the guys because I understand that some of you honestly want to know how it’s possible hell froze over in the time it took your wife to get ready. I believe that there is some innate part of us that enjoys the conflict and challenge that getting ready poses. We enjoy speculating over dozens of outfits, the parts in our hair and the sequence of leather bracelets on our arms; it makes us happy. This is the result of having grown up with a plastic doll whose wardrobe was seemingly limitless. Furthermore, we like complaining, most commonly “that we have nothing to wear.” It will help if you accept that even if we bunkered down in the very closet of Carrie Bradshaw herself, we still would have “no clothes.” Industries have been built on this claim. One last thing, and this is most important: Do not, under any circumstances, comment on your wife or girlfriends mirror face; she is well aware that her eyes go wide, her cheekbones gaunt,  and her lips ducky. Allow us to have our Janis Dickinson moment.
