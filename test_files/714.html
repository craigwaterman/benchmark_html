Website Maintenance Plans

Why do you need a maintenance plan for your website?

Great question! You have just invested hundreds, if not thousands, of dollars in a website to firmly establish your presence on the internet and get found by all of your potential customers. Do you have the time and expertise to maintain it properly and give it the TLC it deserves so that it will continue to work for you?

Regularly updating your website is not just a matter of changing the price here or a graphic there. Regular updates to your website are very important to your Search Engine Optimization and will help ensure that your hard earned dollars don't go to waste by dropping your search engine rankings. Search engines love to crawl web pages, but if your content is stale and outdated, they won't bother, therefore your rankings drop. Can you afford that?

Additionally, your visitors to your site want to see that the content is being updated regularly. It gives them more confidence to buy from you, knowing that you are really out there and providing them with the current information that they want. And it's all about giving your clients what they want, right?

Here are just a few of the added benefits to a website maintenance plan:

One low fee per month - you don't have to hassle with bill paying, it's automatic with PayPal!
Includes phone consultation time - get all your questions answered and learn how to be pro-active in your website's success!
Allows you the freedom to make frequent updates - and keep your site higher in the search engine rankings
Lets you focus on your business, not website maintenance, which means you get to do what you do best - earn money for your business!
Communication with your hosting company to troubleshoot problems, should they arise

What is Microsoft .Net Framework


The Microsoft .Net Framework is a platform that provides tools and technologies you need to build Networked Applications as well as Distributed Web Services and Web Applications. The .Net Framework provides the necessary compile time and run-time foundation to build and run any language that conforms to the Common Language Specification (CLS).The main two components of .Net Framework are Common Language Runtime (CLR) and .Net Framework Class Library (FCL).
The Common Language Runtime (CLR) is the runtime environment of the .Net Framework , that executes and manages all running code like a Virtual Machine. The .Net Framework Class Library (FCL) is a huge collection of language-independent and type-safe reusable classes. The .Net Framework Class Libraries (FCL) are arranged into a logical grouping according to their functionality and usability is called Namespaces.
