5 Tips for First-Time Deer Hunters

Posted by Team Springfield

Oct 29, 2014 9:00:00 AM

  • Tweet

Hunting can be an exhilarating activity that tests a number of skills, such as precision, focus, and determination. For those with no experience, deer hunting can be a very rewarding place to start. However, before going on a hunt, it’s important to consider a number of things to ensure you are prepared and have a worthwhile experience.

Important Tips for First-Timers

Before you can score a trophy buck, you’ll need to start with the basics:

  • Get a license. The first step to hunting is getting your license. Each state has different requirements, so you’ll need to look into the details regarding licensure. Some places simply require you to pay a fee, while others ask you to complete tests and other requirements.
  • Find a good place. Much like real estate, hunting is all about location. You can have all the best equipment, accessories and talent, but it’s useless without something to hunt. If you know someone with hunting property, that’s a good place to start. If you don’t have connections, try getting in touch with a local hunting club.
  • Choose a firearm. Choosing a firearm is another crucial step. When determining which firearm to take, consider things like accuracy and distance. Some firearms are better than others for hunting at close versus long range.
  • Suit up. Hunters don’t dress in camouflage just for the fun of it. Deer are able to spot people from long distances, and camouflage helps hunters blend into the environment. In addition to wearing clothing that matches the environment, you should wear something that’s orange or bright colored. This is a safety feature that allows other hunters—but not deer—to see you; deer are red-green color blind.
  • Be patient. Unless you get lucky, you probably won’t come home with anything your first time out. Hunting requires specialized skills that are developed over time. The best thing you can do is remain patient and focused throughout the hunt. Even a momentary lapse in judgment can cause you to miss a deer.
