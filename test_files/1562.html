Honey. That is the name of one of the most sweet, rich, fresh, tart, delicate, and beautiful perfumes to ever caress my nose. I went to Belk with my mom when she got the fragrance in 2013, and I knew that I could always find it if I just remembered the bold name stamped on the bottle: Marc Jacobs.
Perfume tends to fall more into the beauty sector these days, but in all honesty, it's a subset of fashion. Perfume has been around for a very long time. When perfume really started taking off among the masses, it did so through fashion. Many of the most desired perfumes are by Coco Chanel, Yves Saint Laurent, etc., who all had their beginnings in fashion.

Perfume is encompassed within fashion because it assists in enhancing the over all feel of a particular wardrobe while giving attitude to the clothing and the wearer and making the outfit not only a visual experience but an interactive one. I am not huge on perfume as I am in to more natural moisturizers and such, but when I do wear it, my mood is touched with additional sohistication, confidence, and control. Using fragrance along with la mode gives a positive intensity to whatever set is being brought forward.
This perfume (below) was given to me by my mom. It is a pretty champagne color, and it smells like a formal gathering on a cold night mixed with fresh roses, essential oils, and it has a rich yet low key sweetness. Though this description is a bit abstract, it is the only way to accurately describe this amazing smell. It is Verona Paris by Yves de Sistelle, and it is my favorite fragrance besides my Modern Muse by Estée Lauder (and of course Honey). Due to its heavy smell, I find it best to wear during fall or winter.
Perfume is just as apart of fashion as bracelets and earrings are: not mandatory but fun to add whenever you choose. Even if I don't feel like spraying it directly on me, I may add a touch of fragrance to my scarf of the neckline of my jacket for that faint sense of mystery. Are you into perfume? l'importance de sourire à la mode (The Importance of Smiling in Fashion)

10/25/2015

0 Comments

 
No smiling in fashion. Ever. - The rarely spoken but worldwide motto of fashion.
I can't decide whether I am sad or bored when I look at a model wearing gorgeous clothing who looks like someone poured dirt in her coffee. I get so hyped when the latest preview to a seasonal collection is released, but the excitement dies down a bit when I feel anger radiating off of my computer screen or magazine page. Many of the models present the clothes with a standard look of an emotionless expression, half closed eyelids, and a tight or limp frown. Why is smiling so rare in the fashion industry?
Let's get to the root of it all. 99% of the time the designer dictates whether the model(s) will smile or not depending on the clothing in the show, what message he or she is trying to convey, what reaction he or she wants from the crowd, the audience he or she is targeting, etc.. The main goal is to captivate the audience and draw them in to buy the clothing. There are also tactics welcoming to the critics that can result in phenomenal reviews. Even though many designers have rare, fun, and unique approaches, designers still appreciate respect and being taken seriously putting a heavy amount of this responsibility on the models after clothing production is complete.

The majority of designers who opt for no smiling do so because stern and serious expressions are viewed as mature and sensible. A look of seriousness is a tactic for keeping the attention on the clothing piece(s) and not the model. Those dark expressions are believed to be alluring and admirable and show results of selling the items better, and the same perceptions are held among designers, critics, as well as the general audience because this notion has been programmed as what is right and branded into this community for so long. In regards to her rare smiles, Victoria Beckham even stated, "I'm smiling on the inside, but I feel like I have a responsibility to the fashion community." How sad.

I can't understand why smiling is so frowned upon. Smiles are welcoming, captivating, and beautiful! For example, anytime I've started falling for a guy it began with his smile. The same concept applies to fashion. Do you want to display a collection that people will fall in love with/for? Fill it with smiles! They draw people in! There are rare occasions where designers have smiling models such as in Stella McCartney's Fall/Winter 2010-2011 collection, but they are often bashed for it.

Smiling adds to the entire show and makes it fun! Although Victoria's Secret has a completely different image than our big name designers today, the models always come out with a smile and individual attitude keeping things light and happy. Why is their annual fashion show such a huge deal? Though it may have to do with the attire and celebrity performers, it is overall a time full of festivity which I believe to be an important aspect of fashion shows despite the fact that it is lacking in many. I remember stopping by Bryanboy's site, the father of fashion blogging, to watch the Valentino Haute Couture Fall/Winter 2015-2016 show. I could not even get past the haunted house music. I tried to focus on something else such as the clothing, but again, the models looked depressed. I did make it through a whole minute though.
Don't get me wrong, not smiling is not bad--it just needs to be balanced with the lovely teeth that are being hid. Even a half smirk will do. Half smirks are sassy. In my first modeling photo shoot it was requested that I smile which I loved. I felt like myself. In some of my photos I smile. In some I don't. You can see that for yourself. Some may view the smiling as being unprofessional. I view it as being real.

Thank you lovelies,
Have a beautiful week.

tirer le meilleur parti de votre (hypothétique) panier (Getting the Best out of Your Hypothetical Shopping Cart)

10/21/2015

0 Comments

 
Last week, I did the unthinkable. I shopped at Forever 21...again. Yes, my previous experience with them was not quite up to par, and I gave them an overall rating of 87%. Nonetheless, they have a wide range of products catering to almost all styles, they are affordable, and they are the only place that has everything on my mental wishlist at prices that are acceptable to me. Is that even possible? It is. In this purchase, I got an all purpose body massager, which is really good to use for your feet if you enjoy wearing heels often like I do, and an adorable rotary phone shaped crossbody that says, "Call me..." on the front that costs $17.90.

Since next month is my birthday (November 19th), and Balmain x H&M comes out next month (*screeches*), I have already created a hypothetical shopping cart of the items that I want, but I have just viewed the prices, and I know that I can not get them all. To cope with this reality as well as other shopping dilemmas in the past, I came up with a guide (below photos) for getting the best out of your (hypothetical) shopping cart. Not all of these steps will be easy, so take the time that you need.
#1 Budget-
Establishing a budget is the most important and often overlooked step. It is so much harder to let go of the items you can not afford if you do not already have a budget in place. Having a budget makes it easier to pick out items within that range and eliminate those that surpass.

#2 Fill, Fill, Fill-
Fill up that cart with any items you want that fall within your budget as individual items. Do you like it? Do you want it? Add it to your cart.

#3 Examine-
Thoroughly look over all of the items that you added in #2. Notice what really stands out to you. Observe and take note of the items that you find most appealing. What items do you like more than others? What are the most original/unique pieces? Examine it all.

#4 Repeat #3-
Take a break for a few seconds or minutes, come back, and examine again.

#5 Remove the most and least expensive items-
What?! Remove items?! I know. It has to be done. Without stopping to admire the items, delete the most expensive and least expensive off the list. This way, you are not spending all you have, and you are not settling either. There can be exceptions...sometimes.

-------------------Whenever you reach a price that you are comfortable with, you are ready to purchase your items.----------------------

#6 Examine Again-
Examine again. Going back over what you picked out will really help you narrow things down in the long run--some things will start to lose their appeal.

#7 Remove the Most Logical Item-
Aka, the boring item.

#8 Delete the First Three Items at the Top of Your List-
If your list happens to be that long. I have had lists this long.

#9 Repeat Steps #3-8 Until You Reach an Overall Price that You are Comfortable With.

#10 Admire Your Masterpiece and Claim Your Reward.
Congratulations. You have just accomplished something magnificent. It is now time to reap your reward by purchasing your items with your own money.

Sometimes it's hard to let go of things that we can't have. Letting go can be good though--it helps keep you grounded and makes room for even more splendid blessings in the future!

Thank you lovelies,
Stay fabulous.

Tenues: la vérité derrière (Outfits: The Truth Behind)

10/14/2015

0 Comments

 
Outfits speak whether the wearer is talking or not. Each outfit that premieres has its own personal message to tell what the wearer is thinking, feeling, or dreaming in terms of daydreams or aspirations. Even individual aspects within the outfit such as choice of clothing article, color, etc.. sing their own song. For an on the go cheat sheet to outfit interpretations, I have 5 aspects listed below along with the stories behind them. All of these interpretations, like all of the content I provide, are derived of my own thought and are my own individual ideas. These are my overall definitions, so there are slight variations within each in regards to real life. My interpretations are solely for a closer connection to your inner style as well as the style of others and for fun!
1) Stripes
Stripes represent knowledge in the fashion world. Pertinent to the Parisian chic and classic apparel, stripes grant instant refinement and poise. These sturdy lines can be horizontal or vertical and are easy to find. They are also top on the list of many who have "no idea what to wear" for a particular day. Simply grab a striped item and go.

2) Baggy
Baggy anything exudes extreme confidence and/or exhaustion. Baggy pants such as harem pants, usually paired with a fitted top or bra often is a symbol of confidence that can be found alluring; this look also represents someone who is exciting, fun, and spunky. Baggy items such as a baggy hoodie can result in the same effects if the personality of an individual is compatible, or it can convey tiredness/a lack of sleep and a need to go back home and start fresh the next day.

3) Spandex
This can be a vague term. What I am referring to in this definition are leggings, bodysuits/leotards, shorts, tank tops, and crop tops. All of these items are eye-catching and in your face. Spandex worn on the outside is worn by the dreamer. The ones who have big aspirations. The ones who feel most at ease when they are doing what they love. Spandex allows the wearer to feel free and in his or her own element to achieve the best of their goals.

4) Black
Well hello there edgy. Black takes maturity, elegance, mystery, etc. to their highest levels. Wearing black alone represents edginess all by itself regardless of the clothing piece that it is portrayed in. As I have said before, you can never own too much black; it never gets old. Part of this statement may have to do with the fact that I am a Scorpio, and black is basically the signature color for Scorpios. Regardless, black= the secret sparkle fabulous fashion in a bottle.

5) Pop Culture
Pop Culture clothing is so unique as it takes music, slang, images, and brands from our society, and reshapes them into wearable items. Literally. The most popular example that I can think of is Moschino. From candy to paint cans, the creative directors find a way to revamp just about any thing choose. Those who dress in Pop Culture pieces are very honest, and many times have no filter. The clothing describes theses individuals in the most obvious way possible: bold and blunt.



In all honesty, everyone knows within them what they are trying to reveal to the world through their attire. Sometimes we want to display our feelings out in the open, and subconsciously or consciously, we may wear outfits that reflect these emotions. Maybe you are feeling something you just can't or don't want to talk about but you dress in a way that speaks for you such as an outfit that is an open statement of your feeling or the lack thereof. We all have stories; some are shared with many while others are left untold. When you can't bring your words to the surface, your outfit does it for you.

Thank you lovelies,
Stay fabulous.

Parlons fourrures (Let's Talk Furs)

10/11/2015

0 Comments

 
Fur (as defined by me, and in the order that I deem most prevalent):

noun-
a soft, beautiful, rare, delicate, and taboo organic material protecting many mammals and many times is unfortunately extracted by human hands.

"Did you hear about Stella McCartney? She only uses inorganic materials such as faux fur to protect animals."
"Really?!" Go Stella!"

adjective-
A material, inorganic or organic, having the consistency of fur.

"Hi there, I'm looking for some furry socks."
"I believe the term is fuzzy"
"No, it's furry."
"..."

verb-
To coat someone or something in fur or a fur like material.

"This dress is a bit bland. Would you fur it up for me?"
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ok. Maybe the last definition was a bit over the top. The essential message is that there are so many definitions of fur as well as uses, and it can be a sensitive topic to talk about with it involving animals and a heavy hand from the fashion industry. With this topic, there is a huge divide on whether using real fur in clothing is ethical or not.

From designers such as Stella McCartney and various retailers like American Eagle Outfitters, businesses are taking a stand on the subject and standing by their chosen position. McCartney even has a campaign for this fall that features coats and more of synthetic fur. 

There are others who prefer to stick to primordial means of staying warm with using real fur such as Alexander Wang and one of my favorite adjectives, Gucci. On Alexander Wang's site, he has a rabbit's foot key chain, from a real rabbit, that is sold out. The current price listed is $75, and I do not know the quantity of rabbits feet he sold (is that even sanitary?).

To sum it all up, we are not in the caveman days, and there are other means for feeling the luxuries of fur without harming any animals such as synthetic furs. 

I love synthetic/faux fur and will not wear any real/organic furs or use them in any of my designs. In the simplest of words, I find it unethical. Because of my admiration of faux fur, I have incorporated some into my own fashion with two vests and a pair of gloves that I would like to share with you.

Vest 1: Aéropostale
Vest 2: Belk Juniors
Gloves: JCPenney
Not even gonna lie, I think these items are quite luxurious. My faux fur luxuries: affordable, fun, and chic. Not only do these items save lives, they don't shed.

A closer view of how I tied it:

Mon expérience Vogue Communauté (My Vogue Community Experience)

10/4/2015

0 Comments

 
As I gazed upon the strewn magazine pages scattered on my floor and stuck to my wall, I snuggled up in my bed and picked up the remnants of my Vogue magazine looking for the one thing that felt like no other and would add a new flame to my sector of inspiration. Curling each page under my fingers, I knew what I desired was inside despite the fact that over half the magazine now resided outside of the binding. I flipped and flipped, and right when I started to doubt, there it was before me; not pure inspiration for my walls of fame, but a ticket inside of the Vogue community itself: an opportunity for all to join the Vogue Insiders. I was very excited to sign right up, but for some reason that I cannot remember, I put it off--for months.

This past summer, I finally felt ready to accept my invitation and claim my position in the Vogue empire. I charged my laptop the night before and woke up early to fill out my application. The application prompted me through question after question regarding how long I have been reading Vogue to what expertise I would provide for the community. Everything went successfully until the last question. I was asked my age. After supplying this information, which is 16 years, I was prompted to a message that told me I must be 18 to be a member along with an apology. This is something that they could have told me so that I did not waist 10 minutes carefully filling out their questionnaire.

Less than a week later, I discovered another Vogue community experience known as the Condé Nast Style Society. To avoid recounting the full details to you twice, I have provided the description of that experience following the few photos below in an email that I crafted for Vogue on August 30th, 2015.

Hello Vogue Team,
 
My name is R’Nelle CyRe’ Collins (pronounced ar-nell suh-ray Collins), and I am an avid reader of Vogue. I just recently subscribed to Vogue by mail and look forward to receiving my next issue.
I have a fashion blog called https://rnellesclassicreations.weebly.com , and I just put up a review on the September issue of Vogue.
I have wanted to be a part of the Vogue community for some time and was thrilled when I read about the Vogue Insiders as well as the Condé Nast Style Society. I started an application for the Vogue Insiders and Condé Nast Style Society but was turned down because I am sixteen. I will be seventeen in November, but that does not make a difference regarding the message I encountered during my application. I was shocked when I was prompted through every question of the Vogue Insiders application just to be shut down at the end where I was told I must be eighteen by Melissa Halverson, the Vogue Insiders Director. The Condé Nast Style Society told me there wasn’t a place for me in their society at this time after entering my age. As a reader of Vogue and a maven in fashion, that response is not one that pleases me—it is disheartening. I would treasure being a contributor in the Vogue community whether it be through these organizations or some other means, and I would bring you my best.
I eagerly await your response as I cannot wait to share my experience with my fellow readers, friends, and followers.
Thank you,
R’Nelle CyRe’ Collins
https://rnellesclassicreations.weebly.com
 I did send the email in with the hopes of receiving a response, but the only emails that I have gotten from Vogue since then are their daily updates about various topics along with my invoice for a full year of Vogue starting with the October issue that I have yet to receive. What a wonderful organization I have given almost $20 to for only 12 magazines. Not to mention, I have been buying their magazines long before I subscribed and spreading how wonderful their platform is in regards to their approach and content. They are just so deserving of my time and money.

My disappointment does not solely lie in all the money that I put into Vogue. I am also dissapointed because there are these communities out there that put on a facade of friendliness with welcoming gestures and exclude individuals because of their age. My age does not define my knowledge or what I can do. It does not define my maturity or capacity to be apart of societies such as the Vogue insiders. It is simply my age. Yes, it is significant especially in regard to other things in this world, but not whether I should be admitted to share ideas with those who are interested in the same topics and realms as me. No one seems to really consider or cater to those in the outskirts. Take modeling for instance. Most places want girls who are at least 5'9 or above. There are a few exceptions, but we're told that is what makes a real model. Obviously this is not true. Even with petite models, most organizations want girls who are 5'3 or below. No one really considers the middle girls. What about the girls who are 5'5 like me? Or any height around that? What about the girls who are over 6ft who are told that they are too tall? Where do we get included into the model world/community? Yes, this is a bit different than the Vogue situation. The nature is still the same though.

The service that Vogue has done me so far has been measly. I had expected higher from such a label, but I am learning to stop expecting people and things to be the way that I think they ought to be because it does not change the way that things really are. Yes, they did send me the Vogue purse that I mentioned in My Vogue 2015 Fall Edition Review as promised which is a little burgundy faux alligator skin bag that looks like it cost $1 to make, but they neglected to acknowledge my email with a response, even if that response would have only been a no. I also await my October issue which I should have received by October 1st, because it stated that date in my invoice, and I am supposed to get my issues sooner that I would from the store because I subscribed. Despite their indirect rejection, I know that you do not need anyone's approval to be you and do what you love. I also now know where not to spend $20.

Thank you lovelies,
I hope you have an amazing week.

R'Nelle CyRe'
