Oktoberfest and a Hauntingly Good Time

Oktoberfest, pumpkins, fresh pickin’s and a spooktacular good time, that’s what is in store for October on the Peninsula. With the cooler temperatures breezing in this season, fall is the perfect time to grab a few friends or neighbors to celebrate harvest time. Here are a few events and day trips ideas:

image credit @masonite

Oktoberfest is at hand on the Peninsula October 4 from 11am-5pm at Hillier Aviation Museum! Get your tickets here. Over two hundred years after Crown Prince Ludwig and Princess Therese of Saxony-Hildburghausen wed, the Hiller Aviation Museum joins the celebration with a special Oktoberfest beer, music and dining event. Beer Meister arrives by helicopter at 10:50am. The Oktoberfest event is for all ages but you must be 21 or older to purchase or consume alcohol. The museum exhibit gallery and displays are open all day. The Flight Sim Zone is open from 11am – 2pm (limited seating, tickets required). Last pour at 4 pm.

image credit: downtownsanmateo

Downtown San Mateo presents the Annual Halloween Fun Fest, a free family-friendly event on October 25 from 11-2. Come in costume and trick-or-treat along 3d Avenue! Enjoy crafts, pumpkins, jump houses, and music in the US Bank parking lot (3rd and San Mateo Drive).

Halloween “SPOOK”TACULAR at Hillsdale Shopping Center
On Friday, October 31, local children and their families are invited to a thrilling Halloween “Spook”tacular event at our neighboring Hillsdale Shopping Center! TRICK-OR-TREAT: 5-6pm ONLY, while supplies last. Look for the stores with the window signs. To kick-off festivities, participating stores will hand out treats to costumed children ages 12 and under. Kids will also enjoy Halloween themed special events and crafts from 5:30 – 7:00 pm.

…and for the adults these spooky cocktails are perfect for a spectacular Halloween party!

You know that fall has arrived when you’re surrounded by monster 1,200+ pound pumpkins at the Half Moon Bay Pumpkin Festival. Surround yourself with gigantically enormous champion gourds, the Great Pumpkin Parade and pie-eating contests. Of course, there are plenty of pumpkin patches to wander while finding your perfect pumpkin, big or small. Mark your calendars for October 18-19 from 9-5 on Main Street.

The festival kicks off early on October 13 from 7-11 with the 41st Annual WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP PUMPKIN WEIGH-OFF –– the Super Bowl of weigh-offs. See colossal pumpkins forklifted onto the stage in an attempt to break a world record on the scale and take home $30,000. Last year’s mega-gourd winner came in at 1,985 pounds.

Head north and take in Petaluma’s scenic landscape as you stroll through the spacious organic pastures of Chileno Valley Ranch. About 600 acres of beautiful hilly coastal Marin grassland is home to natural beef, organic apples, restored creek, fish, birds, native plants and other animals on the Ranch. They offer certified organic varieties of everyone’s favorite autumn fruit, such as Orin, Pinova, Molly’s Delicious, Jonafree and William’s Pride, as well as Asian Pears, until the end of October. Chileno Valley is open for U Pick during the months of September and October.

Take home some apples home to make fresh apple fritters the easy way! Simply dip fresh apple rings in buttermilk batter and fry to a golden crisp.

If you are feeling the chill and want to spice things up, be sure to get the October 16th Red Hot Roundup at Bay Meadows on your event calendar. Event details can be found here.
