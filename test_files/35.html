Post Workout Thoughts: Crack? Or Some Serious Motivation

Sometimes it's tough forcing yourself to get to the gym. Some days you just have no energy nor any motivation to get your workout in. You probably would just rather stay in bed or relax at home watching T.V. Usually when this happens to me, I just pull out my Spark pre workout and, in half an hour, I'm bouncing around, talking everybody's ears off and can't wait to workout.

Sounds like the perfect remedy for any lazy, gym lovers like myself, but not everybody agrees that pre workout supplements are good for you.

Pre workouts offer energy and endurance while working out. Many of the ingredients are unpronounceable, let alone incomprehensible without asking Google for help, which makes many health professionals skeptical. Most pre workouts all have around the same ingredients: beta alanine, alpha keto-glutarate, vasodilator, and caffeine. Lots and lots of caffeine.

"Some of these products have as much caffeine as three cups of coffee," Dr. Christopher Balgobin said. "I've heard of products out there that have enough caffeine for 20 cups of coffee." ('It's Like Crack') Too much caffeine can lead to heart problems.

But the most controversial ingredient is DMAA, or 1-3 dimethylamylamine. I can't even pronounce that. It's essentially like Adderall or Ritalin but legalized. It used to actually be banned by our government but still used in the military. This chemical helps with focusing and giving you energy.

In other words; it's basically legalized crack. It really just gets you gone.

Still, I believe that it's about finding the RIGHT pre workout supplement. I've taken pre workouts that make me shake and my heart skips a beat. It took awhile before I found my perfect pre workout; it gets me energized and motivated without the shaking. Just find a pre workout that feels right to you and you'll be golden. If you are worried about the ingredients and heart problems then I suggest you talk to a health professional to see which ones would be better for you to take.

Soy vs Whey Protein

For so many years, whey protein has been thought to have been the absolute best choice when it came choosing which protein powder you should consume. According to most people, whey based protein powders are considered more superior and is the biggest selling protein supplement. What makes whey the perfect post-workout shake is the perfect combination of muscle-building amino acids and it enhances the immune system. It has a powerful anabolic effect on muscle growth.

On the other hand you have soy protein powders. In the past, they have carried a bad rep. Thought to be more "feministic", people believed that this base made it harder for anyone to actually gain muscle. Recent studies have proven otherwise. It not only helps with muscle growth but can actually be MORE effective than whey based protein powders.

Some past studies have shown that the ingredients found in soy have been linked to the decrease of testosterone and an increase of estrogen. A phytoestrogen found in soy are thought to upset hormone in balance, HOWEVER, many scientists believe that soy is innocuous (harmful or offensive).

Soy is considered the only plant-based protein that is considered highly beneficial. It contains all 9  essential amino-acids to help support growth and development. Soy is made up of 30% carbohydrates (15% of which is fibre), 38% protein, 18% of mostly unsaturated oil, and 14% moisture. It seems to be the perfect ration for health and well-being.

Soy and whey proteins compliment each other well when used together in an exercise program. Whey protein contains branched chain amino acids which is an important energy source during exercise. Soy protein, on the other hand, contains arginine and glutamine. Arginine is well known as a stimulant of anabolic hormones. In other words, it stimulates muscle formation. Glutamine is essential during metabolic stress.

The latest studies have proven that soy protein does NOT decrease testosterone and raise estrogen as previously thought in the past. The American College of Sports Medicine found the post-workout consumption of soy drink to be statistically significant in hastening mass, and increases strength when compared to other post-workout formulations in May 2004. In other words, both soy and milk protein were effective in supporting muscle hypertrophy.

In April, 2005, The Solae Company found that daily supplementation of soy protein, whey, or a soy/whey blend resulted in in increase to lean body mass. It did NOT negatively affect testosterone in any way.

Overall, athletes who incorporate both soy and whey protein in their nutrition plans may benefit from the prolonged release of amino acids. In short, both soy and whey are good for you, its all about what you prefer.

Your Metabolism and Why You Should Care

Your Metabolism Can't Change, but It Can Be Influenced

Your metabolism is the way your body processes everything you eat and drink. It's a never-ending, two-part process of anabolism and catabolism. Anabolism is when energy is created and stored in your body. Catabolism is when energy is released. While you can't directly control how your metabolism works (your endocrine system does a pretty good job of it anyway), you CAN control what you eat, how much you eat, and how much exercise you get every day. These three factors and only these three factors have power over your metabolism! 

"Fast Metabolism" is (Mostly) a Myth

Your metabolism is as unique as you are. It is also based on your age, gender, genetics, muscle mass, and certain environmental factors. Even if your best friend is able to eat whatever he/she wants without gaining any fat, it is unlikely that they have a "faster" metabolism or that her metabolic rate is creating such a difference. She or he may have just found that perfect ratio of lifestyle factors. This means that they've managed to balance how many calories they take in, how much exercise they are getting, how much muscle mass they have, and how much sleep they get. Usually these types of people who claim to have "fast" metabolisms are already slender, physically fit, and get tons of uninterrupted sleep each night.

Exercise Boosts Metabolism 

It's insanely basic: the more active you are, the more calories you burn. How much activity you get in a day has more of an impact on your weight loss/gain than your metabolism. We use 10% of the calories in our food just to process that food, but don't counter on that 10% alone to slim your waist! Exercise is a huge part of losing weight because it's something YOU can control!

More Muscle Mass = Better Metabolism

Having muscle mass makes you strong and helps burn more calories both when you are working out and when you're just chilling at home. Muscle mass is usually built up with strength training such as lifting weights. Multiple studies have found that when you add strength training to your weekly workout, your basal metabolism is given a boost!

Healthy Sleep = Healthy Metabolism

Your lack of sleep may be inhibiting your weight loss. A study by the University of Chicago found that, if we don't get enough sleep, "sleep debt" changes the way our endocrine system works and it's not positive. It alters how our bodies regulate blood sugar levels and how we store energy. It changes so much that participants showed early signs of diabetes! If you normally get 7 to 8 hours of sleep a night, then one or two hours gained or lost won't make much of a difference. If you aren't getting more than 4-5 hours a night, you definitely will need to add more hours of sleep at night to help kickstart your weightless!

Metabolism Slows With Age

The older we get, the slower our metabolism works. Studies have found that postmenopausal women have about a 10% decrease in their BMR. To combat this, you will need to step it up! Beginning in the 40's or 50's, you will need fewer calories to maintain your weight. (About 200 calories less). And don't forget to exercise!

Bliss and Commander Go-Pack: NO NO NO

I'm against most caffeinated "weight loss" pills but these two take the cake on my most hated weight loss pills list. I hear advertisements for these two supplements every day on the radio. I was actually tempted to record it but I'm sure almost all of you have heard them.

The Commander and Bliss Go-Pack sold at Supplement Superstores are designed to help with weight loss. The ingredients are designed to work together to increase metabolism, reduce cravings, help control appetite, and boost energy levels. It's sole purpose is to "speed up your weight loss program" and get you results. Sounds perfect right?

If it has to have a "Tolerance Assessment Phase" then you should probably not be taking it. They claim that everyone has different levels of tolerance, which is true, but if they actually put this warning in big letters on their website, then it's obviously something you should pay attention to. Also, in their FAQ page, one question asked if it was OK to take these pills if they have cardiac arrhythmia. Guess what their answer was. "No. If you have cardiac arrhythmia or any other cardiac problems you should avoid these Go-Packs". Oh, you also should't be taking it if you have high blood pressure, or if you are taking any type of MAO-Inhibitor, anti-depressants, aspirin, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs or any products containing stimulants or any other medications. Do you even need anymore warnings?

They even claim that the Go-Packs "will be of great benefit to you" if you don't workout much. WHAT. Working out and being active is what strengthens your body! Nutrition and working out is what gets you results. They don't even mention nutrition anywhere on their site, which may lead to people believing they can eat whatever they want and they will still lose weight while on this pill.

My final argument against this horrible supplement is that it is advertised as a APPETITE SUPPRESSANT. Anything that "suppresses your appetite" is BAD BAD BAD. Take a look at the ingredients. Sure, some of them are natural herbs and plants, but it's the chemicals in it that worry me. I'm very wary of any caffeine pills. Caffeine can become addicting, and if not careful, can hinder your weight loss. Here are three out of the 24 ingredients of the Bliss Go-Pack (from their website):

Niacin 

Niacin has been a popular drug to prevent cardiovascular disease. Studies have revealed that this B-vitamin can reduce "bad" cholesterol levels (LDL) and raise "good" cholesterol levels (HDL). BUT recent studies have found that Niacin failed to show that it reduced the risk of heart attack or stroke. These studies also revealed that Niacin has some harmful side effects and is hard to tolerate.

DMAE

This chemical is used for treating ADHD, Alzheimer's, and autism. I think all I had to write about this was that it was a chemical. 

Caffeine

Caffeine has been proven to boost your metabolism BUT it's tricky. Most weight loss pills are mostly caffeine and pretty much just make you extremely hyper-active enough to workout. These Go-Packs claim that they aren't just full of caffeine. Still, caffeine is a drug, and you can either build up a tolerance to it, or get addicted to it. Both of those results will help hinder your weight loss. In my opinion, caffeine is good in moderation, you should not be taking caffeine pills everyday.

Body Wrap SCAM

Lately I've been seeing all these girls try something called "Body Wraps" that claim to make your waist slimmer and even take away cellulite! My first thought was "this is so bogus", so I researched it. Turns out, I was right. All of these different body wraps claiming to help girls lose weight, inches, and take away skin impurities DO NOT work!

SLIMMING BODY WRAPS: LOSING INCHES IS A LIE
Sandra Fryhofer, MD, and former president of the American Collegue of Physicians agrees. "I don't know any scientific basis for the detox," she says. Any weight loss or loss of inches is TEMPORARY. These wraps do not take the place of healthy eating and working out. It may last a day or two, but that's it.

These wraps temporarily make you feel skinnier. When you weigh yourself, you may be one or two pounds lighter but that is mostly water weight loss.

CELLULITE BODY WRAPS: BANISHING THE HOAX
The truth is this simple: cellulite body wraps won't take away the flawed skin blemishes. While the appearance may LOOK better, these wraps cannot just suck out the fat. That's not how this works. What it actually does is "plump" up the skin, making the cellulite look slightly better than before. These wraps are mostly for moisturizing the skin, not losing a few inches of cellulite.

OVERALL: If you are using body wraps and expect to have a bikini-ready body, you need to start doing your research. Healthy eating and working out is the only FOR SURE healthy way to lose those inches and to become healthier!
