Tie-Dye Obsession

Lately the Tie-Dye trend has been everywhere, & I have to admit that I'm a bit obsessed especially when its really easy to do it yourself at home!

Below are the steps on how to do the Pinch Affect Tie-Dye on a T-Shirt. There are many other ways to do Tie-Dye but I found the Pinch Affect to be my favorite, it's also the easiest and quickest way. 

  

 

  

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


What You'll Need:
-Tie-Dye Kit ( I purchased the one above at Michaels Craft Store, which includes 2 Pairs of Gloves, -Squeeze Bottles containing highly concentrated Dye, 20 Zip Ties) 
-T-Shirt or any article of clothing (Most vibrant on fibers such as 100% Cotton, Rayon and silk)
-Plastic bag or table cloth to make a huge Tie-Dye mess on :)
-Medium sized zip lock bag

Step 1

 

 

First you have to pre-wash your shirt, with the shirt still wet you will then pinch your T-Shirt on a flat surface until the shirt has been pinched all together, like the photo shown below.

Step 2

 

Next, you will have to fill up your Squeeze Bottle with water (**The Squeeze Bottles have a line to show how much water you must fill in the bottle) make sure you shake it until powder is dissolved.

Here comes the messy part, go crazy and start squirting the dye all over the T-shirt like the photo shown to the left. 

Step 3

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Now starts the waiting game, it is recommended to leave the shirt in the plastic for 3-6 hours to let the Dye settle in.  I left my T-shirt in the Plastic for about 3 Hours. The longer you let it sit the brighter the color. 

 

 

 

 

Final Step

After you have let your T-shirt sit in the plastic for a few hours you will then have to re-wash & dry it to make sure you get rid of all the excess dye. (You can hand wash the shirt) After your shirt is fully dried you are then able to see the final result which I'm sure you will love!

Here is the final result to my Tie-Dye T-shirt, I rolled up the sleeves and cut slits on the side of the shirt. Make sure you have fun and make it your own, remember to tag us on Instagram on your Tie Dye Project!
