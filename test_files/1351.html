Changing my Blog Focus

11.23.15

Hi everyone!  Since it’s crystal clear that I will never, ever, ever, ever catch up on this blog and be able to blog every session I have had, not only this year but LAST (GULP), I have decided, since I post my photography and all my favorite images from my family and child photography sessions on my Facebook page, that I want to take this blog in a little bit of a new direction.  I still plan to post many of my favorite images, and showcase photographs I love, but that won’t be it.

While I will continue, happily, to accept client sessions (see here for information), I have also been enjoying the newer aspect of my business – teaching and mentoring other photographers and designing and facilitating mini-photographer workshops we call “shoot-outs.”  As a mentor and teacher, I want to offer more information for other photographers – expert and novice alike.  Think:  nuggets of wisdom, tricks of the trade, things I learned the hard way, before and after demos, videos, and advice.  I would love to see this blog become a place to find not only images I hope will inspire you, but information and advice you can use in your own photography journey.

So here’s to the future of SallyKate Photography – I hope that you and I can learn, grow, inspire and be inspired together!

I’ll finish this post with several images from, oh, a year ago.  Since they are seasonally appropriate, it seems as good a time as any.

Limitless Workshop: May 2015

10.30.15

In May, I had the immense pleasure of conducting a photographer’s workshop in Texas with the amazing Jackie Jean.  We had four shoots (in a day and a half! Whew!), 2 lovely families and 2 styled themed shoots.  Lucky for us, the rain that had plagued North Texas for the ENTIRE month of May, dried up and went away RIGHT as our workshop started.  I’d say that was providential.  While we were exhausted, muddy and filled to the brim with photographs to edit, we were inspired, excited and successful.  The models for our workshop were darling, the weather was ideal, and the company was perfection.

The workshop began with this beautiful family of four, for a 40’s themed urban shoot.

Later that day, a circus-themed extravaganza.  So much going on, it WAS indeed a circus!  But the most fun circus EVER.

Several of the dresses and capes and headpieces provided by the amazingly awesome Honeydrops Designs.

Next day, bright and early, and I mean EARLY, we met this brave family of seven for a beautiful sunrise session.  The family woke up at four a.m. for this, can we all say “wow” to that?

Tutu Du Monde Girls’ dresses provided by Rainey’s Closet.

Gorgeous Green couch provided by Classic Vintage Vault Rentals (Southlake, TX)

And, for the finale, we had some gorgeous girls come join us for an incredible “Glamping” photo session.  Totally fun and a great way to wrap up the whirlwind weekend of photography, community and fun.

Spring Workshops with SallyKate

09.14.15

This February marked the beginning of mini-workshops, something that has become a very important aspect of SallyKate Photography.  I LOVE teaching photography and taking my joy and passion for the craft and aiming to instill that same passion for portrait photography in the hearts of other photographers.  I crave the small-group format, and the opportunity for real relationship, community and inspiration that you can get in a smaller group of like-minded individuals.  I have also been loving the opportunity to work one-on-one with many photographers over the last year, and you will be sure to see posts about that in the (hopefully near) future, here on this blog.

We will start with three indoor mini workshops I conducted in my home in February and March.  I loved the girls who came, and the work they showed me was astounding and inspiring.  AND, we all had so much fun, including our models.  Who wouldn’t want to fish in a bathtub while wearing gigantic flippers and surrounded by bubbles?  Or jump on beds without any adult telling you to stop?  We covered topics like natural light techniques, portraiture with backdrops and window light, lifestyle photography, styling, and using variety.

In April, we had an outdoor workshop, featuring a lemonade stand and an elegant tea party, finished by two 40’s beauties.  Despite the sudden onslaught of heavy rain showers on our tea party, and the storm sirens and looming threat of tornadoes which accompanied us as we all dashed to our cars at the end, it was a fun and inspiring time, and we loved every minute.  I’ll even say that it was worth it when my arm swelled to football size after I laid it in a pile of fire ants.  Hey, I got the photo I wanted.

In early June, I teamed up with the incredible Julie of Julie Peveto Photography, and we hosted an absolutely magical evening of photographic fun at a Lavender Farm in north Texas.  Gold, glittering light, beautiful foliage and surroundings, crazy-amazing models, and an incredible group of photographers made for a spectacular evening that none of us will soon forget.

The Addicted Photographer

04.28.15

{Flower Mound Child Photographer | Plano Child Photographer | Dallas Child Photographer}

I’m continuing on my merry little way, showcasing favorites from personal photography sessions and mentoring sessions throughout the year 2014.  I call myself an addicted photographer, well, because, I am ALWAYS shooting.  That’s why I’m still not done showcasing my personal work! We are finally nearing the last quarter of 2014, and today I’m showing you a few shoots I had with good friends, one in Arbor Hills in Plano, three series of photos taken in Flower Mound and in Trophy Club, Texas, and one (two actually, but that’s a long story, but let me just say that it’s good, if you are going to travel 1.5 hours, check your camera battery before you go), in the gorgeous hills of Davis, Oklahoma (Turner Falls), where we had a magnificent old castle to shoot in, as well as beautiful waterfalls and even a little bit of Autumn color.

Fall in Texas isn’t quite what it is in other parts of the world, but we were able to capture little snippets of color in our photographs.

Check out the work of Jerriann, Dana, Hilary, and Julie, some of my favorite shooting buddies.
