Freshwater Pearl Necklace

Posted on 26. Mar, 2014 by Miriam in Pearl Jewelry

Freshwater pearl necklaces can vary in quality, shape, and color more than any other pearl type. The reasons for that are:

  • Freshwater pearls come naturally in many colors.  Some natural colors are pink, peach, mauve, lavender, beige, white
  • Freshwater pearls that are not of such high quality are often dyed into a rainbow of colors.
  • Freshwater pearls do not normally have a bead nucleus used in the culturing process.  This means they can be many shapes other than round.  Perfectly round pearls do occur but are harder to find.
  • New culturing techniques used with the freshwater mussels have had success in producing very large pearls that do have a nucleus.  They are sometimes called Edison pearls.
  • Unlike the saltwater Akoya oysters, Freshwater mussels can produce pearls from very small to quite large in size.

A Freshwater pearl necklace is often less expensive than others because the mussel can produce multiple pearls at the same time.  The lower prices make these pearls ideal for very long pearl ropes.

The necklace shown in the photo is a single 100 inch strand of AA+ pearls that has been looped around the neck five times, giving the appearance of a five strand pearl necklace.  We also make multi-strand pearl necklaces of higher quality pearls using 14k gold clasp or diamond and gold clasps.  These can be custom made to any length and made with any size or type of pearls you prefer.  Just drop us a line on our contact us page or browse our multistrand pearl necklaces.

AAA Akoya Pearl Necklace

Posted on 18. Nov, 2013 by Miriam in FAQ, Pearl Jewelry

What should you look for in an Akoya pearl necklace or a cultured pearl necklace?  How do you choose the best pearls?

When buying cultured pearls the most important factor to consider is the luster.  Luster rating on Hanadama pearls is the highest in white pearls, followed by AAA Akoya Pearls, followed by AA+ Akoya Pearls, followed by the very highest quality Freshwater Pearls, such as the Freshwater Orient Pearls.

Second in importance is the shape.  Perfectly round pearls are most hightly valued.  Akoya pearls are always perfectly round because they grow around a round nucleus.  Freshwater cultured pearls must be of the very highest quality to be perfectly round.  Most AA+ and many AAA  Freshwater pearls are visibly round, but wouldn’t be perfectly round if you were to actually measure them with callipers.

The third thing to look for is the surface quality.  AAA Akoya pearls and AA+ Akoya pearls may look very similar, but if you look at them up close you will see more surface blemishing in the AA+.  The pearls are still beautiful, but they will have very tiny (pinprick size) irregularites.  These might be slight indentations etc. Over 90% clean surface and you’ll hardly be able to find them, even if you’re looking for them.  Hanadama pearls, which are the very top Akoya grade, are virtually clean.  AAA Akoya pearls are also very clean with minimal surface irregularities that are not noticeable without a magnifying glass and good light.

Another thing to look for in white pearl necklaces is whether the pearls all match.  Pearls may all be white, but the shade of white can vary slightly.  Higher quality pearls tend to be more perfectly matched in shade.

Pearl size, of course is entirely a matter of personal choice.  Usually, larger is better, but not always.  Very petite women and women who like things very dainty often prefer smaller size pearls.  The average size is 7.0-7.5mm.  The pearls shown in the photo are 7.5-8.0mm which is slightly above average size and a very popular size with the ladies.

AAA Akoya pearl necklaces are often found to be the best pearls to buy if you’re buying for someone who is very picky about details and expects the very highest quality. We almost NEVER get anyone return our AAA Akoya pearl necklaces, and never for dissatisfaction about the quality.

If you’re buying for someone who isn’t as demanding, you may find that the AAA Freshwater Pearls or the AA+ Akoya pearls make them just as happy and are easier on your pocket book! Tahitian Pearls

Posted on 30. Oct, 2013 by Miriam in FAQ, Pearl Jewelry

Tahitian pearls are an amazing gift from the sea.  Most Tahitian Pearls don’t actually come from the island of Tahiti but other islands in French Polynesia in the Pacific Ocean.  The black-lipped oyster produces “black” pearls.  But it’s the fantastic blue/green and purple iridescence that draws the eye.  A favorite color is known as “peacock” which is a combination of green, blue and purple or pink. But many other colors occur naturally in these amazing pearls.  Aubergine, cherry and green are among some of the amazing colors.  Many Tahitian pearls are a beautiful gleaming silver, sometimes with an intriguing hint of aqua, blue or other colors as an overtone.

These pearls are considerably larger than the traditional white Japanese pearls.  One outstanding pearl can be worn on its own as a pendant or ring.  Men wear them as cufflinks or on a leather strand as either necklace or bracelet.  Women wear long strands of them, or wear them as earrings and pendants.  Baroque Tahitian pearls are unusual natural shapes that often shimmer with different colors as the light hits them from different angles.  Whether as round pearls or baroque they are gorgeous gems to own.

This gift from the sea is one of nature’s most beautiful surprises.

Pink Pearl Jewelry in Support of Breast Cancer Awareness Month

Posted on 01. Oct, 2013 by Miriam in Pearl Jewelry, Specials & Giveaways

Pearl Distributors supports Breast Cancer Awareness month with Pink Pearl Jewelry.

How do you explain to a seven year old that Mommy has cancer? 

Tracey is a caring Mom who works part time as an efficient office administrator.  Her little boy is seven.  The lump in her breast appeared suddenly.  It felt tender so she made an appointment to see the doctor right away.  It’s surprising how quickly your life can be turned upside down.  The doctors said it was aggressive, invasive cancer.  Surgery was out of the question as it had already spread.  How do you explain to a seven year old?  Tracey says, “At first it was very stressful. You tend to think the worst.”

Chemotherapy was started quickly.  Tracey didn’t want her little boy to unexpectedly see her without hair, so let him watch as his Daddy shaved Mommy’s hair off so the new wig would fit well.  He cried when he saw the hair come off, but the new wig looks amazing and very natural. The chemotherapy hasn’t been as bad as she expected – but she is only part way through the treatment.  She is trying hard to keep a positive attitude, and has gradually succeeded in become more and more positive.

Tracey has found a book written for children ages 4 to 10 who have a mother with breast cancer.  It helped.  Her little boy’s life continues quite normally, even though Mommy is sometimes a bit tense, and has to go to hospital regularly for treatments.  He doesn’t understand all the uncertainties that terrify his parents.

Breast cancer prevention isn’t just scientific research.  It can mean the life of a little boy’s Mommy.  His whole world could have come crashing down.  Multiply that by hundreds of thousands of families.  Statistics show that one in 8 women could get invasive breast cancer.  Thanks to early detection and treatment most of these will survive.  Breast cancer research and treatment has saved over two million lives in the USA already.  The death rate has been going down.  Now only one in 36 women will die from breast cancer.  But more work still needs to be done to conquer this problem.

In October we have a chance to bring this issue to the attention of the world.  Thousands of businesses are going “PINK” for Breast Cancer Awareness Month and many individuals and businesses have fundraising schemes to raise money towards continued Breast Cancer Research.

Here at Pearl Distributors we are going Pink for October.  We have created a Special Pendant with one pink pearl and the Cancer Awareness Ribbon in sterling silver.  This can be worn to show support for someone who is fighting Breast Cancer or to show we care about this issue.  We have a collection of Pink Pearl Jewelry on sale.  25% of all money from our featured 8-9mm Pink Freshwater Orient pearl necklaces will be donated to the American Cancer Society.  Buy pink pearls in October and share in spreading the word and raising money to help those with breast cancer.
