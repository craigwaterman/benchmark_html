Here we go, again.

So now I’ve been requested and suggested I start putting recipes of my homemade delights on the blog. (Also, it’s been requested that I actually blog – haha. I’m going to change this around more & let people start sharing this on social media). Because I have nothing better to talk about today, I’m short for words (which is unusual for me), let’s discover how to homemake pepper sauce. The kind of pepper sauce one might use when they pile black eyed peas, lima beans, cornbread, and corn on top of each other. (I’m hungry now, which is usual – unfortunately)

Homemade Cayenne Pepper Sauce

Freshly Picked Cayenne Peppers

Pick the peppers (Yes, like Peter Piper) and take the top stems off. This is not a necessary step, but it does make it look better & not take up as much room.

Washed and de-stemmed Peppers

After picking all the stems, place them in a strainer & run them under cold water just to wash off the dirt (& it makes them look good!)

Type of bottle they were poured in

Heinz Vinegar – what I used

Depending on how many peppers depends on how much vinegar poured in the bottle. For this amount, I poured vinegar just above the top label. Pouring into the bottle first is simply a measuring tool for the amount.

 

Peppers in Boiling Water

Boil a small pot full of water. Pour the peppers in the water for exactly 20 seconds. I set a timer, but the peppers do not need to stay in the hot water longer than 20 seconds.

Poking holes in the Peppers

After the peppers drain from the hot water, use a fork and poke holes through the pepper. This allows for the flavor to mix with the vinegar in the bottle. (Extra Spicy! :))

Vinegar boiling

After you remove the peppers from the hot water, put the vinegar (that you measured earlier) in a small boiler. Let it boil while you poke holes and place the peppers in the jar (seen below).

 

 

Placing Peppers in bottle

Free Falling Pepper

Peppers in Jar

After putting the peppers in there, take the boiling vinegar off the stove. Let the peppers sit there, while you get some type of funnel to pour the hot vinegar in with (this saves you from burring yourself & making a mess at the same time!)

 

Adding Vinegar

I used an egg separated as a funnel because I couldn’t find my funnel. Whatever you have laying around works just fine!

Poking Peppers with Straw

Once the bottle is filled up with peppers and vinegar, get a straw (or some type of slender object that will fit) to poke the peppers down in the vinegar.

 

Now you are all done and the Pepper Sauce is made! I normally let mine sit for a while before it’s opened. Just gives it the chance to actually soak up the pepper taste & not just taste like vinegar!

 

The morning after

The peppers will start to space our and sink to cover the whole bottle as time goes on. Good sign of the peppers becoming infused with the vinegar!

 

Any questions leave them in the comments. Hopefully this will get a kick start for a new year and new ideas. Look for a new easy & homemade recipe soon!!

 

– SPB

Re – everything.

Revamp, reup, reorganize, redo, well you name it & it needs to be done.

Since it’s been a while, let’s revisit back to January. Let’s talk about how 3 weeks for Christmas should not qualify as a break. It’s more like a tease, which you have work to do in the middle of it anyways. So, needless to say, it’s taking me a while to get back into the swing of things. Honestly, 3 months to get back into it. It didn’t help we’ve had snow days that just made school seem pointless, alongside a strong (new found) liking that isn’t easy to leave every weekend.

So fast forward to last week, Mardi Gras break. It was one of the most fabulous weekends I have had down here. What did I do? Nothing, kinda. I went home & went to baseball games. The definition of F.AB.U.L.O.U.S. to me. Obviously, not nearly as much work got done that should have. (This whole brief that is slowly approaching is stressing me out little by little) After the break, we had two days of class. Two pointless days of class. So I took that time to reevaluate everything. That’s where we stand today.

I decided last week I would train to run a marathon, a full one – all 26.2 miles (I need prayers for that one). It’s the Nike Marathon in DC. They have firefighters (in tuxedo’s, heellooo) that hand out Tiffany’s Necklace at the end of the race. That may or may not be my motivation. Okay, it’s not my full motivation but it doesn’t hurt. Let’s talk about motivation.

Motivation, according to Webster, is a motivating force, stimulus, or influence – as a drive or incentive. So for the past, approximately two years, I’ve put the blame on not consistently working out that I had spent the last 20 years of my life in a gym. I started in a gymnastics gym at age 2, and haven’t left a form of it since. Whether it was a gymnastics gym, basketball gym, cheerleading gym, workout gym, or sometime of work out in a gym for whatever other sport I was playing, I was there. Then I left cheerleading & extracurricular sports, for good. I had had enough. However, the pressure to always work out is still there, it’s expected of a “former” college athlete, & I didn’t like it. So I rebelled. I just said, “I needed time off” or “I didn’t feel like knowing how out of shape I was” – whatever the excuse was, I found it.

Now, the excuses have ran out. There are no more to tell or make up. The gym or distaste for being out of breath (Seriously, who likes that feeling? I don’t get it), either way the gig is up – insert STYX’s Renegade lyrics. So what is my motivation? Actually it’s not a whole lot more than a simple fact, I want to look good on a boat.

So, here’s to the small starting place of the 4 miles I ran today & the staying on tract with this.

Terms of Endearment

Marriage, well there’s a scary thought for me. Actually, let me backtrack. I have been groomed to be the house wife that works (that’s just the way it is down here). I cook, clean, do laundry, and school work. It’s a lot to handle, but I feel like it’s my job. I don’t, to no surprise, don’t touch a grill or a lawn mower. I don’t ever plan on having to touch them either.

However, (like most girls – with a few exceptions) dreaming of being married has been what their whole life is pointed towards. Everything you do growing up is geared towards finding the person to settle down with …. for the rest of your life. That’s a big commitment. The rest of your life y’all, that’s like forever. Let me rephrase that, that’s a HUGE commitment. It’s the type of commitment I have been looking forward to all my life. “The American Dream” (Y’all heard the spill, I’m sure) – 3 kids, a dog, a house, two vehicles, football during the fall, baseball during the spring, beach during the summer. Sounds like paradise to me.

Then it hits me, that’s FOREVER. So I separate myself from the situation and really start to question the whole marriage idea, & if you know me (at all) you know it’s a huge thing for me to even consider to put to the side right now. I’ve been begging for multiple years to have a ring around the finger on the left hand. However, I have come to one startling but simple conclusion. (drum roll)

If I can’t decide on one an outfit to wear during a day, wear it the whole day, and be happy wearing it. I don’t need to decide who the spend the rest of my life with, not just yet at least.

I know this may not be stunning to some or many for that matter, but to me it’s an astronomical revelation that maybe, just maybe, I’m not ready to commit to something for the rest of my life. Who knows, I could meet the person I’m going to be spending the rest of my life with tomorrow or I could’ve already met them or I could be in cahoots with them right now. Who knows.

As for now, we’re just gonna focus on trying to find an outfit I can be happy with for a whole day. Wish me luck.

Double Standards.

So I guess in this day of technology, “pictures” are everything. I don’t get it, so to some (as of recently) it can almost be compared to Changing of the Guards. I know, I know, I know that the latter is WAY more important than to be casually referenced like that, but trust me on this. It’ll make sense. One changes their picture as often as they please because they “simply want a new one,” the other is supposed to respect that and merely mutter okay (knowing they don’t agree). When the other changes their picture apparently the end of the entire workings of the life has been cut. (WHAT!?) Double Standards.

The quote, “you get what you allow” could not ever be more true. So to add to complications of the story, and delay ripping the band aid off – another chance is given. Only to continue the process of the cycle.

Double Standards. Dependent, as defined, is a person who relies on another. Dependency comes like a thief in the night, and no one wants to be around someone who is actually fully dependent on someone else – or so I’ve been told. So what is to be done about it? Simple answer, to be independent again. So the switch is made and one is merely transformed, as soon as it happened before, from a dependent person to an independent person. The switch sets in and becomes a realization of the “first day of the rest of your life.” & to no surprise is realized it isn’t like much anymore. The beggar (wanting the person to be more independent) is now suddenly realizing they want them back to dependency stage. There is a simple answer to the question why, the answer is loss of control. (You can’t make your bed and lay in it too y’all). Double Standards.

Roommate story of the day (RSOTD, from now on): Bugs, they’re common down here. Especially big ones that like the indoors when its 107 degrees outside. However, I guess if you’re not from around here they can be a little scary (even though they aren’t anywhere close to being the size of your big toe, but whatever). So Rule 1: Kill it – if it crawls, step on it. If it flies, catch it or swat it. Just kill it, it’s a bug for crying out loud. In simple terms, if this unapologetic diva can kill a bug, anyone can y’all.
