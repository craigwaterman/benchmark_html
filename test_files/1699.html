The Mountains High and the Valley So Deep – I Can Get Across to the Other Side

Note: I have promised very few chronic illness posts but it is a constant consideration in my life so it does deserve some time.  Here is one of those posts.  To give you a flavor of this post, I found this YouTube video of Frankie Valli and the Four Seasons singing The Mountains High.  Start it playing and listen to it when you read this.

Last Monday, I went to the pulmonary doctor.  I had been having some trouble in the last couple months.  The coughing was worse than when I changed to the new anti-biotic and I have been having sinus problems.  Also the wheezing is keeping me awake.

But…

He said there is some new medicine, an anti-inflammatory for your lungs and bronchial tubes.  It will take care of the wheezing.  By adding this we could probably get off the prednisone!

Yippee!

The Mountain High…

He said actually the fact that we have not had to increase the prednisone means that I am getting better.  The allergy shots are likely helping!

I came home and told my family.  I was so excited.  I was up on that mountain viewing my illness from a whole different perspective.  There is HOPE!

Sliding down that mountain…

  • They said that they did not think there was any improvement in my coughing.
  • By the next day,
  • the wheezing was bothersome again.
  • I was coughing up stuff that looked like infection.
  • I was blowing stuff out my nose that looked like infection.
  • Maybe I should call the doctor to get an additional antibiotic.

The Valley is so low I don’t know if I can get to the other side…

 

I write this art journal page, starting out with the sunshine of the mountain high and what the doctor said.  Notice the little clouds up high.  Then the black curved line and dark blue represent overnight and the doubts that flood in.  By the next day, the clouds are big and creating a big shadow over my life.  What a difference a day makes.  But in the daylight of the next day I make a conclusion…

There is hope.  I need to trust God.

I am determined.

I am not going to give in.

Maybe if I try increasing  …

  • my liquids intake
  • the humidity in the rooms

I do.

It helps.

Here it is a week later and I have made it to IVIG infusion week.  It is okay if I am a little more tired.  It is expected.  I need to keep my fluid intake up to prepare.

I keep taking my new medicine.  I have a  breathing functionality test scheduled for a couple of weeks.  I have not had one since 2012.  I go back to see my pulmonary doctor  in three weeks.  I am confident we will be able to wean me off the prednisone.  He said that since I have been on it so long, we will need to wean off it 1 mg per month.  He said my body will not even realize it.  It will take 10 months.  Alas, here we go!

Don’t you give up til  I reach the other side

Artwork as of 2/8/2015

This update is for 2 weeks worth.

Last weekend, I went to the art museum in Van Wert and then the next day to Michigan for several days so I did not have as much time as I usually do.  I took my little journal and pens to work while on the road.  In fact much of my work was actually while we were driving so there is more jittery lines than I usually would include.

To start with, we were getting windows on the first floor of our house in January with a high temperature of 8 degrees so I had to move everything from in front of the windows in the Scrapbooking Room.  So while I was at it, I decided to beef up my paper files and make an additional one.  I also got new glasses and my box for in the medicine cabinet was too small for the new glasses case so I made a new box.

I worked on a travel journal while gone.  It did not include Stahl’s Automotive Foundation because that was too big to add to the page.

Julie Fey Fan Balzer is have a 28 patterns challenge and so I worked on some patterns for that.  #28PATTERNS (http://balzerdesigns.typepad.com/balzer_designs/28patterns/)

I finished up my Journal 52 Week 5 Television (http://journal52.com/) just in time to get the next challenge.  I used deli paper to add a fold-over to my page because I had more to say than would fit on the 2 page spread.

And, last but not least, I also worked 2 hours on my watercolor painting of Rusty with the goal in mind to just get rid of as much white as possible.  I really got a lot done in that time.

I forgot to mention that I also updated the look of my blog so I could add a link to my Pinterest boards.  This version is the third I tried before I figured it out.  I also contacted Leann Cole (http://leannecolephotography.com/), to ask how to make a gallery.  I saw one on her blog and wanted to try it myself.  I set up a gallery page of my watercolor paintings and I updated my about page.  I actually got a lot done.

New Glasses – I can see clearly now!

New glasses! This is just my second pair of bifocals.

Since I wear contacts all the time, it has probably been 10 years with the same pair but they did not seem to be good enough when living my new artist lifestyle.

So I got new bifocals and picked them up just before our recent trip to Michigan.  Also, I had just had my IVIG infusion on Thursday before we left on Saturday so I was still drinking extra fluids, going caffeine free, and taking it easy.

On Sunday morning, I could hardly keep my eyes open while watching TV in the hotel room so I decided to take out my contacts and allow myself a little nap.  I put on my new glasses.

Within about 20 minutes, our son, Don, called to say he was back and ready for lunch.  I decided it was too much trouble to put my contacts back in so I went to lunch with my new glasses.

We picked up Don, after the lengthy snow engulfed drive to pick him up (see my blog post Field Trip – Michigan in January and Stahl’s Automotive Foundation ).  We went to Olive Garden.

When we sat down, I looked around and saw beautiful pictures on the wall of Italy.  I could pretend I was looking out the window at those sites.  I could also see the deep snow and how fast the plowing trucks were driving through the parking lot.  I was wondering how precise their plowing is, since we have a truck sitting out there that is a little less than a year old.

Then I looked at my food.  I could see every detail.  I realized it had been a long time since I had seen so clearly.

This reminded me of when I was a kid and my Mom would take me to pick up my new glasses, every year starting in 4th grade.  I thought you might enjoy seeing the style back then.  Anyway, I would put on those new glasses and look out the window and I could see every blade of grass and every leaf on the trees.  It was amazing.

It reminded me of that old song: “I can see clearly now the rain is gone.  I can see all obstacles in my way.”

I have to say that I still cannot see all obstacles in my way.  I don’t know what kind of glasses that would require.  I can see some and others, only the Lord can see.  Walking forward in life is trusting God to watch out for you.  I learned a long time ago that control of your life was just an illusion.  I have to continually let go and let God!

In honor of new glasses, I found this video for you.  It is Johnny Nash singing I can see clearly now.  Take a gander and enjoy your day!

Field Trip – Michigan in January and Stahl’s Automotive Foundation

I have always wanted to go to Mackinaw City in the winter.  I was wondering what it was like with lots of snow and cold.  Well, my dear husband, Bob, and I went to Michigan in January – not quite up to Mackinaw City but I think we got the feel for snow and cold.

We went up to Mount Pleasant to visit with our son, Don, who is a graduate student at Central Michigan University.  We got there Saturday and it was relatively nice – a balmy 29 degrees F.  We were excited to be missing the winter snow storm that was going to hit home.

But wait…

The storm decided to hit mid and lower Michigan too!

On Sunday at lunch time, we got out into the truck to this…

The latest forecast said 1 to 3 inches on Sunday and 1 to 3 inches on Sunday night.  By lunch time, there was at least 5 inches.  We turned on the 4 wheel drive and we were off.  I love my truck!  She handled beautifully!

As you can see, Bob wasn’t sure yet.

On Monday, about lunch time, it was now 5 degrees F.  Check mark snow.  Check mark cold.  Check mark Michigan in the winter.  We left Mount Pleasant to drive to Birch Run.  The roads were mostly clear except for the highway rest stops and exits.  We did a little outlet shopping until I needed to rest.  We had a lovely suite in the Country Inn & Suites so we watched a little TV.  We saw Little Women starring Audrey Hepburn.

On Tuesday, we were off to Stahl’s Automotive Foundation in a Detroit suburb.

Wow!

Wow!

Wow!!!!

In the lobby, they have several music machines: a couple played violins and there were several juke boxes.  What fun!

Then we went into the car room.  What fun!

I actually got to see a real 1948 Tucker – up close and in person – I got to lean inside!  I have always wanted to see one up close since I saw the movie and saw the replica they made for the movie in Ypsilanti and saw the real one from a great distance at the Henry Ford museum.  Look, they opened the door so I could get a good shot inside.  Also, look, you can see the light inside the middle headlight and you can see how it pivots to follow the direction you turn the wheel.  It was hard for me to move on.  I was so amazed and in awe.

But I moved on and I was fascinated with all the hood ornaments.

That black car in the middle, above, is a Duisenberg.

They had several woodies and a couple Pierce Arrows.  I have wanted to see a Pierce Arrow since I read Cheaper By The Dozen.  They even had a Pierce Arrow Travel Trailer!  The bottom right picture, above, is on the console of the theater pipe organ which they played while we were looking around.  They even played Phantom of the Opera!  It was lovely!

For my arty friends (if you don’t think the above is art)…

They also had a calliope and I got a few close-ups of the beautiful painted flowers.  The iridescent colors were beautiful.

Of course there was a lot more – like the Shelby Cobra which they started for us.

But, it was time for our trip home.  While on the way, we both sang with Neil Sedaka and I am reminded of the free meals we got at McDonald’s the day before.  Bob had to make his lady smile so he said we had been married for 29 years and we are still enjoying our time together.
