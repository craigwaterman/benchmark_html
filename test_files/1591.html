Monday, January 20

Mani Monday For Audrey French


I am so excited to be back at it doing my Mani Mondays!  Today I did a simple French manicure using two China Glaze colors.  I am loving this mani.  Its cute and girly and all the things I love about painting my nails.  French manis always make me feel so fancy!  I sometimes use tape, but decided to use a different method this time.  Keep reading to see how I made this cute mani.

Materials: 1 or 2 nail polishes colors.  I chose China Glaze For Audrey and Secret Peri-wink-le, Nail polish remover, a nail/makeup brush, paper towels and a small cup.  I also used a high shine clear top coat.
 
1.  Clean and buff nails.  This is especially important when doing a clear nail manicure so nails looks bright and clean.  Apply base coat. Click here to see a review of my current fave.
2.  Using For Audrey, take the brush out from bottle and remove all polish.  Then dip the brush back in a small amount.  Turn sideways and create a thick horizontal stripe on the tip of the nail.  Repeat this on each nail, alternating colors if desired.
3.  Repeat step 2 on each nail tip for your second coat.  Allow to dry completely. 
4.  Pour some nail polish remover into your small cup and dip your nail/makeup brush in.  Dab the excess on a paper towel.  Gently wipe the stripe you made in step 2 to remove polish and make a clean even line.  Do this on each nail.
5.  Using your favorite clear coat, I used Sally Hansen Mega Shine, paint just the clean area of your nails. Allow to dry.
6.  Top with your favorite top coat!  I painted another coat of Mega Shine.

Mani Monday Drippin Dots!

This Monday I wanted to try out a nail art technique I see a lot on Instagram.  There are many different ways to create a "dotticure".  Have your own fun with it.  Dotticure nail art is much easier than it looks.  This one is an optical illusion. Keep reading to see how I made this cute nail art.
 
 
 Materials: 2 polishes of your choice.  I chose Ulta XOXO (pink glitter) and Snow White.
 
1.  Paint your nails with your favorite top coat. I am using Sally Hansen Hard as Nails.
2.  Paint your nails two coats of white.  Allow to dry completely.
3.  Tape nails off halfway diagonally. I taped from corner edge to opposite corner cuticle, with tape over my fingers. Leaving top part of your nails open to paint. Like this: \
4.  Paint untapped half with XOXO. Peel tape when dry. 
5.  Using a large dotting tool, add dots with XOXO. The dots should be touching the line created by the tape over the white polish.
 You will notice that as you create dots, the pattern emerges and looks like you made two sets of dots.  You only have to make that one set of dots. I went back in with white dots to fix a few I didn't like.
6.  Top with your favorite top coat! I am using Julep Freedom these days.

Mani Monday Sliver nails

 
I made this manicure using two Sinful Colors nail polishes.  I love their nail polish.  The blue nail polish is Love Nails and the Pink is Bikini.  This is some more nail art I made using scotch tape.  Scotch tape is so easy to use and creates smooth, clean lines.  Keep reading to see how I made this cute nail art!
 
Materials:  Two bottles of your favorite nail polish.  I chose Love Nails and Bikini by Sinful Colors.
 
1.  Paint your nails with your favorite base coat.  I am using Sally Hansen Nail Hardener these days.

Mani Monday Firework Nails!

 
This past July 4th I wanted to do some festive nailart.  I decided to paint some fireworks. At first I was just playing around.  But as a pattern developed, I was starting to like them.  These nails are kind of random and I really like the way they turned out!  They can be for any occasion, not just holidays. Keep reading to find out how I made this cute nail art. 

Materials: Red, white and blue nail polish, glitter polish, nail brushes or stripers.
1.  Paint a base coat with Ulta Snow White. I did two coats.
2.  After drying completely, Start making asterisk star patterns large or small by dipping a small paintbrush into the red polish. Make some red stars on each nail.
3.  Do the same with each polish, making the pattern as random as you like. I made some strokes light or dark for effect.
4. Go back and add some glitter! Add glitter randomly for that cute sparkly look of fireworks. 
5.  Top with your favorite top coat.

Mani Monday Glitter Stripe


I am loving this new 8 nail polish set I recently picked up at Sephora.com.  You can read my post about that haul here! Slowly I've been trying out each color.  I started with this gorgeous metallic green and blue. This nail art was fairly easy to do! The gold stripe covers any flaws.  Keep reading to see how I made this cute mani.

First gather two of your favorite nail polishes.  I chose the metallic light blue and green from Fashion Week VIP by The New Black.  And grab a glitter striper or nail polish and a striper paintbrush.  I am using Milani #701 Nail Art stripers, which I used a few months ago pictured here.  

1.  Paint all of the nails with your bottom color.  I chose this metallic mint green.  Paint 1-2 coats. 
2.  Paint the top half of each nail with your second color.  I used this pretty periwinkle.  Don't worry about mistakes, they can be corrected later with the striper. Allow to dry completely. 
3.  With your glitter striper or polish with a brush, draw a thin line over where the two colors meet. Try to rotate your finger, not the brush.
4.  After drying top with your favorite topcoat and wrap the tips!

Mani Monday Glitter Gradient

 
This week's  ManiMonday nail art is glitter inspired.  I love glitter!  This manicure I so pretty, it may be my new favorite.  This is my first try at a glitter gradient and I am loving the way it came out! Keep reading to see how I made this cute nail art using only three colors and no tools. 
 
Pantone Reflecting Pond, Ulta Tink-her-Bell, Ulta Love Fern
 
Materials:﻿
I started by gathering these three nail polishes:  Pantone Universe Reflecting Pond,  Ulta Tink-her-Bell and Love Fern.  I didn't use any tools for this nail art.  I wanted two similar nail polish colors and a complimentary glitter.
