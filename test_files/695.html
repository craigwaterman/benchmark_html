SPOTLIGHT: LEE FRASER (HALIFAX)

azra 2 years ago

DJ Mag Canada regularly chats with local DJs across the country who are building a name for themselves in their scene. Here, we talked to Lee Fraser from Halifax, N.S.

DJ Mag Canada: What is your present state of mind?

Fraser: Excited. 2k14 is going to be a good year!

DJ Mag Canada: Tell us about your musical journey up until now?

Fraser: It’s a love affair that’s lasted over 15 years. I really got into music, more importantly electronic music, when I was 12 or 13. I saved up bucks over the summer and bought a pair of Gemini XL100’s – the absolute worst turntables I’ve ever used. I was always in and out of production but I focused more on the technical aspect of DJing like competing in DJ competitions and being behind the scene running and promoting events. At the time I had to teach myself everything…no YouTube tutorials, no other people to help or show me what’s what so at the time music production was like climbing Everest for me…the joys of living in Atlantic Canada. After around ’06/’07 I decided to really focus on my productions but it’s been hard to juggle while travelling around the world working in the offshore diving industry. I linked up with my boy Dezza about five years ago and he taught me a lot.  We launched Dezire Records soon after and have been working together since.

DJ Mag Canada: Name five industry people who have influenced your career as an artist in Canada?

Fraser: It’s hard to narrow down. Artist wise over the last 15+ years it’s changed but early on it was most definitely Bad Boy Bill & DJ Dan. I don’t know how many times we sat listening to the old “Bangin the Box” mixes. Now it’s changed to guys like Mark Knight, Axwell, Dirty South and of course Tommy Trash who really influences my sounds and productions. But what really influences me day to day is my crew I’ve got around me…Bill, Dezza, Neon Dreams, ECL (East Coast Lifestyle) crew, and the whole AFTRDRK Crew.. We’ve all really come together over the last year on a lot of projects and 2k14 is going to be a great year for EDM in Eastern Canada!

DJ Mag Canada: What projects are you currently working on?

Fraser: Well we’ve just finished a eight stop Atlantic Canadian Tour with East Coast Lifestyle guys, Dezza and Neon Dreams I’ve also got all of our AFTRDRK Shows each week at STFX in Antigonish (which are absolutely nuts) as well as lots of upcoming stuff in Halifax. I’m really working hard with my camp on taking AFTRDRK nationally in 2k14 and hopefully internationally after that.

Music wise I’ve got a few singles coming out in the new year – you can check some previews out on my Soundcloud – I’ve also got a remix I did for the Neon Dreams boys coming out very soon. On top of that I’m taking the winter off to work on an artist album which I’m really excited about. I don’t think I ever stop.

DJ Mag Canada: What has been your most memorable event?

Fraser: It’s a tie between having 800 people singing “Happy Birthday” to me on our stop at the Evolve Music Fest during the Heavy Shorts Tour a few years ago and when I won the DJ Olympics in ’08 or ’09. They gave me 2500 big bux and I bought two trays of tequila, which I basically had to drink myself. Apparently Halifax doesn’t like tequila.

DJ Mag Canada: When not making music, where do you direct your energy?

Fraser: Managing events and spending way too much time in a hyperbaric chamber. A fella’s got to make a living ya know!?

DJ Mag Canada: Describe yourself in five words or less.

Fraser: Late nights and loud music.

DJ Mag Canada: Who is your fictional hero and why?

Fraser: Sonny Crockett from Miami Vice. Cars, women, Miami, sunshine and 80’s suits. What more can a guy want!?

DJ Mag Canada: Who is your real-life hero and why?

Fraser: Whoever decided to make Jalepeño Tabasco sauce! Do you really have to ask why?

DJ Mag Canada: If you could take any musician in the past or present to dinner, who would it be?

Fraser: Ashley MacIsaac from the mid-late nineties.

DJ Mag Canada: What did you eat for breakfast today?

Fraser: Coffee, baileys, eggs and double smoked bacon… and more coffee.

DJ Mag Canada: If you could travel in time to witness any one historical event, which would it be?

Fraser: The exact point when Clamatto Juice was invented…who really thinks up a juice combo out of clam juice and tomatoes??

DJ Mag Canada: Finish the following sentence.

“If I had a million dollars, I’d…”

Keck: “…who cares.”
