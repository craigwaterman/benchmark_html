How Safe Are Your
Carpet Cleaning Chemicals?

What's in your carpet cleaning chemicals?  Are you being exposed to toxic, caustic and even carcinogenic ingredients?

Formulations that contain harmful and dangerous ingredients present a real danger to both the user and building occupants. Exposure to these harmful cleaning products pose health risks that range from allergies, asthma, chemical
sensitivities, greater worker absenteeism and even cancer.

The purpose of cleaning the carpet is to remove soil and the nasty stuff that gets dragged in or spilled on the carpet. Where is the logic in removing dirt and harmful contaminants from carpeting using carpet cleaning chemicals that leave
behind toxic residues or volatile organic compounds? 

After all, the real goal of cleaning your carpet is not to just improve the appearance of the carpet. It's to remove dirt and harmful contaminants that can negatively affect your health. To provide a healthier indoor environment.

It's bad enough a homeowner that has their carpet cleaned once every year or two is subject to toxic cleaners. But what about a carpet cleaning technician or janitor that works with these chemicals on a daily basis? Or the building
occupant that spends their workday in an environment full of toxic pollution from cleaning products?

Now you might be thinking the answer is green cleaning products. With regard to cleaning products, "green" is the buzzword.  But the problem with the majority of today's green cleaning products is they give more weight to the environmental impact than the impact on human health and safety.

Many carpet cleaning chemical manufacturers formulate their products just enough to satisfy government environmental regulations, thus allowing their cleaner to be labeled "green". However, further investigation will reveal ingredients are included that are proven to be toxic, caustic and even carcinogenic to humans.

The concern for our environment's well being is given priority over human health and safety. Shouldn't it be the other way around? Just because a product is environmentally friendly doesn't mean it's human health and safety friendly. Formulate carpet cleaning chemicals which give human health and safety priority and the environmental concerns will address themselves.

Which carpet cleaning chemicals should you choose?

The answer is safer and healthier cleaning products that are environmentally responsible. Carpet cleaning chemicals that are non toxic, odor free, detergent free and hypoallergenic. Carpet cleaners that contain NO volatile organic
compounds (VOC's), NO enzymes, NO bacteria, NO bleach and NO toxic oxidizing or reducing agents.
